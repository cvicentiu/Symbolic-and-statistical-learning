Home of the Brooks Dowsett family name. Last updated 12 April 2003

This site will be a family tree of the BrooksDowsett lineage. Only members that are older than 100 years or deceased will be included. I will state that the combination of Brooks and Dowsett is unique because every Brooks Dowsett, which has been found have been linked back to the same lineage.

The name began in Toppesfield in 1811, with the baptism of William Brooks Dowsett. The church baptism record is:

"Dowsett William Brooks, baseborn son of Elizth Dowsett having been baptised on Septm 1811 was recievd into the congregation Decm 26th 1812". (ERO D/P 163/1/3)

The significant point is that the records of baptism in Toppesfield church identify William as "baseborn", a term used to describe an illigitmate child. Williams mother was recorded in the church records as Elizabeth Dowsett. It is reasonable to assume that William's father surname was Brooks and probably a William Brooks.

The Toppesfield "overseers of the poor" records for later years, include payment for "travel to Bardfield twice after Mr Brooks money".

This site is new and will be built over time to reflect the hundreds of hours already spent on this research. The first William (there is more than one in the lineage) maye well have been adopted by an older sister !, was a cordwainer, one of the first policeman, in the Witham division and Essex (no.75). He became the equivalent of todays Seargent pretty quickly.
