Saturday July 14

Field Crew
 Hagen Klieb
Jonathan Brooks
Craig Stull
Eric Van Velzen
Kimberly Monk

Today's fieldwork concentrated on retrieving wood and stone samples from the Sligo site.  A table of the samples retrieved may be found in the archaeology section.  Samples will be sent for analysis to the Center for Archaeological Investigations at Southern Illinois University, Carbondale, where they will be scientifically tested for their respective wood typology. The complications of removing samples from the Great Lakes environment for scientific analysis was fully realized with these dives.  A short bottom time, thick neoprene gloves, silting and tough wood resulted in a Marx brother's routine underwater.  

Jonathan Brooks, on camera,  records provenience of stempost sample being removed by Kimberly Monk.  
L-R: Brian McLennan, Eric Van Velzen, Kimberly Monk, Jonathan Brooks, Hagen Klieb, Craig Stull

This will be the last field log, but the land lubber's work has just barely begun.   The next few weeks will be spent in the archives compiling shipping registers that will better illustrate the markets Sligo was employed for.  Additional time will be directed toward CAD illustrations of the Sligo's associated artifacts and the mosaic compilation.
