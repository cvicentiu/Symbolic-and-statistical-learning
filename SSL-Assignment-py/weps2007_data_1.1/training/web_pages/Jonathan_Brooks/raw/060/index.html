"Blastoise Kid" Deck
Fan Deck
By Jonathan Brooks

August 28, 2001 - Submit your Pokémon Decks to us for an examination by the experts! Check out our submission guidelines to find out how to submit your decks.

"Blastoise Kid" Deck:

Pokémon (21)
4 Cleffa (Neo Genesis)
3 Onix (Neo Genesis)
3 Steelix (Neo Genesis)
4 Squirtle (Team Rocket)
4 Dark Wartortle (Team Rocket)
3 Dark Blastoise (Team Rocket)

Trainer (21)
3 Chaos Gym (Gym Challenge)
3 Double Gust (Neo Genesis)
4 Gold Berry (Neo Genesis)
3 Misty's Wrath (Neo Genesis)
2 Narrow Gym (Gym Heroes)
4 Professor Elm (Neo Genesis)
2 Time Capsule (Neo Genesis)

Energy (18)
14 Water Energy
4 Metal Energy (Neo Genesis)

Where did the deck get its name?
Since we are just starting out our Trainer Deck section, we used the deck that won the 2nd day of the "10 and under" age division of the East Coast Super Trainer Showdown 2001. The maker of the deck, Jonathan Brooks, has been given the nickname "Blastoise Kid," so it was all too appropriate to name this deck after its maker.

What is the strategy of this deck?
Jonathan loves Blastoise, and since he couldn't use the Blastoise from Base Set 2 because of the Rocket On! format rules, he decided he would use Dark Blastoise! Knowing how powerful Steelix could be, Jonathan decided to put Steelix into the deck. By simply having Steelix in play, Jonathan can keep his Dark Blastoise on the Bench until it has enough Energy attached to attack. While he is waiting to power up Dark Blastoise, he can still attack with Steelix. Cleffa is included in the deck so that Jonathan can quickly draw more and more cards to get what he needs.

Why did you use some of the Trainer cards that you did?
Misty's Wrath and Professor Elm are there so that Jonathan can get through his deck quickly. The quicker he can get Dark Blastoise or Steelix ready, the quicker he can win the game. The choice of Stadium cards, Narrow Gym and Chaos Gym, were definitely good for the Rocket On! format. Jonathan can either prevent his opponent from using Trainer cards, or he can quickly lower his opponent's Bench so that he can go through his opponent's Pokémon quickly.

Master Trainer's Review
Of course the Master Trainers have to throw in a word or two! I love this deck! Not only because it is so powerful, but because Jonathan used a completely different idea than everyone else! There wasn't a single other Top 8 deck that used Dark Blastoise! Jonathan was very smart in his choice of Pokémon types. Since Steelix has Weakness to Fire Pokémon, and since most Fire Pokémon have Weakness to Water Pokémon, Jonathan can go up against Fire decks without having to worry about Steelix's Weakness! In fact, his opponents will have to worry about their Pokémons' Weaknesses! The only thing that I would have done differently is taken out the 2 Time Capsules and included 2 Secret Mission Trainer cards from the Gym Heroes set. Doing this would allow Jonathan to draw even more cards throughout out the game. Other than that, this deck is simply amazing! So amazing that it won Jonathan the "10 and under" Championship!
