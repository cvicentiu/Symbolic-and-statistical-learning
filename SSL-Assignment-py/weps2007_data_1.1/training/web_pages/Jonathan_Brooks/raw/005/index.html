Jonathan Brooks

Hometown: Danbury

Major: Biology

WCSU Degree: B.A. Biology – Certification in Secondary Education

Honors and Awards: Dean’s List fall 2000, spring and fall 2001, fall 2003, spring 2004, Cum Laude graduate

Internships: Pharmaceutical company

Jonathan Brooks came to WestConn in 2001. His path to a biology degree and a secondary education certification to teach biology certainly was not direct.

“Music — classical guitar — was originally what I had in mind as a major, as this is something I love doing to this day,” Brooks says. “However, I have always loved the outdoors and the subject of biology. After taking Bio 100, which was taught by Assistant Professor of Biological and Environmental Sciences Dr. Edwin Wong, I realized how great studying the subject would be, hence my decision to further my education on the subject.

“At first I was not quite sure where I wanted to head within the bio field. After taking many factors into consideration, I began working at a pharmaceutical company while still in school. I thoroughly enjoyed doing so and after graduation obtained employment as a microbiologist with another pharmaceutical company, which brings me to present day. My interest in biology has not slowed down one bit, and has pushed me to enter the field of teaching biology. I am currently enrolled in the WCSU Certification Only program for Secondary Education in Biology. I am excited to enter into a career where I can share my knowledge and excitement for the subject.” 

Brooks says he came to WestConn because it was local and because of its reputation. He’s currently working full-time at Watson Laboratories in Danbury while attending WestConn to obtain his teaching certificate.

Asked what he will remember most about his WestConn experience, Brooks says, “Lab activities.”

After graduation, Brooks plans to become a high school biology teacher.

Brooks’ advice to new students entering WCSU:  “Take this seriously. WestConn has a lot to offer.”
