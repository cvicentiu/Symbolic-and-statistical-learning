Captain Mark Johnson
Retired, United States Coast Guard
Principal, C&H Patriot Security
Former Deputy Assistant Administrator of the Transportation Security Administration

Captain Johnson, the Director of Transportation Security of C& H Patriot Security, has over 26 years experience with transportation security, safety, and crisis management.  During his service as Deputy Assistant Administrator for Maritime and Land Security at the Transportation Security Administration, Captain Johnson designed the national transportation security strategy for all non-aviation modes. He led the establishment of security standards to improve cargo, passenger, and infrastructure security as well as response preparedness following a security incident.  He has worked closely with Congressional staff, senior administration and international government executives as well as senior industry stakeholders to craft and implement transportation security policy.

Captain Johnson is a sought after speaker on many transportation security topics including domain awareness, risk management, innovative approaches to supply chain and passenger chain security, and preparing for mitigating the impact of a security incident.

Prior to TSA, Captain Johnson was a career U. S. Coast Guard marine safety, security, and environmental protection officer.  A former Captain of the Port and Officer in Charge of Marine Inspection, Captain Johnson led the successful response to the largest chemical release in the State of Georgia (Powell-Duffryn fire and spill).  He also served as the Chairman of the U.S. Port Security Committee and helped redesign the Coast Guard’s port security mission following the September 11th, 2001 tragedy.

Captain Johnson was awarded a Bachelor of Science degree (History and Government) from the U.S. Coast Guard Academy and a Master of Science degree (Systems Management) from the University of Southern California.  He also served as a Federal Executive Fellow at The Brookings Institution in Washington, D.C.
