   Acerca de Mark Johnson
Mark Johnson started out writing songs and playing guitar for Sheffield indie band The Bollweevils along with singer Sarah Griffiths. They released two 12" singles, "Talk to Me" (co-written with friend Ray Russell) and "Mouth" on London label Decoy/Vinyl Solution, but after a disastrous spell with a dodgy manager which ended with all of the band's equipment being impounded in a North Wales studio, The Bollweevils split. Mark and Sarah went on to form Bolster together with bass player Hugh Ruiz (now with Jim Muir Slideshow) and guitarist Clare Lord (now fronting Lyra), and set up their own Slappa Records label, releasing a 7" single (That's All) and two CD singles (Bedroom Wall and Devil). According to the NME ''Bolster paint with thick brushes - drawing in the stop-start dynamism of the Pixies, the drugsick roar of My Bloody Valentine and the gentle sizzle of The Sundays, finally creating a beautifully abstract picture that whispers as loudly as it yells''. Despite various line-up changes, including Sarah's sister Jane on bass, Bolster continued to write and record, but following the birth of their daughter Mark and Sarah found it difficult to dedicate enough time to the band and things started to wind down. Then suddenly Mark's world was torn apart when Sarah left and that was the end of Bolster, but the start of something new....As well as working on his own stuff, Mark has recently collaborated with fellow Sheffielder Stephen Singleton (ABC, Vice Versa, Bleep & Booster), providing guitar on two tracks of his solo album. Sarah can now be found with sister Jane in Gangs of Feral Youth.
