Dr. T. Mark Johnson

We want to exceed the expectations of our patients. By this I mean their expectations for personal as well as medical treatment.
- Dr. T. Mark Johnson
Dr. Johnson is a surgeon at the National Retina Institute (NRI). He joined NRI from the Kellogg Eye Center at the University of Michigan. Dr. Johnson is a specialist in the diagnosis, medical, and surgical treatment of diabetic retinopathy, macular degeneration, complex retinal detachments, ocular trauma, and ocular infections and inflammations.

In addition to his clinical practice, Dr. Johnson conducts research at NRI. Currently he is in the midst of clinical trials examining the role of drugs that control excess blood vessel growth in macular degeneration and diabetic retinopathy. He is also involved in several studies examining the role of corticosteroids in the treatment of chronic macular edema.

Dr. Johnson lectures locally and nationally in continuing medical education programs.

Recognized for his leadership and research accomplishments, Dr. Johnson has earned awards from numerous national, public, and private groups including the Canadian government, Toronto Eye Foundation, University of Ottawa, and the Kellogg Eye Center.

Dr. Johnson earned his B.S. and M.D. degrees from the University of Western Ontario in London, Ontario, Canada. He completed a residency in ophthalmology at the University of Ottawa in Ontario, Canada, followed by a fellowship in vitreoretinal surgery at the Kellogg Eye Center. He is board certified in ophthalmology in the United States and is a fellow of the Royal College of Physicians and Surgeons of Canada.
