LANGSTON SCOTT ADAMS
The Law Offices of Langston Scott Adams

The Law Offices of Langston Scott Adams, specializes in personal injury, family, probate and criminal law.

Adams, a native Port Arthuran and graduate of Lincoln High School, received his B.A. in Political Science in 1995 and his M.A. in Educational Administration and Supervision in 1998 from Xavier University of Louisiana. He earned his State Bar of Texas license in November, 2002 and received his Juris Doctorate from St. Mary’s School of Law, San Antonio in 2002. 
Adams is a member of the American Bar Association, Association of Trial Lawyers of America, Jefferson County Bar Association, Texas Criminal Defense Lawyers Association and the National Association of Criminal Defense Lawyers.


 

IKE AKBARI
ITEX Property Management, LLC

K. T. “Ike” Akbari has spent over twenty years in all aspects of property development, construction and property management.  He purchased several properties from the Hayes Family and his company ITEX Property Management, LLC manages over 1500 apartments units in Port Arthur.  He is presently working on development of several apartment complexes throughout the area, a single family subdivision known as Greenway Point Estates, and has several houses under construction.
Akbari has served as Director and President of the Apartment Association of South East Texas, Director and President of the Affordable Housing Management Association of East Texas,  Board of Directors of the Greater Port Arthur Chamber of Commerce, and the Rotary Club.  He was awarded “Small Business Person of the Year” by the Port Arthur Chamber of Commerce in 2005.
Akbari has a BS in Physics and a minor in Mathematics from Tehran University, Tehran, Iran and a Master of Engineering in Industrial Engineering from Lamar University.



YOSHI ALEXANDER

City of Port Arthur

 

Yoshi D. KEN BROUSSARD

Magnum Fire and Safety Systems

 

Ken Broussard is president of Magnum Fire & Safety Systems which was established in 1980.  Broussard was raised in Port Arthur, graduated from Bishop Byrne High School, served in the U.S. Coast Guard Reserves and was employed at Gulf Oil for 13 years as a welder and fire fighter.  After starting Magnum Fire & Safety Systems, he was nominated to represent Port Arthur in the Small Businessman of the Year program on the national level.  In 1986, Broussard was awarded third in the United States as Small Businessman of the Year by President Ronald Regan in Washington D.C.   At that time there were 15 million small businesses represented.
Magnum continues to be involved in manufacturing, engineering, design, research and development of custom equipment for the industrial and government market.  Holding several patents, Magnum has recently designed products for the U.S. Army and the U.S. Marine Corps.
Broussard is married to his lovely wife, Linda, of 32 years with one daughter, Shea, who lives and works in Austin.

 

DR. IRMA VARGAS DAILEY, D.O.
Physician


Dr. Dailey is board certified in Family Practice and moved to this area seven years ago.  She decided recently to open her practice because she felt there was a need for a Spanish-speaking female physician in this area. Her practice provides nutritional care, a weight loss program, and manipulation of the spine when indicated. Neuromuscular rehabilitation is also offered as an alternative to pain management, and promoting anti aging procedures is a new area of service in Dr. Dailey’s office.

Dr. Dailey is originally form El Paso, Texas.  She is fluent in Spanish and at one time was able to speak Russian as well. She graduated from medical school in Des Moines, Iowa. She has trained in family practice and internal medicine at Texas Tech and William Beaumont Army Medical Center in El Paso, Texas as well as Doctors Hospital in Groves, Texas.
Dr. Dailey is married and has two children and her husband is an instructor at Lamar University. She is an active member of her church, and a member of several medical associations.  Due to Hurricane Rita damage, she has moved her practice to 3318 Hwy 365, Nederland, Texas.

WAYNE MOORE
Christus St. Mary Hospital
 
Wayne Moore was appointed the Administrator of CHRISTUS St. Mary Hospital on November 1. 2004.  He has been a member of St. Mary’s team for the past thirteen years and member of the Senior Team for the past 6 years. 
He began is tenure at the hospital in April 1992 as Accounting Manager.  He has been active in every aspect of the financial area of CHRISTUS St. Mary, serving as Accounting Director, Financial Analyst for the Southeast Texas Region, Controller, Assistant Administrator and Chief Financial Officer for the hospital. 
Moore is a board member of the CHRISTUS St. Mary Hospital PHO representing the various physician specialists within the membership.  He serves on the Regional Managed Care Committee with responsibility for reviewing hospital insurance contracts, and at the CHRISTUS Health system level, he serves on the Corporate Finance Committee.  Moore also serves on the Board of Directors for Hughen School.
Growing up in the Mid-County area, Moore has established strong roots in Southeast Texas.  He earned a Bachelor in Business Administration from Lamar University in 1986 and a Master in Business Administration in 1990, also from Lamar University. 


 


JUDGE ROBERT "BOB" MORGAN
Jefferson County

Morgan was elected the Jefferson County Precinct 2 Justice of the Peace in 2001.  He ran for Mayor in 1994 to fill an unexpired term and was elected Mayor in August 1994.  He was re-elected in May of 1995, serving until June of 1998. He was appointed to fill the unexpired term of the Jefferson County Precinct 2 Justice of the Peace in December 1996 and was elected to a full term beginning in 1999 and re-elected in 2001.

Born and raised in Port Arthur, Morgan was educated through the Port Arthur Independent School District, graduating from Thomas Jefferson High School. He is a 1963 graduate of Lamar University receiving a Bachelor of Business Administration degree with a major in management. He served in the U.S. Army Reserves.

Morgan moved to Houston in 1964 to pursue a career in personnel management and banking. He moved back to Port Arthur in 1986 for employment with MBank, now known as BANK ONE. He served as president of BANK ONE in the Orange and Port Arthur branches.

He has served on various boards and commissions including the Pleasure Island Commission, Port Cities Rescue Mission, Port Arthur Historical Society, Lamar University Alumni Association, Majestic Krewe of Aurora and the Jefferson County Investment Committee. He is a member of the First Christian Church serving as an Elder and has also served a term as trustee. Morgan served on the Greater Port Arthur Chamber of Commerce Board of Directors from 1994 until 1996.

PATRICK MURPHY

Pat Murphy is the President/CEO of the YMCA of Port Arthur.  He assumed the position July 2005, replacing Mike Kelly, retiring CEO.  Pat brings with him 27 years experience with the YMCA, beginning in 1979 at the Cossaboom YMCA in Houston.  He served 13 years in Houston at four YMCAs as a Program Director, Extension Director and Executive Director.  Pat and his family moved to Fort Worth, Texas for the next 11 years with the YMCA.  He supervised the Downtown YMCA as well as four other branches with budget responsibilities of $7.8 million.  In 2003, Pat accepted a position with the Central Maryland YMCA where he opened a new facility for the first time since 1918.  His experiences include community outreach programs, board development, fundraising and community collaborations.

Pat’s goal for the YMCA of Port Arthur is to build on the strong base of support the YMCA enjoys and expand the positive impact the YMCA has in the community.

Pat is happy to be back in SE Texas, having grown up in the Houston area.  His parents and brother’s family live in Houston as does Jan’s mom and sister’s family.  After graduating from South Houston HS, he attended University of Texas from 1971-74 and then worked at Diamond Shamrock from 1974-76 as a laboratory technician.  He graduated from University of Houston-Clear Lake in 1978 with a Bachelor of Science.  He is married to Jan, his wife of 28 years, who is teaching math at Nederland High School.  Pat and Jan have three children, ages 18-24.  Amy has just left for Texas A&M and son David is a senior at TCU.  Sarah is teaching writing at Waller Junior High School.

In his spare time, Pat enjoys golf, photography, gardening and sports.

St. Elizabeth PHO

Parsley and his wife Stacy have two children and currently live in Port Neches.

GLEN STIFFLEMIRE
The Port Arthur News

Stifflemire is the Regional Publisher for the Southeast Texas Newspaper Group, which includes The Port Arthur News, The Orange Leader, and Triangle Press, the commercial printing division for the SETNG.

Stifflemire is originally from Freeport, Texas, and began his newspaper career in that community at the Brazosport Facts in 1967. Within his first five years in the industry he had worked in all the various departments (advertising, editorial, circulation, and production) within the newspaper business.

He was 29 years old when he published his first newspaper, and he was a publisher at nine different newspapers in Texas, Kansas, Missouri, Louisiana, North Carolina, California, and Iowa before deciding to accept and come back home to Southeast Texas in his current publisher position in Port Arthur in November of 2003.

He was also instrumental in the decision of building a new printing and distribution facility, which currently houses a new, state-of-the-art, 15-unit printing press with twin towers, that enables the newspaper to have additional full process color capabilities, better quality, be more efficient, and print double the amount of papers per hour than the old press downtown could handle.

He has also been involved in various civic organizations during his professional career, and has served as a director on county-appointed boards, economic development, business promotion, and other Chamber boards during his moves throughout the country. He also serves on the board of directors for the YMCA of Port Arthur.

He and his wife Lea were married in Beaumont in 1971.  They have five grown daughters, along with twelve grandchildren.
