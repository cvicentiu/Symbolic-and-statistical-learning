Sample Section: Arthurian Works 1989:  Q - Z

| Annals | Previous |

151. Quill Consort. "Sir Gawain and the Green Knight." In Tales and Music of the Middle Ages. Recorded music. Boise, Idaho: Ars Noel Pub., 1989.

A reading of the medieval poem with lute accompaniment by the Quill Consort: Linda Marie Zaerre and Joseph A. Baldassarre with assisting musicians.

Keywords: Primary; music; Gawain; Green Knight

152. Ramsden, Robert. "Tristan's Secret." Outposts, no. 160 (Spring 1989): 6.

A poem listed in a bibliography of Tristan literature.

Keywords: Primary; Tristan

153. Ratcliffe, Eric. "Sea Grail." In Leo Mysteries. Stevenage, Herts.: Astraport, 1989.

Reprinted in the author's collection Fire in the Bush (1993).

A 19-line poem blending Grail imagery with that of a sea quest.

Keywords: Primary: Grail

154. Rex, Jytte, director. Isolde. Film. Camera Film, 1989.

A Danish film in which the triangular relationship of Isolde, Mark and Tristan is paralleled among three contemporary characters: Isolde, a young librarian; her unnamed ex-husband, a politician; and her lover, a mercenary identified as the Warrior.

Keywords: Primary; film; Tristan; Iseult

155. Richardson, Alan, and Geoff Hughes. Ancient Magicks for a New Age. St. Paul, MN: Llewellyn Publications, 1989.

Third printing 1992.

Accounts of magic rituals in which Merlin was called upon as a spiritual guide. The first series is from the diaries of Christine Campbell Thomson and date from the early 1940's. The others are from the mid-1980's records of Geoff Hughes, edited and with a commentary by Richardson. The magic sessions described include several encounters with Arthur, Morgan le Fay and others.

Keywords: Primary; esoterica; Merlin; Arthur; Morgan le Fay

156. Roberts, Forrester. The Land of Arthur. Illustrated by Courtney Davis. Cardiff: City of Cardiff, 1989.

The brochure of the Exhibition of Celtic and Medieval Legend, presented at The Old Library, the Hayes, Cardiff, and later at Theatre Clwyd, Mold, North Wales, in 1989. Each page--corresponding to a display--presents a brief account of some aspect of the Legend: e.g., "The Search for Arthur," "Arthurian Places," "The Winchester Round Table," "Symbols of the Grail Quest." The brochure is illustrated with drawings by Roberts and Celtic decorations by Davis.

Keywords: Primary; exhibition; Arthur

157. Sale, James. "Nimue." Ore 39 (1989): 28.

A nineteen-line poem suggesting that Merlin has the magic to release Arthur from his fate, but that the time is now almost past. Nimue apparently is the speaker.

Keywords: Primary; Merlin; Arthur; Guinevere; Mordred; Nimue; Vivian

158. Sampson, Fay. Wise Woman's Telling. Daughter of Tintagel, Book One. London: Headline Books, 1989.

Reprinted with the other volumes in an omnibus edition, Daughter of Tintagel, 1992.

The first of a five-volume biography of Morgan, most of the accounts told by individuals whose lives are variously bound to hers. The story of Morgan's childhood in Cornwall is here recounted by her old nurse and teacher, a woman who practices the "old religion" and has a reputation as a seer, though this ability fails her at every crucial point. Morgan, devoted to her father Gorlois, becomes the bitter enemy of Uther Pendragon and Merlin, and after an attempted kidnapping of the infant Arthur, she is sent to live at the convent at Tintagel.

Keywords: Primary; Uther Pendragon; Gorlois; Igraine; Morgan le Fay; Elaine; Morgause; Arthur; Merlin; birth

159. ------. White Nun's Telling. Daughter of Tintagel, Book Two. London: Headline Books, 1989.

Consolidated with the other novels in the one-volume Daughter of Tintagel, 1992.

In this sequel to Wise Woman's Telling, Morgan spends her adolescence in confinement among a group of White Nuns at Tintagel. The story is told by the young nun Luned, who is charged with watching Morgan and is eventually brought to ruin through her own weakness and inability to control the girl, who continues to practice the old religion.

Keywords: Primary; Morgan le Fay; Tintagel

160. Saxby, Maurice. "King Arthur: The Stuff of Heroes." In The Great Deeds of Superheroes, illustrated by Robert Ingpen. Australia: Millenium Books, 1989.

New York: Peter Bedrick Books, 1990.

A brief narrative account of Arthur's life in a collection of stories about mythological and literary heroes, presented in a large color-illustrated format for young readers.

Keywords: Primary; juvenile; Arthur

161. Schafer, R. Murray, composer. "Le Cri de Merlin." In Michael Tippett: The Blue Guitar/Benjamin Britten: Nocturnal/R. Murray Schafer: Le Cri de Merlin. Recorded music, performed by Norbert Kraft. London: Chandos Records Ltd., 1989.

Issued on CD #CHAN 8784.

A six-part guitar composition commissioned by Kraft and first performed in 1987. "The wizard Merlin feels that his powers are diminished by the advance of civilization and retreats into the forest to seek solace with the birds and animals; his cries can be heard, but man cannot interpret them." (artist's note)

Keywords: Primary; music; Merlin

162. Schwader, Ann K. "She of the Lake." Illustrated by Christine Lowentrout. Mythic Circle, no. 8 (Early summer 1989): 33.

The poem is from "Avalon & After," a sequence or collection (publication unknown).

A sonnet spoken by the Lady of the Lake on the passing of Arthur from this world.

Keywords: Primary; Lady of the Lake; Arthur

163. Shakespeare, William, and William Rowley. The Birth of Merlin or the Childe Hath Found His Father. Illustrated by Miranda Gray, with a foreword by Harold F. Brooks, with an introduction by R. J. Stewart, commentary by Denise Coffey, et al. Longmead, Shaftsbury, Dorset: Element Books, 1989.

The volume was published in connection with a production of the play at Theatre Clwyd, Mold, North Wales, in June and July, 1989, directed by Coffey with music by Stuart Gordon and R. J. Stewart.

An edition of the play published in 1662 as by Shakespeare and Rowley, the text based on that edition although with minor revisions based on the edition of Brooke (1908). Accompanying editorial content discusses the play's place in the Merlin tradition, its comedy, and staging considerations. Although the play is "attributed to" Shakespeare, the doubtfulness of his involvement with it is underplayed in the commentary.

Keywords: Primary; edition; Rowley; Merlin

164. Shurlock, Manwaring. Arthurian and Knightly Art from the Middle Ages. Edited by Derek Bryce. Notes by Baron de Cosson. Lampeter, Wales: Llanerch Enterprises, 1989.

A new edition of Tiles from Chertsey Abbey (1885), with an edited text and restored reproductions of the tiles. The accompanying summary of the Tristan story is drawn from Scott's Sir Tristrem (1804).

Keywords: Secondary; published art; Chertsey Tiles; Tristan; Iseult

165. Shwartz, Susan. "Count of the Saxon Shore." In Alternatives, edited by Robert Adams, 121-63. New York: Baen Books, 1989.

A story in a collection of "alternate histories" in which an old Saxon warrior, Beorhtwold, tells how King Artos survived the battle of Camlann and was reunited with Gwenhwyfar. Named Count of the Saxon Shore for his loyalty, Beorhtwold honors Artos' last request years later by stealing the king's body and burying it in a secret place where it will protect Britain.

Keywords: Primary; Arthur; Mordred

166. Sinclair, Andrew. Magog. The Albion Triptych. London: Hodder and Stoughton/Sceptre, 1989.

A new edition of the second volume (1972) of The Albion Triptych, issued to accompany the two novels published in 1988. Unlike the others, this novel contains nothing Arthurian.

Keywords: Primary; edition; non-Arthurian

167. Singer, Sarah. "Guinevere, the Nun." The Round Table 5 (1989): 53.

A villanelle on Guinevere, lonely and isolated, who cannot find comfort or forgiveness for a past she can't forget.

Keywords: Primary; Guinevere; Lancelot; Arthur

168. "Sir Gyro Gearloose." In Disney Duck Tales: Sphinx for the Memory and Sir Gyro Gearloose. New York: BDD Promotional Book Company/Mallard Press, 1989.

A large-format illustrated story book in which Walt Disney characters travel in time in a broad parody of Twain's story. They encounter King Artie, Moorloon the Magician, and Lessdred the villain. The story is based on a television cartoon (S. Clark, 1987). The illustrators are not credited.

Keywords: Primary; juvenile; comics; Arthur; Merlin; time travel; Mark Twain

169. Snydal, Laurence. "The End of the Quest." The Round Table 5 (1989): 56.

An eight-line rhyming poem describing the wasted and lost men at the end of the quest, who now know that we are made "Of thirst and earth and holy cup."

Keywords: Primary; Grail; quest

170. Spielberg, Steven, director. Indiana Jones and the Last Crusade. Film. Jeffrey Boam, screenwriter, story by George Lucas, et al. Paramount Pictures/Lucasfilm Ltd., 1989. 126 minutes.

Released on Paramount videotape, 1989. Adapted as a novel by Rob MacGregor, 1989. Adapted as a children's story by Les Martin, 1989. Adapted as a comic book by David Michelinie, 1989. Adapted as an electronic game, 1992. Screenplay by Jeffrey Boam published, 1995. The character of Indiana Jones appeared in several susequent works with Arthurian elements: W. McCay, 1991; R. MacGregor, 1991, 1992; M. Caidin, 1994; K. Cliffe, G. Farshtey and T. Woodruff, 1995; E. Lee, 1995.

An adventure/action film featuring the popular screen hero, Indiana Jones, who here joins his father, an Arthurian scholar, in seeking the Holy Grail. The Nazis, also searching, are outwitted as usual when Jones correctly identifies the cup from a large group of vessels in the keeping of a medieval knight who has lived for centuries. Indiana Jones is played by Harrison Ford and his father by Sean Connery.

Keywords: Primary; Grail; film; Nazis; Indiana Jones

171. Stafford, Greg. Prince Valiant: The Storytelling Game. Game. Contributors William Dunn, Lynn Willis, and Charlie Krank, illustrated by Hal Foster. Albany, CA: Chaosium Inc, 1989.

A role-playing game in which participants take part in stories set in Arthurian Britain as portrayed in Hal Foster's comic strip (1937). Illustrations from the strip appear throughout the game book as well as background information on both Arthur and Foster's work.

Keywords: Primary; game; comics; Prince Valiant; role-playing; Hal Foster

172. Starr, Kara. Merlin's Journal of Time: The Camelot Adventure. Solana Beach, CA: Ravenstarr Publications, 1989.

The book announces the forthcoming sequels in preparation: Merlin's Journal of Time--The Pathways Adventure, and Magic Erratica--Merlin's Book of Spells, Bindings and Enchantments.

A book of New Age spiritual guidance provided by Merlin to Arthur as he passes through the traditional events of his life, written in the form of conversations revealed to the author by Merlin himself.

Keywords: Primary; esoterica; Merlin

173. Stedman-Jones, Fred. "Camlann." Pendragon 19, no. 4 (Autumn 1989): 4-6.

Reprinted in An Arthurian Miscellany, edited by B. T. Lupack and A. Lupack, 1998, pp. 1-4.

A 95-line poem in which Taliesin sings of the fall of Arthur in a style imitating ancient Welsh verse in form and diction.

Keywords: Primary; poem; Camlann; Arthur

174. ------. "The Trial of Medrawd: Case Papers: Medrawd's Testimony." Pendragon 20, no. 1 (Winter 1989): 16-19.

A transcript of the dialogue between an unnamed prosecutor and the Celtic warlord, Medrawd, in which the accused draws on a variety of early Welsh sources to tell his side of the story of the end of Arthur's reign.

Keywords: Primary; Mordred

175. Stein, Walter Johannes. The Death of Merlin: Arthurian Myth and Alchemy. Translated by Christian Von Arnim and Ingrid von Arnim. Edinburgh: Floris Books, 1989.

Paperback reprint 1990. The essays originally appeared in German or English periodicals in the 1920s and 1930s. The collection first appeared as Der Tod Merlins (Dornach, 1984); it is not clear whether the translations from German are new or whether they are the original translations for periodicals by Christian and Ingrid von Arnim.

A collection of Stein's essays on a variety of subjects, from alchemy to economics, incorporating the author's esoteric theories and speculations. Several essays appeared in English anthrosophical periodicals: "King Arthur and the Problem of East and West" (1932); "Thomas Aquinas and the Grail" (1932); "Tristan and Isolde and an Understanding of Minstrel Poetry" (1934); "Is King Arthur a Historical Character?" (1936); "The Death of Merlin" (1936). The other essays were translated for this collection: "The Revival of the Arthurian Legend in the Fifteenth Century," "Basilius Valentinus in the Context of the Arthurian Legend," "The Polarity between Parzival and Gawan" and "Tristan and Isolde against the Background of Greek Mythology."

Keywords: Secondary; esoteric; Arthur; Merlin; alchemy; Grail; Tristan; economics; speculation; collection

176. Stewart, Bob. "The Girl Who Met Merlin." Illustrated by Miranda Gray. In Legendary Britain: An Illustrated Journey, authors Bob Stewart and John Matthews, 41-46. London: Blandford Press, 1989.

A modern story in which a girl meets Merlin on a walk in the country and "sacrifices" her sandwich and soft drink for the answer to her question about the villainous roles assigned to girls in old stories.

Keywords: Primary; Merlin; return

177. ------. "The Smith King, or Three Rogues Underground." Illustrated by Miranda Gray. In Legendary Britain: An Illustrated Journey, authors Bob Stewart and John Matthews, 53-62. London: Blandford Press, 1989.

A fantasy story set at Wayland's Smithy, and though not explicitly Arthurian, it tells of three drunks who blunder their way into the barrow where an unnamed sleeping lord awaits them.

Keywords: Primary; sleeping lord

178. Stewart, Bob, and John Matthews. Legendary Britain: An Illustrated Journey. Illustrated by Miranda Gray, photographs by Tim Cann. London: Blandford Press, 1989.

A tour of sites throughout Britain with stories based on their legendary history in "an attempt to trace the three essential themes (kingship and the Grail, the Otherworld and prophecy, fairies and ancestors." (Introduction) Each section includes a story by one of the authors, including the following of Arthurian interest by Stewart: "The Girl Who Met Merlin" and "The Smith King, or Three Rogues Underground;" and by Matthews: "Drustan's Ghost," "The Kingly Shadow," "The Struggle for Spring," and "The Island of Sorrow and Joy."

Keywords: Primary; anthology; travel; folklore; esoterica

179. Styles, Showell. Welsh Tales for Children. Illustrators Jon Ward, L. Lovatt, and Jon Davis. Ruthin, Clwyd: John Jones, 1989.

Reprinted, 1990.

This collection for young readers includes the following Arthurian stories: "The Giant Who Collected Beards" (pp. 16-21), about Arthur's refusal to contribute his beard to the giant Rhitta's beard-coat; "The Cave of the Knights" (35-37), about Arthur's sleeping knights; and "Mule's Ears" (42-46), in which the secret that King March has mule's ears is exposed at court. The illustrations are by students of the Wrexham College of Art.

Keywords: Primary; juvenile; Wales; Arthur; sleeping knights; Mark; Rience

180. Swanwick, Michael. "The Dragon Line." Illustrated by N. Taylor Blanchard. Isaac Asimov's Science Fiction 13, no. 6 (June 1989): 108-23.

The copyright date on the story is 1988. A concluding note acknowledges the author's debt to "the unpublished 'Mordred' manuscript of the late Anna Quindsland." Reprinted in Isaac Asimov's Camelot, edited by G. Dozois and S. Williams, 1998, pp. 53-72.

A science fiction story in which Mordred and Merlin confront each other in the modern world, with Merlin as the villain.

Keywords: Primary; science fiction; story; Mordred; Merlin; return

181. Thackery, Anne. Ragnarok. London: Bantam Books, 1989.

A historical novel about a British princess determined to perpetuate the Fellowship of Caer Melot in the years following the death of Arturius. Her marriage to a Saxon king creates the alliance of kingdoms which is to become Northumberland, and she plays a role in creating the legend of the sword in the stone.

Keywords: Primary; novel; sword in the stone

182. Thomas, Roy, and R. J. M. Lofficier, writers. "Dawn of Blood." Illustrated by David Day, and Dan Day. Doctor Strange, Sorcerer Supreme, no. 11 (December 1989). Comic book. New York: Marvel Comics.

A secondary story in a comic book telling how an ancient evil power has passed through the ages in the form of a collection of parchment scrolls. In the Arthurian era, Morgan Le Fey assembles the scrolls into a book and unleashes the evil power that takes control of Modred the Mystic. Merlin calls upon the Church for help and Brendan entombs Modred.

Keywords: Primary; comics; Morgan le Fay; Mordred; Merlin; Brendan

183. Tooke, Eddie, editor. "Sadly My Idylls." Pendragon 20, no. 1 (Winter 1989): 3.

Five lines of light verse facetiously attributed to Tennyson in which the poet dismisses the relevance of the Idylls to Arthurian study.

Keywords: Primary; satire; Alfred Tennyson

184. Travers, P. L. What the Bee Knows: Reflections on Myth, Symbol and Story. Wellingborough: The Aquarian Press, 1989.

Paperback reprint: Harmondsworth: Penguin/Arkana, 1993.

A collection of the author's essays and stories, most from Parabola magazine and including several of Arthurian interest: "Speak Lord" (1982), "What Aileth Thee" (1983); "Sip no Sup and Bite no Bit" (1984), "Lively Oracles" (1986), and "Le Chevalier Perdu" (1987).

Keywords: Primary; folklore

185. Tyson, Neil De Grasse. Merlin's Tour of the Universe. New York: Columbia University Press, 1989.

New edition: Merlin's Tour of the Universe: A Skywatcher's Guide to Everything from Mars and Quasars to Comets, Planets, Blue Moons, and Werewolves. New York: Doubleday, 1997.

A collection of questions and answers about astronomy from Star Date, a magazine published by the McDonald Observatory of the University of Texas. Merlin, who answers questions submitted by readers, is "a visitor from the Andromeda galaxy who is as old as the Earth and has observed the major scientific achievements of recorded Earth history" (author's preface). A sequel was published in 1998.

Keywords: Secondary; reference; Merlin; astronomy

186. Vance, Jack. Madouc. The Lyonesse Trilogy. Columbia, Pennsylvania: Underwood-Miller, 1989.

A special edition of 600 signed copies issued in slipcase, with additional hardcover copies. Trade edition: New York: Ace Books, 1990. London: Grafton Books, 1991.

The concluding novel of the trilogy (1983, 1985) set in pre-Arthurian times with incidental references to Arthurian characters and events to come. This is the story of Madouc, Princess of Lyonesse, and it incorporates her quest for the Holy Grail which resembles a fairy tale adventure much more than an episode in a medieval romance. The girl's father is revealed to be Sir Pellinore.

Keywords: Primary; Lyonesse; Grail; Pellinore; Fairy

187. Veitch, Rick. "Fall of the House of Pendragon." Illustrated by Tom Yeates. Swamp Thing, no. 87 (June 1989). Comic book. New York: DC Comics.

A comic book story in which the title creature, Swamp Thing, is taken back to Arthur's time to save Camelot from destruction by the armies of Modred and Morgaine Lefay. In addition to such comic book characters with Arthurian connections as the Shining Knight and the Demon, the creature encounters Merlin and a Grail-crazed King Arthur as Camelot falls. In the following issue, no.88, Swamp Thing sees the Grail pass from a Neanderthal tribe to the rapacious Homo sapiens in a story written by Doug Wheeler. The story replaced one that would have had the creature meet Christ at the Crucifixion, which was cancelled out of concern about offense to readers and which resulted in the resignation of the writer Veitch from the series, generating some coverage in the national press. Whether the Grail figured in the cancelled story is unknown, although one news article mentions the creature appearing as an angel who offers Christ a cup in the Garden of Gethsemanee.

Keywords: Primary; Arthur; Merlin; Mordred; Morgan le Fay; Grail; comics; the Demon; Shining Knight; time travel

188. Watson, Richard Jesse, author & illustrator. Tom Thumb. San Diego: Harcourt Brace Jovanovich, 1989.

London: Hamish Hamilton, 1990. The story and illustrations were adapted for a children's video by Gary Delfiner, 1991.

A lavishly illustrated retelling of the Tom Thumb story, with emphasis on events in King Arthur's court.

Keywords: Primary; Tom Thumb; Merlin; King Arthur; juvenile

189. Welcher, Dan, composer. The Visions of Merlin. Recorded music. Conducted by Akira Endo. Louisville, KY: First Edition Records, 1989.

The performance was recorded on May 25, 1982; it is issued here as LS 793.

A recording by the Louisville Orchestra of Welcher's tone poem (1980).

Keywords: Primary; music; Merlin; Mary Stewart

190. Whitaker, Mike, composer and performer. "Morgain." In Contabile. Recorded music, produced by G. North. Oakland, CA: Wail Songs, 1989.

A song recorded live at a convention of fantasy fans in February, 1989. In it, a warrior sings of his enchantment by the evil Morgain, with allusions which could apply to Morgan le Fay.

Keywords: Primary; music; Morgan le Fay

191. William of Malmesbury. The Kings Before the Norman Conquest. Lampeter, Dyfed: Llanerch Enterprises, 1989.

A reprint of Books I and II of Joseph Stevenson's translation of the 12th-century Historia Regum Anglorum (1854), which include William's remarks on Arthur and Glastonbury.

Keywords: Primary; edition; William of Malmesbury; Arthur; Stevenson

192. Williamson, Robin. The Craneskin Bag. Edinburgh: Canongate, 1989.

US edition: The Wise and Foolish Tongue. San Francisco: Chronicle Books, 1991. Four of the works are reprinted or retold in Celtic Bards, Celtic Druids, by Stewart and Williamson (1996).

A collection of stories from the author's repertoire of early Celtic material, some of which were released earlier in recorded versions (1985). Arthurian material appears in the following: "The Wooing of Isolde," "Culhwch and Olwen," "The Spoils of Annwn," "Vortigern's Tower," "Arthur, the Early Legend," "The Story of the Gododdin," and "The Birth of Taliesin."

Keywords: Primary; stories; Tristan; Iseult; Culhwch; Olwen; Arthur; Vortigern; Merlin; Taliesin

193. Willingham, Bill. Elementals 2, no. 7-24 (September 1989 to August 1992, 1989-92). Comic books. Illustrated by Mike Leeke and Mike Chen. Norristown, PA: Comico the Comic Company.

Stories written by Jack Herman involving the Merlin figure as a central character appear in the series in 1992 and 1996.

A comic book series featuring a group of superheroes who specialize in sex and violence. A recurring character, the boy-wizard Merlin Ambrose (introduced in 1987), takes on a more prominent role as the husband of one of the heroines and the lord of Avalon with a sequence of stories beginning in September, 1989. Stories in which the character is prominent include: no. 7 (9/89) "Different Worlds;" no. 18 (6/91) untitled story set in Avalon; no. 20 (10/91) "Supernature Summit."

Keywords: Primary; comics; Merlin; Avalon

194. Wilson, Joy. Cornwall: Land of Legend. Bodmin: Bossiney Books, 1989.

New edition as Tristan and Iseult: A Cornish Love Story. Launceston: Bossiney Books, 1999.

A guide to places associated with Cornish legends, especially the story of Tristan and Iseult.

Keywords: Primary; travel; Cornwall; Tristan; Iseult

195. Wolf, Joan. Born of the Sun. New York: New American Library, 1989.

London: Grafton Books, 1990

A historical romance set in the era after Arthur's death in which Niniane, a Celtic princess, and her Saxon husband, a king of Wessex, provide for the merging of the races and ultimately a united England. Although Arthur, Camelot and Badon are referred to throughout, and a convent at Glastonbury figures in the story, the story is not Arthurian.

Keywords: Primary; Nimue; Saxons; Arthur; death
