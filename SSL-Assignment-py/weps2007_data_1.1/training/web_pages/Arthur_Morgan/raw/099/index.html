The Week in Politics

Sunday, 15 October 2006

Presented by Sean O'Rourke

Ahern and Fianna Fáil boosted by new poll findings

  • Enda Kenny, Fine Gael leader, offers his reaction to Friday's Irish Times poll which offered little good news for his party

  • Michael Ring, Fine Gael TD for Mayo, Arthur Morgan, Sinn Féin TD for Louth, and Mary Hanafin, MInister for Education and Science, discuss the results of two recent opinion polls

Paisley says Yes after St Andrews talks

  • Tommie Gorman, Northern Editor, assesses the outcome of three days of talks on the restoration of power-sharing in Northern Ireland

  • Arthur Morgan, Mary Hanafin and Michael Ring consider the prospects for a power-sharing arrangement at Stormont

Proposed changes to Ethics Act

  • Mícheál Lehane considers what the changes will mean for ministers and TDs who will now have to take extra care when receiving a gift from families and friends

  • Ms Hanafin, Mr Ring and Mr Morgan discuss the importance of ethics in public office

Nuacht/TG4 opinion poll - the constituency of Clare

  • Sinéad Crowley assesses the findings of the poll which was carried out by TNS mrbi

  • Michael Ring offers his opinion on the Fine Gael strategy in Clare; Arthur Morgan and Mary Hanafin give their views on their respective party standings in the Clare poll
