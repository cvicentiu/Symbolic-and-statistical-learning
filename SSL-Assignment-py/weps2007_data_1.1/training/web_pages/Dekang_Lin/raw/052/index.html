This is a seminar in computational linguistics and natural language processing for students at an advanced level. Topics include advanced statistical algorithms for parsing, question answering, machine learning of linguistic knowledge, computational models of semantics, and machine translation. This course will involve a lot of reading of papers from the literature as well as a final project which should be capable of being submitted to ACL, COLING, HLT, etc.

Requirements:

  • Lots of reading; you must read every paper. In addition, each paper will have a presenter and a discussant. Each student must act twice as a presenter and twice as a discussant during the semester.
  • You must do final project on a topic of your choosing. The project must be an advanced quality, suitable for publication. Just for your reference, the submission deadlines for ACL-2004 and HLT-NAACL-2204 are in late February/early March. Either solo or group projects are fine.
  • Dates:
    • 1-pgraph project idea: Thursday October 9
    • 2-page project outline: Tuesday October 23
    • Project paper due: Tuesday Dec 9 in class. Format: long conference-paper sized (i.e. on the order of 8-12 page single-space 11 point), including correct bibliography, abstract, etc etc.
    • ACL/NAACL/ICSLP deadlines: February/March

Prerequisite: CSCI 5832 "Natural Language Processing" (this is a strict prerequisite, no exceptions)

Required Texts:

  • Manning and Schuetze "Foundations of Statistical NLP"
  • Jurafsky and Martin "Speech and Language Processing"

Policy on Disabilities

The University of Colorado abides by Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act of 1973 which stipulates that no student shall be denied the benefits of an education "solely by reason of a handicap." Disabilities covered by the law include, but are not limited to, learning disabilities and hearing, sight or mobility impairments. If you have a disability that may require accommodations, please see me during the first week of classes, so that reasonable accommodations may be arranged. You will need to provide documentation of your disability to the Disability Services office in Willard Hall, Room 322.

Policy on Academic Integrity

The University of Colorado (refer to course catalogue) has the following policy regarding academic integrity: "A university's intellectual reputation depends on maintaining the highest standards of intellectual honesty. Commitment to those standards is a responsibility of every student and every faculty member at the University of Colorado. Breaches of academic honesty include cheating, plagiarism, and the unauthorized possession of exams, papers or other class materials that have not been formally released by the instructor."

Just to be clear, plagiarism includes any use of other people's work in project writeups without citation. That means, just as an example, that you cannot copy sentences from a paper or a textbook into your final project writeup, unless you quote them and give explicit citations.

A breach of academic honesty in this class will result in the following actions: 1) failing grade for the particular assignment, and 2) the instructor will submit a letter to the Dean documenting the infraction. More than one instance of academic dishonesty will result in the following actions: 1) failing grade for the course, and 2) the instructor will submit a letter to the Dean requesting a hearing in which the consequences (this may include suspension or permanent expulsion from the University) will be determined.
