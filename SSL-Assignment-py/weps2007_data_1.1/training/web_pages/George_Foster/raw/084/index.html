Pro Career

2005: Foster started all 16 regular-season games and both playoff games for the Broncos at right tackle, paving the way for one of the NFL’s most productive offenses. Denver posted its second-best single-season rushing total (2,539) on the fourth-most attempts in team history, paced by Mike Anderson’s 1,014 yards, and ranked second in the NFL in rushing (158.7 ypg.). The line allowed the third-fewest sacks (23) in the league and team annals and helped Denver set a franchise record with 145 rushing first downs. Foster also was a key component in the Broncos being just the third team in league history to have two 900+yard rushers (Anderson and Tatum Bell), a 1,000-yard receiver (Rod Smith) and a 3,000-yard passer (Jake Plummer) in the same season.

2004: Foster adjusted well to his first season at right tackle with the Broncos after spending his rookie year working on the left side of the offensive line. He started all 16 regular-season games at right tackle and did not miss an offensive snap on a line that set a franchise record by allowing only 15 sacks, shattering the previous best of 22 sacks set in 1971, to rank third in the NFL. The offensive line did not surrender a sack in eight contests and blocked for a 100-yard rusher in eight different games, including a stretch that yielded a 100-yard rushing game by a Bronco in six of seven games midway through the year.

2003: Foster was declared inactive for the first 15 games before seeing his first NFL action at left tackle in a reserve role in the final week of the season at G.B. (12/28).

College Career

2002: Foster dislocated his right wrist in an auto accident in August, which left him sidelined for several weeks. He came back and appeared in the final nine games of the year, making one start at strongside tackle vs. Kentucky, in which he registered eight knockdowns and cleared a rush lane used by tailback Ronnie Powell to score a 1-yard touchdown. Against Auburn, his block leveled a pair of defenders to spring tailback Musa Smith for a 32-yard touchdown run as the team totaled 335 yards. He was the recipient of Bill and Jane Young Endowed Football Scholarship. Foster was a member of a team that won Georgia's first SEC championship in 20 years, defeated Florida State for the Nokia Sugar Bowl title, set school record for most victories in a season (13) and finished with a final national ranking of No. 3. He helped pave the way for the SEC's top scoring offense (32.14) and passing efficiency offense (138.91). At the conclusion of the season, he was selected to play in the Senior Bowl postseason all-star game.

2001: Foster started the final eight games of the season at left offensive tackle, including the Music City Bowl vs. Boston College. He helped the offense generate 4,761 yards, making 55 key blocks/knockdowns as the front wall ranked second in the Southeastern Conference by allowing only 15 quarterback sacks. Against Vanderbilit, he posted seven knockdowns, and cleared a rush lane for Musa Smith's 3-yard touchdown run as the Bulldogs totaled 444 yards. Foster added six knockdowns, including one that allowed fullback Verron Haynes to score on a 26-yard run as the team gained 268 yards rushing against Mississippi. Foster was the recipient of Bill and Jane Young Endowed Football Scholarship.

2000: Foster was a key member of the offensive line's second unit, lining up at right tackle. One of Georgia's top offensive line reserves, he played in every game of the season (12), and in the Bulldog's 37-14 victory over Virginia in the 2000 Oahu Bowl, Georgia's fourth consecutive bowl victory.

1999: Foster played in every game, earning his first career start in the Bulldogs' game against Lousiana State at right tackle. He earned valuable playing time in Georgia's 28-25 come-from-behind overtime victory over Purdue in the Outback Bowl-the biggest comeback in bowl history (from 0-25 deficit) and the first major sporting event of the 21st century (11:07 a.m., Jan. 1, 2000).

1998: Foster redshirted as a freshman.

Personal Bio

Foster attended Southeast High School in Macon, Ga., and was named Class 4-A first-team all-state by the Atlanta Journal-Constitution and the Georgia Sports Writers Association. He was named to the Super Southern 100 and Top 50 in Georgia by the Atlanta Journal-Constitution and the Macon Telegraph's "Sweet 16.” An all-city pick in his junior and senior seasons, Foster also earned all-Middle Georgia honors as a senior by the Macon Telegraph. He was the Most Valuable Team Lineman during his junior and senior seasons and was named a team captain as a senior. Foster had 25 tackles and seven quarterback sacks his senior season and was the recipient of the Coaches' Most Improved Player Award in both his sophomore and junior seasons. George Foster was born June 9, 1980, in Macon, Ga.
