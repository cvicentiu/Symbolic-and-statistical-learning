OBITUARIES

George Foster, 85, CEO of leather firm

January 14, 2007

IPSWICH -- George A. Foster , a World War II Army veteran who was awarded the Bronze Star, died Jan. 6 at his Ipswich home. He was 85.

Born and raised in South Portland, Maine, Mr. Foster graduated from South Portland High School in 1940.

He made his mark there as an athlete, breaking school records for the javelin throw and serving as captain of the football team.

After being drafted into the Army at the age of 20, Mr. Foster relocated to Burbank, Calif.

He was selected for the Army Specialized Training Program at the University of California, Berkeley , where he studied engineering.

When the government discontinued that program, Mr. Foster was transferred to the 11th Armored Division, 21st Infantry Battalion .

He landed in German-occupied Cherbourg, France, in 1943 and operated as a scout and sniper.

During the Battle of the Bulge Mr. Foster was seriously wounded and was one of just six survivors of his company.

He received the Bronze Star upon his return.

Mr. Foster went on to earn his bachelor's degree in business administration at University of Maine and an MBA from Harvard Business School .

He worked as chief financial officer and controller for A.C. Lawrence Leather Co. in Boston and as vice president of Allied Leather Co. in Boston and New Hampshire .

In addition to his wife, he leaves three sons, George Jr. of Harpswell, Maine, Glenn of West Newbury, and Robert of Wayne, N.J.; a daughter, Holly, of Amesbury; and eight grandchildren.

A memorial was held Jan. 11 at Whittier-Porter Funeral Home , Ipswich.

Kathryn Duryea, 75; led political group

HAMILTON -- Kathryn (Lundegren) Duryea , founding member and former president of the Hamilton-Wenham League of Women Voters , died Jan. 4 at the Kaplan Family Hospice House in Danvers. She was 75.

Mrs. Duryea was born in Jackson Heights, N.Y. and grew up in Marblehead. She graduated from Marblehead High School in 1949 and went on to attend Tufts University .

Mrs. Duryea deferred her graduation in order to get married and raise a family. In 1975, she returned to school and graduated summa cum laude from Northeastern University . In 1978 , she earned a master's degree in education from Tufts University.

Mrs. Duryea worked as school psychologist for the Joshua Eaton Elementary School in Reading for 16 years.

When she retired, she was a volunteer with the Head Start Program and the Hamilton-Wenham public schools.

She founded the Hamilton-Wenham League of Women Voters more than 40 years ago.

As vestry of Christ Church in Hamilton, Mrs. Duryea was also director of its thrift shop for 10 years and a Sunday school teacher.

Mrs. Duryea moved to Hamilton 52 years ago with her family. She was married to William D. Duryea II for 55 years .

In addition to her husband, Mrs. Duryea also leaves one son; William D. III, of Beverly; two daughters, Sandra T. of Hamilton and Linda P. of Wenham; three sisters, Jane Bradford of Boston, Judy Sands of North Reading, and Betty Thorgilsson of Marblehead; six grandchildren; and several nieces and nephews.

A memorial service will be held Jan. 27 at 11 a.m. in St. John's Episcopal Church, Beverly.
