Pauline Howard York
Funeral services for Pauline Howard York, 89, of Glencoe will be at 11 a.m. Thursday, May 25, 2006, at Collier-Butler Funeral Home, with burial at 3 p.m. in Hopewell Cemetery in Hanceville.
Collier-Butler Funeral Home is in charge of arrangements.
Mrs. York died Tuesday, May 23 in Glencoe. She was born in Cullman to Paul M. Sr. and Tessie Lou Turner Howard. She was a native of Cullman, graduating as valedictorian in 1935. After her marriage to H. Joe York, she lived in Alexander City, Lincoln and Eutaw, before moving to Glencoe in 1963. She was very active in church activities at First Baptist Church-Glencoe, teaching the Fidelis Sunday School class for over 35 years. She served in the MWU, chaired the sanctuary flower committee, and served in many other parochial positions. She was a member of the Glencoe Civitan Club, an active member in the Etowah County Retired Teachers Association, the Glencoe AARP, Board of Directors Wallace Senior Citizens Center, and the Christmas Bell-Ringers for the Salvation Army. She served as a Pink Lady at Gadsden Regional Medical Center for many years. She was named volunteer of the year for 1989. She will be fondly remembered by a multitude of students at Glencoe High School and Etowah High School where she served as school secretary and registrar from 1963 until her retirement in 1975. She had previously served in the same capacity at Lincoln High School and Greene County High School in Eutaw. The family wishes to express a special thanks to the staff at Coosa Valley Health Center.
She was preceded in death by her husband; two brothers, Albert Ray Howard and Paul M. Howard Jr.; and her parents.
Survivors include two sons, Ted Howard (Jane D.) York of Baton Rouge, La., and David Albert (Dr. Linda S.) York of Dothan; four grandchildren, Lisa Marie York of Eutaw, Amanda Michelle York (Josh) Salzman of Tuscaloosa, Dr. Jake Adam York of Denver, Colo., and Joseph Aaron York of Oxford, Miss.; two great-grandchildren, Tyler Damon York and Gabrielle Salzman of Tuscaloosa; two sisters, Violet Howard Gould Shank and Juanita Howard Schaefer, both of Cullman; and numerous nieces and nephews.
In lieu of flowers, the family requests memorial gifts be made to First Baptist Church in Glencoe, Ala.
Pallbearers will be Jake Adam York, Joseph Aaron York, Josh Saloman, Harold Smith and Hal Smith.
Visitation will be from 10 to 11 a.m. today at the funeral home.

Ethelene Robinson Hamrick
Ethelene Robinson Hamrick, 76, of Oaklandon, Ind., passed away at Community North Hospital on Saturday, May 20, 2006. She was born in Cullman on Aug. 14, 1929, the daughter of Fred and Ethel Robinson. She graduated from Cullman High School and from Sacred Heart Junior College, Cullman. She attended Florence State Teachers College in Florence, Ala. She resided in Indianapolis for 46 years. She was a homemaker and a member of Holy Cross Lutheran Church in Oaklandon.
She is survived by her husband of 51 years, George E. Hamrick, of Oaklandon; four sons, James Hamrick of York, Pennsylvania, Charles (Coleen) Hamrick of Lafayette, Ind., John (Brenda) Hamrick of Gulf Shores, Steven (Linda) Hamrick of Noblesville, Ind.; 10 grandchildren; two sisters, Amilee Hendrix of Cullman and Nell Waldrop of Lexington, Ky.; three brothers, Elwin Robinson of Good Hope, Dewitt Robinson of Fort Payne and Don Robinson of Biloxi, Miss.; and several nieces and nephews.
As a dedicated wife, mother, grandmother and friend, she touched many lives through her work and volunteerism in the community and Cub Scouts, Little League Baseball and on various committees at Holy Cross Lutheran Church. Active as a Cub Scouts leader, in 1972 she was only the third woman to receive the District Scouting Award from the Central Indiana Council. In addition, she was instrumental in the success of Prentice Little League in the late 1960s and early 1970s.
The family extends thanks to Community North Hospital, its physicians and staff.
Funeral services will be at Holy Cross Lutheran Church in Indiana at 11 a.m. Thursday, May 25, 2006. Friends may call one hour prior to the funeral services and from 4 to 8 p.m. Wednesday, May 24 at Shirley Brothers Fishers-Castleton Chapel, 9900 Allisonville Road, Indiana. Burial will be held at Hopewell Cemetery in Hanceville, Ala., following funeral services at Moss-Service Funeral Home at 11 a. m. on Saturday, May 27. Friends may call one hour prior to funeral services.
In lieu of flowers, memorial contributions may be made to the American Cancer Society and/or Holy Cross Lutheran Church, 8115 Oaklandon Road, Oaklandon, IN 46236.
