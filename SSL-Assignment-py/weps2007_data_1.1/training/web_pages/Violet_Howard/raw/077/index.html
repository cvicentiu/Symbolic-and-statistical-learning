In Memoriam (Charlotte)

Anthony H. Coffina

Anthony "Tony" H. Coffina, 67, of Punta Gorda, Fla., passed away Friday, May 12, 2006, at Charlotte Regional Medical Center in Punta Gorda.

He was born July 30, 1938, in New York City, N.Y.

Tony was a successful businessman in the garment industry as a manufacturer and contractor. He was an active community leader in the Hazleton area for close to 30 years, having served as the president of the Jewish Community Council and multiple terms as president of Beth Israel Temple. He was involved in numerous civic and charitable organizations.

An avid boater, he moved to Punta Gorda 10 years ago from Hazleton, Pa. He was owner and operator of Budget Medical Supplies in Port Charlotte until his retirement last year. He served as president of Temple Shalom in Port Charlotte.

He is survived by his children, Bonnie (Jimmy) Ravina of Blue Bell, Pa., and Scott (Kim) Coffina of Voorhees, N.J.; two grandchildren, Rachel Ravina and Alex Coffina; and dear friend and companion, Ruth Sachs.

Funeral services will be held at 11 a.m. Monday, May 15, 2006, at Temple Shalom in Port Charlotte. Rabbi Solomon Agin will officiate. Graveside services and burial will be held at 11:30 a.m. Wednesday, May 17, 2006 at Beth Israel Cemetery in Hazleton.

Donations may be made to the Philadelphia Foundation, Coffina-Ravina Donor Advised Fund, 1234 Market St., Suite 1800, Philadelphia, PA 19107.

Arrangements are by Roberson Funeral Home, Punta Gorda Chapel.

Melvina V. Green

Melvina V. Green, 86, of Port Charlotte, Fla., passed away Tuesday, May 9, 2006.

She was born June 1, 1919, in Kingston, Jamaica, to Felix and Celistia (Brewster) Garcia.

Melvina attended school in Kingston and started a pre-nursing career at Even Tide Home. She moved to United Kingdom in 1957, where she continued her nursing career and was employed at Lady Well Lodge Hospital and Lewisham General Hospital. In 1966 she immigrated to New York and worked at Manning Hospital until she retired in 1986. Melvina enjoyed cooking, sewing, gardening and was very involved with her church activities. She had a special love for children and was always there to help in anyway she could.

Melvina will be greatly missed by her daughters, Norma (Cyril) Robinson, Sheila (Vincent) Clarke and Violet (Howard) Carter; stepbrother, David Brewster; grandchildren, Chrisencia Rockburne, Derrick and Patrick Clarke, Christine Green, Julian and Johnathan Carter; great-grandchildren, Patrice, Rachel and Alicia and two nephews. She was preceded in death by her husband, Granville Green.

The family will receive friends from 10 a.m. to 11 a.m. Thursday, May 18, 2006, at Community Church of God, followed by a celebration of Melvina's life at 11 a.m. with the Rev. Cecil Wiltshire. Burial will be at Restlawn Memorial Park, immediately following the service. To express condolences to the family, please visit www.Ltaylorfuneral.com and sign the online guest book.

Arrangements are by Larry Taylor Funeral and Cremation Services.

Beatrice M. Reagan

Beatrice M. Reagan, 83, of Port Charlotte, Fla., passed away Friday, May 12, 2006.

She was born Oct. 5, 1922, in Garfield Heights, Ohio, to James and Marie Fertig.

Mrs. Reagan was an accountant for U.S. Steel in Cleveland, where she retired after 30 years. She moved to Port Charlotte in 1972. Mrs. Reagan was a member of Faith Lutheran, LCMS, in Punta Gorda, Fla. She was a member of the Florida Ladies of the Elks, Elkettes Lodge No. 2606, Punta Gorda, and the Charlotte Park Civic Association.

Mrs. Reagan is survived by many loving friends. She was preceded in death by her husband, James Reagan, who passed away Jan. 2, 2003.

Visitation will be at 10 a.m. Monday, May 15, 2006, followed by the funeral service at 11 a.m., at Charlotte Memorial Funeral Home, 5200 Indian Springs Cemetery Road, Punta Gorda with the Rev. Barr Chittick, pastor, officiating. Entombment will be at Fort Myers Memorial Gardens, Fort Myers.

Arrangements are by Charlotte Memorial Funeral Home, Punta Gorda.
