Joseph Buford Cox

The following information provided by his former step son, Gary Poole.

He invented what is now known as the chipper type chain for chain saws. He and his wife, Violet, (nee Violet Howard - Poole) worked together in the Seattle ship yards during WW II and he worked on his chain design and other inventions nights and weekends. They moved to Portland, Oregon right after the war ended and started t"The Oregon Saw Chain Co." in the basement of their home. About the time they split up he started a small casting company called OMARK now known as "Omark Industries". He only went through the third grade in school but studied nights when he was working and educated himself. He was probably as knowledgeable about the world around him as anyone I ever knew. His father died when he was young and he provided for his mother and siblings and still managed to educate himself. The first time I ever saw him he was acting as "Bull Buck" for a logging outfit and falling timber by himself with what is known as a "rubber man". ( A bull buck scales the logs in the woods for the fallers and lines them out with a "strip" or designated area to work in .) A rubber man is a device made from a strip of rubber used to pull the saw away from the person on the other end of it. It mainly just keeps the saw from drooping and hanging up when the man pushes it away from him as he is falling a tree. It also serves to hold that end toward the tree so that the man doesn't have to struggle to keep the saw in the cut.

He was to be admired in many ways but was not a fair man in his dealings with Violet. When they separated, he should have voluntarily provided her with a settlement as she worked side by side with him when they were building their business. He did not provide such a settlement and she did not ask for one.
