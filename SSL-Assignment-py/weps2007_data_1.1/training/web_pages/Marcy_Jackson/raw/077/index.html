STORYBRIDGES to WORLD LANGUAGES for CHILDREN: Spanish / French / German / + is a unique program for kids ages four to nine, centering around original adaptations of tales told internationally. Words and phrases are taught in each target language through stories, songs, games and skits on Audio CDs with Teacher's Guides on CD-ROM.

PURPOSE

This is an oral approach that can be used before the basics of the learner's native language, such as reading and writing, have been mastered. Language is most easily learned by hearing sounds, figuring out their meanings and using them. Each disc is a self-teaching tool and can be used for independent study or a classroom program. Students listen to the CDs and figure out the meaning of dialogues in the target language from clues in the carefully crafted English narrative. Students become active participants, playing games, telling stories, singing songs and performing skits. National Standards in Foreign Language Education, Skills and Multiple Intelligences are met in Activities and Assessments.

CONTRIBUTORS

VERONICA FOSTER is a storyteller, author and retired teacher. After receiving a Bachelor of Arts in Education from The College of New Jersey she taught in New Jersey, Pennsylvania, Spain, Texas and Connecticut. She earned a Master of Arts degree in Multicultural Education from Fairfield University in Connecticut. Her first audiocassette language program was StoryBridges To Spanish [to French and to German] For Children (Audio-Forum). Her current program is StoryBridges to World Languages.

Eleanor González, Spanish editor, is presently an adjunct professor of Spanish at Iona College in New Rochelle, NY. She received her M.A. in Spanish from Brown University and was the former chairperson of the World Languages Department at Wilton High School (Connecticut) where she also taught Spanish. Eleanor co-authored the High School Study Guide for Destinos: An Introduction to Spanish (McGraw-Hill) as well as ¡Bravo! 3 , an intermediate Spanish text, (McDougal Littell).

Marcy Jackson, French editor, graduated from Middlebury College with an M.A. in French and a 6 th -Year Degree equivalent with studies at the University of Michigan and Middlebury College . She co-chaired The Department of World Languages at Wilton High School in Wilton, Connecticut and currently is President of Alliance Française of Northwestern Connecticut, Inc.

Elianne Begin, native speaker and helper, is from Quebec, Canada where she graduated from the Université of Montréal. She currently resides in Wilton, Connecticut, where she continues the tradition of bilingual storytelling with her own grandchildren.

Sadie Foster, helper and English editor on Stories 1-6, first performed and contributed to these stories when she was 13 years old. She has since graduated from Cornell University with a BA in English and Theatre and from Columbia University with an MFA in Dramaturgy/Script Development. She currently lives and works in New York City.

Sydney Foster, helper and English editor on Stories 1-6, first performed and contributed to these stories when she was 10 years old. She has since graduated from Brown University with a BS in Public Health and from Columbia University with a MS in Nutrition and Exercise Physiology. She currently lives and works in New York City.
