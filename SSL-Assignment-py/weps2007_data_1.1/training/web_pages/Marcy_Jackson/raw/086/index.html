FULL DAY OPTION - The following seminar will constitute all four sessions
SCADA Security
This course is structured to help students not only understand exactly how attacks against Control Systems can be launched and why they work, but also provides mitigation strategies to increase the cyber security posture of your control system network. INL is part of the National SCADA Test Bed and performs SCADA Security research for DHS and the Dept. of Energy.
Gary J. Finco and Kevin D. Lackey

Note: This is a four-hour course. In order to obtain maximum course content, attendees will need to be present for all 4 sessions in this track. If you select this seminar, please do not register for any other sessions.
 
SESSION 1 - Please select one class from Session 1
(9:00am - 10:00am)
[FILLED] Cyber Track A
Data Privacy and Information Leakage - Trends, Challenges, and Keeping Your Name Out of the News
[Marcus Dolce and Ray Hawkins]
This presentation will define Data Privacy and Information Leakage, what current threats pose, the challenges of current Information Security programs and strategies and tactics that may be used to mitigate the threat. At the conclusion of the presentation the audience will have a better understanding of data privacy and information leakage as well as potential next steps that can be tactically and strategically integrated into short and long term information protection planning.
[FILLED] Cyber Track B
Situational Forensics and the Windows Registry
[Lance Hawk]
Talk to include a demonstration of "simple" way to steal intellectual property which avoids normal detection mechanisms. Talk will cover: (1) Basic computer forensics, (2) Tools used, (3) Windows XP and 2000 registries easily explained, (4) registry access and concerns, and (5) situations where you will need to go to the registry and the exact places to go
Physical Track
Vehicle Bourne Improvised Explosive Device (VBIED) Peroxide Based Explosives
[James E McGinty]
This session will discuss the make-up and impact, presented by a car or truck bomb. The session will also deliver a true understanding of the peroxide based explosives, seen recently in terrorist attacks.
Law Enforcement Track
Computer Security Institute Survey Key Findings
[Robert Richardson]
This session will discuss the key findings of the survey. Some of you may have seen his presentation at the national conference in August.
 
SESSION 2 - Please select one class from Session 2
(10:00am - 11:30am)
[FILLED] Cyber Track A
Hacking the Mobile Workforce
[Daniel Hoffman]
Fiberlink's expert ethical hacker, Daniel V. Hoffman, CISSP, CWNA, CEH will demonstrate a series of four modern-day attacks. Each demonstration takes the attendee through a series of steps that many hackers would follow to exploit mobile and remote systems that lack the appropriate security protection, and provides best practices on how to safeguard your network.
[FILLED] Cyber Track B
Penetration Testing
[Foundstone]
This would cover methodologies and types of issues we find during these types of tests. The presentation would be vendor neutral as the methodologies are widely published in our books and whitepapers and are used by many organizations.
Physical Track
Data Compromises and Privacy
[Marcy Jackson]
Though the news frequently reports incidents of data compromises, many companies may not have first-hand experience in handling breaches of personally identifiable information. All incidents do not look the same, but there are some common themes in identifying whether an incident has occurred, investigating the situation, and implementing corrective measures. The purpose of this session is to help attendees identity and evaluate incidents, while exploring some of the legislative standards involved in responding to the incidents.
Law Enforcement Track
Protecting Your Identity Online - Identity Theft and Fraud Investigations
[Trooper Paul Iannace]
There is a dramatic increase in crimes and other improprieties occurring as a result of the Internet and new computer technology. Most people have access to a computer either in their homes, schools, or public libraries. At the same time, many are unaware of the dangers or lack the knowledge to monitor and protect their identity when they are on-line and generally when in public. This presentation is designed to assist you and your families to learn preventive measures so you aren.t a victim and to provide guidance in the event you are a victim of this fast growing crime.
 
SESSION 3 - Please select one class from Session 3
(1:15pm - 2:30pm)
[FILLED] Cyber Track A
Real World Intrusion Detection
[Dennis Dixon Jr]
Even with the wider acceptance of intrusion prevention technology, intrusion detection continues to play a solid role in securing today's enterprise networks. This discussion covers the foundations that facilitate and concepts that comprise effective intrusion detection in the eyes of top security professionals.
[FILLED] Cyber Track B
Bluetooth and RFID - A Hacking Perspective
[Russell Handorf]
Vulnerabilities in long range radio systems, such as 802.11, have been well documented; but what about the seemingly neglected short range systems such as Bluetooth and RFID? Believe it or not, some of the same problems exist in these as well and are easier to achieve and are actively exploited! Learn and explore with Russell Handorf some of the more interesting security issues with Bluetooth and RFID.
[FILLED] Physical Track
360 Look at Company Due Diligence
[Chuck Campbell]
Most organizations today recognize that performing background checks is an essential part of doing business. However, many firms struggle to determine the proper balance of due diligence, which can vary by industry, company size and even by job position. Chuck will explore critical due diligence factors that directly impact the success of background investigations such as program scope, as well as new and evolving technologies. He will also address key issues faced by organizations performing background checks such as vendor credentialing, global outsourcing and unique investigation strategies.
Law Enforcement Track
Using Hashsets for Investigations
[Charles Garzoni]

Recent Developments in Attack Tools and Techniques
