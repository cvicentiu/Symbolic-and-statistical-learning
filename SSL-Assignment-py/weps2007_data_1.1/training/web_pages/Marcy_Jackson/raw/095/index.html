History of Courage to Teach and CCR

In 1994, under the guidance of Parker J. Palmer, the Fetzer Institute created the COURAGE TO TEACH® (CTT) program, piloting an approach to personal and professional renewal for educators called "teacher formation." The formation process invites educators to reclaim their own wholeness and vocational clarity, and makes connections between the renewal of a teacher's spirit and the revitalization of public education.

Phase I: Early Conceptualization and Beginnings

1992

  • President Rob Lehman asks Fetzer Institute Senior Advisor Parker J. Palmer to write a memo on the spirituality of education to inform "a long-term project Fetzer is designing to aid in the ‘formation of teachers’."

1994

  • Twenty-two teachers attend pilot formation retreat weekend called, “The Courage to Teach: A Renewal Weekend for Teachers."
  • A focus group evaluation affirms extremely positive impact on teachers.

 

Phase II: Developing the Courage to Teach and Testing the Model

1995-1996

  • Conceptualization of two-year CTT retreat model grounded in an "agricultural model" of learning and reflection informed by seasonal metaphors.
  • Participants consistently give CTT the highest marks for its impact on their personal and professional lives.
  • Six "master teachers" are recruited and prepared by Parker Palmer to lead two-year CTT retreat programs at four test sites—coastal South Carolina; inner-city Baltimore, Maryland; Southwest Michigan; and Seattle, Washington (1996-98).

 

Phase III: A Center for Teacher Formation (CTF) Founded

1997

  • Center for Teacher Formation (CTF) established as a Fetzer Institute program, with Parker Palmer as Senior Advisor and Rick & Marcy Jackson as co-directors.
  • CTF develops effective mechanism to leverage Fetzer investments to yield significant ongoing support for local CTT programs by
    1. preparing leaders through a national Facilitator Preparation Program;
    2. providing technical support to facilitators in local devlopment efforts; and
    3. offering planning grants to spur local fund-raising for CTT programs.
  • The Courage to Teach—Exploring the Inner Landscape of a Teacher’s Life by Parker J. Palmer is published by Jossey-Bass.

 

Phase IV: Early Growth of Teacher Formation

1998

  • First cohort selected to participate in national CTT Facilitator Preparation Program led by Parker Palmer and senior facilitators.
  • Teaching from the Heart—Seasons of Renewal in a Teacher’s Life, a video about the CTT retreat program, is completed.
  • "Evoking the Spirit in Public Education" by Parker Palmer is the lead article in Educational Leadership issue devoted to spirituality and education.
  • Major evaluation of four CTT test sites reports outstanding outcomes.

1999

  • The Courage to Teach: A Guide for Reflection and Renewal, by Parker J. Palmer, published by Jossey-Bass.
  • Six new CTT program sites launched with over $600,000 in local support in New England; North Carolina; Fort Worth, Texas; Austin, Texas; Portland, Oregon; Seattle, Washington.
  • Formation Principles & Practices Workshop launched.
  • Second national cohort begins CTT Facilitator Preparation Program.
Phase V: CTF Strengthens Local Programs

2000

  • Longitudinal evaluation documents CTT as exemplary professional development and foundational to successful education reform. The seven key findings are:
    1. Teachers' passion for teaching is rejuvenated;
    2. Teachers undertake new leadership roles;
    3. Teachers seek out interactions with colleagues;
    4. Teachers practice reflective inquiry;
    5. Teachers change the way they teach;
    6. Teachers see tangible benefits for their students;
    7. Teachers practice more mindful living.
  • Local interest and support of CTT gathers momentum; local leaders begin to establish networks of institutional partners capable of sustaining and expanding CTT and formation programs.
  • The Courage to Teach passes 150,000 in book sales.

 

Phase VI: CTF Guides Growing National Movement

2001

  • CTF supports launch of ten locally supported new CTT programs.
  • National advisors meet to chart directions and offer guidance as the CTF considers questions of long-term sustainability.
  • Fourth cohort recruited for national CTT Facilitator Preparation Program.

2002

  • Stories of the Courage to Teach—Honoring the Teacher’s Heart, edited by Sam Intrator, published by Jossey Bass.
  • 55 CTT facilitators convene for the fourth annual Courage Facilitators Gathering.
  • Six Formation Principles and Practices Retreat/Workshops offered at venues including Washington D.C., Wisconsin, California and New England.
  • In response to growing interest among professionals outside of education, initial plans are made for a pilot Facilitator Preparation Program for persons in other serving professions to begin spring 2003.

Phase VII: CTF Becomes Educational Non-Profit

2003

  • Educational nonprofit status was granted to the Center for Teacher Formation by the IRS in May 2003. The board of directors met for the first time in June 2003.
  • The Courage to Teach Collaboration (CTTC) established for the purpose of strengthening collegial relationships, supporting local programs, and taking the next steps in organizational development for our facilitator community.
  • The sixth facilitator preparation cohort was launched to prepare Courage to Teach facilitators; in addition, a pilot program for preparing facilitators in other serving professions was completed.
  • Teaching With Fire—Poetry That Sustains the Courage to Teach published October, 2003 in partnership with the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation and Jossey-Bass Publishers.
  • International interest in teacher formation continues to be received (Singapore, England, Australia); feature on CTT written for the London Times Educational Supplement.
  • Twelve new CTT programs begun with many more continuing around the country; twenty-two CTT programs were concluded or launched in 2003.
  • In February 2003 the Service Mark Registration for "COURAGE TO TEACH®" was obtained by the Center. Specifically the trademark protects the name COURAGE TO TEACH® when "conducting workshops and retreats in the fields of self-awareness and vocational identity and integrity for teachers, educational counselors, and educational administrators."
  • "Honor the Human Heart," an interview of Parker Palmer by Dennis Sparks, executive director of the National Staff Development Council appeared in the Summer 2003 issue of JSD—The Journal of the National Staff Development Council.
  • "Teaching with Heart and Soul: Reflections on Spirituality in Education" by Parker Palmer was the lead essay in the November 2003 issue of the Journal of Teacher Education.

 

Phrase VIII: Courage Work Deepens in Education and Migrates to Other Serving Professions

2004

  • To date the CTF has prepared 78 facilitators in education, and 13 facilitators in other serving professions (from recently concluded pilot program); facilitators serve in 22 states and two provinces in Canada.
  • Thirteen CTT retreat programs begun with many more continuing around the country; three seasonal retreat series launched in other serving professions.
  • Research findings on the impact of Courage to Teach were presented April 2004 at the annual American Educational Research Association citing the distinguishing aspects of the teacher formation approach to personal and professional development and contrasting these with from the more common forms of training offered in educational reform initiatives.
  • A professionally produced DVD on the COURAGE TO TEACH ® was completed in spring 2004. The DVD also includes segments of an extended interview of Parker Palmer organized by themes central to Courage to Teach.
  • A Hidden Wholeness: The Journey Toward an Undivided Life, by Parker J. Palmer, published by Jossey-Bass Publishers, September 2004. Included are several references to CTF for experiencing "circles of trust" and for further information regarding facilitator preparation.

 

Phase IX: CTF Becomes Center for Courage & Renewal

2005

  • Seventeen individuals selected for first cross-professional Facilitator Preparation Program (eight from education, four from healthcare, one from law, two from philanthropy, two non-profit leaders or consultants).
  • In response to strong interest to Parker Palmer’s A Hidden Wholeness, five nationally sponsored circle of trust retreats.
  • New organizational name and tag line proposed and confirmed by CTF board: Center for Courage & Renewal—Reconnecting who you are with what you do.
  • Living the Questions: Essays Inspired by the Work and Life of Parker J. Palmer published by Jossey-Bass Publishers.
  • The Center receives two major grants, one to create a new initiative to work locally and nationally with clergy and congregational leaders, and another to expand our work with school leaders.
