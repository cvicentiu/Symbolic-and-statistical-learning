R.B. Jackson

Obituary



 
R.B. Jackson  
Longtime Homer resident, Mr. R.B. D. Jackson, 77, died June 18, 2003, from metastatic colon cancer at the South Peninsula Hospital in Homer.

Per Mr. Jackson's request no memorial services were held. R.B.'s ashes will be scattered at a site to be determined at a later date.

R.B. was born May 3, 1926, in Snyder, Ok. He completed 10th grade in 1944 and obtained his high school diploma in 2001. R.B. entered into the U.S. Army in Sept. of 1944 as a private first class in the 97th Antiaircraft Artillery Gun Battalion. He served overseas as a military policeman and received the Asiatic Pacific Campaign Medal, Good Conduct Medal, WWII Victory Medal and an honorable discharge in August 1946. R.B. moved to Homer in May of 1958.

R.B. worked as the stern oiler on the Gold Dredge in Yuba, Calif. from 1947-1948 before attending Telephone Company Lineman's Installer & Maintenance School in 1948-1955, after which he began his career as a lineman installer and maintenance for the Pacific Telephone & Telegraph Co. in Tulare, Calif. In 1961-1964, R.B. worked as a general and seasonal construction worker. From 1964-1971 he did seasonal work for the U.S. Dept. of Agriculture as a soil conservation aide in Palmer. R.B. worked as a school custodian from 1966-1967 for the Homer Middle School and also worked as a school bus driver in Homer from 1968-1984, during which time he was recognized for perfect attendance record for six consecutive years. In 1971 he attended trade school for servicing electrical appliances, air conditioning and refrigeration, and graduated with honors.

In 1974, R.B. attended the Alaska Fire and Chemical Fire Investigation School.

R.B. served four years as the Homer Volunteer Fire Department Fire Chief, various terms as the Assistant Fire Chief and served on the board of directors. R.B. received an award for his service to the Homer Volunteer Fire Department for his 16 years of service with them from 1958-1974. In 1994, he attended correspondence courses for locksmithing and electrical security, receiving a diploma with honors.

R.B. was a member of the W.K.F.L. Fount of Alaska, a humanitarian service group in Homer. He recognized all faiths and religions. R.B enjoyed hunting, fishing, fixing things and reading western novels.

His family said, "R. B. was a great guy and a privilege to know. A piece of history is gone and Homer will not be the same without him. We remember R.B. so well; he was always jolly, with courage and a pioneering spirit. He fought a long and hard battle with cancer. R.B.'s encouraging words and smile will be missed. He will live in the hearts and memories of the many whose lives he touched during his many years of service in our community."

R.B. is survived by his wife of 43 years, Helen A. Jackson; sons and daughters in-law, Aaron and Crystal Jackson, Arnan and Marcy Jackson and Konrad and Mary Jackson; daughters and sons in-law, Anna and Michael Thacker, Alinda and John Brodigan and Kathleen and Tom Piecuch; 11 grandchildren, four great grandchildren and many nieces and nephews.

In lieu of memorial contributions, honor R.B. by being good to your fellow man.

Arrangements cared for by the Homer Funeral Home.
