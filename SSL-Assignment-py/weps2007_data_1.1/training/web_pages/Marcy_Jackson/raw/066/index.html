THE COURAGE TO TEACH
LEWIS & CLARK COLLEGE
TEACHER EDUCATION PROGRAM

"Most of us become teachersfor reasons of the heart; we teach because we care deeply about our students and about some subject. But the demands of teaching and the conditions in some schools cause too many of us to lose heart as the years go by. Is it possible to take heart once more so that we can continue to do what good teachers always do, which is to give heart to our students?

Parker J Palmer,
author of The Courage to Teach:
Exploring the Inner Landscape ofa Teacher's Life

The Courage to Teach is a two-year program of quarterly retreats for the personal and professional renewal of public school teachers. The program is especially designed for K- 12 teachers in the public schools, teachers on whom our society depends for so much but for whom we provide so little encouragement and support. We focus neither on teaching techniques nor on the reform of the school; instead we focus on renewing that which is closest to us, the inner life of the teacher.

The Lewis & Clark College Courage to Teach group consists of 24 teachers who gather for quarterly retreats over a two year period in various retreat centers in the northwest. In large-group, small-group, and solitary settings, "the heart of the teacher" is explored by making use of stories from our own journeys, reflections on classroom practice, and insights from poets, story-tellers, and diverse wisdom traditions. Teachers are invited to speak honestly about their lives as educators, and to listen and respond to each other, and to themselves, with encouragement and compassion.

The courage to Teach program was developed by Parker Palmer, Ph.D. and the Fetzer Institute, piloted in Michigan from 1994-1996, and has since been successfully sponsored in 6 other states across the country. The Lewis & Clark facilitators, Jay Casbon, Ruth Hubbard, Greg Smith, and Caryl Hurtig are trained by Parker Palmer, and Rick & Marcy Jackson, national coordinators of The Courage to Teach program.

Because inner work takes time, participants in "The Courage to Teach" are asked to commit themselves to faithful attendance at eight retreats of three days each over a twoyear period. In addition, participants will be offered opportunities between retreats to stay focused on their own inner growth and in contact with one another.
