Nine years after founding the Sister Islands Association, Kim and Ela Esterberg realized a dream of spending an extended time on Ometepe. Kim wrote a journal, illustrated by Ela's drawings and watercolors and photographs they both took. We've reproduced part of their journal here, with thumbnails of the graphics to keep download time reasonable. To see the illustrations, just click on the thumbnails. When Kim Esterberg visited Ometepe in 1986, the island had four telephones. They have more phones these days, but not much Internet access. The Association is out to change that. Read about Association's Ometepe Freenet Project.

In the Spring of 2004 Seattle Times writer Jake Batsel and photographer Betty Udesen visited Bainbridge in preparation for a trip they were going to make to Central America. They were planning an article on coffee and had heard about the Sister Islands relationship. They helped bag coffee and attended a meeting of the Association's board.

Their article was eventually expanded to four that were published in September 2004. You can read Jake's report and see Betty's photographs in the Times archives at the links here (free registration required):
