SVP Seattle Update: Money, Kids, Relationships and More
 November 15, 2005

Got Some Time? We Need Volunteer Consultants!

Financial management, marketing, technology assessments, database requirements gathering, strategic planning and more!  We have several new volunteer consulting requests from our Investees.  Visit the list on the website, and email aaronj@svpseattle.org if one looks interesting.  Volunteering is a great way to learn more about the issues you care about, and the nonprofits that work on them.  For example, check out this marketing opportunity at Metrocenter YMCA.


Courage to Teach – SVP Investee Case Study

Attached is the case study of Washington State Courage to Teach, an investee that recently completed its fifth and final year of SVP funding. SVP began funding WaCTT when it was a new start-up program, operating out of the basement of local co-founders Rick and Marcy Jackson. Since then, WaCTT has grown and evolved. They are now delivering their work to hundreds of teachers each year. Read the case study here to learn more about our partnership.

Money, Kids and Relationships

On December 6, Joan DiFuria and Stephen Goldbart from the Money, Meaning & Choices Institute will join us to present two well-received workshops.  In Children & Money: Raising Kids with Lasting Money Values, participants will learn about the powerful influence of their core money beliefs and how these beliefs affect the earning, saving, spending, and sharing of money, and begin to build a foundation of agreements for how to best handle the challenges of money and children.  In Money and Relationships, strategies for recognizing and handling the many ways that money affects intimate relationships will be discussed in a safe forum. DiFuria and Goldbart will talk about what to do when the impact of money on relationships creates difficulties with trust, power, control, guilt, and envy.  Click on the session links above to learn more and to RSVP.


SVP in the Field: Kindering Center Reflects on Working with SVP

Mimi Siegel, Executive Director of Kindering Center, recently sent us her reflections on the work underway at Kindering and SVP's role in it.  Her email summary was entitled "Convergence" and eloquently captures SVP's impact, especially as we begin our fourth year of partnership.

SVP the Video – Watch It and Share It!

If you missed the fall Partner Meeting, you didn’t get to see the video developed by some Partners to tell the story of SVP Seattle.  But don’t fret!  The video is up on the website for your viewing pleasure.  It is a Windows Media file, and is 10 minutes long.  Video Link:  http://www.svpseattle.org/articles/svpvideo.wmv.  Feel free to forward this link to anyone interested in learning more about SVP.  You could also invite them to attend the New Partner Orientation on November 30 – it would give them a great overview of how SVP works and what they would get out of joining!


SVPI Network Newsletter – Watch Your Mailbox!

SVP Seattle is part of a much larger network of SVP affiliates around the U.S. and Canada.  The network, called SVPI, works to help new affiliates get off the ground, and to help existing affiliates share best practices and find opportunities to collaborate.  SVP Seattle staff and several Partners recently joined over 150 others for the SVPI Annual Conference in Phoenix Arizona.  In order to introduce you to the network and other affiliates, SVPI will be sending you the first network-wide newsletter in the coming weeks.  Watch for it!
