ABOUT THE GATEWAY RETREAT

The Gateway Retreat is designed for persons interested in becoming Courage & Renewal Facilitators. In the Gateway Retreat participants will experience a circle of trust retreat, learn more about what is entailed in becoming a facilitator, and explore next steps for application to the Courage & Renewal Facilitator Preparation Program.

The Gateway Retreat will be led by  Marcy and Rick Jackson, Co-directors of the Center for Courage & Renewal and Bev Coleman, long time courage facilitator.  All three are experienced Courage & Renewal facilitators who have led retreat programs for teachers, educational leaders and leaders in medicine, philanthropy, law, and other serving professions.

In large group, small group, and solitary settings, we will explore the intersection of our inner journeys and our work in the world, making use of personal stories, reflections on professional practice, and insights from poets, storytellers and various wisdom traditions. On the last day of the retreat we will talk about formation principles and practices, the role(s) of the Courage & Renewal Facilitator, and address questions regarding the Courage & Renewal Facilitator Preparation Program, the Courage to Teach, Courage to Lead, and circles of trust.

To apply, please submit the application (attached), your vitae/resume, and a personal statement that addresses the questions on the application form, along with a check or money order for $650.00 (made payable to the Center for Courage & Renewal).

About the Courage & Renewal Facilitator Preparation Program

The Courage & Renewal Facilitator Preparation Program is a leadership and mentoring program for preparing facilitators to lead circle of trust retreats, and to assist them in establishing personal and professional renewal programs in their professions or communities. Some may choose a seasonal retreat model similar to that used in the Courage to Teach program. Others may adapt this model in ways that are appropriate to their particular circumstance or profession. Regardless of the model, our hope and expectation is that these individuals will create personal and professional renewal retreats that work with people in a cohort model, over time, and that ascribe to the key principles and practices of formation. The Center for Courage & Renewal prepares facilitators for work with those in serving professions such as education, healthcare, law, religious life, and non-profit and community leadership.

In addition to planning and facilitating retreats, the facilitator also plays a crucial role in introducing and establishing these personal and professional renewal retreats in a community. This is not done in isolation. Often a small group of "stakeholders"â€”a group of interested advocatesâ€”is involved in these efforts. Thus, our facilitators combine roles of leadership and facilitation with responsibility for program development in their community or profession.

Medicine & Healthcare

Increasing numbers of leaders and educators in medicine and healthcare have offered Courage to Teach and Courage to Lead retreats for physicians, nurses, healthcare administrators, and medical educators. Below is information about two institutions that have introduced this approach to personal and professional renewal to their colleagues, faculty, and students.

Accreditation Council for Graduate Medical Education (ACGME)

In 2001 the ACGME established the â€œParker J. Palmer Courage to Teach Awardï¿½? given annually to ten residency program directors nominated and chosen by their peers as representative of the best in relationship-centered and humanistic medicine. The ACGME is committed to offering the award for at least a decade in support of creating a cadre of exemplary leaders who can support the ACGME in changing the accreditation standards to encourage patient-centered professionalism in medical education across the United States. As part of the award each awardee is invited to an annual Courage to Teach retreat led by Drs. David Leach and Paul Batalden.

http://www.acgme.org/acWebsite/palmerAward/pa_leachMemo.asp

Indiana University School of Medicine

Since 2002, a system-wide relationship-centered care initiative at the Indiana University School of Medicine has included a Courage to Lead retreat series for administrators, faculty, and students. These retreats have had a strong impact on the culture of the medical school and on the quality of relationships of those who teach, practice, and learn there.

Rich Frankel, Researcher and Professor at IUSM and Courage & Renewal Facilitator, reflected on the impact of the Courage to Lead retreats at Indiana University School of Medicine:

We are entering our third year of seasonal retreats for faculty and administrators at IUSM. We were interested to know how the work was impacting individuals in each of the cohorts and devised a simple research design in which we asked participants to write a short narrative about an experience or event in the first retreat that awakened a sense of leading from within. In each of the successive retreats, participants got their original narrative back and were asked to reflect on how their sense of leading from within was deepening or changing. We have been sitting with the first set of narratives for a little over a year doing both a cross sectional and longitudinal depth analysis of them.

What is apparent from these narratives is a deep sense of how courage work impacts individuals not only on an episodic basis but also over time. As we analyze the various threads and strands of the narratives of individual transformation it is also clear that the organization itself is being transformed, one conversation at a time.
