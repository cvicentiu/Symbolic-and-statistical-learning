Staff

CO-DIRECTORS:

Marcy Jackson, M.S.W., M.P.H., is Vice President and Co-Director of the Center for Courage & Renewal (CCR), and has primary responsibility for CCR courses and programs, including the Facilitator Preparation Program. She has been facilitating COURAGE TO TEACH® and circle of trust retreats since 1996. For the past 25 years Marcy has worked extensively with individuals, groups, and families as a child and family therapist. She has led retreats in a variety of settings—in hospitals and health care, educational and retreat centers, community mental health and tribal settings.
marcy@couragerenewal.org

Rick Jackson, M.A.R., M.Div., is President and Co-Director of the Center for Courage & Renewal, focusing primarily on community and financial development, evaluation, and outreach. Rick consults and speaks with non-profit organizations and foundations on a range of topics, including sustaining the identity and integrity of teachers and leaders, the role of youth in culture, community leadership development, and strategies to foster long-term social change. He was ordained a minister in the United Church of Christ in 1979, and was an executive with the YMCA for 25 years.
rick@couragerenewal.org

SENIOR ADVISOR:

Parker J. Palmer, Ph.D., is a writer, teacher, and activist who works independently on issues in education, community, leadership, spirituality, and social change. His work spans a wide range of institutions—colleges and universities, public schools, community organizations, religious institutions, corporations, and foundations. He serves as senior associate of the American Association of Higher Education, as senior advisor to the Fetzer Institute, and is the founder and senior advisor for the Center for Courage & Renewal. His publications include ten poems, some eighty essays, and several books, including The Promise of Paradox, The Company of Strangers, To Know As We Are Known, The Active Life, The Courage to Teach, Let Your Life Speak, and A Hidden Wholeness: The Journey Toward An Undivided Life.

PROGRAM DIRECTOR:

Terry Chadsey, M.S., is program director for Courage & Renewal for School Leaders. He has worked in public education for over 30 years, teaching grades K through 8 in Chicago, Australia and Washington for 22 of those years. He received his principal credential in the first cohort of the Danforth Principal Preparation program at the University of Washington. For the past nine years, he has provided coaching and professional development to districts and schools, principals and teachers. As a central office administrator he recently managed a large Communities That Care® initiative for Seattle Public Schools. He is a lead trainer for Positive Discipline® and is a Courage to Teach/Courage to Lead Facilitator.
terry@couragerenewal.org

DEVELOPMENT DIRECTOR: 

Debra Holland is director of development for the Center for Courage & Renewal.  She has worked in development for over 25 years, raising funds for children’s health and welfare, cancer research, civic and environmental causes.  For the past ten years, she led fundraising efforts for Seattle-based First Place, a school and agency dedicated to social justice and ending homelessness for children and families.  Debra’s volunteer efforts include serving on the boards of the Junior League of Seattle and downtown Seattle Rotary.  She is founder of the Fran Nordstrom Guild of Children’s Hospital & Regional Medical Center (Seattle) and currently volunteers for environmental causes in her community. debra@couragerenewal.org

PROGRAM MANAGER/REGISTRAR:

Robin Gaphni, M.A., is program manager and registrar for programs and courses for the Center for Courage & Renewal. She has a background in Adult Education and has been an instructor in Motheread/Fatheread, a national family reading and literacy program. Robin has been involved in local education efforts, volunteering in the classroom, on committees and is currently on a board that provides funding for professional development for teachers.
robin@couragerenewal.org

COMMUNICATIONS DIRECTOR:

Tracey Denlinger works part-time as the development director for CCR. She is the daughter of a public school teacher and has more than 16 years of experience serving non-profit organizations as a staff person, board member, and volunteer, and has particular expertise in fundraising and political activism. Prior to joining CCR in 2003, Tracey worked for Planned Parenthood of Western Washington for more than ten years.
tracey@couragerenewal.org

FINANCE DIRECTOR:

Teri Shimmin, C.P.A., serves as Finance Director for CCR.  She brings experience in evaluating and designing systems to improve financial efficiencies in small companies and organizations.  Prior to joining CCR in August 2006, Teri worked as the Business Manager for SeaShare, a not- for- profit organization that distributes seafood for hunger-relief efforts nationwide.  She has also held positions with large accounting firms.  Teri has served as trustee and Treasurer on the board of Hospice of Kitsap County, Washington. She is also on the board of Bainbridge Graduate Institute.  terishimmin@couragerenewal.org.

ADMINISTRATIVE COORDINATOR:

Cyndi Merritt works part time as the administrative coordinator of our office.  Prior to joining CCR in June 2006, she served non-profits for many years as a staff person and volunteer in finance, operations, volunteer program management, development and records management, including 10 years with the Nature Conservancy and 8 years with the University of Washington.
cyndi@couragerenewal.org
