Frank keller

 

Born in 1976, designer and illustrator near Waldshut, Germany.
 
During his training, he was drawn to the USA and the Netherlands to acquire valuable practical experience there. Since returning, he has been freelancing as a designer and illustrator. His bandwidth ranges from cheeky cartoon motifs through logos to classical poster design. 

 

Schnapps

Whether a shot of clear or rye, one thing is clear with schnapps.

The finely distilled spirit has left its down-to-earth roots in Germany to join the noble ranks of strong cognacs, bourbons and vodkas. And another thing is clear: the Ritzenhoff collection won't let it down.
 

oSchnapps: ... the short ones! Schnapps – the pocket version of the Ritzenhoff collection for lovers of high-proof drinks or the collector who already has everything. The first collection generated the great success of the small glasses in the black tube – the smallest gift for men since the tie-pin. The project was born in spring 1996 as a trade-fair gift. At the time, the small boozer bird by the swallow researchers at the Düsseldorf Design Office Kunstflug quickly won the hearts of all who saw it. Now the collection of short clear ones for autumn 1996 has been supplemented by ten new motifs, always matching their bigger brothers and sisters in the Beer Collection and always accompanied by the beer-mat motifs specially designed for the glasses. The Schnapps collection shows entirely different design concepts – sometimes abstract, sometimes figurative, sometimes modern and sometimes classical. Here, too, the variety of the other collections is reflected, but all motifs deal with the sharp fire of spiritual drinks - sometimes more and sometimes rather less directly. But as surveys are supposed to have shown, not many drinkers have puzzled for too long about the decorations – the colours really start to shimmer after a few high-proof rounds and reveal their various meanings to those of even limited imagination.
