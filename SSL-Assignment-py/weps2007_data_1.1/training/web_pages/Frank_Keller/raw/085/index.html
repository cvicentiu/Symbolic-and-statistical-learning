Timeline
 
Saturday, October 17, 1964
Torie is born in Heppner, Oregon to James Herbert and Mary Ellen Myers.  She was the youngest child with two older brothers: Jimmy and Andy.
 
Saturday, January 28, 1967

While only a little beyond 2 years in age, Torie loses her father, James Herbert Myers, to a brain tumor.  He is only 38 years old.
 
Sunday, April 23, 1967
Torie is baptised at St. John's Episcopal Church in Hermiston, Oregon.  Her baptismal sponsors (God parents) are her uncle and aunt Tom and Arlene Myers and Pamela Robinson.
 
September 1970 through May 1976
Torie attends West Park Elementary School in Hermiston, Oregon.
 
September 1976 through May 1978
Torie attends Armand Larive Junior High School.  A school in which she will later teach.
 
Summers 1978 and 1979
Torie coaches Little League Girls Softball in Hermiston. In Torie's own words from 1982 "I have worked summers coaching Girls Little League Softball and found this to be most rewarding. This has led me to choose teaching as a professional goal, specializing in coaching and physical education."
 
September 1978 through May 1982
Torie attends Hermiston High School. While there she is a member of the National Honor Society, Girl's League, band, is a Rotary Scholar, and participates in varsity volleyball, basketball, softball, and track. She receives Most Improved in Basketball (1979-80), Most Inspirational Basketball (1980-81), Honorable Mention All-Conference Volleyball (1981-82), and Athlete of the Year (1981-82). She is Team Captain Volleyball (1981) and Team Captain Basketball (1981-82).

 
Sunday, May 23, 1982
Torie graduates from Hermiston High School.
 
September 1982 through December 1986
Torie attends Pacific University in Forest Grove, Oregon. While there she majors in elementary education. She is award an Athletic Talent Grant and participates in varsity volleyball, basketball, softball, and track (discus and shot put). She is co-captain of the varsity volleyball team, receives an honorable mention all-conference, and is Northwest Intercollegiate Conference All-Conference Middle Blocker. She is president of the Elementary Education Club, member of the Activities and Cultural Events (ACE) Board, and is a student body representative.

In Torie's words "I must admit that my choice in my Undergraduate school was because of my ability to plays sports at that level.  I knew I wanted to play college ball. (volleyball, basketball, track, and softball.) When I got there I soon very soon realized that Pacific was very academic.. so I roll up my sleeves and got to work. I am a better person because of it.  Thank God the professors pushed me to think and use my brain.  Something I try to get my kids to do everyday."

 
1986
Torie coaches middle school girl's basketball at Visitation Catholic School in Verboort, Oregon. 
 
Thursday, December 18, 1986
Torie graduates from Pacific University in Forest Grove, Oregon with a Bachelor of Arts degree in Elementary Education.

 
February 1987 through June 1991
Torie is a substitute teacher for the Hermiston, Umatilla , and Stanfield School Districts.
 
Summers 1989 through 1993
Torie is program director for the Ascension School in Cove, Oregon during her summers off from teaching.  Ascension School is a church camp of the Episcopal Diocse of Eastern Oregon. 

In Torie's words "Did I tell you that I worked in Cove OR. for 5 summers?  I was the Program Director at the Episcopal Summer Camp. I was the first and to my knowledge the only director to eat fire.  Our Bishop did it too.  In my younger days where were my brains? Don't worry I was trained.. Or at least coached on how to do it.  I haven't done it in quite a while, but I just want you to know I am a woman of may diverse talents.  = ) I figure fire eating is good training for parenthood. Ha!   Did you ever go out to Cove and do any hiking or swimming in the infamous Cove Pool.  We went everyday at the camp. In fact Desmond Tutu was the speaker for one of the camps and he too swam in the Cove Pool.  I don't think Cove gets many Nobel Prize Winners.  I really like the beauty of the area up there.  We even ventured out to Catherine Creek, and climbed Mt. Fanny."
 
August 1991 through June 1995
Torie heads up a bilingual education program as a migrant resources teacher for the Umatilla-Morrow County ESD at Riverside High School in Boardman, Oregon.  She also coaches softball (JV and assistant for varsity) and volleyball (Freshman, JV, and assistant for varsity) at the school. 
 
August 1995 through June 1996
Torie teaches to English Language Learners at Rocky Heights Elementary School in Hermiston.
 
Springs 1996 through 1999
Torie coaches softball at Hermiston High School first as the JV coach and then as an assistant varsity coach working with Kay Edwards.

 
Summers 1996 and 1997

Torie coaches with Randy Barton the ASA softball team the Columbia Whitecaps. They are State Champions in the 16 and under. They go to the West Coast National Tournament and place 10th.


 
August 1996 through February 2005
Torie teaches 6th grade at Armand Larive Middle School when it first re-opens as a middle school.  She put her heart into planning what this school would be like and lived that out each day. When she first started here, she taught every subject, just like it is in elementary school.  She always had a mind for science and math. When the school was changed, Torie gladly took up teaching of science and math. She was the last original member of the Teal team still teaching sixth grade. She was a team leader, a volleyball coach and half-time athletic director. Torie did it all!

 
Thursday, October 9, 1997
Torie is seriously injured in an accident.  She was on a school bus with her middle school volleyball team headed home from a game when the bus was hit by a car.  Torie has to have her shoulder reconstructed and is out from teaching and coaching for a couple months. More Details from the Tri-City Herald.
 
August 1998
Torie finishes her Master of Science degree in Bilingual Education from Western Oregon University in Monmouth, Oregon.
 
Saturday, June 12, 1999
Torie graduates with a Master of Science degree in Education from Western Oregon University in Monmouth, Oregon.
 
Saturday, April 22, 2000
Torie is confirmed at Our Lady of Angels Catholic Church in Hermiston during the Easter Vigil Mass. Torie's confirmation sponsor is her friend Kay Edwards.
 
Thursday, May 4, 2000
Torie receives the Educator of the Year Award for Armand Larive Middle School.
 
September 2000
Torie and her future husband, Paul, first meet on-line at the website www.catholicsingles.com.
 
Autumns 2000 and 2001
Torie assists Kay Edwards in coaching varsity girls volleyball for Hermiston High School.
 
Saturday, May 19, 2001
Torie meets her future husband face-to-face on their first date at Casa Mia Italian restaurant in Kennewick, Washington.  Torie's favorite food is Italian. In her own words "I really Love Casa Mia.  I have a thing for Italian food.  No place to get it in Hermiston."
 
Saturday, July 6, 2002
Torie Myers marries Paul Keller at Our Lady of Angels Catholic Church in Hermiston, Oregon.  Here's a message Torie sent Paul via the Praire Home Companion website "Paul, the love of my life. On our long awaited Wedding Day, I give to you all I am and all I am to become. Your Bride, Torie"
 
Friday, September 6, 2002
Torie and Paul move into their new home in Richland, Washington that they had built.
 
Wednesday, February 16, 2005
Torie passed away on February 16, 2005 at the age of 40 while at the Oregon Health Sciences University Hospital in Portland, Oregon. She had just been diagnosed with a very rare and generally fatal disease known as amyloidosis.
 
Tuesday, February 22, 2005
A funeral mass is held for Torie in the gymnasium at the  Hermiston High School.  During her funeral, the Rev. Joe Reinig said "She would have smiled broadly at the chosen venue for us, because she loved sports so much."  A eulogy, "Who could forget that twinkle in her eye!", written by Rt. Rev. Rustin Kimsey, a long time friend, is read.  As a final tribute to Torie and her love of sports, everyone joined in singing "Take Me Out to the Ballgame" as her casket was taken away.  Torie is laid to rest at the Hermiston Cemetery.
 
Thursday, April 14, 2005
Torie's father-in-law, Frank Keller, passes away from lymphoma.  http://frank-keller.memory-of.com/
