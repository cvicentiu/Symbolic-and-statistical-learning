Craig crushed in Hughes landslide
Author: Julie DeBruin
Date: Nov 15, 2006
Text Size

Oro-Medonte Township's deputy mayor Harry Hughes is moving to the top job next month after beating current mayor Neil Craig in a landslide victory Monday night.

"It was a surprise to me that the numbers are so wide apart," admitted Hughes, shortly before the final poll results came in, but his lead started much earlier that night and continued to grow. In the end, he tallied 4,539 votes to 1,797 for Craig.

The retired school principal had heard from many residents during his campaign who told him he had their support.

"There were issues in every area, but there were also commonalities," he said.

"It's nice to see the support is coming from across the township," said Hughes, as he led in all five wards.

Many residents in the far reaches of the township felt their concerns were not being addressed and he maintains that he will work in these areas to make sure everyone has a voice.

"People have lots of expectations," he said, of the next four years and he intends to ensure there is consistency across the township.

"The one thing I really enjoy is knocking on doors," Hughes said of the campaign. "It was a great opportunity to find out what people were thinking."

While the race was not without bickering, Hughes said the disagreements were among campaign workers, not the candidates.

"There were some issues that were divisive," he said of the current council, including what he called governance issues. One contentious issue was the sale of waterfront property to nearby property owners for a fraction of its appraised value.

A disappointed Craig was surprised by his numbers as well. "Who knows why people vote the way they do?" he said. Craig was acclaimed in the last election. He was deputy mayor before then, but assumed the mayor's seat after Ian Beard died unexpectedly in 2002. He and Hughes had opposing view points on a number of issues in the time they worked together. "The voters decided they wanted to have a change," he said.

As several dozen candidates and their supporters waited at the township office for the last few numbers to come in, Hughes was already looking to the future. "It's not over. It's just beginning."

One of two current councillors who ran for deputy mayor will be taking over that seat after next month's swearing in ceremony. Ward 1 councillor Ralph Hough also won easily over three challengers.

Here's how the numbers stacked up:

Ward 2 councillor Ralph Hough (3,080) will be moving into the deputy mayor's chair after beating his three rivals. Ron Sommers (1,295) was second, Michael Vandergeest (958) was next, followed by current ward 3 councillor Paul Marshall (917).

Ward 1 will be represented by Mel Coutanche (919) who easily beat out opponents Simon Kennedy (277) and Conor Begley (216).

The ward 2 race was a tight one, but Terry Allison (593) took the lead over Linda Babulic (572)

Former North Simcoe Green Party candidate Sandy Agnew (551) will be representing ward 3. He beat out John Newell (399) and Lynn Fisher (239).

Current ward 4 councillor John Crawford was acclaimed.

The ward 5 race was a crowded one as six newcomers campaigned for the seat.

Dwight Evans (314) took the lead, narrowly beating out Mary O'Farrell-Bowers (302). David Edwards (261) came in third, followed closely by Allan Newton (203). Eric Rechnitzer (94) was next and Janine Anderson (56) rounded out the race.

Voter turnout was higher than the last election, but still below the provincial average. Last night, 31 per cent of eligible voters turned out, compared to 26 per cent three years ago.
