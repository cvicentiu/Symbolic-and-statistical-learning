West Village plans in place

TU breaking ground within weeks; 650 beds in phase one

by Brian Stelter in News

Towson is about to break ground on the first phase of a massive new housing project. The West Village housing complex, along Towsontown Boulevard and Osler Drive, will eventually house 3,000 students, according to the campus master plan. Phase one includes two residence halls with a total of 650 beds.

'Godspell' goes 'Off-Campus'

by Amanda Doran in Arts

If you've ever looked at the buildings across from Stephens Hall and wondered what goes on there, this weekend and next are chances to find out. The Newman Club and friends will be putting on a production of the popular musical "Godspell" in an unconventional venue: a small chapel. "Since I was a freshman, I thought 'Godspell' would be a cool show to do in the chapel," director and 2006 alum, Erin Lorenz said. "Then we just had the right people at the right time and everyone really wanted to do the show."

Men's Basketball: Drexel foils season finale

Six seniors play final home game at Towson Center before 3,218 fans

by Daniel Abraham in Sports

Gary Neal put on a show in the final home game of his career. He made several crowd-dazzling shots on his way to a 30-point finish. However, his efforts weren't enough for the Tigers to pull out the win as they fell to Drexel, 77-68, in front of more than 3,200 fans at the Towson Center Saturday afternoon. Despite the fact that Neal played well offensively and his team nearly upset one of the conference's best teams, the senior remained frustrated with the loss. "Losing games, period, hurts," he said. "A loss is a loss, getting blown out by 30 or playing a close game hurts just as much. But we're going to get back to the drawing board this week and we're going to do everything in our power to get four wins in the tournament this weekend.

Boogying all night for Miracle Network

Annual marathon TigerTHON, runs from 5 p.m. to 5 a.m. in University Union, Potomac Lounge

by Nick Di Marco in News

Students danced from 5 p.m. to 5 a.m. to raise money for the John's Hopkins Children's Miracle Network during TigerTHON, an annual fund-raiser held Friday in the University Union Potomac Lounge. More than 100 people participated and $6,000 was raised. This year the marathon was a little shorter than it was last year.
