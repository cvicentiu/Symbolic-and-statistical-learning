February 2, 2006

UMBI Honors Former Governor Harry R. Hughes and Senator Jennie M. Forehand at Annual Legislative Breakfast

Annapolis, MD-UMBI hosted its annual Legislative Breakfast and Awards Ceremony in Annapolis, MD today, honoring former Governor Harry Hughes and Senator Jennie M. Forehand for their commitment to UMBI. It was under the Hughes administration that the Maryland Biotechnology Institute was created in 1985, with the support of Senator Jennie Forehand, who served on the appropriations committee at the time.

More than 100 people attended the early morning event; including several members of the Maryland General Assembly and biotechnology industry stakeholder. In addition, University System of Maryland Regents Cliff Kendall and former Governor Marvin Mandel, State Treasurer Nancy Kopp, and UMBI founder Dr. Rita Colwell also participated in the program. The award ceremony was one of many events marking UMBI’s 20th anniversary, which correlates with this fiscal year (July 1, 2005- June 30, 2006).

Prior to the award ceremony, attendees were able to speak with UMBI education and outreach coordinators to learn more about the Institute’s loaner lab program – a program which provides Maryland science teachers and their schools with free biotech lab kits which cover seven different research areas. The loaner lab program benefits all regions of the state and has become a very popular program. Officials also had the opportunity to meet some of the new hires at UMBI’s expanded Shady Grove facility and pick up informational literature focusing on the new GMP training and biomanufacturing program and plant and insect transformation cores.

Following the reception Dr. Jennie Hunter-Cevera, president of UMBI, lead the awards ceremony. During her remarks she commended Senator Forehand for her commitment to learning about the science and supporting the biotech community. “From the moment we decided that we wanted to expand our facilities in Shady Grove, Jennie was there to help us and has since become a staunch supporter for biotechnology.” Dr. Hunter-Cevera noted that Senator Forehand has served on the NIH bio-safety committee for more than 14 years and last year co-sponsored the Biotechnology Investment Act of 2005.

“It’s been a pleasure to be a part of UMBI’s success,” said Senator Jennie M. Forehand. “UMBI is really the icing on the cake, with increasing industry partnerships, research, and seminars -- and with Jennie (Hunter-Cevera), the sky’s the limit! UMBI really is an asset to the State, and we’re wholeheartedly excited about what lies ahead.”

Next, Dr. Jennie Hunter-Cevera recognized Former Governor Harry Hughes, noting that he was “responsible for creating the ad hoc Committee on high technology in Maryland back in 1982 which concluded that a strategic investment stimulating high technology in Maryland would yield strong economic development benefits to the State.” She commended former Governor Harry Hughes for putting funds in his budget to support the creation of UMBI in 1985, as well as being a key member of the Maryland Families for Stem Cell Research campaign and a lifelong advocate for the protection of the Chesapeake Bay.

“Governor Hughes is the muscle behind this operation, he put the funds in his budget to support us and is an amazing individual who believes in science and research, and getting people the help and treatment they deserve,” said Dr. Hunter-Cevera. “We are very pleased that he agreed to serve on our 20th Anniversary Honorary Executive Committee and to present him with this award today. Without Dr. Colwell’s vision and the financial backing of former Governor Hughes, there would be no UMBI.”

Governor Hughes, also a former University System of Maryland Regent, jokingly said he knew he was in trouble when John Toll and Rita Colwell came to see him, and he was right because they “got him.” In a more serious moment, he commended Dr. Rita Colwell for her vision and Dr. Hunter-Cevera for her current successes, noting how proud they both must be. “Biotechnology is amazingly important to our state, and I congratulate all of you on your accomplishments,” said former Governor Hughes as he accepted his award.

After the presentation of the leaderships awards Dr. Hunter-Cevera recognized UMBI faculty members who received patent awards in the past fiscal year, as well as faculty honored for service to the scientific community. She concluded the program by inviting everyone to attend UMBI’s 20th Anniversary Gala event set for June 3, 2006.


(Left to Right: Regent Cliff Kendall, Dr. Rita Colwell, Former Governor Harry R. Hughes, Senator Jennie M. Forehand, Dr. Jennie Hunter-Cevera, and UMBI BOV Chair Bill Gust)

Celebrating our 20th anniversary year, UMBI is Maryland's premier biotechnology research institute within the University System of Maryland and was established in 1985. The University of Maryland Biotechnology Institute (UMBI) consists of five major research and education centers and is dedicated to advancing the frontiers of biotechnology. UMBI’s centers of research include: CARB, the Center for Advanced Research in Biotechnology located in Rockville; CBR, the Center for Biosystems Research located in College Park; and COMB, the Center of Marine Biotechnology, MBC, the Medical Biotechnology Center, and IHV, the Institute of Human Virology, all located in Baltimore. For more information, visit www.umbi.umd.edu
