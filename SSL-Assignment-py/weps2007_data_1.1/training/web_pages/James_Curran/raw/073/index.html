Man denies murder outside Dublin pub

Wednesday, 14 June 2006 22:18

A 48-year-old man has denied the murder of a 42-year-old man outside a pub in Dublin in April 2005.

Bernard Dempsey from Golden Lane is standing trial at the Central Criminal Court charged with the murder of James Curran in a public house on Francis Street in the city.

Mr Curran, who was a kickboxing champion, was shot three times in the head at point blank range.

Advertisement

The victim was rushed to the nearby St James's Hospital but was dead within minutes.

Opening the case, prosecuting counsel Brendan Grehan said this was a calculated, cold-blooded, assassination type killing where the clear intention was to kill.

Bernard Dempsey and James Curran were connected through marriage - Mr Curran's brother was married to Mr Dempsey's sister. 

There were up to 30 people in the Green Lizard pub on the night of the killing and many of them are expected to give evidence of what they saw on the night. 

The jury was today told that a variety of accounts of the shooting will be given.
