Areas of Interest

Molecular Mechanisms of Protein Synthesis, Gene Regulation, Nucleic Acid Chemistry, Microbial Evolutionary Mechanisms

Research

The ribosomal translation of the genetic code is a fundamental biological process. Cells are composed mostly of protein, and cellular well-being depends on the accurate and efficient production of that biomass. In addition, there are specific mRNA sites in which errors occur at very high frequencies to generate active proteins. For example, a very high frequency frameshift occurs at a specific site in the AIDS virus mRNA. If we could prevent this "programmed frameshift," then the AIDS virus could not propagate. We have ongoing projects that explore the molecular mechanisms of programmed frameshifting, regulation of the RNA polymerase genes of E. coli, and the evolution of the genetic code.
