James Curran

Dr James Curran is a Canberra-based historian specialising in twentieth century Australian political culture.

He graduated with a PhD in history from the University of Sydney in 2001 and has lectured in Australian studies at the Australian Catholic University.

Dr Curran has contributed articles to the Journal of Australian Studies and the Australian Journal of Politics and History, and has published opinion pieces in The Australian Financial Review and The Australian newspapers.

The Power of Speech, Australian Prime Ministers Defining the National Image (Melbourne University Press 2004) is his first book.

As the John Curtin Prime Ministerial Library Visiting Scholar for 2004 he is researching Curtin's 1944 plan for closer imperial cooperation in the post-war world and his future research interests include political biography and the period of the 'new nationalism' in 1960s and 70s Australia.
