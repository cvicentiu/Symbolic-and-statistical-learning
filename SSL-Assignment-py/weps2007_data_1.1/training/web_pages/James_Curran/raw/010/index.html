ACCOMPLISHMENTS

  • Awarded a two-year postdoctoral fellowship grant by the Foundation of Research in Science and Technology in New Zealand (FORST) to continue and advance my work in statistical applications in the field of forensics, broadening my focus from glass to include DNA evidence. This grant was awarded to approximately 15% of the recent Ph.D. graduates who applied.
  • During my Ph.D., I systematically replaced or enhanced the statistical technology for forensic glass analysis originating in the United Kingdom and developed a rival software package (StAG). My software, which is distributed in the USA, Switzerland, England, Scotland, Australia and New Zealand, is widely considered to be the new standard, and New Zealand has superseded the UK as leader in the statistical analysis of glass evidence.
  • Developed new statistical methods for analysis of glass evidence with respect to elemental composition, implemented in laboratories in the USA, Switzerland and Germany.
  • Developed software for analysis of forensic DNA evidence for the Institute of Environmental Science and Research (ESR), working with Drs John Buckleton, SallyAnn Harbison, and Stephen Cordiner, and Professors David Balding and Bruce Weir.
  • Awarded a three-year PhD scholarship by the Institute of Environmental Science and Research (ESR) – 1994.
