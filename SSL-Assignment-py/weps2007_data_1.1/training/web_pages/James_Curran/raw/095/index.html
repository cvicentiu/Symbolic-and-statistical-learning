Man remanded over Dublin murder

Monday, 18 April 2005 21:44

A 47-year-old man has appeared in court charged in connection with the murder of James Curran, who was shot dead in the Green Lizard pub in Dublin city just over two weeks ago.

Bernard Dempsey, a single man with an address at Golden Lane in Dublin, was arrested in Dublin's south inner city on Saturday afternoon. He was remanded in custody.

He had been questioned at Kevin Street Garda Station under Section 30 of the Offences Against the State Act.

Advertisement

42-year-old Mr Curran was drinking in the Green Lizard on Francis Street on the night of Sunday 3 April last when a man approached him and shot him in the head.

Mr Curran, who was a kickboxing champion, was originally from the south inner city but had been living on Dublin's northside with his partner and family.
