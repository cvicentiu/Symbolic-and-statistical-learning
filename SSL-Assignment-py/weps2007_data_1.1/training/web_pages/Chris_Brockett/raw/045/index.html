Here is a list of Coling 2004 papers that may be of interest. The proceedings can be found in the SSLI online proceedings ( http://ssli.ee.washington.edu/proceedings/coling04/ ) Papers marked with * are especially interesting, in my opinion. --Kevin

Contents

  1. MACHINE TRANSLATION: MODELS
  2. MACHINE TRANSLATION: EVALUATION
  3. PARALLEL/PARAPHRASE CORPORA, CORPORA CONSTRUCTION
  4. LANGUAGE MODELING
  5. WORD SIMILARITY, WORD SENSE DISAMBIGUATION
  6. TAGGING AND HIGHER LEVEL NLP (DEEP PARSING, DISCOURSE, SUMMARIZATION)
  7. MACHINE LEARNING STUFF
  8. ISSUES IN CHINESE/JAPANESE NLP
  9. RELATED TO SPEECH RECOGNITION/SYNTHESIS

MACHINE TRANSLATION: MODELS

Improved Word Alignment Using a Symmetric Lexicon Model 
Richard Zens, Evgeny Matusov and Hermann Ney

Example-based Machine Translation Based on Syntactic Transfer with Statistical Models 
Kenji Imamura, Hideo Okuma, Taro Watanabe and Eiichiro Sumita

Reordering Constraints for Phrase-Based Statistical Machine Translation 
Richard Zens, Hermann Ney, Taro Watanabe and Eiichiro Sumita

*Syntax-Based Alignment: Supervised or Unsupervised? 
Hao Zhang and Daniel Gildea

*Improving a Statistical MT System with Automatically Learned Rewrite Patterns 
Fei Xia and Michael McCord

*A Path-based Transfer Model for Machine Translation 
Dekang Lin

*Linguistically Informed Statistical Models of Constituent Structure for Ordering in Sentence Realization 
Eric Ringger, Michael Gamon, Robert C. Moore, David Rojas, Martine Smets and Simon Corston-Oliver

A Unified Approach in Speech-to-Speech Translation: Integrating Features of Speech recognition and Machine Translation 
Ruiqiang Zhang, Genichiro Kikui, Hirofumi Yamamoto, Frank Soong, Taro Watnabe and Wai Kit Lo

MACHINE TRANSLATION: EVALUATION

Extending MT evaluation tools with translation complexity metrics 
Bogdan Babych, Debbie Elliott and Anthony Hartley

*Confidence Estimation for Machine Translation 
John Blatz, Erin Fitzgerald, George Foster, Simona Gandrabur, Cyril Goutte, Alex Kulesza, Alberto Sanchis and Nicola Ueffing

*ORANGE: a Method for Evaluating Automatic Evaluation Metrics for Machine Translation 
Chin-Yew Lin and Franz Josef Och

PARALLEL/PARAPHRASE CORPORA, CORPORA CONSTRUCTION

*Unsupervised Construction of Large Paraphrase Corpora: Exploiting Massively Parallel News Sources
Bill Dolan, Chris Quirk and Chris Brockett

*Text Induced Spelling Correction 
Martin Reynaert

Multi-level Bootstrapping For Extracting Parallel Sentences From a Quasi-Comparable Corpus 
Pascale Fung and Percy Cheung

LANGUAGE MODELING

Language Model Adaptation for Statistical Machine Translation via Structured Query Models 
Bing Zhao, Matthias Eck and Stephan Vogel

*Statistical Language Modeling with Performance Benchmarks using Various Levels of Syntactic-Semantic Information 
Dharmendra Kanejiya, Arun Kumar and Surendra Prasad

WORD SIMILARITY, WORD SENSE DISAMBIGUATION

Feature Vector Quality and Distributional Similarity 
Maayan Geffet and Ido Dagan

Cognate Mapping - A Heuristic Strategy for the Semi-Supervised Acquisition of a Spanish Lexicon from a Portuguese Seed Lexicon 
Stefan Schulz, Kornel Markó, Eduardo Sbrissia, Percy Nohama and Udo Hahn

*Identification of Confusable Drug Names: A New Approach and Evaluation Methodology 
Grzegorz Kondrak and Bonnie Dorr

PageRank on Semantic Networks, with Application to Word Sense Disambiguation 
Rada Mihalcea, Paul Tarau and Elizabeth Figa

TAGGING AND HIGHER LEVEL NLP (DEEP PARSING, DISCOURSE, SUMMARIZATION)

*Combining Hierarchical Clustering and Machine Learning to Predict High-Level Discourse Structure 
Caroline Sporleder and Alex Lascarides

Modern Natural Language Interfaces to Databases: Composing Statistical Parsing with Semantic Tractability 
Ana-Maria Popescu, Alex Armanasu, Oren Etzioni, David Ko and Alexander Yates

Skeletons in the parser: Using a shallow parser to improve deep parsing 
Mary Swift, James Allen and Daniel Gildea

Generating Overview Summaries of Ongoing Email Thread Discussions 
Stephen Wan and Kathy McKeown

Tagging with Hidden Markov Models Using Ambiguous Tags 
Alexis Nasr, Frédéric Bechét and Alexandra Volanschi

Wide-Coverage Semantic Representations from a CCG Parser 
Johan Bos, Stephen Clark, Mark Steedman, James R. Curran and Julia Hockenmaier

MACHINE LEARNING STUFF

Partially Distribution-Free Learning of Regular Languages from Positive Samples 
Alexander Clark and Franck Thollard

*Why Nitpicking Works: Evidence for Occam's Razor in Error Correctors 
Dekai Wu, Grace Ngai and Marine Carpuat

ISSUES IN CHINESE/JAPANESE NLP

Chinese and Japanese Word Segmentation Using Word-Level and Character-Level Information 
Tetsuji Nakagawa

Chinese Segmentation and New Word Detection using Conditional Random Fields 
Fuchun Peng, Fangfang Feng and Andrew McCallum

*A hybrid back-transliteration system for Japanese 
Slaven Bilac and Hozumi Tanaka

RELATED TO SPEECH RECOGNITION/SYNTHESIS

Thai Spelling Recognition Using a Continuous Speech Corpus 
Chutima Pisarn and Thanaruk Theeramunkong

New Statistical Methods for Phrase Break Prediction 
Helmut Schmid and Michaela Atterer

*Analysis and Detection of Reading Miscues for Interactive Literacy Tutors 
Katherine Lee, Andreas Hagen, Nicholas Romanyshyn, Sean Martin and Bryan Pellom
