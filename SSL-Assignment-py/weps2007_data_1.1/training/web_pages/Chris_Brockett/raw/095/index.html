High school roundup
 
Just happy to be there
Gilford, Kearsarge win Alpine meet

From Monitor staff reports
January 12. 2007 8:00AM

YOON S. BYUN / Monitor staff
Bishop Brady’s Lara Baldwin carves a turn during her first run in yesterday’s nine-team slalom event at Pats Peak. The Green Giant girls came in third place, behind Kearsarge and Gilford. Gilford took first in the boys’ race. For an online photo gallery, go to concordmonitor.com/photoextra.
Zoom
Purchase Photos Online

Y
esterday's nine-team slalom race was not necessarily about winning and losing. Sure, Gilford had the highest score in the boys' group with 380 points, and Kearsarge topped the girls' race with 368. But the real beauty of this particular Alpine meet was that there even was a meet.

"This was most of the teams' first meet," Bishop Brady Coach Sal Alosa said. "There was snow on the ground, it was great to be out actually racing."

After what has thus far been an unusually mild winter, most of the scheduled meets had been postponed or canceled. Yesterday at Pats Peak, the conditions were finally right.

"Racing was something new and fun, the whole crew enjoyed it," said Gilford Coach Steve Selig, whose team hadn't even had a practice race, spending most of its practice time just exercising and working on technique.

That training seemed to pay off for the Golden Eagles, as their boys came in first place with a combined two-race score of 380 points, followed by Kearsarge (367), St. Thomas (364), Brady (350), Oyster River (339), Bow (309), Hillsboro-Deering (308), Trinity (283) and Newport (139).

Gilford's Tom Bobotaf placed first in the boys' race, finishing his two runs in a combined time of 47.59 seconds. Right behind him in second was teammate Eric Ostrand with a combined time of 47.86 seconds. Rounding out the top five finishers were Ryan Serrall (third) and Jake Seidel (fifth) of Kearsarge, as well as Brady's Dan Finnegan (fourth).

Bow's Connor Whalley and Alex Reid raced well, as did H-D's Jeff Moore.

Kearsarge scored 368 to win the girls' race, followed by Gilford (364), Brady (352), St. Thomas (348), Oyster River (340), Bow (312), Trinity (310), Newport (303) and H-D (208).

The Cougars were led by Laura Berger, who finished first in a time of 53.19 seconds. Anneliese Buttinger of Gilford got second place with a 53.87 finish. The rest of the top five were Dani Peck of St. Thomas (third), Kearsarge's Sarah Cotrell (fourth) and Alex Taylor from Oyster River (fifth).

Also racing well were Leslie Stranton of Brady, Elyse Lefebvre of Bow and Sarah Marin of H-D.

BOYS' ALPINE SKIING

John Stark first

The Generals had four of the top 14 finishers in a slalom race at Crotched Mountain to edge Con-Val, 377-362.

Travis Nevins led the Generals with a second-place finish in a two-race combined time of 58.08 seconds, just off the pace set by winner John DeWispelaere of Milford (58.06). Stark's Austin Nevins was third (58.96), Nathan Goldsberry was eighth (1:08.37), Alastair Dowst was 14th (1:12.93), Jack Brown was 44th (1:34.16) and Bobby Stone was 55th.

Hollis-Brookline was third (352), followed by Souhegan (347), Milford (344.5), Timberland (341.5) and Mascenic (151).

GIRLS' ALPINE SKIING

John Stark sixth

Catie Stone was the lone General competitor in a seven-team meet at Crotched Mountain, taking 21st place in a two-run combined time of 1:33.72 in the slalom event.

Timberlane won the event with a score of 381 points, edging Souhegan (378) and Hollis-Brookline (372).

BOYS' NORDIC SKIING

Hopkinton wins

On the strength of placing three skiers in the top five and five in the top 10, the Hawks came out with the victory in a nine-team meet.

Hopkinton (385 points) was followed by Souhegan (370.5), Concord (361.5), Bow (348), Gorham (329), Derryfield (311), Moultonborough (292), Inter-lakes (276) and Sant Bani (232).

Brian Rowe of the Hawks raced to a third-place finish of 13:21, behind winner Tristan Baldwin (12:40) of Souhegan and Chris Brockett (12:56) of Gorham.

Four other Hopkinton skiers made the top 10, including Matt Helrich (fourth), Brad Ramsdell (fifth), Ben Helm (seventh) and Mike Klinker (ninth).

Concord's best finishes came from Nat Nelson, who placed eighth, and Tommy Symmes, who tied for 10th.

Bow received good races from Kevin Baier (12th) and Benjamin Kramer (16th), while Derryfield got points from Kye Birchard (27th) and Patrick Rachel (34th).

GIRLS' NORDIC SKIING

Hopkinton first

Hopkinton's Jenny Helm won the race in 15:04, beating out Leah Hart of Inter-Lakes by 14 seconds. Helm was joined in the top 10 by fellow Hawks Jess Partlow (fourth), Sarah Gilroy (fifth) and Christine Head (sixth) as Hopkinton eased past the competition.

The Hawks scored 388, followed by Inter-lakes (345), Concord (343.5), Gorham (333), Bow (319), Moultonborough (309), Derryfield (303), Souhegan (271) and Sant Bani (160.5).

Lia d'Hemacourt of Concord tied for ninth place and Madison Daniels (15th) also raced well. Bow was led by Stephanie Kramer (14th) and Colby Jacobson (23rd), while Derryfield was paced by Allison Fink (17th) and Paige Houlihan (35th).

BOYS' BASKETBALL

Hopkinton 52, Raymond 45

Hopkinton (4-3) flexed its defensive muscles in the fourth quarter, holding Raymond (2-6) to just three points on nine shots, as the Hawks rebounded from their Tuesday loss to Epping.

"We played with a lot more passion," Coach Dave Chase said.

The defense was led by Jake McManus and John Reen, who helped hold Raymond's top scorer Adam Herod (17 points) to only six points in the second half.

Offensively, point guard Dave Brandt played a great all-around game, with 13 points, eight rebounds, five assists, two steals and only two turnovers.

"He did a nice job controlling the tempo and getting us organized," Chase said.

Dave Harrison led the Hawks in scoring with 16 and also grabbed seven boards. Tom Johnson had nine points and five rebounds, and Jake Nichols added eight points and five boards.
