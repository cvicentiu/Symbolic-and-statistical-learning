New coach, same old Pilots

Thompson takes over experienced De La Salle team

PUBLISHED: April 5, 2005

By John Hetzler
Macomb Daily Sports Writer

Even with a new coach, there are few worries for De La Salle's tennis team.

Advertisement
With nine starters returning from last year's team, the Pilots begin the season as the top-ranked team in Class A in the Macomb County coaches' poll.

"They'll always be strong because their kids do things out of season," Warren-Mott coach Larry Harte said.

Adam Thompson, who has coached the boys team at Kalamazoo Central and the girls team at Galesburg Augusta, moves east to take over as coach of the Pilots.

He has an experienced team to work with, including three top singles players in senior Bill Hackett, senior Matt Cracchiolo and junior Kevin Turnbull.

"We're going to have depth throughout the team," Thompson said.

DLS competes in a tough Catholic League Central Division that includes Detroit Catholic Central, which placed third in the Division 1 state finals, and Brother Rice, which finished fifth at the state finals last season.

MAC Red

Another powerhouse division is the Macomb Area Conference Red Division, where all five teams qualified for the state finals last year.

Led by senior Matt Fisher, Eisenhower is the only Macomb County team in the division.

Defending division champion Grosse Pointe South opens the year ranked No. 6 in Division 1 in the state coaches' poll.

Port Huron Northern (No. 10 in Division 1) and Grosse Pointe North (No. 6 in Division 2) also are ranked in the Michigan High School Tennis Coaches Association poll.

MAC White

Chippewa Valley brings back one of the top singles players in the county in senior R.J. Rodgerson. Last year's MAC White player of the year, Rodgerson will play at No. 1 singles for the Big Reds for the third straight year. The Big Reds received honorable mention consideration in the county coaches' poll.

Ford returns seven starters and has a couple of new faces in coach Jason Battle and freshman Alex Ziajor. "I don't know where he's going to fit since he's a freshman," Battle said, "but he's definitely a top-tier player." Senior captains Kevin Johnson and Charlie Digrego played first doubles last year, but they could play singles this season.

After going 14-1 last year, L'Anse Creuse North is geared for perhaps the best season in school history. Senior Matt Weir will play No. 1 singles and junior Greg Fuhs will play No. 2 singles for the Crusaders. "Our singles are going to be very strong," LCN coach Randy Payne said.

Romeo has just four seniors on its roster, but the Bulldogs are optimistic about their chances. Junior captain Mike Rea moves up to No. 1 singles after playing second singles last season. Junior Justin Gentrer and sophomore Steve Filus will play at first doubles, and senior captain Mike Krieg will play at No. 2 doubles.

Stevenson is led by Nathan Callow. The Titans, who played in the MAC Red last season, return two of their top three players and three of their top eight from last season. Stevenson was honorable mention in the county coaches' poll.

MAC Blue

Cousino returns eight starters, including senior John Sadler, one of the top players in the division and the county. The Patriots, who finished third in the MAC Blue last year, will move from Division 2 to Division 1 for the regional tournament.

Fraser has a new coach in Mark Reznich, who enters hopeful of his team's chances. "It looks promising for us," Reznich said. "We're not going to shy away from it." Senior Tim Leach likely will start out at first singles for the Ramblers. Junior Steve Bower, sophomore Kyle Cosmeriez and senior Kyle Frederick likely will fill out the singles lineup.

L'Anse Creuse returns nine players -- only one of whom is a senior -- and begins the year ranked No. 4 in Class A in the county coaches' poll. Junior Josh Blind will play No. 1 singles for the Lancers.

After going 13-0 and rolling to the MAC Gold title, Sterling Heights moves into the Blue even though just three starters return. Junior Theo Foutris will play No. 1 singles, and senior Dan Kosinski will team with junior Andrei Taut at first doubles. Stallions coach Karie DeBerry hopes to get junior Frank Kwarcinski back soon from an ankle injury, and she is expecting big things from freshmen Jason Gargrave and Julian Coada. "I expect they'll be very competitive and they should come out with quite a few wins," DeBerry said. "I have high expectations."

Utica brings back eight players from last year's team that finished second at the MAC Blue tournament. Seniors Drew Wojchoski, Eric Reitzloff and Jason Wu are in the mix at singles for the Chieftains. Top newcomers for Utica are freshman Jim DelBene and sophomore Gregory Hom.

Warren-Mott returns only five starters from last year's team that did not win a match. Junior Erwin Patalinghug will play No. 1 singles and serve as a co-captain for the Marauders. Senior Matt Wren is the other captain and likely will play either second or third singles.

"This year I think it's wide open," Bates said. "I think our league in particular is wide-open."

MAC Gold

Anchor Bay returns an experienced squad, with two of its top three singles players back and six of its top eight players overall back from last year.

Dakota must replace last year's top player, Chris Green. Four starters return for the Cougars, including sophomores Jeff Lowe and Steve Bloom. Coach Glenn Corey also expects contributions from Chris Brockett and Jake Kennedy. "We're very young," Corey said. "We don't have any seniors and only a couple of juniors."

East Detroit, which played in the MAC Blue last season, moves into the Gold. The Shamrocks must replace their top three singles players, and they have only two of their top eight players from last season back.

Mount Clemens will be led by three seniors -- Jake Westerman, Chris Miller and Josip Knojebod, who will play first through third singles, respectively. Seniors Fred Tinkler and Alex Gravlin will move up to No. 1 doubles for the Bathers. "I have a lot of new kids this year," Mount Clemens coach Don Wisswell said. "I have a decent nucleus coming back."

Warren Woods-Tower moves into the Gold after going 12-1-1 last season in the MAC Silver. Seniors Joe Pysh and Jeff Abrignani lead the Titans at singles, and Jeff Michels heads up doubles for the Titans.

Marysville also competes in the division.

"As far as a clear-cut favorite, it's going to be very, very tight," Wisswell said of the MAC Gold. "It's going to be a competitive division."

MAC Silver

Center Line has seven returning starters, including four seniors. Seniors Alex Schell and Brian McArthur will fill the top two singles spots, and senior Brent Cox and junior Doug Affeldt will play No. 1 doubles.

Fitzgerald returns nine starters from last year's team that finished third in the Silver. Leading the way is senior Josh Sanders at No. 1 singles. Sanders went 5-0 in the division last year, and he will be supported by junior Chris Nowicki, who moves from first doubles to No. 2 singles. Senior Matt Hoard also figures in the mix in singles for the Spartans.

Lakeview will be led by senior Andrew Crabb, sophomore Jeff Hill and senior Justin Janowicz. After low turnout last season, the Huskies have 16 players this season for new coach Kristen Dittmer. "I'm thrilled," Dittmer said of the turnout. "I was really nervous."

South Lake is the defending MAC Silver dual and tournament champion. The Cavaliers return two of their top three players and five of their top eight from last year's team. South Lake enters the season ranked No. 2 in Class B-C-D in the county coach's poll.

Lamphere joins the MAC Silver this season, and Marine City also competes in the division.
