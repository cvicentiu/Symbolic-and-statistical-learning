Engineers' Kapotis Scores 100th Point

WOODSVILLE NH - Dimitri Kapotis scored his 100th career point as the Woodsville boys' soccer team defeated Gorham, 3-1, on Wednesday.

Kapotis wasted little time reaching the historic mark, scoring on a feed by Ben Irwin 55 seconds into the game. He finished the game with three goals putting his career point total at 102.

Everything went as planned. Kapotis had arranged to reach the milestone in front of home fans and coach Mike Ackerman, who missed last week's season opener while serving a one-game suspension stemming from last year's Class S championship game.

"I got those four [points in the season opener] and saved this one for here," Kapotis said. "I wanted to do it when coach Ackerman was here. It was only right."

Kapotis first came to Ackerman's attention as a middle school student. Ackerman described him as a "true scorer."

Kapotis is currently fifth on Woodsville's career scoring list. He trails all-time leader Ryan Ackerman, coach Ackerman's son, by 32 points.

"[Dimitri] knows how to finish," Ackerman said. "Some people might get mad, and say he doesn't do anything, but Ôbang' the ball is in the back of the net. He's always dangerous."

Gorham entered the game aware Kapotis was nearing the 100-point plateau and was determined to spoil his day.

The Huskies tied the game on a Chris Brockett penalty kick 16 minutes in and took a 1-1 tie into halftime.

During the intermission a Gorham player could be heard urging teammates "Dimitri gets nothing else!"

But it was only a matter of time before Kapotis, who continually found the ball in front of the Gorham net, scored again.

Two and a half minutes into the second half, Kapotis headed in a cross pass to put Woodsville ahead 2-1.

Ten minutes later he took a pass in stride and chipped the ball over Gorham keeper Erik Corcoran for his final goal.

Kapotis was assisted on all three goals by Irwin, a senior forward, who joined the starting lineup this season. They are an emerging scoring combination.

"[Irwin] slotted those balls through perfectly, right on Dimitri's feet," Ackerman said. Irwin dribbled the ball to the right endline before sending a perfect cross pass for Kapotis' second goal.

Woodsville (2-0) entered the season with question marks at several key positions. The Engineers lost 11 players to graduation including starting keeper Brandon Beckley. Over the first two games the team's younger, less experienced players - including stopper Donny Robinson and sweeper Zach Maerder - have stepped in to fill the void. Maerder entered the season with no previous soccer playing experience.

Center midfielder Mark Corey, a returning varsity starter, is another player who has performed well.

"The last couple of days [the team has played] better than they were," Ackerman said. "They are coming along."
