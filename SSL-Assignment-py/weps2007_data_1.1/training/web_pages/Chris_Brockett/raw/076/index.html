Young Vikes must grow up fast

Topeka Capital-Journal, The,  Sep 3, 2004  by Rick Peterson Capital-Journal

New faces, coach eager to step up, continue Seaman's success

By Rick Peterson

THE CAPITAL-JOURNAL

It would probably be unrealistic, at least early in the season, to expect Seaman to be the same football team it was last fall when 24 seniors led the Vikings to an 11-1 record and the state semifinals in Class 5A.

Seaman graduated seven All-City picks and All-State lineman Aaron Lockwood. The Vikings return only two full-time starters --- junior defensive lineman Thad Taylor (6-foot-1, 235 pounds) and junior linebacker Ben Carlson (6-3, 225) --- to build around this season.

But while that lack of starting experience doesn't usually bode well for a promising season, a ton of Vikings saw spot duty a year ago and, more importantly, went head to head on a regular basis with the Seaman regulars.

"I think that's huge, the practice time that they got against those guys that they had the opportunity to compete against last year in practice," Seaman first-year head coach Blake Pierce said. "That was really beneficial.

"Plus, we were fortunate enough that we got to practice a few extra times because of the playoffs, so they got even more time."

And now that those players have the opportunity to step into marquee roles, Pierce expects them to do their best to deliver.

"They just have to step up when it's their time," said Pierce, who became Seaman's coach after Bill Lowe accepted an assistant's post at Emporia State. "From what I've seen so far, I've been just really impressed and pleased with the effort. I feel pretty good."

The Vikings' early-season schedule --- games against Class 6A schools Emporia, Washburn Rural and Manhattan --- will test Seaman, but Pierce said that he and his staff are pointing towards steady progress leading up to an Oct. 15 district opener against Topeka West.

"A lot of these kids haven't seen real heat-of-battle time and they're going to have to grow up real fast," Pierce said. "If they do that, then we're going to be OK. If not, then we're going to have some problems."

Senior T.J. Hipsher is expected to take over at quarterback this season after backing up Lewis Hutfles a year ago. Hipsher will also continue to handle Seaman's place-kicking duties this season.

A group of least five players will vie for time at the running back positions, while there are also plenty of contenders for playing time in the interior line on both sides of the ball.

The Vikings will have the luxury of being at home to open the season, with Emporia visiting tonight. Tonight's game will be a non- league game, although there will be years when the Vikings will play the Spartans in league games.

Seaman will then travel to Washburn Rural for a non-league game before hosting Manhattan in the Vikings' Centennial League opener on Sept. 17. Other league games for Seaman include Hayden, Shawnee Heights, Junction City, Topeka West and Highland Park.

The Vikings will open 5A district competition on Oct. 15 at Topeka West (Hummer Sports Park) and will also face Salina Central and Highland Park in district play. The West and Highland Park games will also be league matchups.

Central will likely be the team to beat in the district race, with the Mustangs capturing five 5A state championships since 1993.
