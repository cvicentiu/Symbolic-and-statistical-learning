Anchor Bay, Dakota surprise themselves

Teams share MAC Gold Title

PUBLISHED: May 10, 2006

By John Hetzler
Macomb Daily Sports Writer

The tennis teams from Macomb Dakota and New Baltimore Anchor Bay share more than the MAC Gold Division dual-meet championship this season.

Advertisement
They also overcame significant losses to produce winning programs.

So as they head into today's MAC Gold Division tennis tournament at Mount Clemens, both are savoring their rewards.

"This," Anchor Bay coach Debbie Francisco said, "is quite a nice surprise."

Despite losing six senior starters from last year's squad, Francisco's Tars repeated as dual-meet champions.

In many ways, though, Dakota's rise to the top was more surprising.

"We came from not winning a match last year to tying for the league championship," Dakota coach Glenn Corey said. "That's pretty impressive."

Every starter from last year's 0-11 team returned for the Cougars, and Corey credits the turnaround to hard work during the offseason and a strong junior class anchored by first singles player Chris Brockett.

"He's a good athlete," Corey said. "He just started playing tennis two years ago. ... He has pretty good smarts for the game."

Those smarts will be tested today as Dakota and Anchor Bay seek to resolve the deadlock from the dual-meet season, in which the Cougars and Tars played to a 4-4 tie.

In that match, Dakota won three of four singles matches while Anchor Bay took three of four doubles' flights.

Anchor Bay's sole victory in singles came from junior Lindon Ivezic at No. 1 singles.

In his second season at first singles, Ivezic has gone 19-0 and enters today's tournament as the top seed.

"He just has a lot of weapons," Francisco said. "He's learned to adjust to different styles of play. He stays focused on the match and doesn't get upset."

In addition to Ivezic, all four doubles teams are seeded for the Tars, including junior Matt Walczowski and senior Joey Spezia, who are the top seed at second doubles after compiling a 17-2 record this year.

"It's been steady," Francisco said. "Some days they surprise me. Some days my singles come through, some days my doubles come through."

For Dakota, junior Jake Kennedy at third singles and senior Bryce Benson and sophomore Tony Ciaramella at fourth doubles enter the tournament as top seeds.
