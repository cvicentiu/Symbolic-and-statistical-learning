LISBON -- The Lisbon boys soccer team tallied four unanswered goals to down Gorham 4-1 and pick up their eighth win of the season on Monday.

"The elements were a factor, but our field drains well," said LHS coach Les Poore.

The Huskies' Eric Chabot got Gorham on the scoreboard first when Chris Brockett sent him in off a direct kick with six minutes left in the first half.

It didn't take the Panthers long to answer that goal. At 36:30, Corey Mckean knocked the ball in when he found the ball in a scramble in front of the net.

With one minute to go before the end of the half, Mckean scored the game-winner on a breakaway. His first shot hit the keeper and bounced back to him. He didn't miss the second shot.

In the second half, Lisbon added two insurance goals. Jeremy Dodge picked up the first and Craig Rappa netted the second. Mckean was credited with an assist on the first goal.

Tommy Hartwell had seven saves in goal for the Panthers, while Eric Corco had 12 saves for Gorham.

"It was nice to be tested early on," said Poore. "It was good medicine for us. We reacted in a positive manor, bounced back and made the adjustment."

Lisbon (8-1) travels to Hinsdale on Wednesday for a 4 p.m. game.

Woodsville 10, Groveton 1

WOODSVILLE -- Dimitrios Kapotis and Jeff Robins proved to be too much for Groveton on Monday. Kapotis netted five goals, while Robins had a goal and five assists.

Ryan Hatch had two goals, with Jeff Stewart and Mark Corey each netting one.

The Eagles' lone goal came 14 minutes into the second half when the Engineers held a 7-0 lead. Dylan Curry scored the Groveton goal.

Branden Beckley had five saves in net for Woodsville, while Ryan Shannnon had five saves for the Eagles.

"Kapotis stepped up his game today and played one heck of a game. He really hustled and played the way that he can," said WHS coach Mike Ackerman.

The Engineers (7-2) host Gorham on Wednesday at 4 p.m.
