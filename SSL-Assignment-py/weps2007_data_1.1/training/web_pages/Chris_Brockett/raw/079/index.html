High School update

Monitor staff
October 29. 2006 10:00AM

F
ield hockey

Class M-S final, 3 p.m.

At Manchester Memorial H.S.

No. 2 Derryfield (15-1-0) vs. No. 1 Newfound (15-1-0)

Previous tournament games: Derryfield beat No. 7 Newport, 3-1, and edged No. 6 Winnisquam, 1-0. Newfound rolled over No. 8 Littleton, 5-0, and No. 5 Hopkinton, 4-0. Both teams received first-round byes.

---ADVERTISEMENT---
Head to head: The two teams did not meet in the regular season.

Notes: To a certain degree this matchup could be billed as Goliath vs. Goliath. Newfound's offense and Derryfield's defense have dominated their respective opponents all season.

The Bears are averaging 5.06 goals heading into their first state title game in school history. Maggie Seaver leads the charge offensively, scoring a pair of goals in each of the first two rounds of the tournament. Samantha Escobar is a force at midfield and will be depended upon heavily to get the offense kick-started. Emily Mattson, Christy Maloney and goalkeeper Sarah McGraw anchor Newfound's defense, which is no slouch either, posting 11 shutouts.

The Cougars defense was about as close to flawless as a team can get, giving up just two regular-season goals a staggering 0.19 goals-against average. Goalkeeper Megan Tsai is a steady last line of defense behind the triangle defense led by Julia Maldonado, Hilary Fink and Natalie Coviello. Up front the Cougars are led by sophomore Katherine DiPastina, who has filled in smoothly for a Derryfield club that lost 12 seniors from a team that finished runner-up a year ago.

Girls' soccer

Class I quarterfinals, 1:30 p.m.

No. 11 Con-Val (10-6-1) at No. 3 John Stark (14-1-2)

Previous tournament games: Con-Val upset No. 6 Bow, 3-2. John Stark blanked No. 14 Fall Mountain, 3-0.

Head to head: The teams did not meet in the regular season.

Notes: The Generals used a two-goal effort by freshman Lauren Maklin and strong midfield play in coasting to a first-round win. It will take more than that to get by the Cougars, who pulled off an impressive upset over Bow to get to the quarterfinals.

John Stark uses a midfield of Joan Hamel, Meg Sawyer and Alyssa Charest that helps control both sides of the ball. If Con-Val does make it by the midfield and the impressive defense there is senior Josi Heer, who has played well all season in net.

The Generals have not lost at home this year, but the upstart Con-Val team could be the upset specialists.

Class M quarterfinals, 1:30 p.m.

No. 8 Littleton (8-6-1) at No. 1 Gilford (15-1-0)

Previous tournament games: Littleton blanked No. 9 Inter-Lakes, 3-0. Gilford earned a first-round bye.

Head to head: The Golden Eagles beat the Crusaders twice: 4-3 on Sept. 11 and 2-1 on Oct. 11.

Notes: Gilford is riding a seven-game winning streak into the playoffs, with its only loss of the season coming more than a month ago to No. 3 Hopkinton.

The Golden Eagles most valuable asset this season has been a strong offense. The team has scored fewer than three goals only five times this season, one time being against Littleton. Cassie Crockett, Rachel Petrozzi, Kelley Frick and Julie Hatch are the key components to the ever-explosive offense.

Behind the good offense is a defense that has allowed only 13 goals all year. Junior Jessica Payne and senior Katie Milligan have helped the Golden Eagles defense all year in goal.

No. 5 Epping (11-4-2) at No. 4 Hillsboro-Deering (11-5-0)

Previous tournament games: Epping defeated No. 12 Prospect Mountain, 5-1. Hillsboro-Deering received a first-round bye.

Head to head: Epping defeated H-D, 3-0, on Sept. 27.

Notes: The Hillcats' three seniors, Caitlyn Cawthron, Mariah Powers and Kim Ford, have been instrumental in getting H-D to this position.

Younger players such as Victoria Woolner, Jordan Whitaker, Liz Kingsbury, Antoinette Woolner and Libby Dutton have given the Hillcats the boost they needed to earn a first-round pass.

H-D will have its hands full with Epping, though. When the teams met the first time, Epping controlled the game at its home field. Now that the teams face each other at H-D things may change, where the Hillcats are 7-0, allowed only six goals and registered four shutouts.

(No. 3 Hopkinton's home game against No. 6 Campbell yesterday was postponed and will be played today at 11 a.m. The preview ran in Saturday's paper.)

Class S quarterfinals, 1:30 p.m.

No. 8 Colebrook (10-7-0) at No. 1 Derryfield (14-0-2), 1 p.m.

Previous tournament games: Colebrook blanked No. 9 Hinsdale, 2-0. Derryfield received a first-round bye.

Head to head: Derryfield defeated Colebrook, 5-0, on Sept. 8.

Notes: Derryfield has shown an impressive defense, letting up only six goals. Mollie Volinsky, Tulia LaCroix and goalie Danielle Potter lead a strong backfield by Derryfield.

Up front, Maddie Cole from Bow, Kayla Delahanty of Hooksett and Camille Smith of Henniker have provided much punch for the Cougar offense.

The lone blemishes on the Cougars' schedule this season were ties against Woodsville on Sept. 15 and Campbell in the last game of the season.

No. 5 Pittsfield (12-4-1) at No. 4 Lisbon (11-4-1), 11:45 a.m.

Previous tournament games: Pittsfield overcame No. 12 Moultonborough, 3-2, in overtime. Lisbon received a first-round bye.

Head to head: The teams did not meet in the regular season.

Notes: Senior Tamara Hussey put the Panthers into the quarterfinals with a game-winning overtime goal on Tuesday.

The Panthers are coasting on a five-game winning streak as they have to make the long ride to Lisbon.

Hussey leads a strong contingent of seniors, which includes Kate Osborne, Jillian Veroneau and Kristen Brown. Kelsey Metcalf, Kelli Webber and Kelley Hussey have also been instrumental to Pittsfield's success, but will need to continue to play at the top of their game if they want to get to the semifinals.

Boys' soccer

Class L quarterfinals, 1:30 p.m.

No. 6 Merrimack (12-3-2) at No. 3 Concord (13-2-2)

Previous tournament games: Merrimack stopped No. 11 Salem, 4-2. Concord blanked No. 14 Nashua North, 2-0.

Head to head: Merrimack upended Concord, 3-0, on Sept. 15.

Notes: Concord won its first five games of the season, but just when it appeared as though the defending champions would walk all over its Class L competitors, Merrimack stepped in its path and ended that run of perfection in Game 6. Concord can take solace, however, in the fact that it dominated play.

Offensively, the Tide are led by Ethan Hirshberg and Marial Kocdal, while Messanvi Aboussa returned to the lineup in the final regular-season game and could serve as an inspirational and tangible force in Concord's run for a second consecutive title.

Class I quarterfinals, 1:30 p.m.

No. 9 Lebanon (10-5-2) at No. 1 Bow (17-0-0)

Previous tournament games: Lebanon upended No. 8 Laconia, 3-1. Bow blanked No. 16 Portsmouth, 3-0.

Head to head: The two teams did not meet in the regular season.

Notes: The Falcons made it look easy, giving up just five goals all season with only two of its wins coming by the one-goal variety. Bow hasn't given up a goal since a 5-1 win against Merrimack Valley on Oct. 11 and will look to knock off a red-hot Lebanon team that finished 5-1-1, with its only blemish a 1-0 loss to defending state champion Hanover.

Matt Gloekler and Jason Langevin anchor the defense for the Falcons in front of goalkeeper Nathaniel Royer. Kyle Geddes and Sam Watkins run the offense up front for a Bow, which is attempting to not only extend its winning streak to 18, but keep its hopes of a first Class I title hopes alive.

No. 5 Kearsarge (13-4-0) at No. 4 Con-Val (13-3-1)

Previous tournament games: Kearsarge shut out No. 12 Pembroke, 3-0. Con-Val ousted No. 13 Coe-Brown, 3-0.

Head to head: The two teams did not meet in the regular season.

Notes: It's pretty safe to say that it's the Kearsarge defense that has earned the Cougars their fifth seed and 13-4 record to date. Of Kearsarge's 13 wins, only twice did a team score more than one goal; however, the Cougars have been outscored 9-0 in their four losses, proving that the Cougars can't afford for their offense to be stagnant.

Kevin Coyle and Shamus Heffernan will need to be effective for the Cougars up front, while Sam Bower, Garrett Bauer and Craig McAllister lead a solid defensive core in front of goalkeeper Tom Bloch.

Class M quarterfinals

No. 9 Winnisquam (10-5-2) at No. 1 Gilford (17-0-0), 11 a.m.

Previous tournament games: Winnisquam rallied to beat No. 8 Belmont, 3-2. Gilford blanked No. 16 Inter-Lakes, 4-0.

Head to head: Gilford dominated Winnisquam, winning 6-1 Sept. 5 and 6-0 on Sept. 19.

Notes: If the Bears can take anything away from their first two meetings with the Golden Eagles, it's that they are one of only five teams to get a goal past the Gilford defense.

The defending state champions have been dominant since the opening game of the season, outscoring opponents 88-4 during the regular season. Phil Petrozzi leads the way offensively for Gilford, scoring two goals with an assist in its 4-0 first-round win over Inter-Lakes. Chad Crockett and goalkeeper DJ Feehily pilot a dominating defense for the Golden Eagles.

The Bears proved that anything can happen in its 3-2 first-round comeback win over Belmont, and will likely take that same philosophy into today's game. If it is to pull off the upset it will need a strong defensive effort from Zach Lyford and Danny Rose, rock-solid goaltending from Justin Dewalga and more timely scoring from Luke Player and Rob Hallowell.

No. 10 Epping (10-6-1) at No. 2 Hopkinton (15-2-0), 1:30 p.m.

Previous tournament games: Epping upended No. 7 Raymond, 2-0. Hopkinton eliminated No. 15 Hillsboro-Deering, 3-0.

Head to head: Hopkinton defeated Epping, 2-0, Aug. 28.

Notes: The Hawks circled their Oct. 18 matchup with Gilford before the season began, but dropped a disappointing 4-1 decision to the Golden Eagles, hiccupping their way into the tournament. Now the Hawks will have to win three tournament games to get another crack at Gilford that is if they make it, too.

Hopkinton can't look past an Epping team that knocked off No. 7 Raymond, 2-0, in the first round. Travis Fuglestad leads a potent Hopkinton offense, while Zach Cousens takes on the role as final defense in goal for the Hawks.

Class S quarterfinals, 1:30 p.m.

No. 8 Gorham (11-6-0) at No. 1 Pittsfield (17-0-0)

Previous tournament games: Gorham nipped No. 9 Sunapee, 1-0, in overtime. Pittsfield routed No. 16 Nute, 7-0.

Head to head: The two teams did not meet in the regular season.

Notes: Pittsfield had recorded just two shutouts heading into its last three games of the season, but since has rattled off four consecutive shutouts, including playoffs.

Antonio Bifsha scored a hat trick, while Nick Tuttle and Kevin Gibson each had single tallies in the Panthers' first-round win over No. 16 Nute. The Pittsfield defense, led by goaltender Scott Briggs, could have its hands full against a Gorham squad that is riding a first-round overtime win over No. 9 Sunapee.

Chris Brockett scored Gorham's goal in sudden death in the first round.

No. 6 Derryfield (11-4-0) at No. 3 Lisbon (15-2-0)

Previous tournament games: Derryfield blanked No. 11 Portsmouth Christian, 3-0. Lisbon edged No. 14 Lin-Wood, 1-0.

Head to head: The two teams did not meet in the regular season.

Notes: Derryfield stumbled a bit at the end of the season, dropping two of its last three games. But after shutting out its first round opponent, the Cougars appear to be back on track.

Brothers Clint and Jake Davis lead the charge offensively for the Cougars, while juniors Max Allard and Peter Kutz anchor a very young Derryfield defense.
