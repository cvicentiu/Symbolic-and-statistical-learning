Microsoft Dumps Fundamentalist Evangelist Ralph Reed

According to Seattle Weekly, Microsoft has finally come to it senses and is dumping Ralph Reed from its list of consultants. 

Many questioned why Microsoft had Ralph Reed, head of the Christian Coalition, and one of America's most devise evangelical christian fundamentalists, on its payroll.

Ralph Reed was involved with President Bush's 2000 and 2004 campaigns, Reed had to stop his "consulting" in 2000 after this conflict was noted. 

But Microsoft still hasn't elaborated if Mr. Reed was on retainer at Microsoft at the same time he served as Bush's 2004 Southeast Campaign Manager. This would probably present some ethical difficulties for Microsoft's CFO, a leading Bush reelection campaign fund raiser in Washington State.

US Press Fails in War Coverage

Editor and Publisher reports on a survey done by the Los Angeles Times..

"A remarkable survey by the Los Angeles Times of six leading newspapers and two newsmagazines during a recent six-month period found almost no pictures from Iraq of Americans killed in action. And the publications ran only 44 photos of the wounded."

Delays TRMPAC Treasurer found Guilty of Violating Texas Election Law

Bill Ceverha, Treasurer of Tom Delays Pac TRMPAC (Texans for a Republican Majority) has been found guilty of not reporting hundreds of thousands of dollars in campaign contributions, a judge ruled Thursday in a civil case brought by Democratic candidates. 

Not only were the contributions not reported, but they were used for direct campaign expenses, such as political research, polling, mailing, fund-raising and conferences.  Direct use of corporate donations for direct campaign expenses is illegal in Texas.  Tom Delay's TRMPAC was alleged to have used as much as $600,000 in corporate money illegally to influence Texas House races in 2002, the year Republicans won control of the House for the first time in 130 years.

Contact Your Senator Now on Nuclear Option - PBU21

I read quite a bit on the Nuclear Option, which would eliminate the filibuster.  There are many pros and cons on both sides. 

But many of the arguments being made to kill the filibuster are without merit as noted in Media Matters "Top Ten Filibuster Falsehoods"

In the end, it was a simple decision for me.  Eliminating the filibuster would weaken the Senate with respect to the Executive Branch.  Weakening the Senate isn't good for either party.  Permanently weakening the Senate for short term Republican gains isn't worth the damage it does to our form of government.

Contact you Senator today on this issue, as they will be voting tomorrow (tuesday).  Also, sign the MoveOn petition against the Nuclear Option.  Time is running out to make yourself heard on this issue.

American Jihad & the USAF Academy
