Baseball Database Tidbits:

Color Options -

You can change your color display of data records in numerous ways, by player or team or league, etc. Just click on the Color Options item in the Options menu and there are several choices available.

Navigating the database -

You can use the mouse to manipulate the scroll bars to view the data or you can use the Page Up, Page Down keys to page up and down a screen full at a time. You can use the Home and End keys to instantly move to the left-most column and right-most columns. Use Control-Left Arrow and Control-Right Arrow to page a screenful at a time to the left and right. Use Control-Home and Control-End to instantly move to the top and bottom of the database.

Limits -

When using limits for text fields, to define a range add a 'z' to the end of the second limit field.
Example: if you want to list all players with the last name 'smith', have your limit range from 'smith' to 'smithz'.
Example: to list all teams with names that start with 'ne' (like new york), limits from 'ne' to 'nez'.

However, if the text field has only one, two or three letter lengths, be case sensitive.
Example: to list only the switch hitters, limit the 'at bats' field from 'S' to 'S'.

To select several years of data, aside from using the 'select year' option you can also limit from one year to another year.
Example: to list all statistical data prior to the year 1900, limit the years field from '0' to '1899'.

To list batting records by position, such as all the third basemen batting records, limit the position field (POS).
Example: To limit the batting records to all players who played primarily third base but also other positions, limit by position from '3b' to '3bz'.
Example: To limit the batting records to all players who played only third base, limits by position from '3b' to '3b'.
