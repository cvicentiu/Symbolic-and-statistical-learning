RADM James P Davidson
Supply Corps, USN, Retired


.

RADM Jim Davidson served as Executive Director Supply Operations (DLA O) from July 1989 to August 1991, the most tumultuous time in the history of DLA to that time.  DLA emerged as a valued partner with the services in the deployment and sustainment of forces during Desert Shield and Desert Storm that has grown to the recognition as the premier DOD Combat Support Agency.   

During this two year period, RADM Davidson led major expansions in the DLA mission as directed by Defense Management Review Decisions (DMRDs).  DMRD 902 directed the Consolidation of the 30 service and DLA Supply depots into the Defense Distribution System.   DMRD 926 initially directed the consolidation of the service Inventory control Points and was later modified to transfer the remaining approximately one million service managed consumable items to DLA.  

In September 1989, two months after arrival,  RADM Davidson led the DLA O staff in first the conceptual plan and later an implementation plan,  for consolidation of the 30 supply depots and assumption of wholesale inventory management responsibility for all consumable items.    While directed by DOD, the services resisted the perceived encroachment on service prerogatives requiring extensive coordination and staffing.  .  RADM Davidson led the staff work and negotiations for DLA with each of the services.  This included detailed plans and business cases resulting in projected recurring savings of $200 million per year resulting from the consolidation of the supply depots.   He visited every supply depot to ensure uninterrupted support both locally and nationally with a motto of equal or better service at less cost.  The original depot consolidation plan included a phased implementation over a three year period.  However, with distribution backlogs building within the services during Desert Shield, SECDEF directed the immediate transfer of command and control as of 1 February 1991 during the peak of Desert Storm.  The  result was substantial improvements in service levels in terms of receipt, order and ship times and substantially less cost to the taxpayer.   

In August 1990, in the middle of the DMRD effort , the nation responded to the Iraq invasion of Kuwait with Operation Desert Shield, followed by Operation Desert Storm in January 1991.  DLA responded to the sudden surge in demand with unprecedented performance. RADM Davidson was assigned the primary DLA lead for coordinating the 24/7 response to emergent requirements for  Army, Navy, Marines, and Air Force.  Within 10 days RADM Davidson assigned a full time DLA representative to the CENTCOM logistics staff.     He  participated in daily planning meetings with the JCS J4 and service logistics staff coordinated the emergency funding and procurements for a variety of commodities such as Chemical and Biological protective suits,  Meals Ready to Eat, Desert Cammies, Desert hats and boots as well as special medical support channels for deployed medical units and both hospital ships USS Mercy and USS Comfort.  He worked with US Transcom to establish a special daily Desert Express high priority air lift channel to theater. 

Upon his departure in August 1991, Lt Gen McCausland, Director of DLA, recognized RADM Davidson with the following letter:  

"During your tenure, Supply Operations was catapulted into the forefront of defense logistics for its leadership in supporting the Persion Gulf war and implementing Defense Management Review Decisions.  This period of dynamic change will long be remembered for the creation of DLA as the mega giant in defense supply operations. 

Your personal leadership, dedication, and commitment to the armed services were the essential keys to DLA's unprecedented success in supplying and sustaining more than half a million Soldiers, Sailors, Airman and Marines during the war….to your credit you were the DLA's heartbeat ." 

For his outstanding performance in leading the DLA response to Desert Storm and the successful implementation DMRD 902 and DMRD 926 Radm Davidson was recognized with the nations highest not combat award, the Defense Distinguished Service Medal.  

Since his retirement in 1996 RADM Davidson has remained involved in DOD logistics in industry and  as the volunter Chairman of the Logistics Division of the National Defense Industrial Association. 

For these accomplishments and his continued commitment to DLA and Defense Logistics RADM Jim Davidson is highly qualified and most deserving for recognition in the DLA Hall of Fame.
