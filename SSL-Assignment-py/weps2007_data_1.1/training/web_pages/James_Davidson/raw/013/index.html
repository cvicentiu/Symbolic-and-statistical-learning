Dr James Davidson

[c]

Reader
M.A. (Oxford), M.A., M.Phil. (Columbia) D.Phil. (Oxford)

Teaching:

  • Sex and Gender in Antiquity
  • Democracy and Imperialism in Classical Greece
  • Food and Drink in the Ancient Mediterranean
  • Hellenistic World
Research:

Dr James Davidson works on Greek social and cultural history and historiography. He has written articles on Polybius, Greek public bars and Dido and child-sacrifice and is a regular contributor to The London Review of Books, The Guardian, The Daily Telegraph and The Sunday Times. His first book, Courtesans and Fishcakes: The Consuming Passions of Classical Athens was published in 1997. He is currently working on The Greeks and Greek Love for Weidenfeld and a translation of some Attic speeches for Penguin Classics.

Recent Publications:

'Private Life' in R. Osborne ed. Short Oxford History of Europe: vol. I Classical Greece(2000) pp.139-169

'Gnesippus paigniagraphos: The Comic poets and the Erotic Mime' in J. Wilkins and D. Harvey eds. The Rivals of Aristophanes(London, 2000) pp. 41-64

'Pleasure and Pedantry in Athenaeus' in David Braund and John Wilkins eds. Athenaeus and his World(Exeter, 2000) pp.292-303

'Dover, Foucault and Greek Homosexuality: Penetration and the Truth of Sex' Past and Present 170 (2001), 3-51 - awarded the George Mosse Prize for outstanding contribution to gay and lesbian studies.
