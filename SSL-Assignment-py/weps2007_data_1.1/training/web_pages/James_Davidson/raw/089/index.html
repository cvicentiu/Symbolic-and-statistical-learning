James Davidson prepares to take over as the new city administrator PDF  | Print |  E-mail
CATHY ALLRED - North County Staff   

James Davidson steps into his new office as Lehi's city administrator next week. He is wrapping up his last few days as Sandy's assistant chief administrator.

"I will miss the people," Davidson said. "I have a very supportive city administrator and mayor."

He will replace Ed Collins who vacated his position on June 31. The decision to appoint Davidson as adminstrator was a group effort for Mayor Howard Johnson and the City Council.

"I feel we have been very lucky to replace Ed with somebody of his caliber," Councilman Stephen Holbrook said.

The BYU alumnus has served Sandy City for more than 10 years in various positions -- as a management services director, human resources director, management analyst and assistant to the chief administrator. Prior to working for Sandy, he worked for South Jordan and Provo. For nearly the same number of years, he and his wife Judy Davidson and their five young children have lived in Lehi.

"I'm excited to actually be working in the city that I live," Davidson said. "My family and I participate in many Lehi activities as we can and it's been a real positive experience."

Davidson has watched Lehi go through very similar growing pains as Sandy City has and said he sees commonalities between the two communities, the significant growth and quite a bit of change.

"I've been where Lehi is going," Davidson said. During his tenure in Sandy, he has seen a civic center built, numerous city parks and trailways constructed, new roads made, police and fire services expanded, a soccer stadium completed and $20 million in improvements along State Street.

Economic development brought in the Jordan Commons, South Towne Mall and more. He sees the same growth occurring in the Lehi area.

"We are getting a Costco which I know Lehi is very excited about," he said.

Davidson will be overseeing city staff, departments and policies and will represent the city at agency, municipal meetings as well as plan, direct, budget and supervise.

He doesn't come unprepared. He said the most important bit of wisdom he has worked by so far has served him well.

"One of the things I've learned is whether it's in the community, church or at work, it's important to establish good strong relationships with people," he said. "And those relationships enable you to be successful in what you do."
