Spring Term




    Spring Term has gone by extremely fast and the year is now just about over!  I can't even explain  how I finished everything I did this term. My research paper, unfortunately, utilized most of my time throughout the term. While it required much more time than I anticipated, in the end I pleased with the final draft. When the assignment was first assigned to the class, I was convinced that I would write about Smallpox and how it is still a threat to humans around the world.  As our class moved on into a new subject about climate change, I became fascinated with how much the weather affects our everyday lives.  I then decided to focus my paper on El Nino and how the changes in the weather can significantly destroy a community economically.   I spent many long hours at the library looking up research and trying to come up with a thesis statement.  I went through several drafts and I wasn't satisfied with the statement until I was halfway through my paper.

    The Neighborhood Survey project was by far the highlight of the year.  I worked with Ashley Gregg and Stephan Johnson and we analyzed the Northwest district in Portland.  We picked out two different sections in the “neighborhood” and walked around observing people and looked for interesting differences between people in the area.  We first took a strip of Northwest 23rd Ave and  walked down it several blocks taking pictures.  Later we drove over to another area and observed Wallace Park.  Here we became surrounded by hundreds of small children playing in the park.

    Overall there were not many assignments this term, but the few that we had were time consuming.  I really feel like the the research paper forced me to use really critically think.  Trying to incorporate the four University Studies goals this term was challenging.  Critical Thinking and Communication were used most frequently this term, however we did touch on the other two goals of Human Experience and Ethical Issues and Diversity.  It really depended on what each person chose to discuss in their research paper on how much they used the University Goals.

Neighborhood Project
  The neighborhood survey project was a definite highlight of the term.  I had a great time working with my group, which consisted of Ashley and Stephan.  We decided that we wanted to examine the Northwest district of Portland.  We went and observed two different areas in the neighborhood; first we went by Couch Park where we noticed several people sleeping in the park. Then after we observed the park we walked around 21st street and took pictures of people and interesting things we detected.  After 21st street we walked up to 23rd Ave where the atmosphere is much different.  There is a wide mixture of people that interact there.  There are the upper class people and at the same locations there are the street beggars.  A little past 23rd Ave is Wallace Park.  Here was the other area of the neighborhood that we chose to observe.  Unlike any other part of our neighborhood, Wallace Park was filled with kids.  Hundreds of kids were at the park causing all types of havoc.  We watched as several kids played with toy guns shooting each other, while another group of kids fenced with sticks they had found on the ground.
