2001 IKON Classic Recognition Award Recipients Named

Princeton, NJ (November 8, 2001) – The sixth annual IKON Classic, featuring Arizona, Florida, Maryland and Temple will tip-off the college basketball season November 8th & 9th in“The World’s Most Famous Arena,” Madison Square Garden in New York City. Since the tournament’s inception in 1996, close to $2 million has been raised for the American Cancer Society. Last year, the IKON Classic began honoring local heroes for their outstanding work in the fight against cancer. This year’s honorees include:

Dr. Avi Barbasch MD
Dr. Barbasch currently serves on the American Cancer Society’s National Research and Medical Affairs Committee, where he works to establish screening policies and therapeutic guidelines, and is on the Board of Directors of the American Cancer Society’s Eastern Division. Dr. Barbasch was instrumental in the development of a CD prototype for interactive breast cancer education. In 1999, he was given the St. George Award, the highest national award an ACS volunteer can receive.

Stephan Johnson, Esq.
For more than 25 years, Stephan Johnson has served on more than 20 ACS boards, committees and work groups and has contributed to a number of significant accomplishments, including the passage of New York State’s Clean Indoor Air Act and the reorganization of the ACS Eastern Division. Now, as a cancer survivor himself, Johnson has a whole new perspective on the importance of ACS patient service programs and leadership on quality of life issues for cancer patients and their families.

Marguerite Schlag RN, MSN, EdD
A 23-year volunteer with the ACS, Dr. Schlag has had an integral role in developing and carrying out the Eastern Division’s training program. She has also been a driving force in cultivating both the Division’s breast cancer program and an outreach program that aids medically underserved New Jerseyans. Dr. Schlag has served as chairperson for the organization’s former New Jersey Division Board of Directors and Executive Committee, as well as its Public Issues and Breast Cancer Task Force.

All three honorees will be recognized at the Tip-Off reception at the New York Stock Exchange and during a ceremony on the court at Madison Square Garden during the Championship Game of the IKON CLASSIC.
