Sports briefs


     Manager says boxer's
     condition deteriorates
     
      ATLANTIC CITY, N.J. -- Stephan Johnson, who was critically injured during a boxing match last weekend, took a turn for the worse Wednesday, his manager said.
      Johnson, 31, of Brooklyn, N.Y., remained hospitalized at Atlantic City Medical Center. At his mother's request, the hospital refused to give information about him or list a condition.
      "We're having a tough day today," said Johnson's manager, Kenneth Woods, who was at his bedside. "The numbers are just not good."
     Minnesota's Mason
     spurns LSU's offer
     
      MINNEAPOLIS -- Minnesota football coach Glen Mason removed himself from consideration for the Louisiana State coaching job a day after the Golden Gophers disclosed he was offered a significant raise.
      Mason, who reportedly met with officials from LSU on Tuesday in Chicago, did not give a reason for withdrawing.
     North Carolina State
     fires football coach
     
      RALEIGH, N.C. -- Mike O'Cain, whose team opened with a win over Texas but finished 6-6, was fired as North Carolina State's football coach after seven seasons.
      Athletics director Les Robinson and chancellor Marye Anne Fox made the decision Tuesday.
     
     Louisville player
     wins Unitas award
     
      LOUISVILLE, Ky. -- Louisville quarterback Chris Redman, who passed for 29 touchdowns and 3,647 yards this season, won the Johnny Unitas Golden Arm Award.
      Other finalists were Joe Hamilton of No. 14 Georgia Tech, Tee Martin of No. 7 Tennessee, Chad Pennington of No. 11 Marshall and Tim Rattay of Louisiana Tech.
     
     Las Vegans earn
     all-conference awards
     
      Victor Valley College and Cheyenne High School graduate Marlyndi McNeal was named first-team All-Foothill Conference punter, and Green Valley graduate Justin Vaughn was named second-team wide receiver.
      McNeal led the league in punting with a 40.6-yard average. Vaughn placed third in the conference in receiving with 41 catches for 469 yards.
     
     Ex-Bengal Brooks
     gets six months in jail
     
      CINCINNATI -- James Brooks, the Cincinnati Bengals' career leader in rushing, was sentenced to six months in jail for failing to pay child support to the mothers of his 6-year-old son and 4-year-old daughter.
      Brooks, 41, acknowledged he was at least $107,704 behind in payments.
     
     Ex-Dodgers manager
     hired as Tampa coach
     
      ST. PETERSBURG, Fla. -- Former Los Angeles Dodgers manager Bill Russell was named third-base coach of the Tampa Bay Devil Rays.
      Russell, who will also work with the team's infielders, managed the Double-A Orlando Rays last year, taking the team to the Southern League title in its first year as a Tampa Bay affiliate.
     Kormann to coach
     Olympic gymnasts
     
      INDIANAPOLIS -- Peter Kormann, former Olympic bronze medalist and coordinator for the USA Gymnastics men's national team since 1996, will coach the U.S. team in the 2000 Olympics.
      Kormann, 44, of Columbus, Ohio, was selected coach by the sport's national governing body, subject to approval by the U.S. Olympic Committee.
     Sprinter sues over
     positive drug test
     
      LONDON -- British sprinter Doug Walker, the European 200-meter champion, is suing the national and world governing bodies of the sport over a positive drug test.
      Walker has filed suit in the London High Court against UK Athletics and the International Amateur Athletic Federation, alleging breach of contract and lack of jurisdiction, the Times and Independent newspapers reported.
     
     Las Vegas High alum
     wins running honor
     
      Dustin Blair, a 1994 graduate of Las Vegas High School, has earned Division II All-America honors in cross country at Southern Connecticut State.
      Blair was the New England Collegiate Conference's runner of the year.
     Rams end Rebels'
     volleyball season
     
      PROVO, Utah -- Three players recorded double-doubles in kills and digs for No. 12 Colorado State as it defeated UNLV 15-9, 3-15, 15-4, 15-9, in the first round of the Mountain West Conference Volleyball Tournament at Brigham Young's Smith Fieldhouse.
      Colorado State (26-2, 12-2 MWC) was led by Courtney Cox's 16 kills and 10 digs.
      Angela Knopf had 15 kills and 10 digs, and Catie Vagneur had 14 kills and 17 digs for the Rams.
      The loss ends the season for UNLV (12-14, 4-10 MWC), which was led by Jeanette Graves' 14 kills.
