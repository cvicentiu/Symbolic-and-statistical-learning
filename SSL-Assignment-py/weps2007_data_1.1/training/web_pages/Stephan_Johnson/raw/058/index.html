Important Questions to ask when Buying Boxing DVD's

1. What is the Quality of the Video.
Each Fight should be rated for quality and content.  Just because the fight is on DVD does not mean the Quality is DVD Quality. The video may have been transferred incorrectly, or the original video may have been from poor video source. Quality for a DVD is dependent on the Video Source, and the Video bit rate settings on the DVD. A DVD that has more that 120 minutes of video on a DVD will require more compression, and therefore quality will suffer. No more than 24 rounds, or 115 minutes of video should be on a DVD.
2. What is the Layout of the Menus.
The reason why DVD's are so popular is because of the the options you get when viewing video. Menus are important to easily choose which video you would like to view, And chapter points are used to easliy Navigate to each specified point in the video. Lets say you want to watch Round 9 of Gatti vs. Ward I, it would be a pain to have to hold the Fast Foreward button to scan to round "9". If the DVD contained a menu that chaptered the beginning of each round, then going through the menu to find round 9 would be easy.
3. What brand of DVD media is used.
Not all DVD media is made the same. There are only a few DVD media brands that have the highest compatibility, and the best overall quality. Media Brands such as TDK,Verbatim(Datalife PLUS), and Maxell are considered the Best media. The cheaper brands have been known to breakdown faster, and become unplayable in the less than a year. It would be upsetting to have purchased a DVD set, just to find out the DVD's no longer work after 1 year.
4.  Who is the seller you are buying from.
As with any purchase, know your seller. How long have they been in business? Do they have references/Feedbacks? Do they answer your e-mails? There are people that are looking to make a buck, then take off. There has been sellers that open up for business for a few months, then close down, then reopen under a new Website name.EX. xxxxxxx.net was open for a few months, then closed without warning, and left customers out to dry. A few weeks later, the same owner opened up xxxxxxx.com and sold DVD's for a few months, then closed. Each time Leaving all his customers without any contact information, or support.
5. Request a Sample DVD, before making a large purchase.
Any seller that stands behind his DVD's will gladly furnish a free sample DVD to anyone who requests it. Remember that quality is in the eye of the beholder. For example, A seller says the Quality of a Tyson fight is 9.0 out of 10, when in reality the video is a 7.0 out of 10 to you. Quality video is extremely hard to find. Therefore if someone is stating the quality is excellent, but is offering the video real cheap, then there may be a letdown when you receive the DVD. This is the reason rating each fight for quality is important. Requesting a sample DVD will allow you to gauge what the sellers quality ratings are on the other DVD's.
6. When will the DVD Ship
If you paid for it, then you should receive the DVD in a timely manner. 
7. What kind of guarantee is offered.
Each DVD order should be guaranteed to arrive at your location in a timely manner
Each DVD order should be guaranteed to work in your specific DVD player.
Each DVD order should be guaranteed to reflect the video quality rating that was given at time of purchase.
A replacement DVD guaranteed should be offered in the event that a DVD becomes corrupted over time(Not due to scratches, abuse,or  sticky labels). The Seller should replace the corrupt DVD with a new one. This is why knowing your seller is important, if the seller is only in for business for a short time, then leaves. You may have no way of obtaining a replacement DVD, if the seller used cheap media, and your DVD set no longer works.

By asking these questions, you will be sure to get the best quality DVD, and best Service possible.
