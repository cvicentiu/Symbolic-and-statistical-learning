NORTHWEST HOOPS OREGON & WASHINGTON PLAYERS OF THE WEEK  - WEEK 2

Jefferson's Mac Hopson, Issaquah's Garrison Carr, Kent-Meridian's Stephan Johnson, Shorewood's Rob Diedrichs and Curtis' Isaiah Thomas

Mac Hopson, senior 6'-0" point guard from Portland's Jefferson High is Northwest Hoops Oregon Player of the Week. Hopson, and the Democrats made a huge statement by traveling to the Les Schwab Hoop Challenge at Brougham Pavilion on the campus of Seattle Pacific University Saturday night. Hopkins, scored 17 points in a 69-56 win over the defending 3A Champions from Washington. Washington's 3A basketball is among the best at that level anywhere in the United States, with highly respected programs like Rainier Beach and Mercer Island, arguably could be where the best basketball is played in the state.  O'Dea is considered a favorite to make it back to back titles this season. 

As for Hopkins and his teammates, they will be taking a lot of confidence with them into the Les Schwab Invitational which begins December 27-30 at Liberty High School in Hillsboro. Hopkins, who has the tendency to hit timely three pointers and is a very good ball handler that excels when things seem to break down, came up big against the Fighting Irish. Look out Oregon, the Democrats could be for real.

In a week that featured several great individual performances which included wins over higher ranked opponents, its just too tough to give it to a single player.  Garrison Carr of Issaquah, Stephan Johnson of Kent-Meridian, Rob Dieterichs of Shorewood and Isaiah Thomas of Curtis are Northwest Hoops Washington Players of the Week.

Carr, 5'-11" senior point guard, who is headed to American University, who was a critical member of Issaquah's run to a state title which fell two years ago in the championship game against Rainier Beach, scored 37 against Mt Si and 33 points in a huge 59-58 win against Interlake. Issaquah (5-0, 2-0) rely on Carr to carry the load, averaged nearly 25 points per game last season, will again be among the scoring leader in the state this season.

Stephan Johnson, 6'-0" senior point guard, whose team had high hopes last season, defeated defending state champion and highly ranked Kentwood, 64-58. Johnson, who is going to be at the University of Pacific next season had 24 points and is looking to put Kent-Meridian (4-0, 2-0) back into the title picture. Johnson also had 21 points in a 68-31 win over Sumner and 22 points in a 67-55 win over Roosevelt. The athletic point guard has a really nice all-around game, which includes plays on the defensive end.

Dieterichs, 6'-7" senior wing, helped Shorewood (3-0, 2-0) defeat highly touted Mountlake Terrace 51-48, by contributing 28 points, in dominating performance,  which included 2 critical threes down the stretch. Dieterichs was the difference. Dieterichs also had 19 in a 52-49 win against Mariner, who had beaten Mercer Island last week. Dieterichs can beat you inside as well as from the perimeter.
