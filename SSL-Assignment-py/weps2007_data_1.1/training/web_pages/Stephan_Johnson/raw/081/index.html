Monday, November 22, 1999

Grant's dramatic comeback victory over Golota sets stage for Lewis fight

Associated Press
 

ATLANTIC CITY, N.J. - Michael Grant got knocked down twice, with his left eye swollen shut. Still, he made an important addition to his unbeaten career.
   "He needed a sensational win," said Lou DiBella, senior vice president of HBO sports. "It was a sensational win."
   His chances of a shot at undisputed heavyweight champion Lennox Lewis had been dimming by the minute. Then Grant knocked down Andrew Golota and stopped him at 1:49 of the 10th round Saturday night at Trump Taj Mahal.
   Lewis was at ringside.
   "I met Frank Maloney (Lewis' manager) on the way back to the dressing room and he said Lennox was definitely interested in fighting Michael," DiBella said.
   He added that Grant might not be Lewis' next opponent, but "I definitely thing you'll see the fight next year."
   "I'll always remember this fight," Grant said.
   A BOXER REMAINS IN CRITICAL CONDITION: Stephan Johnson, who took two brutal punches to the head and was placed on oxygen in the ring, was in critical condition with a brain injury following his junior middleweight bout against Paul Vaden.
   His manager said after the Saturday night bout that surgery was planned. But the wife of the 31-year-old boxer said Sunday her husband was not operated on.
   Bonnie Johnson said her husband was in critical condition. Atlantic City Medical Center would not release information at the request of Johnson's mother.
   "Everything's the same as it was last night," said Bonnie Johnson, who was in the hospital lobby with the fighter's mother and four family friends Sunday afternoon.
