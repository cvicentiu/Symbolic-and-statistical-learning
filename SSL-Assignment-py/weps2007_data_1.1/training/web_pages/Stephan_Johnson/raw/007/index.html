Biography  Stephen is perhaps best know for his original children's books including the Publishers Weekly bestseller My Little Red Toolbox  and Alphabet City which was the recipient of  numerous honors and awards including a Caldecott Honor, a New York Times Best Illustrated Book of the Year and was featured in its entirety on ABC's "Good Morning America."  He has illustrated nineteen children's books to date and has many more in different states of completion.   

 In addition, his work has graced the covers of Time and Forbes magazines, record and CD covers, posters for The Missouri State Ballet, The Association of Kansas Theater , and The Children's Book Council.  His sixty-six foot, mosaic mural for the New York City subway system can be seen at the DeKalb Avenue Subway Station in Brooklyn, New York.  Other paintings have been exhibited at the Art Institute of Chicago (IL), the Katonah Museum of Art (NY), and the Norman Rockwell Museum (MA) among others. His original works of art can be found in the collections of the Spencer Museum of Art (KS), the New Britain Museum of American Art (CT), and the National Portrait Gallery in Washington D.C. and numerous private collections, such as that of Paul Simon and Eddie Brickell.

Among the many awards for his artwork are two Gold Medals and two Silver Medals from the Society of Illustrators in NYC, and five awards from the Pastel Society of America where he was elected a Master Pastelist in 1995.  In 2005, Governor Kathleen Sebelius awarded him the prestigious Kansas Governor¹s Award for outstanding individual artist.
