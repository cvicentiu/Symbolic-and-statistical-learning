Fast paced, smart, thought-provoking

An engaging and interesting class with surprisingly practical application. The textbook is particularly amusing; so much so that one of my friends started reading it for fun!

Johnson has a dynamic personality. He's very animated, and thinks and talks fast fast fast. In fact, I felt that was his only weakness - the lectures and blackboard notes were often disjointed and hard to follow. As a result, I found my own notes often inadequate, as i was too busy just trying to keep up! Also, missing class is a particularly bad idea with this instructor.

Tests were take-home, which *sounds* easy (ha!). Most questions were of the type, "Provide an example of _______(fallacy)______." THis meant you really had to understand the material, as opposed to simply regurgitating it. THey weren't unfairly difficult, however. THe reviews were comprehensive and he was very open to questions.

Homework was assigned each week, but was used only for class discussion, not for grading. Discussions were frequent and I was impressed by his ability to keep the class on track despite the few loonies who wanted to talk about their life history every period.

Overall, low but mentally intense workload.

There were a number of students in my class who had taken other courses from him previously, which is always a good sign.

I actually enjoyed going to his class each week. It reminded me that I was going to school to learn and improve, not just to do time and get a piece of paper.

best instructor I've had

This is one of the best instructor's I have ever had. His lectures are interesting and thorough, and he is eager to help anyone after class who needs it. Be prepared to write an essay almost every week. Text reading is usually only about 5 pages per week due to the density of the material. This class takes some work, but is pretty rewarding.
