Academic Interests I developed and have worked to maintain the Athletic Training Emphasis into a state of the art program. I hope to provide the student with the knowledge and skills needed for the practice of Athletic Training in an evolving professional health care environment. I have attempted to influence the Athletic Training student in the development of a professional philosophy that will focus on providing a scientific based, hands-on approach to the practice of Athletic Training in the traditional setting. I am interested in encouraging Athletic Training Accreditation students to consider attending Physical Therapy school after attaining their Athletic Training education.

I am interested in exploring and teaching the basis and practice of Therapeutic Exercise and Joint Mobilization Techniques. Proprioceptive Neuromuscular Facilitation is my major area of expertise in Therapeutic Exercise. I teach courses in PNF locally, nationally and internationally. Prevention of Athletic Injuries is an area of interest as well. I have also focused on Emergency Field Care of Athletic Injuries. An additional interest is the medical effects of Athlete travel. I am active in the research arena of Vestibular Physical Therapy and Myokinematic.

I also maintain my Physical Therapy knowledge and skills by working as a Physical Therapist in Clinical and Hospital Physical Therapy facilities in Orthopecics, Sports Medicine and Vestibular Physical Therapy.. Examples of my work in these areas of interest follows.

Biographical Information

I was born in Chicago Illinois. I served over four years enlisted active duty the US Air Force and eight years in the USAF Reserve, where I was trained and functioned as a Aeromedical Evacuation and Rescue Medical Technician, Para- Medic, Aircraft Loadmaster and Physical Therapist, Orthopedic and Surgical Technician. I was a Physical Therapy Officer in the US Army Reserve. I am retried from the Military after 25 years of service I was an intercollegiate wrestler and rugby player. I continued to play rugby for a total of 18 years. I am married to Kim R. Gottshall Ph.D., PT, ATC, COL USAR (SP). We work together professionally teaching workshops in PNF, Joint Mobilization, Athletic Training locally, nationally and internationally. We also work together doing research in Vestibular Physical Therapy. We spend our off duty time traveling, reading, swimming, running, attending the theater, and caring for our two Siamese cats.
