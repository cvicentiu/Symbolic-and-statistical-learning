Robert L. Moore, II has been involved in a broad range of federal tax matters since joining Miller & Chevalier in 1967. Mr. Moore currently represents corporations before the Internal Revenue Service and the U.S. Department of the Treasury and handles tax controversies at the audit level and in administrative appeals, as well as in litigation before the U.S. Tax Court, the U.S. Court of Federal Claims, the U.S. District Courts and the U.S. Courts of Appeals.

In recent years, Mr. Moore has focused principally on tax litigation, serving as lead counsel in the following cases in which the taxpayer prevailed:  Exxon Mobil Corp. v. United States, 2003 WL 1564247 (N.D. Texas 2003) (percentage depletion); Exxon Mobil Corp. v. United States, 244 F.3d 1341 (Fed. Cir. 2001), aff’g in part and rev’g in part, 45 Fed. Cl. 581 (1999) (percentage depletion); Exxon Corp. v. Commissioner, 113 T.C. 338 (1999) (creditability of U.K. Petroleum Revenue Tax); Amoco Corp. v. Commissioner, 138 F.3d 1139 (7th Cir. 1998), aff’g T.C. Memo. 1996-159 (creditability of Egyptian income taxes); Exxon Corp. v. United States, 88 F.3d 968 (Fed. Cir. 1996), rev’g 33 Fed. Cl. 250 (1995), cert. denied, 520 U.S. 119 (1997) (percentage depletion); Weyerhaeuser Co. v. United States, 92 F.3d 1148 (Fed. Cir. 1996), rev’g 32 Fed. Cl. 80 (1994), cert. denied, 519 U.S. 1091 (1997) (casualty loss deduction); Exxon Corp. v. Commissioner, T.C. Memo 1993-616 (section 482 adjustment); Exxon Corp. v. United States, 931 F.2d 874 (Fed. Cir. 1991), aff’g 19 Cl. Ct. 755 (1990) (bad debt deduction); Exxon Corp. v. United States, 785 F.2d 277 (Fed. Cir. 1986), rev’g 7 Cl. Ct. 347 (1985) (bad debt deduction).

Mr. Moore frequently speaks and writes about various subjects involving federal taxation. He is a member of Phi Alpha Delta, Phi Beta Kappa, and the Order of the Coif. In the past, Mr. Moore has held numerous management positions at Miller & Chevalier, serving as a member of the Executive Committee and more recently as Chair of the firm’s Tax Department.
