Child Outpatient Mental Health Service Use: Why Doesn't Insurance Matter? (Article)
Author(s): Sherry Glied, Bowen Garrett, Christina Hoven, Maritza Rubio-Stipec, Darrel Regier, Robert Moore, Sherryl Goodman, Ping Wu, Hector Bird

Several studies of child outpatient mental health service use in the United States have shown that private insurance has no effect on the propensity to use services. Some studies also find that public coverage has no beneficial effect relative to uninsurance. We explore several potential explanations, including inadequate measurement of mental health status, bandwagon effects, unobservable heterogeneity, and public sector substitution for private services. Limits on coverage under private mental health insurance combined with a relatively extensive system of public mental health coverage are the most likely explanations of those we examine for the lack of an effect of coverage. (The Journal of Mental Health Policy and Economics 1:173–87.)
