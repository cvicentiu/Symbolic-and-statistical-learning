  The colorful and light filled paintings of Robert Moore clearly communicate his deep respect and appreciation for nature. Raised on an Idaho farm close to the Snake River, he discovered art early on, as a way of expressing himself. His increasing desire to pursue fine art eventually brought him a full scholarship to the Art Center College of Design in Pasadena where he majored in illustration and graduated with honors.

 His studio is situated along the Snake River in Idaho, Robert is surrounded by the same scenic beauty that first captivated him as a child. Robert is colorblind, and he would say that it is by God’s grace alone that he is able to paint. Using vivid colors and high key values, he prefers to work on location in order to best capture the immediate impression of each subject. Several trips abroad, including China and the Tuscan countryside, have been the springboard for many canvases.

 In recent years, Robert has become a highly sought-after instructor and he teaches several workshops each year at his Idaho studio. He has been profiled in several magazine articles including Southwest Art and Art of the West. Robert and his wife, Rebecca reside in Declo, Idaho with their children.
