Robert Moore, BA, MA, Ph.D.
Professor

Robert Moore, Professor, began his professional adult life as a actor, director and playwright (his plays have been performed across Canada). He came to UNBSJ in 1990 after ten years as the Chair of the Department of Drama at Augustana University College in Alberta. Dr. Moore, whose dissertation focused on the "psychoanalytic issue" in the fiction of the American postmodernist, Donald Barthelme, currently teaches in the following areas: postmodern literature, literary theory, American literature, drama production, and film. In addition to being the founding and current Associate Editor of the journal Dianoia: A Liberal Arts Interdisciplinary Journal and the poetry reviewer for The Winnipeg Free Press, he has recently published poems in The Fiddlehead, Wascana Review, Ink Magazine, The New Quarterly, Canadian Author, Prairie Fire, Pottersfield Portfolio, The Gaspereau Review, and Quadrant. His book of poetry, So Rarely in Our Skins (finalist for both the 6th annual Atlantic Poetry Prize and the Margaret and John Savage First Book Award), came out in 2002.

Dr. Moore is Chair of the Department of Humanities and Languages, and Advisor for the Majors option in English (Drama).
