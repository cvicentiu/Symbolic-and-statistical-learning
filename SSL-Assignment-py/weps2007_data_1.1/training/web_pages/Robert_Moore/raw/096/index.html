Vita (pdf)

Specialties:

International Trade
Economic Development
Applied Microeconomics

Moore taught at Wells College and Colgate University in New York state before coming to Georgia State University. His primary research interests fall broadly within the areas of international trade and development with a focus on trade policy between developed and developing countries and income inequality. Secondary research interests include issues related to labor supply and other aspects of household economics. His research on economic development and income inequality has appeared in academic journals such as the Quarterly Journal of Economics, the Journal of Development Economics, Economic Development and Cultural Change, and the Southern Economic Journal among others. His research on international trade has appeared in the Journal of International Trade and Economic Development, the Journal of Developing Areas, the Journal of Economic Development, the Journal of World Trade, and the International Economic Journal. His research on household economics has appeared in the Industrial and Labor Relations Review, the Journal of Population Economics, and the Eastern Economic Journal.
