  My research of the Edwards family, specifically Abel Edwards and Polly Potts and their
descendants has taken me to a new area of interest.
  The inter-married families of Sams and Edwards.  This Paper will follow these two families on
their migration from Virginia to North Carolina(Pre-1796) to Early to Pre-Tennessee.
  There are other surnames that were well known in Virginia, North Carolina and Tennessee such
as Tilson, Clouse and Erwin.  As of the completion of this paper these surnames have not been
researched in great detail.  There is also a possibility that these families migrated together with the
Sams and Edwards families.
  I first found the surname of Sams mentioned in the (1)Edwards Bible(after this will be referred
to as EB).  The genealogical record within the bible concerning the descendants of Abel Edwards
and Polly Potts mentions the marriage between Littleberry Sams and Nancy Edwards born 1777.
The only documented occurrence found that mentions the union of Littleberry and Nancy is the
EB.  This marriage is a problem with in the Edwards research circles because this family can't be
found in any other source other than the Edwards Bible.  Is there proof other than the Edwards
Bible that documents the union between Littleberry and Nancy?  Concrete proof, no.
Coincidences that proves to this researcher that it is to coincidental for the union to not have
taken place, yes.
  The book titled, (2)"THE SAMS FAMILY OF VIRGINIA"(after this will be referred to as
SFOV) has many clues to the union of Littleberry and Nancy, but unfortunately does not
mention the union specifically. To address the possible reason why there isn't any mention of
Littleberry Sams and Nancy Edwards other than the written entry in the EB we must turn to two
other sources, the book SFOV and the land records of (3)Sullivan County Tennessee.

  There are two (2)Littlebury Sams that could be the (1)Littleberry Sams which married Nancy
Edwards b.1777, daughter to Abel Edwards and Polly Potts.
  (2)Littlebury Sams b.1754, son to William Sams Sr. b.1717 could possibly be the (1)Littleberry
Sams that married Nancy Edwards.  Making the assumption that this L. Sams b.1754 did marry
Nancy Edwards he would have been 41 years of age when Nancy Edwards turned 18.  It is a well
known fact that woman married very young at times in early America.
 
{SFOV, PG.23}
Littlebury b.1754 probably died shortly after he was recorded on the tithables(Henry Co. Va.)
when he was 16 years of age as there are no records pertaining to him in any county or state.

  The above entry might explain why Sams genealogical records don't show the union of
(1)Littleberry Sams and Nancy Edwards, Littlebury Sams b.1754 can't be found after 1770.

  The other L. Sams is (2)Littlebury Sams b.1773, son of John Sams b.1749.  Littlebury Sams
b.1773 married Elizabeth Burns in 1793.  From this union there was a child named Littlebury
Sams b.1811.
The following are some possibilities concerning this L. Sams:
a.  (2)Elizabeth Burns might have died after or before the birth of Littlebury Sams b.1811, E.
Burns dying before L. Sams b.1811 would  make L. Sams b.1811 a child of another mother
maybe (1)Nancy Edwards b.1777.  Then the marriage of Littlebury Sams b.1773 and (1)Nancy
Edwards b.1777 was obviously not recorded in any other source than the EB.
b.  (2)Littlebury Sams b.1811 could have been the child of (Littleberry Sams and Nancy Edwards,
Nancy Edwards might have passed away and Littlebury Sams b.1773 then married Elizabeth
Burns in 1793.

(NOTE)
  (2)Littlebury Sams b.1773 before the birth of his son Littlebury Sams b.1811 purchased 160
acres {SFOV, PG.26}, in Sullivan County Tennessee.  The (3)Sullivan County Tennessee Deed
Records clearly mentions the deed transaction between Abel Edwards which married Polly Potts,
selling 160 acres to a "Littleberry Sams" in 1807.  This is a very LARGE coincidence which has
occurred.  The SFOV states that Littlebury Sams b.1773 purchased 160 acres in 1807 in the same
county of Sullivan, and Sullivan Co. Tenn. Deed records state that a Littleberry Sams bought the
same amount of acres in the same year.  It is a fact that in early Tennessee in the year of 1807
there were only two Abel Edwards in the county of Sullivan, one of which was the father of
Nancy Edwards which (1)married Littleberry Sams, the other being the son of Abel Edwards and
Polly Potts, Abel Edwards b.1783 and in 1807 he would have been 24.  There is no way of
knowing today just which Abel Edwards sold 160 acres of land to Littleberry Sams, I believe that
there are two reasons which Abel Edwards b.1783 was not the Abel which sold land to L. Sams.
One reason would be there are no deed transactions before the date of 1807 which documents the
transaction of the land between Abel Edwards and Polly Potts and their son Abel b.1783.
Secondly Abel b.1783 would be very young to have acquired all of this land with older brothers
having first rights on the land if it was given as a gift or legally left to them upon the death of their
father Abel Edwards.
Whether the land was sold to Littleberry Sams by Abel Edwards and Polly Potts or their son Abel
b.1783 doesn't matter.  Littleberry Sams purchased 160 acres from either Abel Edwards and Polly
Potts or their son Abel b.1783.
{refer to the land records at the end of this paper for reference}

  An even more interesting entry in the SFOV which adds more questions to the Sams and
Edwards relation is the mention of a "Martha Edwards" b.1763 which married William Sams Jr.
b.1755.  This marriage was united in Henry County Virginia.  I will remind the reader that Abel
Edwards and Polly Potts migrated to the early Tennessee area from Henry County Virginia.  This
Martha Edwards, assuming that she was at least close to her husbands date of birth would be of
the same generation of (1)Abel Edwards b.1739/1755 which married Polly Potts.  It could be
possible that this Martha Edwards which married William Sams Jr. b.1755 is related to Abel
Edwards which married Polly Potts.  The above mentioned Martha could be the sister or
cousin or maybe the aunt of Abel Edwards.  If this is true then we might have proof of the
father/uncle or the brother of Abel Edwards.  {SFOV PG.44} It is written that the above Martha
Edwards was the daughter to a "Thomas Edwards" which died on November 17, 1790 and was a
neighbor of John Sams b.1749 which was the father to Littleberry Sams b.1773 the most probable
Littlebury Sams to have married (1)Nancy Edwards.

(NOTE)
  (2)John Sams b.1749 lived on Turkey Cock Creek in what is now Henry County Virginia.  This
could be the area where are Edwards lived before they were found on Snow Creek in Henry
County Virginia.

(NOTE)
  (2)Martha Edwards Sams died on July 19, 1824 at the age of 61 years making her birth date
1763 as shown on her tombstone where she is buried next to her husband William Sams Jr. in the
Sams Family Cemetery, south of Jonesboro, Union County, Illinois.

(NOTE)
  (2)William Sams Jr. b.1775 returned to Henry County Virginia to be married to Martha Edwards
b.1763 on 223 acres south of now Martinsville and east of Ridgeway on Simon Creek, a branch of
Matrimony Creek which flows into the Smith River east of Ridgeway where route 622 now
crosses Smith River.

  In closing I now believe that (2)Littlebury Sams b.1754 and the (1)Littleberry Sams which
married Nancy Edwards to be one and the same person.  The reason that this union was not
recorded in the Sams Family History is most likely lost to time.
  I also now believe that the Martha Edwards b.1763 which married William Sams Jr. b.1755 to be
directly related to my 5th great grandfather Abel Edwards which married Polly Potts.  I also
believe that Martha Edwards father Thomas Edwards to be related and very possibly the father or
uncle to Abel Edwards, father to Nancy Edwards b.1777 wife to Littleberry Sams.
  I lastly believe that our Edwards lived prior to Snow Creek Henry County Virginia 1783 near
the Sams family of Virginia in Henry County and possibly {SFOV PG.23} "Old Rapphannock
County in the tidelands, or possibly Spotsylvania County, or lastly Pittsylvania County Virginia".
  I do believe that there is much needed research on the Edwards family in early Henry County,
specifically researching the possible ancestral tie between Abel Edwards which married Polly
Potts and Martha Edwards father Thomas Edwards which died on November 17, 1790.

NOTE#1:   After John R. Jones received land from Thomas Jones in 1844 could not find a
John/John R./J.R. Jones through 1900 as selling land to anyone.  The land might have been kept in
family, or sold by family members in  small acres.  We will Probably never know.  Also the land
might have been willed, or another type of distribution of this land could have happened after
1844.  Another possibility's the descendants of John R. Jones left the land and no one bought the
land after they left.
    
NOTE#2:   There is no mention of a Squire/or "S" Jones selling land, 50 acres through 1900.
This person might have willed this land to his descendants or there was some other type of
distribution of this 50 acres after Squire Jones sold it or willed it.  Another possibility is that the
descendants to Squire Jones just left the land and no one bought the land after they left. 
    
-NOTE#3:  There was allot of activity with deeds on February 21, 1825 in Sullivan co. Ten.
There might be some correlation between the four (4) different deeds mentioned during this day.
I believe that these deeds do have something in common with Littleberry Sams selling 31 acre
parcels, which probably was the original land Abel Edwards purchased in 1780.
1.  There were the following deeds signed on Feb. 21, 1825.
a.  Obediah Sams 20 acres to John R. Glover
b.  Obediah Sams 7 acres from A. Vancevender (see Note#7)
c   Obediah Sams 29 1/4 acres from  Littleberry Sams

-NOTE#4:  There were four (4) different deeds completed during this day.  There is some kind of
association between these four (4) different deeds.  Possibly because of a deceased family
member.  Unknown at this time.

-NOTE#5:  (Item 14 and 15) have something to do with one another and the association between
the "T-bond" and the deeds of these two (2) items do have some hidden meaning.  The names of
these people who were involved in (item 14 and 15) are not the same people who are mentioned
in (item 11), with exception to Susanna Sams.  Susanna Sams mentioned in (Item #11) could be
the same person mentioned in (Item 14 & 15).  What makes me think that the people mentioned in
(item 11, 14, 15) are some how related, and that these land transactions are probably from the
original land that was once owned by Abel Edwards is because Littleberry Sams on October 6,
1838 sold four (4) 31 acre plots to four (4) different people which equal a total of 124 acres, and
on February 21, 1825 Littleberry Sams sold 29 1/4 acres which puts the total of 153 acres that
Littleberry Sams sold from the original 160 acres which he purchased from his father in law, my
5th great grandfather Abel Edwards.
This is under the assumption that the multiple deeds in question are from the original land
purchase from Abel Edwards.

-NOTE#6:  The earliest land transaction that Littleberry Sams was involved in was the transaction
with his father in law Abel Edwards, which he purchased 160 acres.  There are only 7 missing
acres from the original 160. 

-NOTE#7:   In note 6 there is mentioned 7 missing acres from the original 160 which Abel
Edwards sold to Littleberry Sams.  On February 21, 1825 An Obadiah Sams bought 7 acres of
land from Abraham Vandevender on the Holston River.  Ironically Littleberry Sams ended up
selling to an Obadiah Sams 29 1/4 acres on the same day as O. Sams bought 7 acres from A.
Vandevender.  Could this be some how the missing 7 acres of land and again why are all of these
deeds going on at the same time? 

I believe that this is very likely the missing 7 acres missing from the total of 160 acres that
Littleberry Sams purchased from Abel Edwards in 1807.

-NOTE#8:  Obediah Sams Sr. on September 29, 1854 bought land from Edmond Carrier, 31
acres.  This 31 acres is probably one of the three 31 acre parcels that Edmond Carrier purchased
(see item 14-15) from three different people in 1849.

-NOTE#9:  After 1854 (see item 16) there is not another mention of 31 acres parcels sold or
conveyed in any other manner.  The owners of the 31 acre parcels possibly willed the land to
family, or there was some other type of distribution of the acreage.  Another possibility is the
families who owned the 31 acre parcels left the land and no one bought the land after they left.

-NOTE#10:  Littleberry Sams isn't mentioned anywhere in Sullivan County Tennessee after
October 6, 1838, (see Item 12).
