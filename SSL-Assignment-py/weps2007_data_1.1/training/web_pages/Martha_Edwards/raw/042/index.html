Edwards Estate

Family legend has for generations held that EDWARDS heirs are entitled to a substantial fortune as a result of  inheriting Robert's land in New York City.  The claim is based on a lease dated "June first 1778" in which Robert Edwards describes the property and leases it "for 99 years at a thousand pounds and a peppercorn yearly." The lease specifically instructs that "at the completion of the 99 years lease said land together with all such improvements shall revert to the living heirs which will be descendants of my brothers and sisters as follows. William Edwards, Jacob Edwards, Leonard Edwards, Joshua Edwards, John Edwards, Thomas Edwards, Martha Edwards."  A show airing on Wednesday 30 Dec 1998 on BBC Radio 4 examined this "battle for Manhattan" and valued the "world's richest 78 acres of land" at $680 billion.

This page organizes links to genealogical information on two generations of descendants of William Edwards. While I regret that disk space limitations force users to visit a variety of sites to accumulate complete information, I hope that the availability of a single location to link these people together provides a contribution.  The "coordinator" links allow users to contact those individuals who have agreed to assemble information on descendants of the generations shown here.  If you have a web site that connects to the individuals listed here, or if you can contribute additional information on these individuals, please e-mail me with details.
