RUTH A. ROELSE

A memorial service for Ruth A. Roelse, 90, of Edmond will be 1 p.m. Monday at Anderson-Burris Funeral Home Chapel, Enid. The Rev. Ted Kuschel will officiate. Burial will be in Enid Memorial Park Cemetery.

She was born Aug. 12, 1915, north of Covington to Fred and Regina Diede Boepple and died Friday, Dec. 9, 2005, in Edmond.

She graduated from Covington High School in 1933.

She married Arthur Roelse Dec. 21, 1933. They made their home in Douglas for 14 years before moving to Enid. He preceded her in death.

She was a homemaker while working in food services at Enid High School, Phillips University and Champlin Refining Company.

She was a member of Washington Avenue Church before joining Emmanuel Baptist Church, where she was a Sunday school teacher.

Surviving are one son, Alvin Roelse of Houston; one daughter, Margaret Graham of Edmond; one sister, Esther Simpson of Covington; four grandchildren; and five great-grandchildren.

Besides her husband Arthur, she was preceded in death by five brothers and two sisters.

Memorials may be made through the funeral home to Emmanuel Baptist Church or a favorite charity.

WAUNITTA HISEY

Waunitta Hisey, 76, of Hunter, died Friday, December 9, 2005, in St. Mary’s Regional Medical Center after a period of ill health. Funeral service will be 10:30 a.m. Tuesday, December 13, 2005, in the Lad-usau-Evans Funeral Chapel with Bishop Scott Shields and Jim Evans officiating. Burial will follow in Memorial Park Cemetery under the direction of Ladusau-Evans Funeral Home. Memorials may be given to Arthritis Foundation with the funeral home custodian of the funds.

Waunitta was born in Enid, March 16, 1929, to Dennis and Edna Walton Cockrell. She graduated from Enid High School and worked in retail sales at Newman’s Department Store until 1958.

She and Johnnie “Dean” Hisey were married August 2, 1958, in Hunter and they made their home in Hunter. She was a member of the Church of Jesus Christ Of the Latter Day Saints, and the Eastern Star.

She is survived by her husband, Dean of the home; son, Brian D. and wife Earline Hisey, Wagoner; a granddaughter, Kortney Hisey, Edmond; two sisters; Norma Kretchmar and Wanda Barnett.

She was preceded in death by a son, Bruce Dennis Hisey, and her parents, Dennis and Edna Cockrell.

(Paid Obit 12-11-1)

RUTH ANN NAY

Ruth Ann Nay, 71, of Liberty, Mo. passed away Thursday, December 8, 2005, at Liberty Hospital. Services will be at 10:30 a.m. Tuesday at Liberty United Methodist Church. Friends may call from 6 to 8 p.m. on Monday evening at the Church-Archer-Pasley Chapel, 119 E. Franklin St. in Liberty. In lieu of flowers, the family requests that memorials be made to The American Heart Association or to the church.

Ruth Ann was born on February 19, 1934, in Enid, Oklahoma. She attended Phillips University and Oklahoma University. On April 6, 1957, she married W. Dean Nay who later became the Director of William Jewell College Campus Living for over 30 years.

Ruth Ann was a devoted wife, mother, and grandmother. She enjoyed Christian fellowship in the Liberty United Methodist Church choir, Spice of Life Fellowship and the Sojourners Class. She was a long standing member of Chapter DT of the PEO Sisterhood, Cultural Heritage, Modern Forty-Niners, Red Hat Club, FortNightly, William Jewell College Women’s Auxiliary Committee. She also found time to support the Liberty Symphony and the Liberty Hospital Foundation.

She deeply loved and appreciated her family, faith and friends.

Ruth Ann was preceded in death by her loving husband of 35 years, Dean, and parents, C.L. and Hazel Dyer.

Survivors include two sons, Mike and wife Shelly of Liberty, Mo., Mark and wife Dana of Kansas City, Mo; and daughter Carla Nay of Liberty, Mo; seven greandchildren, Tessa, Tyler, Anna, Leigha, Madison, Zackory and Danielle; a brother-in-law, Darell Nay and wife Gladys of Waxahachie, TX.

(Paid Obit 12-11-1)



GUSTAV ‘GUS’ E. KRIENKE

The funeral for Gustav “Gus” E. Krienke, 93, formerly of Enid, was held at 3 p.m. yesterday, December 10, 2005, at Trinity Lutheran Church. The Revs. Gary Hendrickson and David Krepel officiated. Burial was in Trinity Lutheran Cemetery. Arrange-ments were by Hennin-ger-Allen Funeral Home.

He was born Sept. 1, 1912, on a farm near Enid to Herman and Elizabeth Rutz Krienke and passed Thursday, Dec. 8, 2005, in Cimarron Nursing Home, Kingfisher. He married Adalyn Holtmann June 1, 1947. They farmed in the Texas panhandle until retirement in 1976, when they moved to Enid.

He was a lifelong member of Trinity Lutheran Church being baptized and confirmed there. He treasured his church and his family and enjoyed farming, fishing and playing pitch at Men’s Club.

Surviving are his children, Mike (Renie) Krienke of Enid, Paul (Karen) Krienke of St. Louis, Dan (Katie) Krienke of Perryton, Texas, Beth Thomas of Oklahoma City and Lois (Ronnie) Maxwell of Omega; ten grandchildren, John, Amy, Stacie, Stephanie, Hollie, Charles, Charlie, Jane, Chad and Brad; six great-grandchildren and one brother Arthur Krienke of Lahoma.

He was preceded in death by his wife Adalyn six years to the day on Dec. 8, 1999, one daughter, Phyllis, his parents, four brothers, and four sisters.

Memorials may be made through the funeral home to either Lutheran World Relief Fund or Russell-Murray Hospice.

Condolences may be made online at www.enidwecare.com.

(Paid Obit 12-11-1)



MARTHA EDWARDS

TRAYNOR

Funeral services for Martha Edwards Traynor, 89, will be Tuesday, December 13th, at 1:00 p.m. in the First Presbyterian Church. The Rev. Barry Williams will officiate. Burial will follow in Memorial Park Cemetery under the direction of Henninger-Allen Funeral Home.

Martha Edwards Traynor was born on March 14, 1916, in Delaware, Ohio, as the seventh and youngest child of the Rev. Dr. Dan Luther Edwards and Elizabeth Lloyd Edwards. She moved with her family at an early age to Ponca City, Oklahoma, where her father served as minister to the First Presbyterian Church of Ponca City. In 1920, she moved again with her family to Enid where her father enjoyed a long and fruitful tenure as minister at the First Presbyterian Church of Enid. Except for a short term in Blackwell, Oklahoma, where her father served the Presbyterian Church in that community, she had continually resided in Enid until her death on December 7, 2005.

Martha attended Enid schools and graduated from Enid High School in 1933. She then attended Lindenwood College in St. Charles, Mo., and the University of Oklahoma where she was a member of Kappa Alpha Theta sorority. On October 19, 1941, she married Arthur E. (Art) Traynor and they made their home in Enid where she remained active in her family’s pursuits and served as a homemaker, wife, mother, grandmother, and great-grandmother for the remainder of her life. She was a faithful member of the First Presbyterian Church all of her life and was also a member of the Junior Welfare League and Chapter Q, PEO.

Martha’s inner strengths included a fierce loyalty to her extended family, an exceptional ability to get to the heart of any matter, and a willingness to laugh at herself when the occasion, although rare, demanded. She was an eloquent storyteller and repository of her birth family’s oral history, and a beloved wife to her doting husband, Art. She was a great cook, but willingly played second fiddle to Art’s specialty meal, barbecued chicken. Her bravery and fortitude in facing the vicissitudes and trials of previously having lost all six of her siblings and all but one of their spouses while enduring her widowhood and final illness are qualities revered by her friends and loved ones. Finally, her joy at the recent birth of one great-grandson and the eminent arrival of an infant great-granddaughter reflect her inherent love of life and her great faith in God’s plan for her life.

Martha was predeceased by her parents, her husband, Art; three brothers, Paul Edwards, Lloyd Edwards, and Phil Edwards; and three sisters, Betty Edwards Dillingham, Jane Edwards Champlin, and Mary Edwards Cromwell.

She is survived by one son, Tim Traynor, and his wife, Suzy, Enid; two grandsons, Brad Traynor, his wife, Annessa, Edmond, and Dan Traynor, his wife, Amy, and their infant son, Timothy Liam, Houston; one sister-in-law, Anne Edwards Baker; also numerous nieces and nephews, grandnieces and nephews, and great-grandnieces and nephews.

Memorials may be made through Henninger-Allen Funeral Home to the First Presbyterian Church of Enid.

Condolences may be made at www.enidwecare.com.

(Paid Obit 12-11-1)
