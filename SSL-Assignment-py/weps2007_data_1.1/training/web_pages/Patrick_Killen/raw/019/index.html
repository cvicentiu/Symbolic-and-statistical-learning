Bush Officials, Proponent Square Off Against Question 7 in Dayton

An official of the Bush administration says the Nevada ballot initiative to legalize possession of up to an ounce of marijuana is being orchestrated by interests in Washington DC.

But a backer of Question Seven pointed to the Capitol connections of Scott Burns, who's deputy director of the White House Office of National Drug Control Policy for State and Local Affairs.

Patrick Killen of Las Vegas is Question Seven communications director. He says it's not an out-of-state effort because 86,000 Nevadans signed petitions to put it on the ballot.

Burns spoke at a panel discussion at the Dayton Community Center sponsored by Healthy Communities Coalition of Lyon and Storey Counties, which opposes the measure.
Killen and about ten others protested outside.
