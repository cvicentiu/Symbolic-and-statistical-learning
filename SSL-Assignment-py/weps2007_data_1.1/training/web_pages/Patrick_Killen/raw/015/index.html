MINUTES OF THE CULTURAL ARTS COMMISSION

October 13, 1998

CALL TO ORDER

The Cultural Arts Commission (CAC) of the City of Manhattan Beach, California convened in a regular session on Tuesday September 8, 1998 at 6:00 p.m., City Hall, Council Chambers. The meeting was called to order at 6:10 p.m.

ROLL CALL

Commissioner Killen called the meeting to order.

Present: Commissioners Kendra Carmon (arrived at 6:15 p.m.) Delpha Flad, Patrick Killen, Dale Robinson and Mitch Ward. Absent 0.

APPROVAL OF MINUTES

Minutes from the September 8, 1998 regular session were approved.

PUBLIC HEARINGS

There were no public hearings.

GENERAL BUSINESS

97/0909.1 Manhattan Heights Annex/ Arts Education Building

Staff reported eight architectural firms responded to the RFQ. A Selection Panel has been created. Staff members include Richard Gill, Neil Miller, Richard Thompson, Howard Spector and Barbara Johnson. Cultural Arts Commissioner Dale Robinson is also a member of this Panel. Mr. Spector stated another Staff member from the City’s Engineering Department will be added shortly to this Panel. Staff stated the Selection Panel would narrow down the eight architectural firms to three finalists, interview them and recommend one firm to the City Council for award of contract.

97/1111.1 – Art in Public Places for 97-98

Staff stated that one artist out of the three presenting would be recommended to Council, but all three proposals would be presented to Council at their October 20th meeting. The first artist to make their presentation was Adam Leventhal, please see the attached description. See the 3-D model for details.

The second artist to make his presentation was Dayton Claudio, please see the attached materials list. Mr. Claudio stated City Hall’s northeast entrance was the intended site for his piece. The piece consists of concrete tiles encircling the inner curve of the round sitting area near the entrance. Another large tiled area above the northeast doorway, consisting of several small tiles similar in theme to the other area would be installed. The intended theme would center on the ocean and beach. The imagery would be representative of abstracted ocean motifs. The texture and colors would also be suggestive of both ocean and beach. The surface of the tiles would have an anti-graffiti sealer applied for easier maintenance. See the 3-D model for details.

The third artist, Rod Baer did not attend the meeting. All Commissioners agreed to disqualify Mr. Baer because of his unexcused absence. No alternative artists were part of the selection process.

After the artists made their presentations, the Commission’s discussed the following:

Commissioner Robinson stated that the Veterans Parkway would be the best location for the Rail Bender piece, as it was a strong community piece and deserved a prominent spot. Commissioner Ward stated he felt the Rail Bender piece was the strongest of both presented. He did not like the texture and materials of Mr. Claudio’s piece. Commissioner Ward stated the Polliwog Park location would be most suitable for the Rail Bender piece, as the space is very public and prominent. Commissioner Robinson concurred, and added the placement of the artwork would be directly across the street from the intended Arts Education Building. Commissioner Flad and Carmon stated they supported the Rail Bender piece. Commissioner Killen stated by far, the most playful and artistically sophisticated work presented was Adam Leventhal’s Rail Bender. He added Polliwog Park would be a good location for the piece however felt the Veterans Parkway location would be an excellent "happened upon" and deserving location for this piece. He added, the Veterans Parkway location was also a very publicly accessible location, and was historically more appropriate for this artwork. Commissioner Robinson stated he agreed with Commissioner Killen on this point. Commissioner Killen stated the tile piece was not as strong and was representative of a "standard" type of public art piece. He felt Rail Bender was more representative of the community and history of the area.

A motion was made to recommend to City Council to accept Adam Leventhal’s public artwork Rail Bender, to be placed either along the Veterans Parkway (Valley/Ardmore at 1st and 2nd Streets), or second location of Polliwog Park along Manhattan Beach Blvd., west of the Historical Society’s red house.

MSC: approved 5:0. Both proposals will be presented to City Council at their October 20th meeting.

 

There was no further business discussed and the meeting was adjourned at 7:30 pm. to the next meeting scheduled for November 10, 1998.
