CITY OF MANHATTAN BEACH

MEMORANDUM



TO: Honorable Mayor and Members of City Council

THROUGH: Geoff Dolan, City Manager

FROM: LaVonne M. Harkless, Assistant City Clerk

DATE: October 17, 1995

SUBJECT: A Resolution of the City Council of the City of Manhattan
Beach, California, Amending Section 2.d. of Resolution
No. 4710 Establishing a Cultural Arts Commission
Pertaining to the Qualifications for Seat No. 5; and
Approving the Correction of Assignments to Seats No. 2
and 3

RECOMMENDATION

a) That the City Council adopt a resolution amending Section 2.d.
of Resolution No. 4710 changing the qualifications for Seat
No. 5, to read as follows:

d. One (1) commissioner shall be nominated by the
"Manhattan Beach Friends of the Arts" group and
ratified by City Council. This commissioner shall
be an individual interested in the arts, who is an
elector of the City; capable of engaging
effectively in a committee process; and open minded
and responsible.

b) That the City Council approve the correction in the
assignments for Seats No. 2 and 3, as follows:

Seat No. 2 (At-Large) - Homeira Goldstein

Seat No. 3 (Representative of Business and
Development Community of Manhattan Beach) -
Patrick Killen


BUDGET IMPLICATION

None

DISCUSSION

SEAT NO. 5 QUALIFICATIONS

At the Adjourned Regular Meeting held on Tuesday, October 10, 1995,
the City Council declared vacant Seat No. 5 on the Cultural Arts
Commission due to the relocation of Commissioner Grossman. In the
report to Council it was indicated that in accordance with Council
Resolution No. 4710, one of the qualifications for this office is
that the commissioner be a board member of the Manhattan Beach
Friends of the Arts. Subsequent to that action being taken by
Council, and upon further review of the files, staff discovered
that on June 5, 1990 the Council unanimously approved the deletion
of this requirement and requested that the Manhattan Beach Friends
of the Arts nominate someone to fill this seat. At that time it
appears that staff failed to amend the resolution accordingly,
therefore I am requesting that the language of the resolution be
modified to reflect that change at this time.

Due to the statutory requirements for the posting of unscheduled
vacancies, the appointment to fill Seat No. 5 cannot be made until
Tuesday, November 7, 1995. The adoption of this resolution tonight
allows staff time to contact the Manhattan Beach Friends of the
Arts and request that they submit a nominee to be ratified by the
Council on November 7, 1995.

CORRECTION OF ASSIGNMENT OF SEATS 2 AND 3

Upon reviewing the files for the Cultural Arts Commission, it came
to my attention that the assignments for Seats No. 2 and 3 have
been inadvertently switched. Patrick Killen is currently listed as
serving in Seat No. 2 (At-Large), however he should be serving in
Seat No. 3 (Business and Development Representative). When this
appointment was made it was Council's intent that Mr. Killen serve
in Seat No. 3 since this seat does not require that the
commissioner be an elector of the City. Mr. Killen is a Hermosa
Beach resident and a business owner at 912 Manhattan Avenue in
Manhattan Beach. Homeira Goldstein is currently listed as serving
in Seat No. 3 (Business and Development Representative), however
she should be serving in Seat No. 2 (At-Large).

The commissioners will retain their current term schedules and this
correction will have no affect on their maximum tenure. The
correction is actually ministerial in nature and does not require
Council approval, however since the resolution does require Council
approval, I felt that it was appropriate to make all of the
adjustments at the same time in order to correct the record.

ALTERNATIVES

1) That the Council adopt the resolution modifying the
qualifications for Seat No. 5 as recommended and approve
staff's recommendation to correct the assignments for Seat No.
2 and 3.

2) That the Council adopt a modified version of the resolution to
request that the Manhattan Beach Friends of the Arts nominate
and submit a maximum of three (3) names for consideration by
The Council for Seat No. 5, and approve staff's recommendation
to correct the assignments for Seats No. 2 and 3.

3) That the Council discuss and provide direction to staff.
