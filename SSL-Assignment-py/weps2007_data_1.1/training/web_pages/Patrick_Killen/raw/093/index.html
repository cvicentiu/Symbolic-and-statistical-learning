PLANNING COMMISSION

CITY OF ROLLING HILLS ESTATES

LOS ANGELES COUNTY, CALIFORNIA

RESOLUTION NO. PA-27-03

A RESOLUTION OF THE PLANNING COMMISSION OF THE CITY OF ROLLING HILLS ESTATES, APPROVING A PRECISE PLAN OF DESIGN, A MINOR DEVIATION TO EXCEED ALLOWABLE COVERAGE OF THE LOT, AND A GRADING PLAN TO PERMIT IMPORTATION OF APPROXIMATELY 167 CUBIC YARDS OF FILL AND A MAXIMUM CUT DEPTH OF 4 FEET FOR A 5,760 SQUARE FOOT OFFICE/COMMERCIAL BUILDING ON A .51-ACRE PARCEL. APPLICANT: JUDY CHAI, P.O. BOX 2843, RANCHO PALOS VERDES, CA, 90274; LOCATION: 5883 CREST ROAD.

WHEREAS, Ms. Judy Chai filed an application with the Planning Department requesting permission to construct a 5,760 square-foot office/commercial building on a .51-acre parcel; such an application as required by Chapters 15.04, 17.24, 17.28, 17.58 and 17.66 of the Rolling Hills Estates Municipal Code; and

WHEREAS, an Initial Study was prepared by the City in conformance with the requirements of the California Environmental Quality Act (CEQA). It was found that the project would not have a significant impact on the environment with proper mitigation. As such, a Mitigated Negative Declaration was prepared; and

WHEREAS, in accordance with Section 65033 of the Government Code, the public, abutting cities, affected agencies and districts were notified of the availability of the Initial Study and Mitigated Negative Declaration and were given an opportunity to review and comment; and

WHEREAS, the Planning Department responded in writing to said comments in the Initial Study; and

WHEREAS, upon giving the required notice the Planning Commission conducted Public Hearings on the 6th day of July, 2004 and the 16th day of August, 2004. All interested parties were given full opportunity to be heard and to present evidence; and

WHEREAS, as a result of studies and investigations made by the Planning Commission and on its behalf, revealed that the facts as set forth in the Initial Study and Mitigated Negative Declaration, and those discussed during the public hearing show the following:

That the granting of the approval will not be materially detrimental to the public welfare or injurious to property and improvements in the Zoning District and neighborhood in which the property is located because the proposed improvements will be regulated via a Precise Plan of Design and conditions of approval for the project.

That the granting of the approval will not be contrary to the objectives of the General Plan because the property is designated Neighborhood Commercial which provides for business, professional service, and retail uses.

That the granting of the approval will not authorize a use or activity stipulated in the Zoning District which is not otherwise expressly authorized by the zone regulations governing the parcel of property because the Commercial Limited (C-L) Zone provides for office and commercial uses.

NOW, THEREFORE, the Planning Commission of the City of Rolling Hills Estates does hereby resolve as follows:

SECTION 1. That the foregoing facts constitute conditions necessary to the granting of a Precise Plan of Design, Minor Deviation, and a grading plan; and to adopt a Mitigated Negative Declaration for a 5,760 square foot office/commercial building on a .51-acre parcel; such an application as required by Chapters 15.04, 17.24, 17.28, 17.58, and 17.66 of the Rolling Hills Estates Municipal Code, and that said Permits be granted subject to the following conditions which must be met at all times by the applicant, unless otherwise stated, in order to enjoy the use of the subject property for any and all uses permitted by the granting of the subject permits.

STANDARD CONDITIONS

1. That the development shall be located and constructed as shown on Exhibit A.

2. That any substantial modification including, but not limited to, exterior building elevations, parking lot design, and landscaping, shall receive prior approval of the Planning Commission; minor modifications may be approved by the City Manager.

3. That all applicable requirements of the State, County, City and other Governmental entities, must be met.

4. That prior to issuance of Building Permits or Grading Permits, a Zone Clearance shall be obtained from the Planning Department.

5. That, unless the use is inaugurated or construction of that project is commenced and being diligently pursued not later than one hundred and eighty (180) days after the date this approval is granted, the approval will automatically expire on that date. However, if there have been no changes in the proposed plans or adjacent areas, the Planning Commission may grant a time extension for use inauguration up to an additional one hundred and eighty (180) day period.

6. That all proposed new utilities shall be placed underground to the nearest off-site facility, per Municipal Code Chapter 13.12.

     

  1. The applicant shall defend, hold harmless and indemnify at his or her own expense the City, its agents, officers and employees, from any claim, action, or proceeding, to attack, set aside, void or annul the approval granted in this resolution and shall reimburse the City, its agents, officers and employees for any damages, court costs and attorneys’ fees incurred as a result of such action. The City at its sole discretion may participate in the defense of any such action but such participation shall not relieve applicant of his or her obligation under this condition.

     

  1. That prior to the issuance of building permits, the project shall be reviewed for compliance with National Pollution Discharge Elimination System (NPDES) requirements. Project shall incorporate design elements to:
    1. Minimize impacts from storm water and urban runoff on the biological integrity of Natural Drainage Systems and water bodies in accordance with requirements under CEQA (Cal. Pub. Resources Code § 21100), CWC § 13369, CWA § 319, CWA § 402(p), CWA § 404, CZARA § 6217(g), ESA § 7, and local ordinances ;
    2. Maximize the percentage of pervious surfaces to allow percolation of storm water into the ground;
    3. Minimize the quantity of storm water directed to impervious surfaces and the MS4;
    4. Minimize pollution emanating from parking lots through the use of appropriate Treatment Control Best Management Practices (BMPs) and good housekeeping practices;
    5. Properly design and maintain Treatment Control BMPs in a manner that does not promote the breeding of vectors; and

       

    6. Provide for appropriate permanent measures to reduce storm water pollutant loads in storm water from the development site.

AIR QUALITY

     

  1. The Project Applicant shall prepare a fugitive dust control plan that meets the requirements outlined in SCAQMD Rule 403. The plan shall be submitted for review and approval of the SCAQMD prior to issuance of grading permits.

     

  2. The Project Applicant shall properly maintain equipment and vehicle engines and as per manufacturers’ specifications and per SCAQMD rules to minimize exhaust emissions.

CULTURAL RESOURCES

     

  1. A cultural resources monitor shall be present during grading activity on the site. In the event that previously unknown cultural resources are uncovered during this activity, the monitor shall have the authority to cease work until the extent and importance of the find can be determined. Any artifacts uncovered shall be recorded and removed for storage at a location to be determined by the monitor.

    GEOLOGY AND SOILS

     

  2. Proposed structures shall be designed to comply with 1997 UBI seismic design parameters.

     

  3. An allowable bearing value of 3,000 pounds per square foot, for square and continuous footings shall be used for foundations placed at a depth of at least 24 inches below the lowest adjacent final grade bearing 12 inches into the firm bedrock. This value shall be increased by 200 pounds per square foot, for each additional foot in depth over 2 feet, and 100 pounds per square foot for each additional foot in width over 1 foot, to a maximum of 3,000 pounds per square foot.

     

  4. An allowable lateral bearing value against the sides of footing of 400 pounds per square foot per foot of depth, to a maximum of 6,000 pounds per square foot shall be used provided there is positive contact between the vertical bearing surface and bedrock.

     

  5. Type II cement shall be used due the sulfate content in the soil.

     

  6. Retaining walls shall be designed to the following parameters:

A proper drainage system shall be utilized to prevent hydrostatic pressures behind the retaining wall.

     

  1. Temporary excavation slopes in the existing surface soil and bedrock shall be made vertical for cuts of less than five feet. For deeper cuts, temporary excavation slopes shall be made no steeper than 1:1. In areas where soils with little or no binder are encountered, shoring or flatter excavation slopes shall be made.

     

  2. All excavations shall be made in accordance with the regulations of the State of California, Division of Industrial Safety.

     

  3. Site drainage shall be dispersed by non-erosive devices in accordance with the grading regulations of controlling agencies to preclude concentrated runoff and erosion over the site.

     

  4. Slab on grade shall be a minimum of 4 inches thick and reinforced by No. 3 bars, 16 inches on-center each way. Subgrade soils shall be moistened to a depth of 24 inches prior to the placing of the membrane and pouring of floor slabs.

     

  5. The following general grading specifications shall be provided:

       

    1. Areas to be graded or paved shall be grubbed and stripped of all vegetation, debris, and other deleterious material. All loose soil disturbed by the removal of trees, structures and existing fill shall be removed. In all cases where the ground slope is steeper than 5:1, the existing ground shall be benched, as the fill thereon is brought up in layers. All new fill shall be brought to near optimum moisture content, placed in layers not exceeding 6 inches thick and compacted to at least 90 percent.

       

    2. The existing subgrade loose soils within the building and paved areas shall be compacted prior to construction of floor slabs and paving to secure uniform support and to minimize differential settlement.

       

    3. All new fill shall consist of clean, granular, non-expansive soil, free of vegetation and other debris, and shall be placed in layers not exceeding 6 inches at optimum moisture content. No rocks over 3 inches in greatest dimension shall be used. No soil shall be imported to the site without prior approval by the geotechnical engineer.

       

    4. The compaction characteristics of all fill soils shall be determined by ASTM D-1557-97. The field density and degree of compaction shall be determined by ASTM D-155, or by other acceptable ASTM standard methods which are acceptable to the governing public agency.

       

    5. No jetting or water tamping of fill soils shall be permitted.

       

    6. No fill shall be placed, spread, or rolled during unfavorable weather.

     

  1. As a necessary requisite to the use of the geotechnical report, the following shall be observed by the geotechnical engineer:

       

    1. Inspection of all grading operations.

       

    2. Inspection of all backfill wedges, drainage blankets, and weep holes for retaining walls.

       

    3. Inspection of premoistening of subgrade soils, and placement of sand cushion and vapor barrier beneath slabs.

       

    4. Inspection of all foundations, excavations for the structure, or retaining walls.

NOISE

     

  1. All construction activity shall be limited to between the hours of 7:00 A.M. and 5:00 P.M. Monday through Friday, and 9:00 A.M. and 5:00 P.M. on Saturday. No work shall be permitted on Sundays or holidays (New Years Day, Memorial Day, Independence Day, Labor Day, Thanksgiving Day and Christmas Day).

     

  2. No queuing of trucks or arrival of construction materials and/or workers to the construction site shall be permitted outside the permitted construction hours and days.

     

  3. Contractor shall prohibit off-site heavy truck activities in local residential areas as well as establish City-approved haul routes.

     

  4. Contractor shall ensure that construction equipment is fitted with modern sound-reduction equipment.

SPECIAL CONDITIONS

     

  1. That the storm drainage from building roofs and paved areas shall be collected on-site and discharged to the street under any sidewalk and/or roadway.

     

  2. That all roof-mounted equipment shall be screened from view. Any screening features shall be architecturally integrated with the proposed structure and shown on Exhibit A, as approved by the Planning Commission.

     

  3. That a Lighting Plan shall be submitted subject to review and approval of the Planning Director.

     

  4. That, prior to the issuance of Zone Clearance, a method of control to prevent dust and windblown earth problems, and the route for trucking soil, shall be submitted to, and approved by, the City Manager.

     

  5. That permits are required for all work within public rights-of-way, and shall be subject to review and approval of the City Manager.

     

  6. That all handicapped spaces are to be posted and painted to meet the State Handicapped Parking Requirements.

     

  7. That the sign location, size, materials and colors, etc., for all signing of the site, shall be approved under a Master Sign Plan to be approved by the Planning Commission prior to issuing any Sign Permits.

     

  8. That all final exterior building materials shall conform to those materials shown on the building schedule as approved by the Planning Commission, as a part of Exhibit B, incorporated herein by reference.

     

  9. That the applicant shall comply with the City's Noise Ordinance, both during the construction phase of the development and during the operation of the complex after construction is completed.

     

  10. That trash enclosures shall be architecturally compatible with the proposed construction as approved by the Planning Commission and shown in Exhibit A, incorporated herein by reference. The trash receptacle and debris shall be contained and maintained within the enclosed area. A solid roof cover shall be provided for the trash enclosure to be constructed of materials compatible to other enclosure materials subject to review and approval of the Planning Director.

     

  11. That the total Gross Leasable Area shall be limited to not more than 5,760 square feet, and that retail use of the building shall not exceed 1,190 square feet; and that 21 parking spaces shall be provided.

     

  12. That, prior to issuance of a Zone Clearance, the applicant shall: (A) submit a Landscaping and Irrigation Plan prepared by a licensed Landscape Architect for the subject site; and (B) shall have that Plan reviewed by the Park and Activities Commission and approved by the City Council; and (C) shall have the landscaping and irrigation installed to the satisfaction of the City Manager prior to occupancy. The Landscape Plan shall provide a uniform 10’-high hedge along the northerly property line if desired by the Seaview Villas Homeowners Association as determined by the project applicant during Landscape Plan review. If installed, the hedge shall be maintained by the subject property owner at a height not to exceed that of the proposed office/commercial building (eighteen (18) feet).

     

  13. That the Landscape Plan shall utilize low-water consuming plants where possible and shall be consistent with the General Plan Landscape Guidelines.

     

  14. That the irrigation systems shall be geared to seasonal fluctuations in vegetative water needs.

     

  15. That all landscaped areas shall be provided with an automatic irrigation system. The detailed specifications shall be reviewed and approved by the City Manager prior to the issuance of Zone Clearance.

     

  16. That the Landscape Plan shall utilize extensive mulching.

     

  17. That the development shall not produce odors which would exceed State or County Sanitation Standards or odors determined to be offensive by the County Health Department.

     

  18. That the Conditions of Resolution No. PA-27-03 and all subsequent amendments, shall remain in full force and effect unless modified or superseded by subsequent City action.

     

  19. That the requested uses shall be permitted to operate between the hours of 7:00 a.m. and 8:00 p.m.

     

  20. That the cut in the northeasterly portion of the project site be softened to better conform to the natural slope contour.

     

  21. That medical office, convenience store, video sales/rentals, and Laundromat uses shall be prohibited.

     

  22. That excess curb cuts along Highridge Road and Crest Road (within the City of Rancho Palos Verdes right-of-way) shall be removed with accompanying right-of-way repair at the sole expense of the applicant.

SECTION 2. This approval shall not be effective for any purpose until applicant has filed an Affidavit of Acceptance stating that he/she is aware of and accepts all of the conditions. If applicant does not accept the conditions within thirty (30) days from the effective date of this approval, all rights hereby granted shall be void. The Affidavit of Acceptance must be received by the City prior to Zone Clearance.

SECTION 3. If any portion of this approval is violated or held to be invalid or if any law, statute, or ordinance is violated by the issuance of this approval or by any one or more of the requirements thereof, said use shall be void and privileges herewith shall lapse and such use shall thereupon cease.

SECTION 4. The City Clerk shall forward a copy of this Resolution to the applicant and to the Building Department, respectively, for their attention.

SECTION 5. The Planning Commission hereby approves the Mitigated Negative Declaration, finding that the proposed project, subject to the conditions stated herein, will not result in any adverse environmental impacts.

SECTION 6. That, unless appealed pursuant to the requirements of Article VIII of the Rolling Hills Estates Municipal Code, this Resolution shall become effective twenty (20) days from the date of adoption.

SECTION 7. That the City Clerk shall certify to the adoption of this Resolution.

ADOPTED this 20th day of September, 2004.
