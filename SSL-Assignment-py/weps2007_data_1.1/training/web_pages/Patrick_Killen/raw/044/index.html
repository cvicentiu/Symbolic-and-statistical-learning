Press Release: October 23, 2006

FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE October 23, 2006

Marijuana Initiative Protests Deputy Drug Czar for
Interfering in Nevada’s Election Matters
Campaign Challenges Federal Government’s Intervention Against Question 7

CONTACT: Patrick Killen, Director of Communications, 702-425-2242, ext. 808, or
Eliet Henderson, Assistant Director of Communications, 702-425-2242, ext. 805

DAYTON, NEVADA -- The Committee to Regulate and Control Marijuana (CRCM), which supports Question 7 -- the initiative to tax and regulate marijuana in Nevada -- today confronted Deputy Drug Czar Scott Burns, Deputy Director of the White House Office of National Drug Control Policy (ONDCP) for State and Local Affairs, at a local community center in Dayton, Nevada. A dozen supporters of Question 7 protested Burns’ taxpayer-funded campaign to interfere in Nevada’s election, holding signs and chanting, “Czar, go home! Leave Nevada alone!”

Supporters of Question 7 turned out at the deputy drug czar’s scheduled event to protest his intrusion into a Nevada state election issue. Burns traveled to Dayton from Washington, D.C., to oppose Question 7 at a public forum.

Patrick Killen, CRCM Communications Director, was on hand for the protest. “We’re here to make sure the deputy drug czar knows that Nevadans don’t want the federal government meddling in our state’s election matters. The voters of Nevada have the right to decide Question 7 for themselves -- without interference from Washington.”

In 2003, then-Nevada Attorney General Brian Sandoval criticized Drug Czar John Walters’ interference in a 2002 marijuana initiative in the state. “It is unfortunate that a representative of the federal government substantially intervened in a matter that was clearly a State of Nevada issue,” Attorney General Sandoval wrote in an opinion. “The excessive federal intervention that was exhibited in this instance is particularly disturbing because it sought to influence the outcome of a Nevada election.”

CRCM Campaign Manager Neal Levine said, “Washington bureaucrats like this deputy drug czar need to stop trying to force their bad ideas on Nevada. With Congress’ auditors calling for the federal drug czar’s budget to be slashed, and members of Congress demanding that the drug czar be fired for mismanaging his office, we believe the drug czar and his deputies have better things to do with their time than spend tax dollars interfering in Nevada’s election.”

Question 7 removes marijuana from the criminal market and places it into a tightly regulated and taxed market. The initiative will remove criminal penalties for marijuana use by adults aged 21 and older in the privacy of their own homes. The initiative creates a system of manufacture and sale of up to one ounce of marijuana to adults aged 21 and older, with strict regulations on retail establishments. The initiative doubles the maximum penalty for killing or seriously injuring someone while driving under the influence of marijuana, alcohol, or any other drug. It also doubles the penalty for giving or selling marijuana to a minor. Question 7 earmarks half of the revenue from the sales of marijuana to go toward drug and alcohol treatment programs, while the other half will go to the state’s general fund, generating millions of dollars for Nevada’s most pressing needs. The Question 7 Web site is www.RegulateMarijuana.org.
