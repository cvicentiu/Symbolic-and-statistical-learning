Drug Laws and Enforcement

topic posted Fri, October 27, 2006 - 8:08 AM by  Joe
When I think about our current laws on illegal drugs they seem so ridiculous, they do nothing to help people addicted to them, they do nothing to stop them from being sold, and they do nothing but populate our prisons with a lot of nonviolent people that have been arrested for possession or selling illegal drugs.

I find the way that Libertarians propose to help the growing drug problem in America very interesting, but it seems to be based on a lot of wishful thinking and unknown factors. If drugs were made legal and taxed and regulated by the government, in similar ways that alcohol and tobacco are, would that help the drug problem in America? Would it solve more problems than it created? I'm under the impression that if drugs were made legal there would not be a huge rush of people suddenly taking more drugs or starting to take drugs all of a sudden just because they are legal. I don't think people DON'T do drugs just because the are illegal it's because they don't want to. And there are millions of people that can use drugs and not make it a problem in their lives. Is smoking a joint a week and worse than drinking a six pack on a friday evening?

The upside I see to legalizing drugs are the enormous amount of resources that can be taken back from funding the current "war on drugs", billions of dollars to be pumped into awareness and drug prevention programs for schools to teach children about the ill effects of drugs. Also we can use the money to fund rehab programs for people that are currently addicted to drugs that need help. It would lower taxes by not having to fund this bloated and ineffective prohibition on illegal drugs, we can also taxes the people who choose to use drugs by taxing the product the purchase.

The downside I can envision is that if drugs we made drugs legal there is the potential for a huge surge in drug abuse and a potential social decline, but while this is an unseen possibility the upsides are clear. What do you all think?
