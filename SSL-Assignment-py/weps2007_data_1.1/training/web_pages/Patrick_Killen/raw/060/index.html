GOP head Gorham under attack again

By Shea Andersen
Tribune Reporter

Ramsay Gorham is fighting for her job.

Again.

This time Gorham, a Republican state senator from Albuquerque and the chairwoman of the New Mexico GOP, is fighting a challenge to her ability to do both jobs at once.

"We went over this," Gorham said. "They knew I was a senator when they elected me. They need to honor the election."

Gorham won a hard-fought election for the party's top post in June, taking the seat from longtime chairman John Dendahl.

But almost from the day she took the job, there have been challenges to Gorham's leadership.

The latest comes from six members of the party's Rules Committee, which governs the party's adherence to its rules and ethics guidelines.

In a letter sent to Gorham on Thursday, the six committee members asked her to stop running for re-election to her state Senate seat in the North Valley.

Gorham has a primary opponent in the race, former GOP executive director John Ryan. Two Democrats are also vying for the seat: Victor Raigoza, a stockbroker, and John Hooker, former mayor of Los Ranchos.

The letter said Gorham's candidacy and simultaneous chairmanship of the party has created a "tremendous ethical and legal problem that must be dealt with immediately."

The letter was signed by Ken Batson, Charles Coll, Nora Espinoza, Candi Hacker, Patrick Killen and Arthur Ortiz.

"Continuing this race puts the party at significant peril on a number of fronts both legally and politically, not to mention ethically," the letter states.

The group threatened to call a meeting of the full Central Committee if Gorham refused to back out of the race.

"We will have to do it in order to save the party embarrassment, maintain credibility and unite the party for the fall campaign," the letter states.

One member of the state central committee is Dendahl himself.

"You might call it a threat, or a promise," Dendahl said of the letter. "It's too bad that party officials are having to prod Ramsay to come out and do the right thing."

Gorham was called out on this issue before, that time by Ryan. One reason he decided to challenge her in the election, he said, is to force her to focus on her job as state party chairwoman.

Ryan and the six party officials signing the recent letter said Gorham is violating rules prohibiting the party's leader from taking sides in a race between Republican candidates. Ryan focused his attention on Gorham's executive director, Tom Carroll, who has been quoted in news reports taking Gorham's side over Ryan's in this dispute.

Ryan and the committee members said that violated party rules.

In her defense, Gorham has released a letter from former Lt. Gov. Walter Bradley supporting her. Bradley, too, is a member of the GOP's Rules Committee and said Gorham isn't violating any party rules.

Gorham said the problem was Ryan's in the making.

"The party isn't going after John Ryan. He's challenged me," Gorham said. "He's the one creating the primary."

She pointed out that in the unpaid state Legislature, almost everyone has a second job.

"That's why we're called a `citizen Legislature,' " Gorham said.

The party chairman post is unpaid.

Gorham said the dispute boils down to sour grapes over her election last June.

"This is Dendahl's executive director that wasn't kept on by me," Gorham said. "So now, here he comes."
