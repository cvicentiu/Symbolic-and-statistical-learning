Campaigns & Elections magazine Names 2006 Rising Stars

May 23, 2006

Washington, D.C., May 23, 2006 - Campaigns & Elections magazine today named its 2006 Rising Stars.  Rising Stars are those under 35 who are beginning to make their mark in political consulting or advocacy.  Campaigns & Elections magazine chose ten Democrats, ten Republicans, and four nonpartisan leaders out of a pool of several hundred nominees. 

 

Past Rising Stars have included Paul Begala, Ed Gillespie, Alexis Herman, Karen Hughes, Laura Ingraham, Jim Pinkerton, Ralph Reed and George Stephanopolous, to name just a few.

 

“This year’s Rising Stars competed in one of the largest and most competitive pools in recent years,” Morgan E. Felchner, editor of Campaigns & Elections said. “We expect to see these 24 people soar to uncommon success in the near future.” 

 

The Rising Stars of 2006 are:

 

Michael Bassik (D), vice president of Internet marketing for MSHC Partners, a voter contact firm that offers mail, targeting and Internet marketing services.

 

Jeffrey Coleman (R), political consultant and once the youngest member to serve in the Pennsylvania House of Representatives.

 

Erin Delullo (R), fundraiser for Ken Blackwell for Ohio Governor, Katherine Harris for Senate and a number of conservative individuals and organizations.

 

Kyle DeBeer (D), executive director of the Wyoming Democratic Party.

 

Zach Dietch (R), national field coordinator for the National Republican Senatorial Committee.

 

Brian Donahue (R), senior vice president of Jamestown Associates, a media and direct mail consulting firm.

 

Vince Galko (R), campaign manager for Senator Rick Santorum’s re-election effort.

 

Amanda Hydro (R), executive director of the College Republican National Committee.

 

Patrick Killen (R), director of communications for the Committee for Regulate and Control Marijuana in Nevada.

 

Justin Lapatine (D), vice president of the Global Strategy Group, a market research and polling firm.

 

Lance Mangum (D), manager of policy and political affairs for CapitalOne Financial Corporation.

 

Matthew McMillan (D), president of BuzzMaker, a new media and online consulting firm.

 

Susan DiLiddo Michels (D), vice president of GMMB, a political advertising and consulting firm.

 

Julian Mulvey (D), principal of the Julian Mulvey Group, a Democratic media consulting firm.

 

Aimee Nichols (R), account executive at Valis & Keelen, a government relations firm that specializes in PAC fund-raising, advocacy and branding representation.

 

Ashley O’Connor (R), founder of Red October Productions a film production post-production company.

 

Kevin O’Neill (N), director of online campaigns for Grassroots Enterprise and former executive director of the American Association of Political Consultants.

 

Max Pulsinelli (N), founder Maximum Impact Public Relations, a public relations and publicity firm which focuses on political books and authors as well as political advocacy organizations.

 

Mark Riddle (D), partner at Fletcher Rowley Chao Riddle, a Democratic media firm based in Nashville, Tenn.

 

Frank Salazar (D), campaign manager for Bob Filner for Congress.

 

Heather Smith (N), director of YoungVoter Strategies, a project of the Graduate School of Political Management at George Washington University that provides candidates, parties, consultants and nonprofits with data on young voters and tools that they can use to mobilize that demographic group.

 

Giovanna Gray Torchio (D), senior vice president of business development at NorthTrack Media, an integrated strategic communications and media firm.

 

Jim Wilson (N), manager of political education programs for the American Medical Association’s PAC.

 

Ray Zaborney (R), campaign manager for Lynn Swann for Pennsylvania Governor.

 

Rising Stars will be honored at the 23rd annual campaign training seminar “The Art of Political Campaigning” being held on June 1st 2006 in Washington D.C.

 

Founded in 1980 by Stanley Foster Reed, Campaigns & Elections magazine (www.campaignline.com) (published 10 times per year) covers the strategies, techniques, and personalities of modern politics. It is read by tens of thousands of federal, state and local elected officials, candidates for public office, party activists, issue campaigners, political consultants, campaign staffs, lobbyists, PAC directors, university professors, news reporters and numerous behind-the-scenes opinion makers.
