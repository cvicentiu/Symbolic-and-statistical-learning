CALL TO ORDER

The Cultural Arts Commission (CAC) of the City of Manhattan Beach, California convened in a regular session on Tuesday May 12, 1998 at 6:00 p.m., at Manhattan Beach City Hall, at 1400 Highland Ave. The meeting was called to order at 6:10 p.m..

ROLL CALL

Commissioner Killen called the meeting to order.

Present: Commissioners Peggy Johnson, Patrick Killen, Dale Robinson and Mitch Ward.

Absent: Commissioner Homeira Goldstein.

APPROVAL OF MINUTES

Minutes from the April 14, 1998 session were approved.

PUBLIC HEARINGS

There were no public hearings.

GENERAL BUSINESS

End of Cultural Arts Commission Terms

Mr. Spector presented Commissioner Johnson with a certificate of Accommodation from the City Council for her service as a Cultural Arts Commissioner. Mr. Spector also personally thanked her for her lending her wisdom, advice, experience and knowledge to the Commission. Commissioner Goldstein was not present to accept her certificate. Cultural Arts Division staff mailed the certificate to her.

New Cultural Arts Commission (CAC) Commissioners

Mr. Spector announced new CAC Commissioners Delpha Flad and Kendra Carmon would attend the June 9th CAC meeting. He also added, Commissioner Robinson has been re-appointed for another term.

 

Next CAC Regular Session to be Held at the Manhattan Heights Annex

Mr. Spector stated that at the request of Parks and Recreation Director Richard Gill, the next CAC meeting will be held at the Manhattan Heights Annex, as future plans for the site will be discussed.

97/1111.2 - Art in Public Places for 97-98:

Mr. Spector reported that a Request for Qualifications (RFQ) for a public art commission for FY 97-98 has been drafted. He felt Council would like three sites to be chosen by the CAC and approved by the City Council prior to soliciting artwork from artists. The sites would then appear on the RFQ. Mr. Spector stated that originally the CAC had approved a motion to the City Council for the artists to pick three possible sites for their proposed artwork.

During a discussion among the Commissioners, the following points were made:

  1. Commissioner Robinson felt some artistic value would be possibly lost, if the artists

can not choose the sites, specific to their work. Mr. Spector replied, that the RFQ would then be "site specific", a condition artists are familiar working with, as all RFQs for public art commissions by public or private agencies are either "site specific" or "non-site specific". He added Council’s decision would not effect the artists’ artistic values.

2) Commissioner Ward stated if sites were pre-determined, it would give all artists an "equal playing field".

  1. Mr. Spector stated the artist selection process would be the following:

F A panel would select from artists’ requested materials, and chose 3 candidates.

F The three selected artists would investigate the three pre-determined sites.

F The artists would make presentations to the CAC and panel.

F The CAC and panel would chose an artist and project based on the artists’ presentations.

F The CAC would make a recommendation to the City Council.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

4) Commissioner Johnson suggested possible sites be listed and discussed. Commissioners present mentioned the following sites:

Gateways:

Buildings: Valley/Ardmore at about 2nd Street (on grass)

City Hall Metlox (intersection of Manhattan Beach

Library (Civic Center) Blvd. & Valley/Ardmore)

Joslyn Center the Strand (South end)

Manhattan Heights Annex El Porto (Highland)

Mall Rosecrans and Sepulveda

Rosecrans and Aviation (by studios)

Parks: Other:

Parque Culican Veterans Pathway

Polliwog Park (Southeast corner) Water Tower site

Marine Ave. Park

5) Commissioner Killen recommended the east public entrance of City Hall, as there is already an art piece at the west entrance, by personnel. He felt many people use the east entrance more than the west, as parking is along that side of the building. He added, Parque Culican would also be a very good site for a public art piece to be located, as it is a visible and accessible location for the public. He stated it would be a good site to "bring the community together".

6) Commissioner Ward agreed with Commissioner Killen’s suggestion. He asked Mr. Spector if the emergency call box locations along the Veterans Parkway project would be a good site for art placement? Mr. Spector replied by stating he felt it would not be good to mix projects at this point in their development.

7) Commissioner Johnson suggested the corner of Manhattan Beach Blvd. and Redondo Ave. (Southeast corner of Polliwog Park), would be an excellent site, as it is highly accessible to park-goers and auto traffic. She suggested the intersection of Valley/Ardmore and close to 2nd Street (on grass) would be a great site also, as people walking, jogging or biking along the Veterans Parkway would experience it, as well as those driving by. It would also act as a gateway into Manhattan Beach from Hermosa.

8) Commissioners Killen, Ward, and Robinson agreed with Commissioner Johnson.

A motion was made to recommend the following sites be recommended to Council: City Hall (east entrance); Parque Culican; intersection of Valley/Ardmore and close to 2nd Street (on grass) for art placement. MSC: approved unanimously (4-0).

 

 

 

 

98/0512.2 – Arts Festival 1998:

Mr. Spector reported the Cultural Arts staff was gearing-up for the upcoming 1998 Arts Festival. The Festival will take place on September 13th , and will probably take place on Manhattan Ave. . He stated it would be similar to last year’s festival, however this year there will be no large bands involved. Funds that were formerly utilized for contracting the larger bands would be used to offer more participatory art related activities.

Other Business:

Commissioner Killen announced he will be submitting a plan for the development of the Metlox site, the deadline is Friday, May 15th. He added there might be a conflict of interest if his plan were accepted, but felt able to resolve the situation if necessary.

There was no further business discussed and the meeting was adjourned at 7:25pm. to the next meeting scheduled for June 9, 1998.
