Shelton, Elijah Franklin and Elizabeth Lynn

 

The words of my grandfather, Rufus Sigler, spoken to my father in my presence were "Robert, they are all buried there (at Chalybeate), all the way back.  Many of them do not have stones.  Let the dead bury the dead."

I have no record of a stone in the Chalybeate Cemetery for either Elijah Shelton or his wife Elizabeth.  However, two of their children have stones in the Chalybeate Cemetery.  There is also a record of four stones for the Lynn family.  According to the notes that my father, Robert Henry Sigler left me on a 7.5 minute USGS quadrangle map of the Chalybeate area, the home of "Oldest Elijah Shelton" was located about 3000 feet from the Chalybeate Cemetery.  A new modern house now exists at or near the location of the home of the oldest Elijah Shelton.  I have checked the four Webster County cemetery books published by the Webster County Historical Society and have found no entry for this Elijah Shelton.  I am therefore going to list the descendants of Elijah Shelton and Elizabeth Lynn on this page as descendants of Chalybeate until someone proves that they are not buried in the Chalybeate Cemetery.

The information below on the descendants of Elijah Franklin Shelton and Elizabeth Lynn was sent to me by Todd Shelton (United States Navy - career).  Todd has requested that credit for this information also be given to Cousins Meridith Gibson and Tina Hall.  Both the Gibson and Hall families have stones in the Chalybeate Cemetery.
