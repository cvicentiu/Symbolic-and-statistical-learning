IN MY SIGHTS — THE MEMOIR OF A P-40 ACE, by Col. James B. Morehead, Ret.

IN MY SIGHTS — THE MEMOIR OF A P-40 ACE, by Col. James B. Morehead, Ret.

PRESIDO PRESS, 1997; 288 pgs., $24.95 — ISBN 089141634X.

    In My Sights is a WWII Army Air Corps pilot's memoirs of his experiences and action in the Pacific, European and Korean theaters. James Morehead relates how his early days growing up on a farm during the tough depression days in Oklahoma prepared him for service in the Army Air Corps. There are interesting descriptions of basic training and transport to combat areas.
    Col. Morehead describes his combat flying in the Curtiss P-40 Warhawk against the much superior Japanese Mitsubishi Zero. The V-12 liquid-cooled Allison engine Warhawk was so outclassed by the Zero it is a wonder any airman survived an encounter with the Nakajima radial engine Zero. Col. Morehead survived by careful tactics and shooting skills he learned hunting in Oklahoma.
    Col. Morehead flew missions in the Australian-Darwin-New Guinea area where he was credited with shooting down four Betty bombers and three Zeros and was awarded two Distinguished Service Crosses, the Silver Star, two Distinguished Flying Crosses and a personal commendation from General Marshall.
    In 1943 he was transferred stateside for a year's assignment on the West Coast. The 333d F.G. was in Van Nuys, CA, and Col, Morehead describes how replacement pilot training took place while he was assigned there. In Feb. 1944 he was reassigned to the "I" F.G. in Foggia, Italy, flying Lockheed P-38 Lightnings. These fighters were equipped with two Allison engines but were turbo super-charged giving them performance at altitude. The missions were all dangerous and some very bloody.
    In 1944 the assignment was a staff job in IR-38 replacement training at Santa Rosa, CA, and then to Chico AAFB in California, where B-29s and the new Bell P-59 jet operated. Morehead was discharged in 1945 and in July 1946 reentered the Air Corps with the temporary rank of lieutenant colonel flying P-51s and B-26s. Then the flying got interesting with F-80 shooting stars and jet tactical training at Nellis AFB, Las Vegas flying F-80s and F-86s before being sent to South Korea as a replacement pilot fighting the Chinese and North Koreans. The story ends with duty at the Pentagon and retirement. In My Sights is a story of 27 years of flying, fighting and hunting and is an interesting insight of one man's military career.
