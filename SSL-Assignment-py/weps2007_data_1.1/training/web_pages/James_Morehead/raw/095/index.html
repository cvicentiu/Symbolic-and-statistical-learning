Notes for CAPT. CHARLES MOREHEAD:
[keren1.FTW]

Lived in Farquier County, Virginia. He was a Captain in the Revolution. His descendants moved to Kentucky in 1807.

From Virginia Heraldica:
The first of this family was Charles Morehead, a Scottish gentleman, who settled in what is now Prince William county, in the early part of the eighthteenth century. He is known to have had at least one son, John Morehead, whose will was probated in Fauquier county, 24 Oct., 1768, in which he mentions sons Charles, Joseph, John, Alexander, William, Samuel and Presley Morehead, and daughters, Mary Lawrence and Elizabeth Brixtraw. His son, Charles Morehead, a captain in the Revolution, died in Leeds Parish, Fauquier county, in 1783. His will mentions, son Turner, son Charles, sons Armistead, James and Presley; daughter Kerrenhappuch Morehead, and wife Mary. Joseph Morehead, grandson of Charles, the immigrant, moved to North Carolina, and married Elizabeth Turner, the daughter of James and Kerrenhappuch Turner of Maryland, whose sons and grandsons were with General Greene in the Revolution. Another daughter, Mary Turner, married Charles, the brother of Joseph Morehead, and left offspring in the West, of these, Governor Charles S. Morehead of Kentucky, and his cousin, Governor James Turner Morehead of the same State, have been eminent statesmen, serving not only as Governor, but also in the Senate of the U. S. from that State. The North Carolina branch has also produced the late Governor John M. Morehead, and his brother, Hon. James Turner Morehead, who, at one time, represented his District in Congress. Another descendant in the West is the Hon. Charles R. Morehead, some time Mayor of El Paso, Texas, who served with gallantry in the Mexican War.

From History of the Culpeper County Normans:
Their son Charles Morehead, m. Margaret Slaughter, went to Kentucky and served in the legislature. His son, Charles Slaughter Morehead, was Governor of Kentucky and four years in U. S. Senate. Armistead Morehead's son, James Turner Morehead, was also Governor of Kentucky and U. S. Senator. A great-grandson of Charles and Mary Turner Morehead was General Simon Bolivar Buckner, U. S. A.

Notes for JOSEPH MOREHEAD, SR.:
[keren1.FTW]

From Morehead Family:
Joseph Morehead son of John Morehead of Northumberland and Farquair counties, Virginia received a tract in Halifax County, Virginia. He married Elizabeth Turner and they raised a large family in Halifax county, Virginia. Sometime after 1766 they moved to North Carolina and settled in Richmond County. One report is in Steele's Township, on the River Road near Stanback's Ferry.

Many of the children married locally and one went to Georgia, Elizabeth who married a Redman. Nancy married David Turner (Daniel Thomas?), Sarah a Cathrell (spelling?), Joseph Miss Jennings, Mary the widower Thomas Stanback and John married Obedience Motley. His descendants moved from Virginia to North Carolina.

Land grants have been found in the Secretary of State's office, Raleigh, N.C., as follows:
Joseph Morehead for 100 acres of land on Naked Creek, Richmond County - Entered December 2, 1799, Entry No. 34. Another grant for 25 acres in Richmond County, Book 115, p. 371. Another for 100 acres. Entry 347 book 115 p. 368. Entered September 17, 1802. And another for 68 acres "front of his Hill tract." June 7, 1799, book 104, p. 282. (Some of these may be Joseph Morehead, Jr., land grants).

No deeds of transfer are found in the county records. However, Joseph Morehead's will gives to Charles land on Naked Creek and to Mary and Nancy the east end of the Hill land, until they marry, and then to Charles and his heirs.

The will of Joseph Morehead is found in Richmond County Will Book No. 1 page 107 and is dated July 11, 1806.

There is a will recorded in Bladen County, North Carolina, of a James Morehead April 4, 1759. Sons, William and James; daughters, Jane and Sarah. Wife and Alexander McCaskey executors. J. Burgwin, Clerk Court.

  vi.   CAPT. JAMES MOREHEAD60,61,62, b. 1750, Virginia62; d. 181562.
  Notes for CAPT. JAMES MOREHEAD:
[keren1.FTW]

From Morehead Family:
James Morehead the son of Joseph and Elizabeth Turner Morehead was born 1750. He was appointed a Lieutenant in Captain Lyttle's company of the Tenth North Carolina Continentals and served with General Sumner at the Battle of Stono (Ferry) near Charleston, (S.C.) and in the Battle of Elizabethtown (Bladen County) N.C. 1781. "Biographical History of North Carolina" by Capt. S. A. Ashe, Charles Van Noppen, publisher, Greensboro, N.C.

The Battle of Stono was fought June 20, 1779. General Prevost in command of the British forces after falling back from Charleston, left a force under Lt. Col. Maitland at Stono Ferry on John's Island a few miles west of Charleston. General Lincoln in command of the American Forces made the main attack on the British at Stono Ferry, while Gen. Moultrie made an attack from James Island. After a fierce battle the Americans withdrew and the British gave up their positions withdrawing toward Savannah within three days. The British lost three officers and twenty-three privates with ten officers and ninety-five privates wounded. The Americans lost five officers and thirty-five privates with nineteen officers and one hundred and twenty privates wounded. ("Pictorial History of America" by John Frost. Vol. I p. 223-225. Williams A. Leary and Company Phila. 1850).

Captain Morehead participated in the Battle of Elizabethtown which took place August 29, 1781.

"Morehead commanded the nine months' men sent to the South." ("Chronicles of the Cape Fear River" by James Sprunt p. 118). (Edwards and Broughton Printing Company, Raleigh, N.C., 1916).

"Here a force of some 300 Tories who controlled the general territory were defeated in a surprise attack and driven into a deep ravine. Since called the Tory Hole by a small force of Americans which dispirit the Tories, so they never after were so troublesome. (Sprunt p. 117).

James Morehead received a state land grant No. 1249 Richmond County, N.C. for 25 acres, grand 1232, January 1, 1800. Entry 173. Entered 1787, Book 109 p. 370. He also received a Military Grant R. 91 p. 469 No. 2794, File 639.

The Will of James Morehead is found in Richmond County Will Book 1, page 170. Made January 25th, 1813, proven June term 1815.

Estate of James Morehead is listed in the Inventory Book p. 54 Richmond County, N.C. Made September 22, 1815. Total value $5,310.57.
