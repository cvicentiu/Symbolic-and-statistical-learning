James Jr. came from a long line of military men, and this influenced his thinking throughout his life.  His father graduated from Annapolis, resigned his commission, and received an appointment from President Grant to the Army. His grandfather, Major John Henry Gore, a brilliant and promising officer with the 4th Infantry, was brevetted twice during the Mexican War and had his life cut short by cholera. His great-grandfather, W.I. Newton, was a Major with the U.S. 2nd Dragoons; and his great-great-grandfather, Walker Keith Armistead, was a Brevet Brigadier-General, who graduated from West Point.
 
After serving as Assistant Lighthouse keeper under his father at The Farallones and Alcatraz and succeeding his father as Head Lighthouse keeper at Alcatraz in the late 1890’s, James enlisted in the Army during the Spanish-American War and was sent to the Philippines. When he returned home, he met and married Stella Celina Baccus.
 
In 1918, when the U.S. entered World War I, he applied for an officer’s commission as Major in the Quartermaster’s Corps. His application was being processed when the war ended; this was a great disappointment to him, as he was proud of his military heritage and wished to serve his country, as his forefathers had.
