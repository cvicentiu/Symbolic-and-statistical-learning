Handheld and Wireless Computing Update

Darwin J. Liao, MD, MPH

Online Program

  
   

12:30 p.m. - 2:00 p.m.
Electronic Medical Records Best Features and Practices
JC Noreika, MD, MBA, Managing Partner, Eye Care of Medina, Inc.
Jim Messier, VP, Sales and Marketing, Medflow, Inc.

A physician end user and an industry specialist explore the latest issues and best practices in selecting, implementing, and maintaining an EMR system. In particular, productivity enhancing features such as patient tracking, data management, electronic prescribing, and intra-office instant messaging will be discussed. Audience questions are encouraged.

2:30 p.m. - 3:30 p.m.
Ophthalmology Resources on the Go
H. Jay Wisnicki, MD, Director, The New York Eye & Ear Infirmary Dept. of Ophthalmology at Beth Israel Medical Center

A range of useful ophthalmology resources are available on the Internet, if you just know where to look. This session tours a number of new and useful Web sites and search tools to help you efficiently find the resources you need. The presenter will also examine his favorite mobile devices and technologies to keep information with you on the go.

4:00 p.m. - 5:00 p.m.
Voice Technologies to Benefit Your Practice and Patients
JC Noreika, MD, MBA, Managing Partner, Eye Care of Medina, Inc.

This session will examine how digital telephony using the Internet (VOIP) and new voice recognition technologies can be employed to save money, build your practice, and enhance patient satisfaction. Learn how other large and small businesses have integrated and benefited from these technologies and what advances we can expect in the near future.

Sunday, Nov. 12

9:00 a.m. - 10:00 a.m.
Microsoft PowerPoint Advanced: Ask the Expert  (1,069k)
Jude Brown-Bellamy, Senior Instructor, Digital Acumen, Inc.

For those already familiar with the basics, this interactive session takes an in-depth look at how to effectively apply PowerPoint's robust data linking to spreadsheets, video, and Adobe PDFs. Our expert will also field “how-to” questions from the audience.

10:30 a.m. - Noon
Wi-Fi and Broadband Security and Trends (122k)
Jonathon Santilli, Senior Network Engineer, Digital Acumen, Inc.

Viruses, spyware, and unwanted visitors are constant threats to anyone with a high-speed, “always on” wireless Internet connection. This session describes in detail the tools and software settings you should use to keep your network safe and secure from damaging and costly crashes or data theft. Particular emphasis is placed on best practices for securing wireless networks at home and at the office, as well as on the performance and security benefits of new wireless standards.

12:30 p.m. - 2:00 p.m.
Desktop Video Editing, Step by Step (726k)
Jude Brown-Bellamy, Senior Instructor, Digital Acumen, Inc.

New, inexpensive, but powerful tools make it possible to edit video and package it for Web distribution or burn it to DVD. Learn how to acquire video from a digital camcorder and enhance it with professional visual effects and titles.

2:30 p.m. - 3:30 p.m.
The Electronic Office Is Here: Automation of Clinical Workflow, From Patient Registration to Billing
Lloyd Hildebrand, MD, Chair, Academy Medical Information Technology Committee
Linda Wedemeyer, MD, Veterans Health Administration
Mark Horton, MD, Indian Health Service

Electronic health records, standards for communication, and "plug and play" abilities allow automation of tasks and accurate communication of patient information, from registration to diagnostic testing, billing, and viewing diagnostic test results. The session will describe how the automation of workflow works from a clinical perspective, highlighting the benefits and time savings for ophthalmologists. (IHE)

4:00 p.m. - 5:00 p.m.
Maximizing the Neuro-Ophthalmology Virtual Education Library (82k)
Nancy Lombardo, Systems Librarian, Eccles Health Sciences Library, University of Utah

Thousands of images, case videos, lectures, and presentations are available free for educational use through the Neuro-Ophthalmology Virtual Education Library (NOVEL). The presentation will provide an overview of the collection and will demonstrate how to download the materials for educational purposes and use in your own teaching.

Monday, Nov. 13

9:30 a.m. - 10:30 a.m.
Consumer Digital Imaging Tips (2,300k)
Jude Brown-Bellamy, Senior Instructor, Digital Acumen, Inc.

Now that you have a digital camera, what are you going to do with all those images? This session examines useful tips for acquiring and manipulating digital images, reviews desirable hardware and software features, and explains file types and optimal resolutions for different output needs, such as those for Web, print, or PowerPoint.

11:00 a.m. - Noon
Electronic Health Records Connecting With Imaging Devices: All the Patient Data at One Workstation
Donald Van Syckle, DVS Consulting, Inc.
Peter Kuzmak, U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs
Allen Brewer, PhD, Washington National Eye Center

Recent developments and forces are accelerating the adoption of electronic health records, particularly by the federal government. IHE, Integrating the Health Care Enterprise, fosters the connection of electronic health records with diagnostic imaging devices and other instruments so all the data and images can be displayed in one place. The session will describe the IHE Eye Care process, and why it makes sense from a clinical, IT, and business perspective. (IHE)

Exploring Microsoft Windows Vista (24k)
Jude Brown-Bellamy, Senior Instructor, Digital Acumen, Inc.

Windows Vista, Microsoft's upcoming next-generation desktop operating system, aims to greatly improve reliability, security, and the end user experience. See it in action as we examine its new features and help separate its real benefits from the marketing hype.

Tuesday, Nov. 14

9:30 a.m. - 10:30 a.m.
Creating Effective Electronic Documents With Adobe Acrobat (125k)
Jude Brown-Bellamy, Senior Instructor, Digital Acumen, Inc.

This session will show you how to convert forms and patient education content into compact, interactive, cross-platform files with Adobe's Portable Document Format (PDF) to distribute by e-mail or from the Web.  

11:00 a.m. - Noon
Electronic Health Records: Electronic Workflow to Reduce Errors, Reconcile Billing and Incorporate Legacy Systems
Donald Van Syckle, DVS Consulting, Inc.
Jim Riggi, Medflow, Inc.
Michael Schmidt, Carl Zeiss Meditec, Inc.

The implementation of electronic health records has made it possible to envision full electronic workflow, eliminating the need for paper, film, or removable media to transfer information and making it possible to have all the data available, anytime and anywhere. The automated exchange of information minimizes transcription errors and efficiently sends billing information for all procedures performed. This session will describe the different elements of IHE, Integrating the Health Care Enterprise, and will explain how IHE facilitates the transfer of health information within the different systems in an ophthalmologist's practice. (IHE)
