Families salute Georgian battlers

You can join the Georgia Supporters' Club here, or read on for more information...

The Browns sign on to support Georgia (from left): Jessie, Zoe, Jude (mother), Georgia and Amy.
Entire families are among the hundreds of people signing up eagerly to join the Georgian Supporters' Club, which the POST has arranged as part of the Rugby World Cup round being held at Subiaco Oval.

This week, the POST sent two supporters' kits to a couple in Canada as well as to supporters in Hong Kong, NSW, Queensland and South Australia.

And five members of the Brown family, of West Perth, arrived as a group sporting their Georgia on My Mind caps.

"We were really taken with the idea of supporting the children in hospital in Georgia and at PMH," said Jude Brown, mother of four daughters.

"And we're all for the underdogs. We wanted to get involved in the Rugby World Cup. Australia is not playing here, so we're backing Georgia."

The Georgian players are truly underdogs for the Cup series - bookmakers quoted the team at 20,000:1 this week to win the title, and they are rated 17th in the world rankings, up from 18th.

The unpaid team members from the economically-wrecked former Soviet state on the Black Sea are looking forward to arriving here on October 1, "poor but proud".

David Reble and Malaka Hendela, from Ottawa, signed in on the POST website.

"We were looking at the World Cup online and found your supporters' club," David said.

"We can't be in Australia for the festivities, but joining the club let us support the Georgian team and become involved in the event in a more connected way than just watching the games on TV.

"Best of luck to your Supporters' Club and the Georgian Lelos."

You can join the Supporters' Club for $20, $10 of which will help children in hospitals here and in Georgia (see letter page 2).

Each Supporters' Club member gets a cap with the Georgia colours, a certificate and their name placed in a draw for 10 sets of two free seats at the Georgia v England game at Subiaco Oval on Sunday, October 12.

Australian Rugby Union has weighed in with more support for the club, donating a set of tickets for all five games, including the sold-out England v South Africa, whose tickets are being offered by scalpers for $5000.

These will be auctioned separately by the Princess Margaret Hospital Foundation. Watch the POST for details.

Fun supporters' functions have been organised for each Wednesday evening, starting on September 17, at Clancy's Fish Pub in the Subiaco Village complex (the site of the old Henry Africa's tavern, opposite Waldecks) and on match day.

All Georgian supporters and their friends are welcome to the sunshine hour from 6 to 7pm, and memberships can be obtained on the night.

The first night will be to meet fellow Georgia supporters and practise the war chant "Lelo, Lelo Sakartvelo".

The Georgia team is nicknamed "Lelos, sons of the sun" because of the sunburst design on their logo.

The chant urges the team to score a try.

Most of the Georgian squad of 40 play professional rugby in France.

Last weekend, Georgia played Italy in Asti in Italy - Italy won 32:23 after two of the Georgians were "sin binned".

The Georgian team includes a TV celebrity, prop Levan Tsabadze, who competed in the Greco-Roman wrestling in the 2000 Olympics in Sydney.

Big Levan is known in Georgia by the nickname Leoncio after a burly mansion-keeper from a Brazilian TV soap.

* Georgia - birthplace of wine, the golden fleece and the Caucasian race, see page XX
