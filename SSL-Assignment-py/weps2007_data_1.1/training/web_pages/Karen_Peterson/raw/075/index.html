Sunday, January 28, 2007

Sen. Karen Peterson Supports HB 4

I just talked to Sen. Karen Peterson about HB 4 going down to defeat in the Delaware House of Representatives. HB 4 would have required that the annual Budget Bill be released 3 days before the end of legislative session and the Bond Bill 2 days before the end of the legislative session. She said she was “disappointed” to see HB 4 defeated.

When asked if she felt that HB 4 in any way jeopardized her FOIA bill, SB 4, she said “Not at all. They are different bills.” She explained that HB 4 isn’t really a FOIA bill because HB 4 doesn’t provide information to the public that the people of Delaware don’t get anyhow eventually. HB 4 is only addresses “the timeliness of the budget and bond bills.” But timeliness is important, she said, because there have been times when she has been given the lengthy bond bill literally 5 minutes before it is voted on. There is no way the bill can reviewed in five minutes, she said. Sen. Peterson claimed she co-sponsored an identical during the last legislative session. According to Senator Peterson, Delaware needs both HB 4 and SB 4 and she would love to co-sponsor HB 4 if it comes back up for a vote.

What, then, are we to make of John Kowalko’s following assertion?

At no time was my decision prompted by any decision to maintain a "Democratic Caucus" unified front. I considered HB4 on its own merits and lack thereof. I am a co-sponsor and staunch supporter of Sen. Karen Peterson's open government, FOIA bill and will continue to support that initiative as the only legitimate attempt offered to date that addresses FOIA and open government responsibilities of the General Assembly. (link)

Let’s see if we got this right. Kowalko voted against HB 4 to protect Senator Peterson’s bill when she wanted HB 4 to pass and thought it in no way threatened her bill! Hopefully, no one will die laughing from the absolute absurdity of Kowalko’s claim.

If Kowalko had been really concerned about the effect HB 4 would have on Peterson’s bill, then why didn’t he call her up and ask her? Did he know that she might have said to him, “Don’t do me any favors, John. Support HB 4.” It seems that Kowalko voted against HB 4 not because of its so-called impact on SB 4 but because he got pressure from within the Democratic caucus and he compromised the public interest for the sake of inner-caucus politics. Let’s hope that he will now publicly disavow his vote against HB 4 and publicly state that he will vote for HB 4 when it is reintroduced.

Thursday, January 25, 2007

Open letter from John Kowalko regarding HB4

I don’t intend to use various sites as an apologist for my decisions in Dover but I feel that I owe my most vocal and avid supporters the respect of an explanation regarding my vote on Greg Lavelle’s HB4 during Wednesday’s session.

At no time was my decision prompted by any decision to maintain a “Democratic Caucus” unified front. I considered HB4 on its own merits and lack thereof. I am a co-sponsor and staunch supporter of Sen. Karen Peterson’s open government, FOIA bill and will continue to support that initiative as the only legitimate attempt offered to date that addresses FOIA and open government responsibilities of the General Assembly. Despite Rep. Lavelle’s “outrage”, I believe his bill does not address open government concerns in a responsible way and should not be construed as such. HB4 does not approach “halfway there” nor “baby steps” in the direction of open, accountable government reform. In fact he should realize that we are obligated, as responsible grownup public servants, to take “big-boy” steps toward reform. Political posturing is not the path we should be walking. Every politically motivated misadventure taken that falsely purports to be a step in the “right” direction risks giving an excuse to those who procrastinate and take advantage of a false sense of accomplishment in the public’s mind. If Rep. Lavelle chooses to sign on to Sen. Peterson’s open government initiative, which would actually permit public scrutiny of Budget, Bond-Bill and Epilogue discussions, I would gladly welcome further discussion with him on the merits of his proposal.

I end this dialogue with an apology to anyone who may have misconstrued my motives in opposing HB4 as anything other than a thoughtfully arrived at decision to do what I felt was right.

Respectfully,
John Kowalko

Sign the Petition to Save HB 4

A good bill that would have given the people of Delaware more transparency in Delaware's government went down to a heartbreaking defeat yesterday in the Delaware House of Representatives.

House Bill 4 made no outrageous proposals. It did not slip into its language any provision for a special interest, unless more transparency in the Delaware Legislature is a "special interest." It simply stated that the Budget bill be released 5 days prior to the end of the legislative session and and the Bond bill be released 3 days prior to the end of the legislative session. This bill, if it had become law, would have had the following immediate good effects:

1. It would allow YOU Delaware citizen to go over the bills prior to the vote on them so that you could contact your legislators and voice your view about the bills.

2. It would allow Delaware legislators an opportunity to study the bills in greater depth before they cast a vote on them. As it is now, Delaware legislators can get these bills immediately before the vote on them. Legislators can end up voting to spend your tax dollars on matters delineated in a bill THEY HAVE NEVER READ. How is that responsible governance?

3. It would help to make the Delaware Legislature more disciplined in getting these bills (arguably the most important bills enacted every year) done earlier, instead of employing their usual practice of waiting till the last minute.

Although HB 4 was defeated, it can be resurrected during this legislative session again and voted on once more. Linked here is an online petition that many Delawareans have already signed asking that HB 4 be brought up for a vote once more.

In spite of what some party cultists might say, supporting HB 4 and signing this petition isn't about partisanship. It's about good citizenship. Please sign the petition.

“NO” VOTERS ASKED TO RECONSIDER ROLE
IN DEFEATING
GOOD PUBLIC POLICY BILL


Common Cause of Delaware is urging all members of the House Democratic caucus, as well as 3 House Republican representatives, to reconsider their "No" vote on House Bill No. 4.

Common Cause has asked, in a letter, those representatives voting “No” to vote to bring H.B. 4 up for reconsideration and another vote by the House of Representatives.

House Bill 4 would require that the General Assembly Budget Bill be introduced no later than five legislative days prior to June 30, the end of the legislative session, and the Bond Bill no later than three legislative days prior to June 30.

Too often these important money bills are not available for close scrutiny by legislators and the public before the final vote.

Common Cause believes that passage of H.B. 4 is not only in the public interest but also, and more especially, in the interest of all members of the General Assembly who often vote on these important bills before having the opportunity to review the bills.

The haste with which the Bond and Budget bills are presented for voting has led to the public perception that behind the scenes deals are the reason for the haste with which the bills are presented to the General Assembly and the public.

Common Cause believes H.B. 4 represents good public policy.
