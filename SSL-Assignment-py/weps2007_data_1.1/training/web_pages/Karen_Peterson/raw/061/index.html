New Technologies Driving Supply Chain Management

7 February 2003



Tremendous increases in efficiency can be achieved by improving supply chain management: better choice of goods, more competitive prices, faster execution and better resource allocation.

Obviously ideas like these contributed to the meteoric rise of the subject in 1999. And then people realized that the problems were actually far more complex than anticipated. In "Supply Chain Management: Evolving Beyond Linear Interactions" Karen Peterson and her team show how improvements in supply chain management are built on three pillars:
  • Managing many kinds of data (in particular, better information awareness)
  • Exchanging data to ease integration with the processes of trading partners
  • Controlling processes within the organization
