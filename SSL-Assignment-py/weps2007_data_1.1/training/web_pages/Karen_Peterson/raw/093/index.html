Daycare Provider Charged In Baby's Death
It happened Friday night at the Onalaska home of daycare provider Karen Peterson.

The 15 month old died after suffocating in her crib, when her neck got pinned against the railing.

Peterson says she had checked on the girl just an hour before.

One after another, safety concerns at Peterson's in-home daycare are brought to light. From garbage scattered through the house, to Peterson being under the influence while watching other people's children.

"The more we are digging into this, the more information we are finding which does not bear well for the defendant," says Deputy District Attorney Loralee Clark.

On Friday evening, Karen Peterson says she put a 15 month old down for a nap. She was one of seven children being cared for that night.

Two hours later, peterson found her in her crib without a pulse. Onalaska police officers arrived at the home, and tried to save the girl.

"Did provide CPR upon arrival but they were unsuccessful and this was a tragic loss," says Onalaska Police Chief Randy Williams.

Peterson had been drinking, and a test showed her blood alcohol level at .089. The complaint states police noticed that the defendant seemed unsteady on her feet. Peterson said she had a couple drinks but it was friday so she was trying to relax.

A search of the house showed numerous signs of neglect. According to the criminal complaint, in the bedroom where the infant was, an officer saw a few empty vodka bottles on the floor and there were stains on the carpet.

Officers describe the home as filthy, adding the kitchen counters and island were completely covered with dirty dishes, food and another bottle of cleaner. they even saw fruit flies in the kitchen and a butcher knife handle hanging over the edge of the counter.

But despite the condition of the house, it was in the basement where the girl died. Prosecutors may never know exactly what happened. Peterson has changed her story about the events of that night.

If convicted of causing the girls death by neglect, she faces up to 25 years in prison.

In court this afternoon, the D.A.'s office said Peterson has had a problem with alcohol. She'll be back in court on December 13th.
