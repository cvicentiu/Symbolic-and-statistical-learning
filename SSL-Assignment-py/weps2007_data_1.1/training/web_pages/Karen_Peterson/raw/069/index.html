SIGNIFICANT & HISTORICAL VERDICTS AS LEAD TRIAL COUNSEL

Karen Peterson and Jeffrey Peterson v. Hill Brothers Chemical Corp. (2002) (represented a 42 year old plaintiff in one of California's largest verdicts for 2002 at $20+ million asbestos case). Alameda County Superior Court 2001-031817. This verdict was the largest net asbestos verdict ever awarded to a plaintiff in an asbestos case in California's history.

On June 4, 2002, after six weeks of trial, an Alameda County Superior Court jury returned a verdict in excess of $20 million for 42 year old Karen and Jeffrey Peterson. Total responsibility (100%) for plaintiff's mesothelioma cancer was assessed against Hill Brothers Chemical Company. The jury found that defendant acted with malice, oppression or fraud, permitting the action to proceed to a punitive damage phase. Defendant settled the action at trial for $19.5 million for a waiver by plaintiffs of punitive damages. Following Karen Peterson's death in 2002, Ms. Farrise brought a wrongful death suit on behalf of Karen's husband, Jeffrey Peterson, and her 8 year old two twin boys. The wrongful death case was settled.

Wimberly v. Yellow Cab (1997), $25 million (represented plaintiff in head injury case) Alameda County Superior Court 749417-2. In one of Ms. Farrise's first solo trials, a Superior Court Judge awarded damages of $25 million to her client, who had sustained severe closed head injuries following an automobile collision. At the time of the collision Mr. Wimberly was 21 years old. Following the company defendant's bankruptcy, Ms. Farrise successfully pursued a bad faith insurance rights assigned to her client which resulted in a confidential multi-million dollars settlement for the plaintiff.

Franklin v. USX Corporation (2000), $6.5 million (represented plaintiff in asbestos cancer case) Alameda County Superior Court 816407-0. Simona Farrise made United States asbestos litigation history with a verdict of $6,500,000 in an asbestos cancer case that arose after childhood household exposure to asbestos - a so-called 2nd hand asbestos case.

Plaintiff Jeanette Franklin was a little girl in the 1940s when both of her parents worked at USX Corporation's Western Pipe & Steel shipyard in South San Francisco. Her parents unknowingly carried deadly asbestos fibers home on their clothing, and their young children were exposed. In March 1999, Jeanette Franklin was diagnosed with mesothelioma, an asbestos-caused cancer. By February 2000, the case was settled with almost all defendants except USX Corporation. USX refused to offer even $1. The case was tried over six weeks to a jury who awarded plaintiffs $6.5 million. The verdict was overturned on an appeal due to an issue of corporate successorship which Ms. Farrise did not handle.
