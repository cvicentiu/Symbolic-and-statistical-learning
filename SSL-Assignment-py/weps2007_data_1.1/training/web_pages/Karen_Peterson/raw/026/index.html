Web Site Design

 

·  Who are the intended audiences of your site? (e.g., parents, students, colleagues) Your site should include at least two different audiences.

The audience for my web site will be for other teachers, students, and parents.

·  For each of these audiences, what do you want to communicate? (e.g., project or assignment information, introduction to you as a teacher, important Web sites) What do you want them to learn? (e.g., your values as a teacher, course requirements)

For parents/students: I want parents to go to the web site to get an overview of what is happening in class, and be able to view any assignments.  The site will include helpful links for parents to help their students with assignments.  I’m not going to provide parents with a day to day assingment list, as this would be too difficult to keep up-to-date.  Instead, I’m going to give parents a general overviews of the concepts and units we are studying.  At this site parents and students will also be able to get an overview of me as a teacher at my “About Mrs. Peterson page.” Also at the site parents will be able to view the current Rayder TV broadcast, and view any current newspapers or articles.  Not only will the site keep parents up on what we’re doing in class, it will also showcase students assignments.

 

Other teachers: I think this site will be helpful for teachers as they assist students during our seminar time.  They will be able to get assignments for students and help them with missing work.
