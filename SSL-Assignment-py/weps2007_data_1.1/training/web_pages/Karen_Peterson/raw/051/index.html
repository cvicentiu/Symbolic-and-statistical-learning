Head coach Karen Peterson

Karen Peterson will begin her third season as head coach of the Grace College volleyball program with high expectations following a dramatic turn-around for the team a year ago. After the team went 4-23 in her first season at the helm, the program improved to 18-16 in 2004 with just one senior on the squad.

Peterson has a 221-264 career record, surpassing the 200 win plateau during the 2003 season. She spent six years as head coach at The Master's College, advancing to the NAIA Regional Tournament in 1998 and being named Independent Conference Coach of the Year three times. The team gained its first national ranking in school history in 1999 and finished third at the NCCAA National Tournament in 2002.

Peterson has quickly rebuilt the strong tradition of the Grace volleyball program. The Lancers won 54 games in 1994 which ranks as one of the greatest seasons in NAIA history. As well, the squad went 44-5 in 1995 and won the NCCAA National Championship that year. The team also won the NAIA Regional Championship in 2001, advancing to the NAIA National Championships for the first time in school history. With last year's win increase, the program has now finished .500 or better in nine of the past 11 seasons.

Before coaching at The Master's, Peterson held positions at Missouri Western State College and Dodge City Community College. Peterson is also the assistant athletic director at Grace.

Assistant Kelly Manes

The Grace College athletic department has announced the hiring of Kelly Manes as assistant volleyball coach for the 2006-07 season. Manes will begin in this role as a very familiar member of the Grace College athletic department.

Manes played volleyball and softball at Grace College, graduating in 2000 with a business administration and accounting major. She was then an assistant coach for the softball program from 2002-05. Manes will work under head volleyball coach Karen Peterson.

"I am excited to continue the improvement that Coach Peterson began with the program a few years ago," Manes said. "I look forward to getting to know the girls and aim to make it a positive and enjoyable experience for them."

Manes will step into a program that has made marked improvement under Peterson. In 2003, her first year at the helm of the program, the Lancers went 4-23 overall and 0-7 in the Mid-Central Conference. However, the volleyball team had the largest win improvement in the region in 2004, finishing 18-16 overall and 4-4 in the Mid-Central Conference. In 2005, the program finished 17-16 overall and 5-3 in the MCC, placing third during the regular season and hosting a conference tournament match for the first time in four years.

"I am very excited to work with Kelly next season," Peterson said. "She has the knowledge and maturity that will be a great asset for the team, and her prior coaching experience will be beneficial."

Manes grew up in Yorkton, Saskatchewan, Canada. She works full-time at Grace as the staff accountant and is married to Andy, a 2000 Grace graduate who played baseball for the Lancers.
