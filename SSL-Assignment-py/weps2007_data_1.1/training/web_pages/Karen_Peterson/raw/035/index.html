Department of Psychiatry

LtCol Karen M. Peterson, USAF, MC

Dr. Peterson is assigned to the Department of Psychiatry, USUHS as an Assistant Professor.  Additionally, she is also a Scientist associated with the Center for the Study of Traumatic Stress.  Dr. Peterson is board certified by the American Board of Psychiatry and Neurology, is licensed in both the state of Pennsylvania and California and is a general member of the American Psychiatric Association.  Her most recent experience has been as Director, Inpatient Psychiatric Services of the 60th Medical Group, Travis AFB, California.  Dr. Peterson’s deployment experience includes a rotation to Camp Arifjan, Kuwait as the Commander, 2nd Combat Operational Stress Team in support of Operation Iraqi Freedom.  Additionally, she was re-deployed from Kuwait to Kandahar Airfield, Afghanistan and served as the psychiatrist to the Combat Stress Team.  Dr. Peterson is particularly interested in the treatment of depression and launched “Depression Management Screening Day” while assigned to Yokota AB, Japan.  She has always had a keen interest in educating others and has had the opportunity to teach primary care and family practice physicians throughout her career.  During her deployment, Dr. Peterson had the opportunity to teach Afghani medical students.  Recognized as an expert, Dr. Peterson has given forensic testimony on many occasions throughout her career.  Dr. Peterson graduated with high honors from University of California, Santa Barbara (Go Gauchos!) in 1985 with a Bachelor of Arts in Biologic Sciences.  A career in medicine was always the plan, however Dr. Peterson took the next several years supporting her husband and enjoying being mother to their son, Kyle before applying to the Uniformed Services University of Health Sciences.  Dr. Peterson graduated from USUHS in May 1993.  She chose to enter into the Transitional Internship at Malcom Grow Medical Center in order to expand her general medical training before entering specialty training.  Dr Peterson served as Chief Resident for her internship class and graduated in July 1994. From there she continued to Wilford Hall Medical Center where she again earned the distinction of Chief Resident and completed Psychiatry Residency in July 1997.
