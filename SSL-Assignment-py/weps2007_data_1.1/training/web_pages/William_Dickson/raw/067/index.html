Julia S. Becmer
WATERFORD - Julia S. Karwiel Becmer, 92, of Davis Avenue in Waterford, died June 7, 2005, at St. Mary's Hospital in Troy, embraced by her loving children.
Born in Waterford, she was the daughter of the late Anthony and Sophie Naumiec Karwiel and wife of the late William Edward Becmer.
Julia received her education in Maplewood schools and was a lifelong Capital District area resident.
A devoted mother and homemaker, she was employed with various textile mills in the area and also at the former Behr Manning. She retired from Siena College as a food service employee.
Julia was a gifted gardener and an accomplished cook, specializing in desserts. She was also an avid reader.
Julia was a member of the Cohoes Senior Citizens and a communicant of St. Michael's Church in Cohoes, where she was a life member of the Ladies Rosary Society and a member of the former Sister- Parent Association of St. Michael's School.
She is survived by her devoted children, Sister Donna Mary Ann Becmer, Mercedarian Missionary of Rome, Italy, and Gregory William Becmer of Waterford; her grandsons, Nicholas Edward and Jonathan Gregory Becmer; her siblings, Josephine Buszuwski of East Greenbush and Joseph Karwiel of Watervliet; and many nieces and nephews. She was predeceased by her sisters Stasia Kosek, Frances Karwiel and Jadwiga Buniak and her brothers John and Louis Karwiel.
Funeral from the Fitzgerald Funeral Home, Ltd., 105 Vliet Blvd., Cohoes, Saturday morning at 9 with a Mass of Christian Burial at St. Michael's Church at 9:30. Interment St. Michael's Cemetery in Waterford.
Relatives and friends are invited and may call at the funeral home Friday evening from 5-8.
Those wishing to remember Julia may make contributions in her memory to St. Michael's Church, 36 Page Ave., Cohoes, NY 12047.

Margaret Clermont
SCHENECTADY - Margaret Mahar Clermont, 93, passed away Wednesday, June 8, 2005, at St. Clare's Hospital in Schenectady after a brief illness.
She was born, raised and educated in Troy, and was a lifelong member of St. Patrick's Parish in Troy.
Margaret loved her family very much. She was an avid gardener and enjoyed crocheting for family and friends.
Margaret was predeceased by her beloved husband Earl Clermont, her brother Joseph Mahar, and her sisters, Sister Mary of the Immaculate Heart Op., and Rose Ann Mahar Mirabito.
Survivors include her daughters Lynn Osborne and her husband Tom of Rotterdam, Nan Plantier and her husband John of Lake Luzerne and Joseph and Carol Evers of Loudonville; grandchildren Lesley Osborne of Rotterdam, Angel Tudor of Lake Luzerne, Joseph Evers of Palo Alto, CA, and Brock Evers of Queens. She is also survived by her brothers Philip Mahar of Watervliet, John Mahar of Troy, and sisters, Sister Mary Matthew and Sister Ruth Mahar of Menands and Veronica Mahar Norton of Troy; and many nieces and nephews.
Relatives and friends are invited and may call Friday, June 10, from 6 to 8 p.m. at the DeMarco Funeral Home, 1605 Heldeberg Ave., Rotterdam.
Services will begin at 8:15 a.m. Saturday from the funeral home thence to St. Gabriel's Church on Hamburg Street for a Mass of Christian Burial at 9 a.m. Interment will follow the Mass at St. Mary's Cemetery in Troy.
Contributions may be made in Margaret's name to Community Hospice of Schenectady.

Ann T. Hammond
WATERVLIET - Ann T. Hammond, 98, longtime Watervliet resident, beloved wife of the late Bernard James Hammond, entered into eternal life on Wednesday, June 8, 2005, at the Van Rensselaer Manor.
Born on April 20, 1907 in County Mayo, Ireland, she was the daughter of the late Matthew and Delia Reilly Murphy. She resided on 14th Street and then at the Hudson Shores Apartments in Watervliet.
Mrs. Hammond and her husband ran Ann's Grocery Store on 14th Street during the late 1950s and early 1960s. She had also been employed at Watervliet High School.
She was a communicant of Sacred Heart of Mary Church in Watervliet as well as a member of the Church's Rosary Society and a member of the Watervliet Senior Center.
Devoted mother of Kathleen E. (Eugene) Eaton of Troy, Carole F. (Thomas) Coyne of Latham and the late Anne M. Grady, also the mother-in-law of James Grady of Watervliet. Cherished grandmother of Colleen McAllister of Waterford, Kelly Moscatiello of Troy, Kathleen Grady of Watervliet, Michael Eaton of Brunswick, Karen McMahon of saratoga Springs, Sheila Barna of Troy, Christine Buckley of Edgewater, MD, kathleen Merrill of Greenwich, PFC Matthew Eaton, U.S. Army of Fort Bragg, NC, Thomas Coyne of Latham and Maureen Ottati of Schenectady. "Grammie Hammie" is also survived by several nieces, nephews and great- nieces and nephews.
The funeral will be held in St. Agnes Cemetery, Menands.
Relatives and friends are invited and may call at the funeral home on Friday from 4 to 8 p.m.
Memorial contributions may be made to Watervliet Senior Citizen's Center, 1541 Broadway, Watervliet, NY 12189, in memory of Ann T. Hammond.
Funeral arrangements have been entrusted by the family to the Nealon & Sanvidge Funeral Home, 312 Hoosick St., Troy, NY 12180, 518 273-1404.

Beverly Jones
ORANGE CITY, FL - Beverly Diehl Jones, 82, wife of Robert G. Jones, died at her home on February 4, 2005.

She was the owner of the Jack and Jill Nursery School in Wynantskill for many years and also was employed by the Rensselaer County Social Services Department for several years, retiring in 1984.
Survivors in addition to her husband include her three children, Barbara Derleth of Boynton Beach, FL, Glen Terry DeLong of Hypoluxo, FL, and Patricia Warnaar of DeBary, FL; her two stepchildren, Dale R. Jones of Clearwater, FL, and Beth Anne Lobdell of Averill Park; her stepmother, Helene Behrens of Defreestville; three sisters, Joan DeGuile of Rensselaer, Laurel McEniry of Colonie and Diane Salvi of Troy; and three brothers, Roger Diehl of Averill Park, Donald Diehl of Saranacand Barry Diehl of Wynantskill. Mrs. Jones was predeceased by three sisters, Gladys Diehl, Marion Nightengale and Joyce Meilenz; and two brothers, Warren and Harry Diehl. She is also survived by 11 grandchildren, two great-grandchildren; and 30 nieces and nephews
Relatives and friends are invited to attend her graveside service at Evergreen Cemetery at 11 a.m. Saturday with the Rev. Kerri Haveman, pastor of the First Reformed Church in Wynantskill, officiating.
Donations to the Hospice of Volusia Flagler, 3800 Woodbriar Trail, Port Orange, FL, 32129, in memory of Mrs. Jones would be appreciated.
Arrangements are by the Thomas S. Connolly Funeral Home, 294 Whiteview Rd. Wynantskill, NY 12198.

John J. Lowery
TROY - John J. Lowery of Troy died Wednesday, June 8, 2005, at home. Born May 30, 1914, in Troy, he was the son of the late John and Helen Quinlan Lowery and brother of the late Helen Lowery Colby.
A lifelong resident of Troy, John graduated from Troy High School and attended Siena College.
Retiring in 1976, he had been employed with the state Department of Labor for 35 years.
John was a veteran of World War II (5th Air Force), participating in the New Guinea Campaign and the Philippine Campaign.
Survivors include two nieces, Sheila Donnelly (Thomas) Comerford and Nancy Donnelly (Leo) McGrath. John is also survived by four grandnieces, three grandnephews, three great-grandnieces and two great-grand nephews.
Family and friends may pay their respects on Friday from 4-6 p.m. at the Wm. Leahy Funeral Home, 336 Third St., Troy.
A Mass of Christian Burial will be celebrated at 9 a.m. on Saturday at St. Bernanrd's Church in Cohoes. Burial will follow in Oakwood Cemetery in Troy.
In lieu of flowers, donations in John's memory may be made to the Leukemia Society of America Inc, Upstate New York Chapter, 6 Automation Lane, Albany, NY 12206.

Daniel J. Sullivan
TROY - Daniel J. Sullivan, 65, of Seventh Avenue in Lansingburgh, died Monday, June 6, 2005, at the Community Hospice in Samaritan Hospital.
Born in Fall River, MA, he was the son of the late Dennis Sullivan and Mary Quinn Sullivan and husband of the late Marilyn Toohey Sullivan who died October 2, 1994.
Raised in Fall River, he was a graduate of BMC Durfee High School and Rensselaer Polytechic Institute in Troy with a bachelor's degree in civil engineering in 1961.
Mr. Sullivan was the principal safety and health engineer with the state Department of Labor in Albany for 35 years, retiring in 1995.
He was a 35-year member, past president and assigner with the Capital District High School Football Officials Association.
Dan was past president and current treasurer of the Society of the Friends of St. Patrick, a 2005 inductee to the Friends of 112th Street Athletic Hall of Fame, former Democratic committeeman in the city of Troy, and former coach and manager of Lansingburgh Little League. He was a communicant of St. Augustine's Church and past president of its school board.
Dan was an avid golfer and fan of the Boston Red Sox, New York Giants and Notre Dame football.
Survivors include, his beloved children, Michael J. (Melissa Fallon) Sullivan of Troy, D. Patrick (Tina Chorwat) Sullivan of Rensselaer, Erin A. (Rel) Teta of Troy, Sean W.(Sarah Mick) Sullivan of Warwick and Colleen M. Sullivan of Boston, MA. Beloved Papa of Michael J. Sullivan Jr., Marilyn Sullivan, Valerie Chorwat, Patrick Sullivan, Jarred, Alexandra, Hayden, Logan and Kennedy Teta and Emily and Seth Sullivan. Also survived by two brothers, Dennis Sullivan of Virginia Beach, VA, and Michael Sullivan of Medfield, MA; and several nieces and nephews.
Funeral services will be at 8:45 a.m. Friday from the McLoughlin & Mason Funeral Home, 109th Street and Third Avenue in Lansingburgh, and at 9:30 a.m. at St. Augustine's Church where the Mass of Christian Burial will be celebrated. Burial will be in St. John's Cemetery in Troy.
Calling hours will be 4 to 8 p.m. today at the funeral home.
Memorial donations to the Oncology Department at Samaritan Hospital, 2215 Burdett Ave., Troy, NY 12180, or the American Cancer Society, Northeast Chapter, 260 Osborne Road, Loudonville, NY 12211, would be appreciated.
For directions and guestbook, please visit www.mcloughlinmason.com.

Carlton H. Swanson, 83
LOUDONVILLE - Carlton H. Swanson, 83, passed away on Tuesday, June 7, 2005, at Albany Medical Center Hospital due to complications from a motor vehicle accident.
Carlton was born on July 12, 1921, in Rome, Oneida County, to the late Carl and Irene Zinglein Swanson.
Carlton served in the U.S. Air Force during World War II as a navigator on B-24 bombers, completing 32 missions and volunteering to retrieve damaged planes from Belgium, France and Germany. He flew during the D-Day invasion.
He managed supermarkets in the Capital District until 1957 and co-owned a small supermarket during the 1960s. He then became a sales representative for electrical manufacturers until his retirement in 1989.
He retired from the Air Force Reserve as a lieutenant colonel.
He was a member of Colonie Elks Lodge 2192 and St. Matthews Church in Latham. He enjoyed bingo and bowling.
Carlton is survived by his wife of 59 years, Carol Baker Swanson of Loudonville; four children, David (Lenora) Swanson of Elgin, IL, Lisa (Allen) Hibner of Penfield, Alan (Donna) Swanson of Glenmont, and Wendy (Rocco) Swanson of Schenectady; and seven grandchildren, Matthew, Katie, Nicholas, Jenny, Danielle, Justin and Dana; two step- grandchildren, Tina and Toni and six great-grandchildren. In addition to his parents, Carlton was predeceased by his stepmother, Margaret Swanson and a sister, Susan Sweetser.
Funeral services will be held on Saturday at noon at New Comer-Cannon Family Funeral Home, 343 New Karner Road, Colonie (Route 155 south of Central Avenue). Interment will follow at Memory's Garden in Colonie.
Calling hours will be on Friday from 5 to 8 p.m. at the funeral home.
Memorial contributions may be made to the Juvenile Diabetes Foundation, 6 Greenwood Drive, East Greenbush, NY 12061, in memory of Carlton H. Swanson.
