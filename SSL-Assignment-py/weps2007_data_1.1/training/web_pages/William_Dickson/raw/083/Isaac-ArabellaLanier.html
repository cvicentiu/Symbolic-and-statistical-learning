Isaac Lanier
        1767 - 1827

 8 Isaac Lanier           1767 - 1827
   +Arabella Clinton    ???? - 1793

            Isaac Lanier, son of Burwell and Elizabeth (Hill) Lanier, b. 1767 Carteret Co. N.C.
            lived later in Anson Co. N.C. m. Duplin Co. N.C. May 6, 1790 Arabella Clinton,
            daughter of the Rev. Richard Clinton.   They had Burwell Clinton Lanier, b. Duplin Co.
            and named for his two grandfathers, and twins, Arabella, and Isaac Hill Lanier b. Anson
            Co.  The mother Arabella died 1793, and Isaac Lanier married again in Duplin Co. about
            1796 Mary Dickson b. Oct. 20, 1776, daughter of William Dickson, and sister of
            Ann Dickson who maried Isaac's brother.  In 1809, Clement Lanier, younger brother,
            died in Montgomery Co. N.C. which had been part of Anson Co., leaving 3840 acres
            of land in Stewart Co. Tenn. to his brothers and sisters, ten in all.  James, son of
            Sampson deceased, sold his one-tenth to Joseph Pickett of Anson Co.; Isaac Lanier
            bought from his brother William, brother Thomas, and sister Elizabeth Jennings their
            portions, all except Elizabeth Jennings are listed in the 1810 Tax list of Stewart Co.
            the others were Thomas Clinton and wife Catherine, William Bell and wife Patsy.
            William Marshall and wife Sarah, William Carr and wife Frances, and McClelland
            heairs(sic), daughters of Nancy Lanier and John McClelland.  Isaac Lanier moved to
            Steward Co. Tenn. where he was engaged in business in Dover, until about 1820 when
            he moved to Madison Co. Ala.  His brother William had settled there some thirteen
            years earlier, and their brother Thomas had preceeded (sic) Isaac there by a few months,
            also from Stewart Co. Tenn.  On September 10, 1827, Isaac Lanier made a noncupative
            will; he died December 15, 1827 in his sixty first year.
                                                                    (LANIER, Ingersoll, p. 210-11)

                        10 Caroline LaFayette Boddie    1825 - 1913
                             +Addison Coleman Love       1812 - 1867

            An article appeared in The Montgomery Advertiser, on April 23, 1911 in which
            Caroline LaFayette Love was quoted with several of the following remarks:

           "Where did I get my name, 'Caroline Lafayette'?  I was christened in honor of the
            State of North Carolina from which both my lines of parentage had immigrated,
            and in commemoration of General Lafayette, who was in Alabama the year I was
            born, 1825."

            "My grandfather, Mr. Lanier, was a prosperous planter and merchant, but he wanted
              to come further South, and so he located in Autauga County.  There he built a home
              between old Washington and Vernon, Alabama River settlements, now obliterated,
              but in those times all boats stopped at them, and there were many attractive houses there. 
              The house my grandfather built was later owned by General Fair, who married my old
              schoolmate, Anne Robertson.  They kept up the place for a summer residence, even
              after they made Montgomery their home."

             "Do I remember Montgomery as a village?  To be sure, I do.  I remember when there
              were but two stores in the place, one owned by Mr. John Gingrat and the other by
              Mr. Winter.  The latter firm was interested in hardware and sold all the iron utensils
              used for agricultural and domestic purposes."

             "I have been a great family nurse in my time," said Mrs. Love.  "There was never
              a one of my sixty-one grandchildren that came into the world but I was there to bless
              its first hour."

             "I have forty living great-grandchildren, and I think so many precious babies have helped
              to sweeten my life.  They are like angels, and I love them every one."

             "My father, Thomas Boddie, at one time owned the site where Demopolis now stands. 
              My step-father Colonel S. H. M. Dixon, of Demopolis, located in Autauga, after his marriage
              to my mother and the descendants of this union live in Arkansas."
