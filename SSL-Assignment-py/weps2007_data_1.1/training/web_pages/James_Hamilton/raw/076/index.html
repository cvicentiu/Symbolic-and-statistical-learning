Mission Statement

This page is devoted to the genealogy of the Hamilton, Pennock and Maule families of Chester County, Pennsylvania. A family of two US presidents, two frontier legends, a famous Union Civil War General, colonial aristocracy, a baseball Hall-of-Fame legend, industrial giants, magnificent farms and manors, colonial farmers, contemporary farmers, professors, teachers, doctors, dentists, hunters, and just plain folks.

Most of the information on these pages is the work of others, especially my cousin, Dr. Virginia Trexler, our family historian. It was her voluminous work that enabled these pages to be developed. Any photographs (they will be returned) or additional information would be greatly appreciated. Please e-mail Richard Hamilton with information: Click here to e-mail. This page is dedicated to Dr. Virginia Trexler for all her work on the Hamilton Family History, to my parents, Harry and Kathryn Hamilton and to my late brother, Robert Harry Hamilton. Also my brother, Ronald Hamilton, who has a great interest in the Hamilton genealogy and who has submitted a great deal of information to this site. I can remember the day in 1993 when myself and my brothers took our father to Chester County for the last time. We visited all the gravesites of his parents and grandparents and the old family farm. It was a very poignant visit. Just memories now. Even though I was born in Reading, PA, (Berks County) and raised in Fleetwood, PA, (Berks County), I know my roots and love will always remain in Chester County, Pennsylvania, my ancestral home. I will return often.

      In Chester County, the past is the present, it is impossible to escape from it: the inhabitants of today are less real than the men and women who have gone; the shadows of the departed still possess the land. Voluntarily or involuntarily the sojourner must hold commune with those old relatives; for the whole air vibrates with a vast purpose and energy which has been and is no more. What a wonderful family, the Hamilton's, Pennock's and Maule's of Chester County, Pennsylvania.
.
