Neil Clark: true or false?

This morning's apologia for Slobodan Milosevic was ludicrous fiction.

Oh well, someone has to do it ...

Suggesting as Neil Clark does on this site today that Slobodan Milosevic had little or nothing to do with the war crimes and mass bloodshed carried out in the former Yugoslavia during his time at the helm of Serbia is akin to suggesting Hitler had no responsibility for the invasion of Poland and what followed.

Like most malicious historical revisionism Clark's apologia barely merits a rebuttal but for those interested in the facts, as opposed to his ludicrous fiction, I suggest two sources.

Firstly, there is the website of the international criminal tribunal for the former Yugoslavia and, in particular, the amended indictment against Milosevic and his close associates.

I'd also recommend a read of British journalist Adam Lebor's biography of Milosevic.

From that book one can quickly discover how inaccurate Clark is.

Here is Clark:

Milosevic, the inciter of ancient ethnic hatred at Kosovo Polje? All you have to do is to read the English translation of the speech. "More and more successfully citizens of different nationalities, different faiths and races are living together. Socialism, in particular, being a progressive and just democratic society, should not allow people to be divided by national or religious identity" - if that's a Serb nationalist rant then my name is Jose Mourinho.


Nice rhetorical sentiments from Slobo, but Clark could have, and chose not to, quote Milosevic promising the Serbian crowd: "No one should dare beat you again," and then this section of his speech delivered in the tense atmosphere and threat of violence between Serbs and Albanians:

This is your land, your fields, your gardens, your memories are here. Surely you will not leave your land because it is difficult here and you are oppressed ... You should also stay here because of your ancestors and because of your descendants. Otherwise you would disgrace your ancestors and disappoint your descendants. I do not propose, comrades, that in staying you should suffer and tolerate a situation in which you are not satisfied. On the contrary: you should change it.


We all know how the Serbs in Kosovo went about trying to bring about change and how the bloodshed was eventually halted.

Clark is as underhand and as untruthful in each of his other attempts to whitewash Milosevic of responsibility for the crimes committed under his rule. Check the facts for yourself.

Quite why anyone would attempt to revise the truth about a dead dictator and his role in the Balkan wars is hard to understand.

After all, aren't there still living and breathing dictators in need of western apologists?
