Harold S. 'Jobe' Bernard

Harold S. "Jobe" Bernard of Nashville died Monday, Dec. 11, 2006, at his home.

Born April 19, 1920, in Rockwood, he was the son of Adele Stone Bernard and Joe Bernard, both of whom preceded him in death. He attended public schools in Rockwood and was a member and president of Zeta Beta Tau fraternity at Vanderbilt University, Nashville, where he was a member of the Owl Club, the Ace Club and lettered in baseball before graduating in 1941 with a bachelor's degree in economics. A World War II veteran of the U.S. Navy, he was promoted to lieutenant senior grade officer and participated in the invasion of North Africa and fought in the Pacific Theater, where his ship was engaged in the invasions of Okinawa and Iwo Jima. One of his proudest memories was being present when the American flag was raised on Mount Suribachi. He settled in Nashville after the war and began a career in the wine and spirits industry of Middle Tennessee by establishing B. and G. Wholesalers Inc. Recognized as one of the more influential promoters of the appreciation of fine wines in this region, he was a charter member of the Ete du Vin charity wine auction and was president of the Wine and Spirits Association of Tennessee for seven years. He enjoyed golf and fishing and was an avid spectator sports fan and longtime supporter of the Vanderbilt athletic program. He retired from the business in 1988 after suffering a major stroke, but maintained his wonderful spirit and remained a joy to his family and many friends. He will be best remembered for his love of people, his generosity, loyalty and good humor.

In addition to his parents, he was preceded in death by sister, Carolyn Bernard Greif; and brother, Jack Bernard.

Survivors include his wife of 64 years, the former Ann Ryan; daughter, JoAnn Bernard of LaJolla, Calif.; son, Tommy Bernard of Nashville; brother and sister-in-law, Dr. Stanley and Adele Bernard of Nashville; brother-in-law, Maurice Greif of Rockwood; and sister-in-law, Margie Landau of Dallas.

Funeral service will be at 11 a.m. CST Wednesday, Dec. 13, in the Temple at 5015 Harding Road, Nashville, with Rabbi Mark Schiftan and Cantor Bernard Gutcheon officiating. Graveside service and burial will follow at The Temple Cemetery. Marshall Donnelly Combs Funeral Home, Nashville, is in charge of arrangements.

In lieu of flowers, the family requests memorial contributions to the "Harold S. "Jobe" Bernard Stroke Symposium, Gift Records, Vanderbilt University, VU Station B No. 357727, Nashville, TN 37235, or Alive Hospice.

Lowell B. Furman

Dr. Lowell B. Furman, 74, of of Boone, N.C., died Friday, Dec. 8, 2006, in Watauga Medical Center, Boone.

Born Feb. 21, 1932, he was a Harriman High School alumnus who received his undergraduate degree from the University of Tennessee and his doctorate degree from the UT Medical School. He was a flight surgeon in the U.S. Navy and was active in many aspects of life, but what was foremost in his desires was his love of his Lord, Jesus Christ. He usually ended up being in charge of getting any endeavor done that he attempted. He saw the need for sending physicians to mission hospitals around the world and became co-founder of World Medical Missions. He was also on the board of Samaritan's Purse, and he spent a large portion of "vacation time" operating in hospitals in Third World countries as a witness to his patients about the Lord. He served as president of the North Carolina Chapter of the American College of Surgeons and the North Carolina Surgical Association, A two-term governor for North Carolina to the American College of Surgeons, he was president of Watauga Surgical Group for 34 years, chief of medical staff at Watauga Medical Center on three occasions, and chief of surgery at Watauga Medical Center for many years. He rafted the Colorado River through the Grand Canyon twice, as well as rafting smaller western rivers numerous times. He was an avid golfer, tennis player and racquet ball player. One of his most cherished positions was team physician for the Appalachian State University football team since 1963. He will be fondly remembered as a man who loved his surgical practice, life and, most of all, the Lord.

He was preceded in death by his father and mother, Delbert and Amy Furman.

Survivors include his wife, Faythe R. Furman of Boone; sons and daughters-in-law, Lowell B. Jr. and Beth Furman of Bloomington, Ind., Robby and Michelle Houck of Charleston, S.C., and Chris and Angie Houck of Asheville, N.C.; daughters and sons-in-law, Susan F. and Mike Clawson of Manassas, Va., Barb F. and Steve Hall of Sherrills Ford, N.C., and Laura F. and Donald Harrison of Blowing Rock, N.C.; grandsons, Drew Clawson of Manassas and Taylor Furman of Bloomington; granddaughters, Samantha Hall and Sydney Hall, both of Sherrills Ford, and Gracie Houck of Charleston; brothers and sisters-in-law, James C. and Dolores Furman and Richard W. and Harriet Furman, all of Boone; and a number of nieces and nephews.

Funeral service was at 2 p.m. Sunday, Dec. 10, in Alliance Bible Fellowship, Boone, with Pastor Scott Andrews and Franklin Graham officiating. Burial was at Mount Lawn Memorial Park and Gardens, Boone. Hampton Funeral Service Inc., Boone, was in charge of arrangements.

In lieu of flowers, memorial contributions may be made to the World Medical Mission, P.O. Box 3000, Boone, NC 28607, or to the Alliance Bible Fellowship, 672 George Wilson Road, Boone, NC 28607.
