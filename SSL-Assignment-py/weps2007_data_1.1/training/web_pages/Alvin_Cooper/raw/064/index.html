A brief summary of assessment and treatment issue for compulsive online sexual activity--Part 2

By Gale H. Golden and Alvin Cooper | Mar 22, 2003 | 3223 words, 0 images

Abstract

This article will briefly summarize the salient issues in the assessment and treatment of compulsive online sexual activity. These sexual behaviors can be "obsessive and compulsive, can appear to have many of the qualities we associate with addiction, and may be censored or tolerated by society" (Golden 2001). Kafka (2000) has distilled the facets of hypersexuality into a united taxonomy that categorizes these behaviors into two subtypes: 1) paraphilic, which are generally socially censored and considered illegal behaviors; and 2) non-paraphilic hypersexuality disorders or paraphilia-related disorders (PRD) for those behaviors which are legal and not socially sanctioned.
