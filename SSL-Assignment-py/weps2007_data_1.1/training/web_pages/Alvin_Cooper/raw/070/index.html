Yvonne Phillips, 69, Wartburg

Yvonne Phillips, 69, Wartburg, went to be with her Lord on Tuesday, Jan. 23, 2007. She was born Nov. 27, 1937 in the Devonia Community.

She was a member of Shiloh Baptist Church for 59 years, where she was very active singing in the choir and serving as Sunday School Clerk. She enjoyed singing, cooking, and canning foods that she and her husband had raised in their garden. She loved worshipping the Lord and traveled locally with her family to sing for Him.

She was preceded in death by her parents, Raymond and Elmia Daugherty; a brother, Harold Daugherty; father-in-law, Dewey Phillips; a great-granddaughter, Grace Morgan Phillips; and nephews, Kim "Bodie" Daugherty and Larry Wayne Daugherty.

She is survived by her husband of 52 and 1/2 years, Estel Phillips; sons, Lester and wife Kathy Phillips of Wartburg and Danny and wife Lesley Phillips of Farmington, Mo.; daughter, Anita and husband Donald Daugherty, Jr. of Petros; mother-in-law, Bessie Phillips of Devonia; grandchildren, Vickey and husband Alan Boyd of Sweetwater, Scott and wife Lara Phillips of Sevierville, Brian and wife Alana Phillips of Rockwood, John Fair and Mikey Phillips of Farmington, Mo., Trey, Philip and Justin Daugherty of Petros; four great-grandchildren, Taylor and Landen Boyd, Dawson and Mason Phillips; brothers, Condy and wife Lana Daugherty of Devonia, Larry and wife Carol Daugherty of Joyner, Lonnie and wife Wilma Daugherty of Oliver Springs; sisters, Brenda and husband Chuck Justice of Coalfield, Kay and husband Gary Armes of Coalfield; and several nieces, nephews and a host of family and friends and brothers and sisters in Christ.

The family received friends Friday evening, Jan. 26, from 6 to 8 p.m. in Schubert Funeral Home, Wartburg. Funeral service was held Saturday at 2 p.m. in Shiloh Baptist Church, Devonia with Bro. Gary Armes and Bro. Lee Phillips officiating. Interment followed in the Anderson Memorial Gardens.

Jackson Rather Jr., 81

Jackson King "Jack" Rather Jr., 81, Oliver Springs, died Saturday, Jan. 27, 2007 in his home. Mr. Rather was born Sept. 15, 1925 in the Wheat Community of Roane County, moving to Oliver Springs in 1940.

Mr. Rather was of the Baptist faith. He served his country in the United States army during WW II. He retired from the Tennessee Department of Correction at Brushy Mountain Prison as a Food Service Manager after working 18 years.

He was preceded in death by his parents, Jackson King Rather and Elsie Hembree Rather; by two brothers, Jesse Floyd Rather and Richard Clifton Rather; and by a sister Jean Freels.

He is survived by wife of 48 years, Geraldine Vann Rather of Oliver Springs; daughter, Diane Turnbill and husband Roy of Oliver Springs; son, Jackie King Rather and companion Robin Byrge of Oliver Springs; granddaughter, Jennifer Rather of Oliver Springs; sisters, Lillie Hatfield of Baltimore, Md., Ruth Stout of Kingston, Irene Jones and husband Roger of Coalfield and Shirley Goodman and husband Fred of Knoxville; brother, Don Rather and wife Ann of Oliver Springs; former sister-in-law, Margaret Patterson of Oliver Springs; and several nieces and nephews.

The family received friends Monday, Jan. 29, 2007 between the hours of 6 and 8 p.m. in Sharp Funeral Home. A private graveside service was held in the Rather Cemetery in the Wheat Community with Tammy Edwards officiating.

Sharp Funeral Home was in charge of the arrangements.

Ryan Cooper, 27, Florida

Ryan Cooper, 27, Cape Coral, Fla., formerly of Knoxville, passed away Thursday, Jan. 18, 2007.

He was a medical student at St. Matthews University and an Honors graduate of Florida State University. He worked as a physicians aid, he also taught and tutored various education courses, such as, Bio. Chemistry, Organic Chemistry, Human Physiology and Physics. He was a former member of Liberty Baptist Church of Wartburg.

He was preceded in death by his mother, Catherine Cooper and step-mother, Pamela Richards Cooper; grandfather, Manford Brewer; and grandparents, Hayes and Jewel Cooper.

He is survived by his father, Alvin Cooper and brother, Adam Cooper, both of Florida; grandparents, Edith and J.D. Overton; aunts and uncles, John and Pat Cope, Ken and Karen Cooner, Kenny and Jennifer Brewer and Jerry Brewer; also special cousins and friends.

The family receive friends Saturday, Jan. 27, from 1 to 2 p.m. in Schubert Funeral Home, Wartburg. Funeral services followed at 2 p.m. with Bro. John Cope officiating. Interment followed in the Morgan Memorial Gardens, Wartburg.

Darlene Pickard, 54, Harriman

Darlene Manis Pickard, 54, Harriman, passed away Saturday, Jan. 27, 2007 in Methodist Medical Center in Oak Ridge. She was a loving and devoted wife, mother and grandmother who loved to sew and spend time with her granddaughter.

She was a member of the Elverton Primitive Baptist Church. She loved her church family and attending services when her health would allow.

She was preceded in death by her parents, Victor and Mary Elizabeth Cox Manis; one brother, Billy Jo Manis; and one sister, Sharon Manis Pickard.

She is survived by her husband, Kenneth Pickard; one daughter and son-in-law, Kelly and James Hall of Harriman; one granddaughter who was her pride and joy, Brianna Hall; one brother, Charles Edward Manis of Harriman; five sisters and brothers-in-law, Margaret Davis of Harriman, Patricia and Jackie Greene of Kingston, Barbara and Larry Murray, Vicky and Danny Gouge, Brenda and David Bailey, all of Harriman; four brothers-in-law, Jerry, Harold, Willie and Bobby Pickard; a special friend and caregiver, Ruby Jenkins; and a host of nieces, nephews, family, friends and loved ones.

The family received friends Monday, Jan. 29, 2007 from 6 to 9 p.m. in Davis Funeral Home in Harriman. Funeral services were Tuesday, Jan. 30, 2007 at 2 p.m. in Elverton Primitive Baptist Church in Harriman with Bro. Roger Buck officiating. Interment followed in the church cemetery.

Arrangements were by Davis Funeral Home, Harriman.

Lydia Montgomery, Oliver Springs

Lydia (Lida) Bell Hall Montgomery, 92, Oliver Springs, departed this life Friday, Jan. 26, 2007, in her home in Oliver Springs, after a long illness. Lida was a native of Oliver Springs and a member of Little Leaf Baptist Church, Oliver Springs.

During her lifetime she enjoyed fishing, crocheting, quilting, cooking, and being an advocate of the Oliver Springs Community Action Group.

Lida was born Sept. 6, 1914 in Oliver Springs, to the late Harry Hall and Nannie Bell Hall Fritts. In addition to her parents, she was preceded in death by her husband of 64 years, Ulyess Grant Montgomery; four brothers, Charles Washington Hall, Arvil David Hall, Freeman Alfred Hall and Claude Clark Hall; and by eight sisters, Ada Mae Hall, Dortha Loy Hall, Mamie Hall Hayward, Mary Wilder Hall, Susannah Hall Knaff, Florence Marie Hall Knaff, Willie Ellen Hall Thompson and Rosa Claudine Hall Young.

She is survived by one daughter, Mary Louise Montgomery Brooks (Willie James); two grandchildren, Gary Grant Montgomery her caretaker of the last ten years and Lydia Lorraine Brooks all of Oliver Springs; two sister-in-laws, Clara Hall Hughes of Oak Ridge and Mildred Montgomery Harrison of Clinton; special nieces, Marcella Curd and Anna F. Anderson; devoted nephew and wife, William and Faye Curd; and a host of nieces, nephews, great nieces, great nephews, and other relatives and friends; and extended family, Leslie V. Knaff of Bronx, N.Y., Yvette Woods of Oliver Springs, Carrie D. Brown, Chloe A. Brown and Deborah E. Brown of Durham, N.C.

Friends called Wednesday, Jan. 31, 2007 between the hours of 7 and 9 p.m. in Sharp Funeral Home. Funeral services were held Thursday, Feb. 1, 2007 at 2 p.m. in Little Leaf Baptist Church with Elder Thomas Dews officiating.

Sharp Funeral Home was in charge of the arrangements.

He is survived by his sons, Robert and John Bingham of Chicago, Ill.; sister, Myrtle Bingham of Louisville, Ky.; brothers, Oscar Breedlove of Newport, Tenn., and Gerald Bingham of New Berlin, Wis.; and several nephews and nieces.
