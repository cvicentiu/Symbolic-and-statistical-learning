Deaths


     
     Joseph Barreca
     
      Joseph Barreca, 87, died Thursday in a Las Vegas hospital.
      He was born Sept. 20, 1911, in Brocton, N.Y. A retired electrical engineer, he was a 14-year resident of Las Vegas.
      He is survived by his sons, Leonard of Springfield, Mo., and Richard of Buffalo, N.Y.; sister, Phyllis Suppo of Fredonia, N.Y.; seven grandchildren; and two great-grandchildren.
      Visitation will be from 3 to 5 p.m. today in Palm Mortuary-Jones.
     
     Mary Brown
     
      Mary F. Brown, 96, died Wednesday.
      She was born July 29, 1902, in Brooklyn, N.Y., and was an eight-year resident of Henderson.
      She is survived by her daughter, Patricia Lee of Henderson; two grandchildren; and five great-grandchildren.
      Private services were handled by Davis Paradise Valley Funeral Home.
     
     Robert Candee
     
      Robert Candee, 82, died Friday.
      He was born May 22, 1917, in Moscow, Idaho. An Army veteran of World War II, he was a business executive, a 10-year resident of Las Vegas and a member of Beta Theta Pi, Elks Lodge and American Legion.
      He is survived by his wife, Ann of Nevada; son, Christopher of Colorado; and sister, Carolyn Anderson of Arizona.
      Services will be held in Colorado. Valley Memorial Crematory handled arrangements.
     
     Alvin Cooper
     
      Alvin Cooper, 70, died Wednesday.
      He was born June 4, 1929, in Hope, Ark. A veteran, he was a retired police officer and a three-year resident of Las Vegas.
      He is survived by his wife, Emma of Las Vegas; daughters, Acra of Omaha, Neb., Concepcion Nichols, Valencia Nichols, Lisa Nichols and Erika, all of Las Vegas, and Deborah Nichols of Compton, Calif.; sons, Alvin Jr. and Nat, both of Omaha; sisters, Enola Jennings and Betty West, both of California, and Maxine Williams of Denver; brother, Lou Jennings of California; 14 grandchildren; and one great-grandchild.
      Visitation will be from 3 to 5 p.m. Sunday in Davis Paradise Valley Funeral Home, where services will be at 11 a.m. Monday. Burial will follow in Paradise Memorial Gardens.
     
     Pauline Horn
     
      Pauline E. Horn, 82, died Wednesday.
      She was born Aug. 20, 1916, in Easton, Pa. A homemaker, she was a 52-year resident of Las Vegas and a member of Order of Eastern Star Southgate Chapter 18 and Calvary Lutheran Church.
      She is survived by her daughters, Patricia Stambaugh and Paulette Hutchison; son, Philmar; stepson, Paul; and brother, Robert Rapp, all of Las Vegas; five grandchildren; and four great-grandchildren.
      Visitation will be from 2 to 7 p.m. today in Palm Mortuary-Downtown, where services will be at 3 p.m. Sunday. Burial will follow in Palm Memorial Park.
     
     Richard Keep
     
      Richard N. Keep, 65, died Wednesday.
      He was born Feb. 6, 1934, in Los Angeles. An Army veteran of the Korean War, he was a retired driver for a dairy, a 36-year resident of Las Vegas and a member of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints.
      He is survived by his wife, Marlene of Las Vegas; daughters, Kim of Las Vegas and Tina McComas of Ojai, Calif.; stepsons, Jay Kalbfleisch and Jim Kalbfleisch, both of Las Vegas; sisters, Shirley Robinson of Sandy, Ore., and Barbara Paramore of Salt Lake City; brother, Harry of Huntington Beach, Calif.; and six grandchildren.
      Services will be at 10 a.m. Monday in Palm Mortuary-Cheyenne with graveside services at 11:30 a.m. in Southern Nevada Veterans Memorial Cemetery. The family requests memorial donations be made to Marian Miller Alzheimer's Center, 6190 Bronco Loco Court, Las Vegas, 89108.
     
     Chanel Lauderdale
     
      Chanel Lauderdale, 17, died June 27 in Los Angeles.
      She was born Nov. 18, 1981, in Pascagoula, Miss. A 1999 graduate of Rancho High School, she was a 10-year resident of Las Vegas and a member of Girl Scouts of America.
      She is survived by her mother, Mary of Las Vegas; sisters, Veronica and Andrea, both of Virginia, Veria of Houston, Felicia of San Diego and Erica of New York; brothers, Dwight of Las Vegas, Eugene of San Diego and Derrick of Washington, D.C.; and grandmother, Ann Henson.
      Memorial services were held Friday in Mt. Sinai Missionary Baptist Church. Palm Mortuary-Downtown handled arrangements.
     
     Blanca Machado
     Norma Machado
     
      Blanca A. Machado, 51, and Norma J. Machado, 24, died July 4.
      Blanca Machado was born March 3, 1948, in El Salvador. A homemaker, she was a 20-year resident of Las Vegas. Born Jan. 25, 1975, also in El Salvador, Norma Machado was a homemaker and an eight-year resident of Las Vegas.
      Blanca Machado is survived by her husband, Carlos Melara; and daughter, Bianca Fernandez, both of Las Vegas; sons, Carlos of Nevada, and Ramon Quintanilla and Noe, both of Las Vegas; father, Antonio Vasquez of El Salvador; mother, Maria of California; brothers, Jonas of El Salvador, and Carlos and Jose', both of California; and four grandchildren.
      Norma Machado is survived by her husband, Noe of Las Vegas; and mother, Angelica Melara; father, Sergio Martinez; brothers, Jose' Florez and Ector Florez; and sister, Ofelia Florez, all of El Salvador.
      Visitation will begin at 6 p.m. today in Iglesia Cristo Viene at 950 "A" St., where services will be at 10 a.m. Sunday. Burial will follow in Bunkers Eden Vale Memorial Park. Bunkers Eden Vale Mortuary handled arrangements.
     
     Mong Nguyen
     
      Mong Quang Nguyen, 52, died Tuesday.
      He was born Nov. 15, 1946, in Vietnam. A cook in a restaurant, he was a four-year resident of Las Vegas.
      He is survived by his son, Jimmy; and sisters, Jeannie Pham and Kim, all of Las Vegas; and brother, Gian of Vietnam.
      Visitation will be from 10 a.m. to 7 p.m. today in Bunkers Eden Vale Mortuary, where services will be at 3 p.m. Sunday. Burial will follow in Bunkers Memory Gardens Memorial Park.
     
     Hyman Ogulnick
     
      Hyman Ogulnick, 77, died Thursday.
      He was born Sept. 8, 1921, in Tecumseh, Mich. An Army veteran of World War II, he was the retired owner of Ojai Gift and Stationery Store, a five-year resident of Las Vegas, past president of the Chamber of Commerce, and a member of Ojai Rotary, Shriners and Jewish War Veterans.
      He is survived by his wife, Elaine; and son, Ross Ninberg, both of Las Vegas; daughters, Susan of Chicago and Donna Sottile of Las Vegas; sister, Annie Berman of Palm Springs, Calif.; brothers, Peter of Las Vegas and Sy of Reno; and one granddaughter.
      Graveside services will be at 10 a.m. Monday in Bunkers Memory Gardens Memorial Park. The family requests memorial donations be made to the American Heart Association. Bunkers Eden Vale Mortuary handled arrangements.
     
     Dorothy Ohriner
     
      Dorothy Ohriner, 91, died Thursday.
      She was born Jan. 18, 1908, in New York City. A homemaker, she was a 28-year resident of Las Vegas and a member of Temple Beth Shalom and Congregation Ner Tamid.
      She is survived by her sons, Harvey and Neil, both of Las Vegas; brothers, Harry Anders and Phillip Auswacks, both of Camarillo, Calif.; two grandchildren; and four great-grandchildren.
      Services will be at 10 a.m. Sunday in Palm Mortuary-downtown followed by graveside services at 11 a.m. in Woodlawn Cemetery. The family requests memorial donations be made to the James Ohriner Memorial Fund at the UNLV Library or the Adat Ari El grade school program.
     
     Michael Sousa
     
      Michael Raymond Sousa, 33, died Tuesday.
      He was born May 21, 1966, in Rhode Island. An actor and floor host for local clubs, he was a resident of Las Vegas.
      Memorial services will be held from 4 to 8 p.m. today in the Hard Rock Hotel and at 1 p.m. Tuesday in St. Joseph Husband of Mary Catholic Church. Davis Paradise Valley Funeral Home handled arrangements.
     
     Agnes Urbish
     
      Agnes A. Urbish, 85, died Friday in a Las Vegas care center.
      She was born Aug. 31, 1913, in Penelope, Texas. A homemaker, she was a 22-year resident of Las Vegas.
      She is survived by her husband, Alfred; and daughter, Jeannette Cooper, both of Las Vegas; son, Glenn of Fort Lauderdale, Fla.; sisters, Rosie Firasek, Hattie Allen and Margie Thompson, all of Fort Worth, Texas; and two grandchildren.
      Visitation will be from 9 a.m. to 4 p.m. Sunday in Palm Mortuary-Jones with services at 9:30 a.m. Monday in St. Joseph Husband of Mary Catholic Church. Graveside services will follow in Palm Valley View Memorial Park.
