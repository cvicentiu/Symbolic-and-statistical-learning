Alvin Cooper
Chief Academic Officer
acooper@techboston.org

Mr. Cooper has worked for Boston Public Schools for thirteen years. He received his B.S. from Delaware State University in Elementary Education and earned two Master's in Education from Cambridge College, one in Integrated Studies and the other in Education Administration. He was a classroom teacher for eight years, a technology support specialist for the Office of Instructional Technology for three years and the Training and Events Coordination for TechBoston last year. Mr. Cooper led and coordinated the training of teachers in fifteen of the city's schools. He has also worked on several grants, including the MetroLINC grant funded by the U.S. Department of Education. Mr. Cooper was Teacher of the Year in 1997. Mr. Cooper has traveled to over forty-three countries including six of the seven continents.

Mary Skipper
Headmaster
mskipper@techboston.org

Ms. Skipper was the Director of TechBoston, an initiative in the Boston Public Schools aimed at preparing middle and high school students for careers and post-secondary education in high tech areas. She has also worked for the past three years on a design team for the Massachusetts State Department of Education's VES (Virtual Education Space) Project, which is now serving 30,000 educators statewide. Mary has been in education for fourteen years, serving as a classroom teacher, administrator, project manager within the of Office Instructional Technology, program director and now a Headmaster. She holds her B.A. and M.A. in Latin, Greek, and English from Tufts University. She is also certified in seven computer-programming languages. She serves on many local, state and national advisory boards in educational technology and is committed to raising the educational bar for all children. She enjoys her husband and three children, teaching, learning new things, and softball.
