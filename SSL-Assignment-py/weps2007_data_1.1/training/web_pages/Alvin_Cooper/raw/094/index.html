Support

New Funding Supports CAST Projects


d
James Earl Jones makes a Boston appearance to present the Bell Atlantic Foundation EdLink Award to CAST's Mary Brady (center). Brady was accompanied by (left to right) Bill Ganter, Agassiz School science teacher; Tiana Richardson, a star fifth-grade science student at the school; Boston Mayor Thomas M. Menino; Alvin Cooper, Agassiz school fifth-grade teacher; Wayne Budd, Bell Atlantic group president; and Helen Murphy, Tiana's mother.

With generous contributions from two donors, CAST will be able to expand its applied research and software development projects. The Bell Atlantic Foundation has awarded CAST $35,000 to train fourth and fifth-grade teachers of students with disabilities and low-literacy skills at Boston's Agassiz Elementary School to create and use accessible, Web-based science lessons. This gift falls under Bell Atlantic's 1998 EdLink Awards, which were established to reward not-for-profit organizations' efforts to integrate technology into the curriculum. The Foundation focuses its support on children from the communities it serves.

To help expand CAST's research and development efforts from its current Macintosh-based systems to the Windows/Intel platforms, the Arthur K. Watson Charitable Trust has presented CAST with a $300,000 grant over three years. CAST will use the grant to purchase Windows equipment and network infrastructure to begin product research and development, and to train its staff and hire Windows programming experts. Perhaps most important, this grant will support CAST's efforts to develop innovative, universally designed Windows products.

CAST is grateful to the Bell Atlantic Foundation and the Arthur K. Watson Charitable Trust for their generous support.
