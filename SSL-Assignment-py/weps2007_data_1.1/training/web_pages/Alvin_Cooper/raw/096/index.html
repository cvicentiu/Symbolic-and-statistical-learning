Raid nets eight arrests

Cassie DeLozier
Police Reporter

The Stillwater Police Department, along with agents from the District Nine Drug Task Force, arrested eight people Friday on drug and public intoxication charges.


Police Officers served arrest warrants at 1115 1/2 and 1123 South Lewis St. at about 9 p.m. Friday.


Materials obtained from the house included substances suspected to be marijuana and crack cocaine. Related paraphernalia and firearms were also found in the houses, according to police reports.


Alvin Cooper III, Vakeisah Nichole Johnson, Bryan Rhaye Wilson, Sherron Gray Lamor, Ronnie Rayon Majors, Gregory Alan Propes, Lori Lee Lovan and Ora Renee Longstreet were found in the residence and arrested on various charges.


All but one of those arrested are Stillwater residents. Wilson is from Oklahoma City.


Department of Human Services workers were present and took several children at the residence into custody.


According to court records, Cooper, 48, and Johnson, 30, were charged with possession of marijuana and paraphernalia.


Wilson, 20, was charged with obstructing an officer. Wilson was arrested in January on a complaint of unlawfully carrying a concealed weapon and pleaded guilty. Lamor, 39, Majors, 25, and Propes, 42, were arrested on complaints of public intoxication.


Possession of marijuana and paraphernalia, obstructing an officer and public intoxication are all considered misdemeanor counts in the state of Oklahoma.


Lovan, 27, was arrested on four counts — unlawful possession of a controlled drug with intent to distribute, maintaining a dwelling where drugs are kept or sold, distributing controlled drugs within 2,000 feet of a school and unlawful possession of drug paraphernalia.


Longstreet, 34, was arrested on three counts — possession of a controlled dangerous substance within 2,000 feet of a school, possession of a controlled substance and unlawful possession of drug paraphernalia.


Unlawful possession of a controlled drug within 2,000 feet of a school, maintaining a dwelling where drugs are kept or sold and distributing controlled drugs within 2,000 feet of a school are considered felony counts by the state of Oklahoma.


According to court records, individual court dates have been set for the end of November.
