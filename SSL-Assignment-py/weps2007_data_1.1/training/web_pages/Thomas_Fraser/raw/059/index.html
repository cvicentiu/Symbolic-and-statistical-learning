In Response To: Thomas Fraser and Amelia (Glenys Rasmussen)

Dear Sir or Madam,

I stumbled across your web-site initially in search of a VIOLET M. FRASER [1883-1919] whose father was a THOMAS FRASER of New Jersey, U.S.A. I'm currently vacationing in a small town in Serbia & Montenegro where I happened to chance upon an old tombstone belonging to the above lady, unfortunately however the inscriptions indicate her mother to be a MRS. FRASER GONASHAN of Stirlingshire, Scotland. The tombstone was erected in her memory by the Serbian Relief Fund in grateful appreciation of her services, no doubt as an aid-worker or nurse during the first World War. My aim is purely to bring this information to the attention of her family whom I believe would be delighted to hear, aw well as the fact that there lies a beautifully inscribed stone which time had otherwise forgotten. I hope this may have in some small way shed some light on your own search, though naturally of course I appreciate tha small probability involved. I apologise if this letter may have raised false hopes though I wouldn't have forgiven myself if I hadn't at least tried.

Please feel free to reply and offer any suggestions, I would be most happy to hear from you.

Yours faithfully, Mr. D Milenkovic.
