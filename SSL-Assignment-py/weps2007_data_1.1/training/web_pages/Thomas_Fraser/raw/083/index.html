Simon Fraser was impeached for high treason and executed. The lands of Lovat were returned to his son Simon after he (the son) had served well for his country in the wars in Portugal and America. TSP suggests that his sons (Simon and Archibald) were de jure Lords but BP1934 indicates that the attainder was not formally reversed for them but for their kinsman Thomas who is shown below as the 12th Lord.
