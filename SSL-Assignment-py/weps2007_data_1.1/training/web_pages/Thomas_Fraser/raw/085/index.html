The Lands of Balneaves

There are several references to the 'Lands of Balneaves' in old documents, referring to grants of land. In the Register of the Great Seal (1424-1513), there is confimation of transfer of the lands of Balneaves in the barony of Kinnell in Forfar, to David Liel, son and heir of William Liel of Balneaves. Later, in October 1501, the Lands of Balneaves were transferred from Thomas Fraser, Laird of Lovat to George Lyon, Laird of Glammis (ref lb4). In 1609,there is an extract giving details, in Latin (see text below), of a land transfer to Thomas Fraser of Lovat (ref lb1). In 1621, the Lands of Kinnell including Balnaves and Rynmuir were transferred from Thomas Fraser to James Durham.(ref lb2) The lands of Kinnell were still in his possession in 1634 when there is reference to his wife, Alison Clark (ref lb3).

The Lands of Balneaves were located in the area of Kinnell, just east of the small town of Friockheim (pronounced freekum). The old house of Balneaves is reported in about 1930 to have been located in the corner of a field on the south bank of the Lunan Water, opposite the manse of Kinnell, and marked by three venerable trees. However, while the current 1:50,000 Ordnance Survey map shows Balneaves Cottage close by (Grid Ref NO 604497), the original 6" OS County series map of 1865 has no reference to Balneaves in the area.. The site now houses the Balneaves Garage.

Translation of Latin Documemt
Charter to Thomas Fraser of Kinnell

The King confirmed to Thomas Fraser of Kinnell a charter whereby for the evident benefit of his family of/at Brakie, he granted to Thomas Fraser, the legitimate son of his firstborn son Robert Fraser & to his heirs & assignees whatsoever, the lands of the barony of Kynnell (viz Brakie with its castle, Newbiging, Lynmylne & Boighall, Haltoun of Kynnell [with] mill & field, the lands belonging to the mill &c., domain lands called Mains & Mainsbank, the lands of Balnavyis & Rynmure, with their tenants &c) in the vicinity of Forfar; RESERVING to the said Thomas the elder the freehold, & after his death to Euphemia Durham, wife of the said Robert, the revenue during her lifetime of Maynis & Maynisbank; and also to the said Thomas senior the power to alienate an annual revenue of 200 marks from the said lands, with a reversionary right of 2,000 marks; RESERVING also a letter of lease by the said Thomas senior in favour of his executors composed of the domain lands of the laird of Brakie for one year after his death; with power to the said Thomas senior to lease to any persons whatsoever the said lands pertinent to himself as proprietor, with the exception of those in which the said Euphemia is the feudal tenant, during all the years after his death, until the said Thomas junior should reach the age of 21; RESERVING also alienation effected by the said elder (Fraser) to his son David Fraser by inheritance of Haltoun of Kynnell with its mill, with a reversionary right of 5,000 marks; PROVIDED that it be not permitted for the said Thomas junior to alienate the aforementioned (properties) or their annual revenue, or to convey the lease of the same with reduction of rental, without the consent of James Durham of Pitkerro, David Lychtoun (resident) in Ethie and Alexander Campbell feudal tenant of Crwnane and their heirs; & that it be permitted for the said Thomas senior or his agent (excluding his heirs) to redeem the aforesaid (with the exception of Mains of Kynnell & Balnavyis) by the payment of the sum of £6 in the parish church of Kynnell, or by written proof of the same in the hands of any farmer resident in the parish of Kynnell, upon 6 days' notice. (This charter) to be held from the king. Due and customary rights and services to be rendered. Witnessed by Master Alex Ramsay, notary. George Watson, servant to Mr James Ogilvie. Andrew Robertson, servant to the said Thomas senior. Charter written by Master John Dunmuir, servant to Master Alexander Wedderburn, clerk to the Town Council of Dundee.

Wester Braikie, 14 July 1609, Witnessed as in other charters &c. xlv 378
