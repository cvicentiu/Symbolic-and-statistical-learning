Exerpts from Yesterday''s People, The Upper Dublin Story by Suzanne Hilton

This booklet is available for purchase ($5, $6 by mail, price may have changed) at the Upper Dublin Public Library, 805 Loch Alsh Ave, Fort Washington, PA 19034, 215-628-8744.

Thomas Holme bought Claridge's Upper Dublin land, but he never had the chance to use it. After his death, his son-in-law Silas Crispin sold it and in 1697 the buyer of half the property was a young man named John Kirk.

John Kirk had great plans when he bought the 500 acres from the township line to where Dreshertown is now. But he died in 1705 while his six sons were still too young to inherit. Godfrey, John, Samuel, Joseph, Isaac, and Thomas Kirk all had to wait until Godfrey had reached 21 before they could own their own land. Eventually John, the third son, became owner of it all and he built the first Kirk mill on the stream, Pine Run, that ran through the land. Before 1750, he had also built a saw mill and grist - extremely important to a developing township. (page13)
