STEPHEN CLARK's previous play, Stripped, won a Jefferson award in 2003 for best new play in Chicago (Circle Theatre). His other plays include Takeaway (Lyric Hammersmith, London, and national tour), Critics' Choice - The Guardian, The Independent and Time Out) and Cordelia, (RNT studio, London). In 1990 Stephen participated in the Stephen Sondeim Masterclasses at Oxford University. He has since written the book and/or lyrics for the musicals Icarus (Baltimore), Eyam (London), Sing to the Dawn (Singapore), Killing Rasputin (London), and Forbidden City (Singapore). In 1997 he won an Olivier award for his lyrics for the reworked version of Martin Guerre for Cameron Mackintosh, which then toured Britain and North America.

Stephen Clark
Making Waves

Mike, father of the Tanner family, is the coxswain of the local lifeboat. The demands of his job take priority over the needs of his family. But he's a hero. What can they do?

" Theatre is never more satisfying than when it bears the stamp of authenticity, and few theatrical experiences are more authentic than this....a scorching family drama in which you can smell the danger and taste the salt."
