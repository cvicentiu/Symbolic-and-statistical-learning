Stephen Clark

December 8, 2003

Stephen Clark has been a member of the Action News family since January 2003, when he first joined Diana Lewis as the co-anchor of Action News at 6PM. Since then, Stephen has also joined Diana at the helm of the 11 p.m. newscast.

Clark, a veteran broadcast journalist, brings over 20 years of experience to the Channel 7 family.

"I’ve done it all on TV," he says. And it’s true.

Stephen Clark started out as a newspaper photographer in Colorado. Since then, his career has taken him from radio reporter and D.J. to TV photographer, reporter, and finally the anchor’s chair.

He has worked across the country in markets from KRDO in Colorado Springs to Washington D.C. to Denver’s KCNC, San Diego’s KGTV and in New York.

Stephen’s covered momentous events including the conflict in Bosnia, the L.A. Riots, and more recently, the terrorist attacks of September 11, 2001.

Clark was a correspondent for CBS News at the time of the 9/11 attacks, and covered them from right there in New York. He has been back to ground zero every year since, and has invited Action News viewers to follow him on his annual memorial reports from the city.

For years, Stephen’s job required him to work long and late hours, limiting the time he could spend with his wife and two daughters. He finally left his job in New York - with his family in tow - to take a four-month journey across America.

Their trek took them from coast to coast, northeast to southwest, and was "a great experience" for the family. Stephen claims that his teenage daughters didn’t argue during the long car trip, and as those Channel 7 viewers with children know, this is a pretty amazing feat! Both Oprah and the Wall Street Journal covered the family’s travels.

Clark and his family currently reside in Oakland County. The family is attempting to build their own home. "We’re swinging the hammer quite a bit," Stephen says of the effort. He has built a kitchen and a garage before, but never a full home. He says he is up to the challenge, and we wish him luck.

Stephen Clark received his Bachelor's Degree in Journalism from the University of Northern Colorado-Greeley. He grew up with a father in the Air Force, in a family that moved around quite a bit.

Off work hours, Clark keeps busy with a host of hobbies. He’s a songwriter and performer, playing traditional country-folk music, which he describes as somewhere between James Taylor and Garth Brooks. Stephen has recently played a few fun Detroit gigs: the Hamtramck Blowout and the Detroit Hoedown.

Stephen writes fiction, and has at least one fully finished novel waiting to be read. He is a self-proclaimed "computer geek," and loves the technology side of TV journalism.
