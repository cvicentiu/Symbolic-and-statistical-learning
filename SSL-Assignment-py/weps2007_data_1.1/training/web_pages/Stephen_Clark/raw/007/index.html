Stephen has experience in employee communications, public relations, speech writing, education, corporate communications, and religious publishing.


 
STEPHEN R. CLARK is a native of New Castle, Indiana where he graduated from Walter P. Chrysler High School. Later he attended Evangel (University) College in Springfield, Missouri earning a B.A. in English and Biblical Studies, with a minor in Psychology.

A writer and communications professional, he served as the Editor of Christian Bookseller Magazine in Carol Stream, Illinois (now Christian Retailing), the Managing Editor of Bridge Publishing, Inc. (now Bridge-Logos Books) in South Plainfield, New Jersey, and the Public Information & Publications Director for a Christian liberal arts college in Ohio.

Since he began writing in 1968 he has authored short stories, poetry, online content, ad copy, scripts, speeches, direct mail, articles, and more. He has ghostwritten several Christian books and contributed chapters to compilations, devotionals, and other books.

Over a seven year period he held positions in employee communications, proposal development, technical writing, quality & process management, and more in various units of AT&T in New Jersey, including AT&T Network Systems (now Lucent), AT&T Treasury, and AT&T Solutions.

He returned to Indiana in 1996 operating a free-lance writing and communications business (which he has done full time and part time for years) until joining Rolls-Royce Aerospace (formerly Allison Engine). He then served for three years as the Corporate Communications/PR Manager with Best Access Systems (formerly Best Lock and now owned by Stanley Works) in Indianapolis.

In addition to the abovementioned experience he has also has done call center tier 2 technical support, factory labor, home construction, greenhouse labor, janitorial, lumber yard labor, direct home sales, directory keylining (graphic layout), and menswear sales. He has taught a continuing education course for IUPUI in business writing.

Stephen is the Publisher of Epiphany Lane Press and a current member of the Evangelical Press Association (EPA) and the Associated Church Press (ACP), and a former member of the Fishers, Indiana Chamber of Commerce, the IABC, and the PRSA. He resides in Fishers, Indiana and attends Fall Creek Community Church in Fishers (www.fallcreek.org).

He is currently a Project Editor, Consumer Dummies (www.dummies.com) with Wiley Publishing, Inc.

Walking on Fire!

In 2001, at a management conference in Kauai, Hawaii, Stephen took part in a fire walking activity.

When asked why he did it, he said simply, "Because it was there!"

"While it was exciting," Clark continues, "it was not the life-changing event that others have described. It was definitely a challenge. I'm glad I did it -- once. But I have no plans or need to do it again."
 
Statement of Faith

I believe the Bible to be the inspired, the only infallible, authoritative Word of God.

I believe that there is one God, eternally existent in three persons: Father, Son, and Holy Spirit.

I believe in the deity of our Lord Jesus Christ, in His virgin birth, in His sinless life, in His miracles, in His vicarious and atoning death through His shed blood, in His bodily resurrection, in His ascension to the right hand of the Father, and in His personal return in power and glory.

I believe that for the salvation of lost and sinful people, regeneration by the Holy Spirit is absolutely essential.

I believe in the present ministry of the Holy Spirit by whose indwelling the Christian is enabled to live a godly life.

I believe in the resurrection of both the saved and the lost; they that are saved unto the resurrection of life and they that are lost unto the resurrection of damnation.

I believe in the spiritual unity of believers in our Lord Jesus Christ.

This Statement is essentially identical to those of the National Association of Evangelicals, the Evangelical Press Association, and the Evangelical Christian Publishers Association.
