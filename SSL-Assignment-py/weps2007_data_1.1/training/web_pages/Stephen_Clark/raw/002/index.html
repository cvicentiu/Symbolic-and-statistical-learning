Professor Stephen Clark
Professor of Law
 

 
 News Channel 9 (2003)

Office Hours
Spring 2007 - by appt
J.D., Yale Law School, 1995
B.A., University of Tennessee, 1991

Admitted to bar in Illinois. Staff Asst., Senator Al Gore (1992); Notes Editor, Yale Law & Policy Review (1994-95); Fellow, ACLU of Illinois (1995); Clerk, Chief Justice Randall T. Shepard, Indiana Supreme Court (1995-96); Assoc., Labor and Employment Relations Dept., Winston & Strawn, Chicago (1996-99); Visiting Asst. Prof., U. Toledo College of Law (1999-2000); Asst. Prof., Albany Law School (2000-03); Assoc. Prof. (2003-06); Full Prof. (since 2006); Member, Bd. of Dir., NYCLU (since 2004).

Prof. Clark's current research interests include employment discrimination, gay rights, constitutional law, and conflict of laws.

He lives in Washington, DC, with his partner of 11 years, Andrew Potts, who is an associate at Nixon Peabody LLP. They have been registered reciprocal beneficiaries under Hawai`i law since 1998.
