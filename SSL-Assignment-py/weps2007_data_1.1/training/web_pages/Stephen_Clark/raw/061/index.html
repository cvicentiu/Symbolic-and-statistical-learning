Stephen Clark






Have a question for Stephen?

Write to him here.


(WCBS) Stephen Clark joined News 2 on April 21, 1997, as a weekday co-anchor for the News 2 at 5 and 11 with Dana Tyler. In January 2000 his assignment changed to co-anchor of News 2 at 4,6 and 11.

Clark is an experienced anchor and seasoned reporter. So far at News 2, he has covered numerous breaking news stories from the field and from the anchor desk. He traveled with Pope John Paul II to Cuba in January 1998 and again to St. Louis in 1999, providing live reports for News 2 and CBS network newscasts.

His anchoring and reporting has earned him numerous awards including a coveted Emmy for his coverage of the war in Bosnia. He also earned a First Place Investigative Reporters and Editors Award for a series of stories exposing the illegal dumping of human waste onto farmlands. His reporting has taken Clark from the battlefields of Somalia and the Berlin Wall to the riots of south central Los Angeles and the earthquakes of Southern California. From the studio, Clark served as host and moderator of President Clinton's first electronic town hall meeting.

Clark writes a weekly column for this web site dealing with the lighter side of technology. "Technology Bytes" can be found in the News 2000 section of this site.

Clark came to News 2 from San Diego's KGTV where he worked for nearly nine years as front-line anchor and reporter. He began his television career in 1979 at KRDO in his hometown of Colorado Springs, Colorado. From 1981 to 1984, he reported and anchored for KSN-TV in Wichita, Kansas. He worked from 1984 to 1988 as Washington DC bureau chief, reporter and anchor for KCNC-TV in Denver, Colorado.

Clark, an occasional novelist and professional musician, lives with his wife and two daughters in New York.
