Research Interests

My research is in Natural Language Processing (also known as Computational Linguistics), a subfield of Artificial Intelligence. Applications include automatically translating sentences from one language to another, summarising scientific articles, answering questions, and extracting information from text. I have developed a large suite of language processing tools with James Curran from the University of Sydney (accessible through the link opposite).

Recent Publications

  • Stephen Clark and James Curran, 'Partial Training for a Lexicalized-Grammar Parser' in Proceedings of the Annual Meeting of the North American Association for Computational Linguistics, 144-151, New York, 2006
  • Stephen Clark and James Curran, 'Parsing the WSJ using CCG and Log-Linear Models' in Proceedings of the Annual Meeting of the Association for Computational Linguistics, 104-111, Barcelona, Spain, 2004
