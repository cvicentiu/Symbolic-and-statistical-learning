Stephen Clark, PT, DPT, MHS, OCS, MBA - CEO and Founder

Dr. Stephen Clark is recognized as one of the premier sports medicine physical therapists in the nation having successfully treated Olympic, professional, and collegiate athletes. One of the few Board Certified Physical Therapists in the country representing the top two percent of the profession, Dr. Stephen Clark has devoted the past 17 years of his career to treating and caring for injured people. An avid athlete who has successfully recovered from four knee surgeries, one shoulder surgery, one thumb surgery, and three broken ankles, Dr. Stephen Clark knows how difficult it is to live with a debilitating injury. Thus, his philosophy is to partner with the patient to achieve his or her goal of returning to sports and activities of choice free from pain or dysfunction.

Education is the solid foundation for Dr. Stephen Clark's practice. Dr. Stephen Clark holds an undergraduate degree in physical therapy from Ithaca College, a Master's in Health Science from the University of Indianapolis, an MBA in Healthcare Management from California State University San Marco, and a Doctorate from Western University. He holds a National Board Certification as an Orthopedic Clinical Specialist. Dr. Stephen Clark's commitment to education coupled with years of practical experience enables him to deliver patients the best possible care.

Prior to opening Athletic Physical Therapy, Dr. Stephen Clark served as the Los Angeles-based Director of Physical Therapy at the Sports Club Company, as the president and founder of Golden Triangle Rehabilitation in La Jolla, CA, as well as the president of Steve Clark Seminars—a national teaching organization where physical therapists learned Clark's specialized evaluation and treatment techniques.

Dr. Stephen Clark is an active member of organizations including the American Physical Therapy Association (APTA) and the Orthopedic and Sports sections for APTA's California chapter, has served as an adjunct professor at New York University, and lectures regularly nationwide on topics including "Sports Injuries of the Knee," "Mechanics of the Shoulder," and "Prevention and Treatment of Weight Lifting Injuries".

Steve and his wife enjoy living in their new home in Calabasas, CA.
