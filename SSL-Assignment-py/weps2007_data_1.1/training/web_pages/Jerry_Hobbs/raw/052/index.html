Father Charged In Girls' Murders

News Conference Held To Announce Charges

POSTED: 6:35 am CDT May 10, 2005
UPDATED: 6:34 am CDT May 11, 2005

Email This Story | Print This Story
Sign Up for Breaking News Alerts
ZION, Ill. -- Lake County State's Attorney Michael Waller announced Tuesday that charges have been filed in the murders of two girls found stabbed to death in Zion Monday morning.

Jerry Branton Hobbs III, 35, is charged with the the stabbing and beating deaths of his 8-year-old daughter, Laura Hobbs, and her 9-year-old best friend, Krystal Tobias. They had disappeared after taking a bike ride Sunday.

Lake County Coroner Richard Keller said both girls had been beaten and stabbed multiple times in the area where they were found and then left to die in the woods.

Waller said that he was unable to share the details of the crime prior to a bond hearing scheduled Wednesday morning for Hobbs, but he did say, "This horrific crime has terrorized and traumatized the Zion community and I think it's safe to say people of good will everywhere."

Amy Jacobson reported after a Monday afternoon news conference that authorities told her that Hobbs had confessed to the crimes. Jacobson said that Laura Hobbs had been grounded for the weekend and that her mother had lifted the punishment on Mother's Day, angering the girl's father. Jerry Hobbs apparently told authorities that he went to the park looking for his daughter. When he found her with Krystal Tobias, he punched both girls in the face, beat them and stabbed them to death.

The bodies of the girls were found in a field in a nature preserve Monday morning after being reported missing Sunday night.

NBC5 reported that Laura's father was questioned Monday night and Tuesday morning by investigators.

Records show Jerry Hobbs has an extensive criminal history dating back to 1990 in Texas, including arrests for assault and resisting arrest.

Arthur Hollabaugh, 51, said Tuesday that it was he and his son-in-law, Jerry Hobbs, who found the bodies of the girls Monday morning. The two led police to the bodies just off a wooded bike path, with Hobbs claiming he had spotted them while searching for his missing daughter with the girl's grandfather.

Jerry Hobbs

Hollabaugh said that police searched the home where Laura lived and said they took measurements of Hobbs' shoe soles.

"They went through our stuff, took clothes. They've taken his and my daughter's and taken the computer to see if my granddaughter was on an Internet chat room," Hollabaugh said.

Hollabaugh said investigators also talked to Laura's siblings about Hobbs. He said Hobbs had just returned to the area about a month earlier after serving time in a Texas prison.

"Jerry just got out of prison for aggravated assault and I think they're holding that against him," Hollabaugh said. "I don't think he did it."

NBC5 has learned that Hobbs has two convictions on aggravated assault charges in 2001 and 1990, and was also charged with assault in 1997. Hobbs has also been arrested on drug charges and for evading arrest.

The Chicago Tribune reported Tuesday that charges in the case could be filed by the end of the day, but police weren't commenting on the case Tuesday morning.

Hollabaugh said that Laura's bike was in the bushes part way down the ravine when he and her father spotted it after searching for two missing girls through the night. Minutes later, he said the girl's father was screaming that he had found the bodies.

Authorities said Monday they had not recovered a weapon and did not believe the girls had been sexually assaulted.

Police, though, were also pursuing dozens of other tips in the case, and are trying to get in touch with about 85 sex offenders who live in the area, NBC5's Amy Jacobson reported.
