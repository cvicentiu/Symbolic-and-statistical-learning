Police Filing Murder Charges Against Slain Girl's Father

Girls Found Stabbed To Death In Chicago Suburb

POSTED: 4:35 am PDT May 10, 2005
UPDATED: 3:55 pm PDT May 10, 2005

Email This Story | Print This Story
Sign Up for Breaking News Alerts
ZION, Ill. -- Authorities say they are filing murder charges against the father of one of two girls found stabbed to death in a suburban Chicago park.

Images: Community Seeks Answers

Lake County State's Attorney Michael Waller announced Tuesday that Jerry Branton Hobbs III, 34, is charged with the stabbing and beating deaths of his 8-year-old daughter, Laura Hobbs, and her 9-year-old best friend, Krystal Tobias. They had disappeared after taking a bike ride Sunday.

Lake County Coroner Richard Keller said both girls had been beaten and stabbed multiple times, then left to die in the wooded area where they were found.

Waller said that he was unable to share the details of the crime prior to a bond hearing scheduled Wednesday morning for Hobbs, but he did say, "This horrific crime has terrorized and traumatized the Zion community and -- I think it's safe to say -- people of good will everywhere."

Chicago television station WMAQ reported that authorities said that Hobbs had confessed to the crimes. Laura Hobbs had been grounded for the weekend, but her mother had lifted the punishment on Mother's Day, angering the girl's father, WMAQ reported.

Police said Jerry Hobbs told them that he went to the park looking for his daughter, and when he found her with Krystal Tobias, he punched both girls in the face, beat them and stabbed them to death, WMAQ reported.

The bodies of the girls were found in a field in a nature preserve Monday morning after being reported missing Sunday night.

The father was questioned Monday night and Tuesday morning by investigators.

Records show Jerry Hobbs has an extensive criminal history dating back to 1990 in Texas, including arrests for assault and resisting arrest. Hobbs has also been arrested on drug charges and for evading arrest.

Arthur Hollabaugh, 51, said Tuesday that it was he and his son-in-law, Jerry Hobbs, who found the bodies of the girls Monday morning. The two led police to the bodies just off a wooded bike path, with Hobbs claiming he had spotted them while searching for his missing daughter with the girl's grandfather.

Jerry Hobbs

Hollabaugh said that police searched the home where Laura lived and said they took measurements of Hobbs' shoe soles.

"They went through our stuff, took clothes. They've taken his and my daughter's (clothes) and taken the computer to see if my granddaughter was on an Internet chat room," Hollabaugh said.

Hollabaugh said investigators also talked to Laura's siblings about Hobbs. He said Hobbs had just returned to the area about a month earlier after serving time in a Texas prison.

"Jerry just got out of prison for aggravated assault and I think they're holding that against him," Hollabaugh said. "I don't think he did it."

Hollabaugh said that Laura's bike was in the bushes part way down the ravine when he and her father spotted it after searching for two missing girls through the night. Minutes later, he said the girl's father was screaming that he had found the bodies.

Authorities said Monday they had not recovered a weapon and did not believe the girls had been sexually assaulted.

Girls' Killings Shock Community

A public meeting was planned for Tuesday night in Zion to help area residents deal with the tragedy.

Flowers and cards of sympathy were left at the Hobbs' home Tuesday morning.

The family was receiving visitors, but not commenting on the case, WMAQ reported.

At Krystal's house, her brother, Alberto Seguro, told reporters that police have had little contact with his family.

"Before she died, I never got to say I loved her. I just hope they work as hard as they can to try to find him," Seguro said.

Crisis counselors on Tuesday were at Beulah Elementary, where the girls were in the same second-grade class.

Most parents took their kids to school, WMAQ reported.

"I just could not sleep last night. I went from room to room checking on my kids," parent Brydie Nixon said.

"She didn't want to come to school," parent Cheryl Maldonado said. "It's just hard. Both of the girls were very good friends of my daughter and now they're not here. All she has is pictures for memories."
