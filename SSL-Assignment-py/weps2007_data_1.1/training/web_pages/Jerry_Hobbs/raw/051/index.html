Police: Father confessed to girls' murders in Zion park

May 11, 2005 (Zion, IL) - Bond was denied for a man accused of murdering his daughter and her best friend. Jerry Hobbs allegedly beat and stabbed Laura Hobbs, 8, and Krystal Tobias, 9, to death in a Zion park on Mother's Day. Prosecutors said he confessed to the killings while in police custody.

The 34-year-old admitted to killing his daughter and her best friend, according to prosecutors, in written and videotape confessions. An argument and confrontation with Laura over the second-grader's punishment led to the murders, prosecutors said. Officials say the ex-convict, who was recently reunited with his family in Zion, has a serious anger management problem.

When investigators saw the bodies for the first time Monday morning, they knew immediately that the killer acted with extraordinary rage. Laura Hobbs had been stabbed 20 times and Krystal Tobias had been stabbed 11 times. They were laying together with their shoes placed neatly along side their bodies.

"You can see through the injuries to the individuals the rage that was exhibited. This is the slaughter of two little girls," said Jeffrey Pavletic, assistant state's attorney.

Laura's father, Jerry Hobbs found the girls on Monday. They had been missing since Sunday night. Prosecutors say he became a suspect several hours after the girls were discovered when he somehow described the girls' wounds after he claimed never to have been within 20 feet of either corpse.

"He gave a full oral statement. He then signed a statement as to what had happened, and then he gave a videotaped statement as well," said Pavletic.

Released from a Texas prison in early April, Hobbs reportedly told police that his daughter, Laura had stolen money from her mother. As punishment, she was grounded. However, her mother decided to let the girl play with her best friend on Sunday. Hobbs reportedly did not agree with the decision so he allegedly followed Laura and Krystal to the park and told his daughter to go home. When she refused, Hobbs allegedly hit her. Hobbs claimed Krystal had a knife of some sort with her and came to her best friend's defense. That is when Hobbs reportedly struck her and then stabbed them both several times.

"He then stated that during this period of time, Krystal, who was coming to the aid of Laura, had pulled out what he described as a potato knife. He said once that knife was pulled out that is when he struck Krystal. He then took the knife from her and repeatedly began to stab both girls," said Pavletic.

State's Attorney Waller said he did not believe Hobbs' claim that Krystal attacked a 6'1" 34-year-old man with a potato knife, which investigators say still has not been recovered. ABC 7 has learned that police have recovered what they believe is physical evidence, including blood-stained clothing that will link Hobbs to the murders.

"Decisions about a case like this should not be made in the heat of the moment. My heart goes out to the family, friends, and the loved ones of these two little girls," said David Brodsky, public defender.

In court Wednesday, Hobbs told the judge he was unemployed and needed to be represented by the Public Defender's Office. A preliminary hearing is scheduled for June 9.

Laura Hobbs' grandfather told ABC 7 that authorities are "railroading" Hobbs and using him as a scapegoat. Krystal's older brother spoke for the Tobias family.

"You know, he just sits in jail and rots. I don't want him to die fast," said Alberto Segura, Krystal's brother.

Even though Hobbs was in court, police insist that the investigation is still open and ongoing.

ABC7 have learned some details about Krystal Tobias' funeral. The visitation will be Friday from 5 p.m. to 8 p.m. at a Camden funeral home in Zion and the funeral is scheduled for Saturday morning at 10:30 at First Baptist Church in Winthrop harbor. Funeral arrangements for Laura Hobbs are pending.

Hobbs has violent history

Sheila Hollabaugh, mother of Laura Hobbs, hugs a friend. She was the victim of beatings by Hobbs.

Hobbs has an extensive criminal history dating back to 1990 in Texas, including prior arrests for assault and resisting arrest. The ABC 7 I-Team has learned that there are some disturbing similarities between Hobbs' past criminal history and the murders in Zion.

Police in Wichita Falls say that Jerry Branton Hobbs III had one of the longest rap sheets in town. At age 34, Hobbs' criminal arrests have covered nearly half his life, beginning in 1990.

The ABC I-Team examined Hobbs' six-inch thick police file. Most are aggravated assaults committed while in drunken fits of rage. While Hobbs attacked friends, strangers and police officers, many of the charges list Sheila Hollabaugh as the victim of beatings by Hobbs. Hollabaugh is the mother of Hobbs' three children -- one of whom he allegedly stabbed to death on Mother's Day.

Wichita police say women were frequently targets of Hobbs' rage.

The I-Team has learned that it was on a rundown Wichita Falls street in 1990 that Hobbs was arrested for his first knifing offense. It was the result of an alcohol-fueled quarrel over somebody burning rubber with his car.

According to his police file, then-19-year-old Hobbs was wearing a pink bandana when he produced a wooden handle hunting knife and plunged the five-inch blade into his landlord's gut and then stated, according to witnesses, "I'm not going to stab you, I'm going to kill you." The victim lived.

Before last Sunday's child killings in Illinois, Hobbs' most infamous incident occurred in a trailer lot when police say he revved up his chainsaw and threatened bystanders.

"The assault continued outside of their trailer. Some of the neighbor men intervened and tried to stop him from beating her. He obtained a chain saw, started it, and was trying to cut these people with the chain saw," said Sgt. Cindy Walker, Wichita Falls police.

John McElroy subdued Hobbs by hitting him in the back with a shovel.

"Well he started chasing the kids, swinging the chainsaw. To me it was kind of strange cause in my mind I was thinking I gotta stop this guy, at the same time I'm thinking Texas Chainsaw Massacre you know. So I follow him just before he got up to the kids, about a few feet. My youngest son got in front of him and he swung something the same time I took my shovel and hit him in the square of the back and knocked him down," said John McElroy, witnessed the incident.

David Roosevelt is Hobbs' brother-in-law and they lived together for a time. Roosevelt says it didn't happen that way. He claims Hobbs was merely defending himself.

"He got away from him and he made it to his truck and just so happened there was a chain saw back there," said Roosevelt.

Hobbs' relatives prefer to recall the time he saved the life of his six-month-old niece who shocked by an electrical cord.

"He brought her back in the house and filled up the bathtub with cold water and put her in the bathtub and I guess it shocked her. And she went to crying and stuff and he called 911. Even the doctors said if it wasn't for him, she wouldn't be here today," said Roosevelt.

That incident though is greatly outnumbered by instances of Hobbs' hysterical violence.

However, Jerry Hobbs' relatives in Texas say they are mortified by the murders.

"He would never start trouble or nothing like that," said Roosevelt.

Hobbs' criminal history also includes a 1990 arrest for aggravated assault, a 1994 conviction for driving with a suspended license and marijuana possession.

"We're glad he's not here anymore, but it's just sickening," said Walker.

Zion residents mourn two young girls

Flowers sit in front of a victim's home.

The charges have closed a very emotional chapter for residents in the small community of Zion who first rallied together to search for the girls when they were reported missing. They have now come together to heal.

Where Laura Hobbs lived, the once talkative family sent out a spokesperson Wednesday.

"The entire family wants to express their deepest sympathy for the Krystal Tobias family and thank the Zion community for their support throughout this trying time," said Jeremy Harter, Hobbs family spokesman.

Down the block where Laura's best friend Krystal Tobias lived, her family now in seclusion sent out a priest.

"They have absolutely no thoughts about anything beyond their daughter and how this happened, why this happened, right now it's just to try to console themselves," said Fr. Gary Graf, Tobias family pastor.

Many people in Zion were shocked to hear the graphic details of the killings in court, including that Jerry Hobbs allegedly stabbed his own daughter 20 times.

"He struck her at least twice in the face -- I'm referring to Laura -- knocking her to the ground and at that point turned and struck Krystal in the face and took a knife and began to stab them," said Jeff Pavletic, assistant Lake County state's attorney.

"That is so sad. How could anyone do that to their child?" asked Laura Brown, Zion resident.

It is a question no one has an answer for as people continue to visit the growing memorial in Beulah Park near where the girls were left to die.

"I am just choked up with empathy for the family and it is hard to say, just like you can't breathe," said Sherene Jones, Zion resident.

Constance Collins, superintendent of Zion schools, say she does not believe Hobbs' claims that Krystal was carrying a knife which he says he used to kill the girls.

"I think that is probably the furthest thing from the truth. And I think that as we all have read that Krystal 'had a knife,' no way," said Constance Collins, school supt.

Second-graders at the school where both of the girls attended made hearts Wednesday that they wore in honor of the two young victims.
