Jerry Hobbs; A Good Example Of Why We Have A Death Penalty

By Wayne Adkins
May 14, 2005

Jerry Hobbs is a perfect example of why there is a death penalty and why there always should be. Here is a man who has admitted to the brutal beating and stabbing to death of his own daughter and her best friend on Mother’s Day. He had previously served time for assault and had once chased his neighbors with a chainsaw. Jerry Hobbs deserves to die.

There are those who would say that the death penalty is just vengeance killing; a murder for a murder. They would argue that to take any life devalues human life. I would argue that not avenging the heinous deaths of two second grade girls would devalue their lives. Jerry Hobbs let us all know how much value he places on a human life, even the life of his own daughter. Is there any reason why society should place a higher value on his life than he placed on his victims lives? Hobbs has allegedly told police that he was enraged because his daughter had taken forty dollars from her mother’s purse. He gave his own daughter and her best friend the death penalty for petty theft. He made a decision about what life is worth when he hacked those two children to death and we should hold him to that decision. If alleged petty theft warrants death in his mind, then double murder should warrant no less.

I’ll go a step further. As soon as he enters a guilty plea he should be executed within the week. The families of those victims should not have to work every day and pay taxes to house, feed, educate and provide medical care and legal representation for their daughter’s killer. I shouldn’t either. In fact, there should be far fewer life in prison sentences and a lot more death penalty recipients. When a person does something like this and admits it to police and pleads guilty to it in a court of law they should die immediately. It’s a simple matter of expedience at that point. We already know this person will die in prison. It’s just a matter of whether we get it over with or drag it out for decades while tax payers bear the burden and other criminals, perhaps some like Jerry Hobbs, are released because we don’t have the space or resources to keep them in jail for their entire sentences. Let’s free up some of that space and resources. Let’s find our collective backbone and give those criminals what they have coming to them. And most of all, let’s give the victims and their families the justice they have coming to them.

Our system of justice is not perfect. But it is not in the trial or sentencing phases that the biggest problem resides. It is in the punishment phase that our system falls apart. The death penalty is just that, a penalty. Allowing condemned men to live out their natural lives means that they have not paid the penalty they were sentenced to. We all die eventually. The death penalty means nothing if we keep the guilty on death row until they die of natural causes. Granted, death row is not a pleasant place to spend ones life, but I’m sure it beats being stabbed to death when you’re in the second grade.
