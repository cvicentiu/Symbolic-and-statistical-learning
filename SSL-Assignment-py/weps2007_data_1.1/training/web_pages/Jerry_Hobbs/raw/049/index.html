A General Theory of Parallelism and the Special Case of VP Ellipsis

Jerry R. Hobbs, SRI International, Menlo Park CA

I will provide a general account of parallelism in discourse, and apply it to the special case of resolving possible readings for instances of VP ellipsis, showing how several problematic examples are accounted for in a natural and straightforward fashion in this framework. By combining this with accounts of other, interacting phenomena, a greater range of cases is handled than in previous approaches. The generality of the approach makes it directly applicable to a variety of other types of ellipsis and reference in discourse. This is a joint work with Andrew Kehler.
