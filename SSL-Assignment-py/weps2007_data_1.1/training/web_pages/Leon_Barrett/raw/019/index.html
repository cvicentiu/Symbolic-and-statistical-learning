Seton Hall men's basketball: Allen, Barrett say they'll stay

Friday, March 08, 2002

BY PAT BORZI
Star-Ledger Staff

The rumors have been out there for weeks, spread through Internet chat rooms and message boards or by old-fashioned word of mouth. They caught up with John Munson, John Allen's father, last Friday night at Coatesville (Pa.) High -- Allen's alma mater -- when Munson took in the Coatesville-Chester district playoff game.

"Guys were coming up to me and saying, 'Is John going to Maryland? Is he going here (another school)?'" Munson said.
The perception that freshman forward Allen and sophomore point guard Andre Barrett, the foundation of Seton Hall's future, may transfer after a turbulent 12-18 season poses the biggest off-season dilemma for the Pirates. Losing either would be devastating for first-year coach Louis Orr and the program, especially given the strength of New Jersey high school juniors considering the Hall, Seton Hall Prep's Jamar Nutter among them.

For now, Barrett says he isn't going anywhere. "I'm not going to transfer or anything like that," Barrett said Wednesday night after the Hall's seventh consecutive loss, a 64-58 loss to St. John's in the first round of the Big East Tournament, ensured its first season without an NCAA Tournament or NIT appearance since 1997-98.

Allen also pledged to stay, then hedged.

"I'm on the ship," Allen said. "I'm not going to jump off the ship when it's going down. I'm not going to do that.

"If I decide to transfer, if it comes up in my mind ... right now, I'm not thinking about it. I'm going to work, try to get better, and try to make Seton Hall better. That's my plan right now."

A season brimming with controversies and discord, from ill-timed player comments to the George Jackson/Desmond Herod film-session scuffle, has basketball intelligentsia speculating about the future of both players. Each would have to sit out one season if they transfer, two within the Big East. Orr was not available for comment yesterday.

Both players were Tommy Amaker recruits; Allen signed a letter of intent before Amaker departed for Michigan and would have been forced to sit out two seasons if he reneged.

Both bought into Orr's motion offense and scrappy defensive philosophy. Barrett led the team in scoring (16.9 points), assists (5.03) and minutes (34.5) while turning into Orr's most vocal supporter. Allen, an Orr favorite who took Marcus Toney-El's starting job, finished third on the team in scoring (11.4) and second in rebounding (5.5).

But neither expected the team to flounder like it did, especially after the Hall nearly beat Duke in the second game of the season at the Maui Invitational.

"I'm hearing all the rumors," Munson said. "It's not coming from his mother's mouth, my mouth or his mouth. We sit down and discuss everything, and the only discussing has been summer school. Right now, our objective is staying where we are."

However, Munson said his son's future is linked to Barrett's.

"When Amaker left, if we had a point guard, we were staying," Munson said. "If not, we were going to do what we had to do. We had a point guard, and we stayed. We still have a point guard, as far as I know."

Leon Barrett, Andre's father, said the Hall still does.

"(Transferring) is out of the question," the elder Barrett said. "Last year, when Amaker left, we seriously considered it. When he made the decision to stay, that meant, whatever happened, he had to accept it. I don't like him bouncing back and forth when things are going bad.

"As much as I'd like to see him play in the NCAAs, how he deals with all this will help him and make him more of a man in the long run. And he's not going to run out on Andre Sweet."

The 6-6 Sweet, Barrett's teammate at Rice High School in New York City and his current roommate, transferred from Duke and is eligible next year.
