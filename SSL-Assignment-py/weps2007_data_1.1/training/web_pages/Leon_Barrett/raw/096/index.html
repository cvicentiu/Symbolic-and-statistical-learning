Weir out of hospital following head clash

COLIN STEWART

DAVID Weir was discharged from hospital yesterday afternoon but is expected to be out of action for up to three weeks following a clash of heads in Everton's Carling Cup victory over Luton on Tuesday night.

Everton cruised to a 4-0 win that included a goal from Scotland striker James McFadden, but the game was marred by an innocuous challenge on Weir, who was left unconscious on the pitch before being carried off and taken to Fazackerley Hospital. "We will be assessing him on a daily basis," said the club's physio, Mick Rathbone.

Weir clashed heads with Luton's Leon Barnett and required five minutes of treatment. The Scotland defender was kept in hospital overnight for exploratory head scans and Everton assistant manager Alan Irvine revealed the concern the Goodison Park players had for their skipper.

"The lads were in the dressing room and they did not know how David was. They were very concerned and wanted to find out how he was before they went back on to the pitch," said Irvine. "Everyone was very relieved at that point.

"You do not like to see things like that, although it was an accident. There's no blame attached to Leon Barrett. Our physio and doctor were able to reassure everyone that David was awake, OK, and that he was only going to hospital for precautionary tests. You have to do those with a head injury these days and then you are out for two or three weeks."
