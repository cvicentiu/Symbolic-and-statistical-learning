Albion bags Hammers deal

From correspondents in London
 January 09, 2007

WEST Ham United has signed West Bromich Albion's Scotland midfield player Nigel Quashie in a £1.5 million ($3.8 million) deal.

The fee will rise to £2 million ($5 million) if the struggling London side avoids relegation from the English Premier League.

West Brom manager Tony Mowbray confirmed overnight: "Nigel recently expressed an interest in getting back to the Premier League.

"When West Ham lodged their interest in signing Nigel, coupled with my desire to create some revenue to bring in fresh faces, I thought it was an opportunity which suited all parties."

Quashie joined the Baggies from Southampton in a £1.2 million ($3 million) deal minutes before last January's transfer window closed.

The 28-year-old made 30 appearances for the Championship club, scoring once in a 3-1 defeat at Arsenal in April.

His departure will give Mowbray extra spending power to press on with his own plans to bring in new blood.

He is set to lodge a £4 million ($10 million) bid for the Hibernian midfield duo of Scott Brown and Kevin Thompson from his former club.

And Mowbray is also keen to bring the Luton Town pair of striker Rowan Vine and defender Leon Barrett to The Hawthorns.

Agence France-Presse
