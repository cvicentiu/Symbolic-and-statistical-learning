WEIR GETS ALL-CLEAR
By Neil Cameron

SCOTLAND star Davie Weir was discharged from hospital yesterday after being told his head injury was not serious.

The 36-year-old Everton defender was knocked unconscious following an accidental clash with Luton's Leon Barrett during the first half of Tuesday night's Carling Cup match.

It was feared Weir had become the latest Premiership player to suffer a serious head knock but after a night's stay in hospital he was released.

Although he will be out of action for up to three weeks, Everton are happy with the results of skull and neck X-rays. Club physio Mick Rathbone said: "We will be assessing him on a daily basis."

Everton No.2 Alan Irvine revealed the team were more concerned with the condition of their skipper than the cup tie.

Irvine said: "The lads were in the dressing room at half-time and did not know how David was. They were very concerned and wanted to find out how he was before they went back on to the pitch. You do not like to see things like that, although it was an accident and there's no blame attached to Leon Barrett."
