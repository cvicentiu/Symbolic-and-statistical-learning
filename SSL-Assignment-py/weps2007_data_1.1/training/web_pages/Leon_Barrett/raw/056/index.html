Everton hopeful over Weir

By Peter ORourke -  Created on 25 Oct 2006

Everton are waiting to find out the full extent of David Weir's head injury sustained in the Carling Cup win over Luton.

The Toffees eased into the fourth round with a comfortable 4-0 win over Luton on Tuesday night, but the victory was marred by the injury to Weir.

Weir picked up a nasty head injury after an accidental collision with Luton's Leon Barnett.

The Scottish international was knocked unconscious from the clash and had to be taken to hospital after being carried off the pitch.

Everton assistant manager Alan Irvine is hopeful that the injury is not too serious and that Weir will only be out of action for two or three weeks.

"The lads were in the dressing room and they did not know how David was, they were very concerned and wanted to find out how he was before they went back on to the pitch," said Irvine. "Everyone was very relieved at that point.

"You do not like to see things like that, although it was an accident, there's no blame attached to Leon Barrett.

"Our physio and doctor were able to re-assure everyone that David was awake, okay, and that he was only going to hospital for precautionary tests, you have to do those with a head injury these days and then you are out for two or three weeks."

Irvine also praised Everton for their display in dispatching Luton.

"I felt we were fabulous, we played some excellent stuff and it could have been many more," added Irvine.

"Had it not been for their 'keeper Marlon Beresford it would have been.

"We knew Luton were a decent team, they had players who could have caused us a few problems. They had chances, hit the post and Iain Turner made some very good saves.

"But our play was very good and we could have scored a lot more goals than we got."

Luton boss Mike Newell had no complaints with the result and admitted that there was a gulf in class.

"There is a gulf between the Premiership and the Championship and you saw that here," said Newell.

"Everton have some fine players and frankly ours did not do themselves justice, they didn't give a good account of themselves.

"That is a shame because they have been playing well, but they did not have conviction or belief and maybe at times were a little scared. We have players who just did not play to their best."
