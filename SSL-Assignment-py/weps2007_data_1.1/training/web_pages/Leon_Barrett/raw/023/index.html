Young farmers recognized for class attendance

Press Release

The Marshall County Young and Adult Farmer Educaiton Banquet on March 21 was sponsored by area businesses and recognizes both class attendance and Kentucky Young Farmer Contest participation. Those recognized for class attendance include: one year attendance, Mike Seibert, Marty Barrett, Bill Gibson, Ruble Powell, Zakery Riley, Wyatt Riley and Keylee Miller; five year class attendance, Leon Barrett; ten year class attendance, Wendell Norwood; 15 year class attendance, John Nimmo, Tracy Jarvis, ANnie Futrell and Brenda Lamb; 20 year class attendance, Galon Riley.

Those recognized for Young Farmer Associaiton Contest Participation included: Travis Miller, state winner in white corn production; Will Edwards, second place in state ag mechanics proficiency and lcoal winner in soybean production; Brian McClain, second place in state in beef cow production and Marshall County outstanding member and Keith Harris, state winner in dark fired tobacco production and diversified farming, second place in state in dark air cured tobacco production and third place in state in burley tobacco production.

The adult farmer education classes are held at the Marshall County Technical Center and is a program funded by KCTCS. McClain is chapter president of the Marshall County Young Farmer Association and Jeff Futrell is advisor. For mroe information about the program call the Marshall County Technical Center.

This press release was submitted by Jeff Futrell.
