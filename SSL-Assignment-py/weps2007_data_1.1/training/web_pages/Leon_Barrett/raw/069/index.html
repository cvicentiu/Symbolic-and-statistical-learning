Death Notices

April 23, 2002
 
Allen, Vira Estelle

NASHVILLE — Vira Estelle Allen, 88, died April 20, 2002, at Baptist Hospital. Mrs. Allen was preceded in death by her husband, John William Allen, Jan. 7, 1970; three grandchildren, Steven Leon Barrett, Mark Stanley Barrett and James Dalvin Allen; and son-in-law, William Radford Jones. She was a member of Southside Church of Christ. She is the daughter of Henry Mason and Marietta Bell McBroom Jared of Baxter, Tenn. Survivors include her children, Thelma Jones, Wilda (William) Luttrell, John D. Allen and Charles D. (Barbara) Allen; 13 grandchildren; 14 great-grandchildren; sisters, Bessie Patton and Elizaeth Owen; sister-in-law, Odel Jared; and brother, Tommy (Betty) Jared. A funeral service will be conducted at 2 p.m. today at Woodbine Funeral Home, Hickory Chapel, 5852 Nolensville Road, with Hugh O. Price and Mark Parkhurst officiating. Interment will be in Woodlawn Cemetery. Grandsons will serve as pallbearers.
Woodbine Funeral Home
331-1952


Brock, George L. Jr.

NASHVILLE — George L. Brock Jr., 78, died April 21, 2002. Mr. Brock was preceded in death by his parents, George L. and Lillian Brock Sr.; his sister, Carolyn Brock; and his brothers, Allen and Tommy Brock. Survivors include his sister, Martha Johnson Nocton of Nashville; and brother, Billy M. Brock of Kansas City, Kan.; and nieces and nephews. Visitation will be held from 5-9 p.m. today at Woodbine Funeral Home, 3620 Nolensville Road. A graveside service will be conducted at 1 p.m. Wednesday at Woodlawn Cemetery with Mike Brasher officiating. Pallbearers are nephews and great-nephews.
Woodbine Funeral Home
832-1948


Curtis, Michael Duane

NASHVILLE — Michael Duane Curtis, 34, died April 19, 2002. He was a member of Cofer’s Chapel Free Will Baptist Church and previously worked for Johnny Hobbs of Quick Pic and was presently employed by K.B. Toys. Survivors include his parents, Floyd Wayne and Nellie Curtis; brother and sister-in-law, Danny and Mary Curtis; grandparents, Roy Lee and Elizabeth Davison and Alice Curtis; uncles and aunts, Roy Lee and Alice Davison Jr., and Joe and Joanne Clark. Visitation will be held from 10-11 a.m. today at the church. A funeral service will be conducted at 11 a.m. today at Cofer’s Chapel Free Will Baptist Church, 3915 Franklin Pike, with the Rev. Michael Waddell officiating. Interment will be in Woodlawn Cemetery. Honorary pallbearers are friends at Saturn Drive Baptist Church, Gilda's Club Nashville, members and parents of Glencliff High School Band and Band Boosters. Active pallbearers are men from Cofer’s Chapel Free Will Baptist Church. Memorial contributions may be made to Gilda's Club Nashville or Alive Hospice.
Woodbine Funeral Home
832-1948


Frazier, Nadine Whitfield

WEST NASHVILLE — Nadine Whitfield Frazier, 73, died April 21, 2002. She was a member and past president of both the National Women Executives and Women in Construction. She was also a member of the Order of the Eastern Star. She was preceded in death by her brother, Bill Whitfield. Survivors include her husband, Joseph H. Frazier; sons, Michael (Debbie) Frazier, Frank (Kristy) Frazier and Jack Frazier, all of Nashville; daughters, Carol (Rick) Quarles of Franklin, Tenn., and Susie Hare of Nashville; and nine grandchildren. A funeral service will be conducted at 2 p.m. today in Middle Tennessee Veterans Cemetery on McCrory Lane. Donations are encouraged to the Alzheimer's Association. No visitation is scheduled prior to the services.
West Harpeth Funeral Home & Crematory
352-9400


Geddes, Johnetta Worley

Her remains are at Mount Olivet Funeral Home, where a funeral service will be conducted at 2:30 p.m. today. Bishop Kerry Gillespie will officiate. Interment will be in Mount Olivet Cemetery. Jones, William Charles

NASHVILLE — William Charles Jones, 82, died April 21, 2002. Mr. Jones retired as director of engineering after 42 years of service with Nashville Electric Service. A member of the West End Church of Christ, he was a U.S. Navy veteran of World War II. He was preceded in death by his wife, Ada M. Jones. Survivors include his daughters, Donna Jones and Carol (Mike) Yinger; two grandchildren, Katie Yinger and Rachel Yinger; and sister, Jane (James H.) Mackey of Florence, Ala. A funeral service will be conducted Wednesday at Woodlawn Chapel of Roses, with Jim Bill McInter officiating. Active pallbearers are elders and deacons of West End Church of Christ. Honorary pallbearers are West End Church of Christ Men's Bible Class and NES retirees. Memorial contributions may be made to: Churches of Christ Relief Effort Inc., 410 Allied Drive, Nashville, TN 37211. Visitation with the family will be held from 4-7 p.m. today and from noon-2:30 p.m. Wednesday.
Woodlawn Funeral Home
383-4754


Marshall, Ethel Knight Rich

NASHVILLE — Ethel Knight Rich Marshall, 83, died April 20, 2002. She was preceded in death by two husbands, Fred Rich and Burton Marshall. Fred and Ethel owned Fred Rich Millwork and Lumber Co. from 1946-70, when a tractor turned over, killing Fred. In 1974 Ethel married William B. “Burt” Marshall. They, too, had a full life together until he went home to be with the Lord in 1999. Survivors include her daughter, Nancy Rich (Bobby) Stanley of Brentwood; son, Fred Wayne (Peggy) Rich Sr. of Nashville; four grandsons; one granddaughter; nine great-grandchildren; and one great-great-grandchild; sister, Fannie Knight; brother, James Knight; and good friend, Nancy Ratcliffe. A funeral service will be conducted at 1 p.m. Wednesday at Woodbine Funeral Home, Hickory Chapel, 5852 Nolensville Road, with Wendell Byrd officiating. Visitation will be held from 4-8 p.m. today. Interment will be in Woodlawn Cemetery. Grandsons will serve as pallbearers. Memorial contributions may be made to New Horizon.
Woodbine Funeral Home
331-1952
