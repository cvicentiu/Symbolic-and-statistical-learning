Everton wait on Weir

Everton expect to hear on Wednesday that David Weir has no more than a bad headache after the injury that saw him taken to hospital for a head scan and tests.

Everton cruised to a 4-0 win over Luton in the Carling Cup on Tuesday with goals from Tim Cahill, James McFadden, Victor Anichebe and a Keith Keane own goal. But Everton assistant boss Alan Irvine revealed the team were more concerned with the condition of their skipper, who had been unconscious on the pitch before being carried off and taken to Fazackerley Hospital.

Irvine said: "The lads were in the dressing room and they did not know how David was, they were very concerned and wanted to find out how he was before they went back on to the pitch. Everyone was very relieved at that point."

He added: "You do not like to see things like that, although it was an accident, there's no blame attached to Leon Barrett.

"Our physio and doctor were able to re-assure everyone that David was awake, okay, and that he was only going to hospital for precautionary tests, you have to do those with a head injury these days and then you are out for two or three weeks."

Weir spent the night in hospital, and will have been delighted to hear of his team's convincing victory.

Irvine said: "I felt we were fabulous, we played some excellent stuff and it could have been many more. Had it not been for their 'keeper Marlon Beresford it would have been.

"We knew Luton were a decent team, they had players who could have caused us a few problems. They had chances, hit the post and Iain Turner made some very good saves.

"But our play was very good and we could have scored a lot more goals than we got."

Luton boss Mike Newell, disappointed on his return to his former club, said: "There is a gulf between the Premiership and the Championship and you saw that here."
