Children and Politics in Pennsylvania

There are 2,922,221 children under 18 in Pennsylvania.  Many of them face substantial challenges every day.

·          372,192 children live in poverty

·          270,000 children do not have health insurance

·          178,500 teens used alcohol in the past month

·          98,300 teens used an illicit drug in the past month

·          36,733 children were reported abused

·          23,700 of the state’s 143,475 two-year olds have not been immunized

·          1,487 children under 14 died last year and 138 child fatalities have been confirmed as abuse-related since the year 2000

Early Education/Child Care Challenge

In Pennsylvania:

·          60.2% or 508,870 children under six either have both parents working or live in a single-parent household where that parent works

·          73.4% or 168,415 children in single-parent households have a parent who works

·          Costs for child care - $7,384 for 12 month olds and $6,188 for 4 year olds in urban areas, greater than costs of tuition at the state’s public universities

·          65,100 children received federal child care assistance in the most recent year examined, representing 3.1% of children under 13

Pre-school education programs for three and four year-olds have been shown in many studies to have a direct impact on later academic success.  Currently, 152,634 of the states three and four year olds, 51.0% of these kids, currently are not enrolled in a nursery school, pre-school, or pre-kindergarten education programs.  The Governor has proposed $250 million to a block grant that districts can use for pre-K porgrams as well as all-day kindergarten, teacher training, and class size reductions in K-3 and other education programs.

 

Education Challenge

In 2001 Congress passed the landmark No Child Left Behind Education Act that provided for the largest federal commitment to education in history.  However, in the most recent proposed federal budget, Pennsylvania would receive $249,205,500 less than what is mandated by full funding of the act.  However, too many students are not at even the most basic levels for math, reading, science, and writing:

Registration Qualifications
  ·  Be a US citizen for at least
   one month before the next
   election
  ·  Be a resident of PA and your
   election district for at least 30
   days before the next election
  ·  Be at least 18 years of age
   on the day of the next election

Application Information for Individuals Applying to Register to Vote!
  ·  Pennsylvania Voter Registration
   Mail Application Forms must be
   completed and submitted to
   your county voter registration
   office. A link to County Contact
   Information is available on the
   Secretary of State's website.
  ·  Federal Voter Registration
   Applications must be mailed to
   the address provided for
   Pennsylvania citizens.
  ·  Pennsylvania law provides for
   permanent voter registration,
   unless the voter's registration
   should be cancelled in
   accordance with law. All
   electors, except those in the
   military service of the United
   States and bedridden or
   hospitalized war veterans
   unavoidably absent from their
   county of residence, must be
   registered to be eligible to vote.
  ·  Once registered to vote, an
   individual is not required to
   register again unless he or she
   should change his or her
   residence, name, or political
   party affiliation.
  ·  For more information, call
   1-800-552-VOTE.
