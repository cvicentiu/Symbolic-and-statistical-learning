PA Celebration on the Hill Ambassador Registration Deadline Approaches

If you haven’t signed up to be a community ambassador for our first ever Pennsylvania Relay For Life Celebration on the Hill on September 29 in Harrisburg, time is running out!

At the event, our mission will be to convince our state lawmakers to make cancer issues a priority. We will also honor cancer survivors with special victory laps and ceremonies and a stage will host a variety of entertainment and speakers. At dusk, hundreds of luminaria will surround the capitol fountain to honor cancer survivors and remember those lost to the disease.

Go to cancer.org/pacelebration to download an application today!

PA Congressman Todd Platts Introduces FDA Tobacco Authority Legislation

Just recently, Representative Todd Platts introduced a strong, bipartisan bill in the US House to provide the U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA) with meaningful regulatory authority over tobacco products. Because of his efforts, Congress has the opportunity to take action this year and pass a law that would protect the health of millions.

Under this legislation, the FDA would have the authority to require changes in tobacco products, such as reduction or elimination of lethal chemicals, to make them less harmful or less addictive. They could also restrict advertising and promotions that are purposefully targeted at children. In addition, the FDA would have the authority to require tobacco labels to fully disclose ingredients and evaluate whether the tobacco industry is making truthful claims about their products.

Every year tobacco use kills more than 20,000 Pennsylvanians and costs our nation more than $75 billion in health care bills. Congressman Platts’ support for the measure is a vital step toward eliminating tobacco as the leading cause of preventable death from cancer.

The American Cancer Society also supports a similar bill in the US Senate, introduced by Senators DeWine and Kennedy.
