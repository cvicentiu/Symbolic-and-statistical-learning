CENTRAL CUMBERLAND COUNTY TASK FORCE ON REGIONAL DEVELOPMENT

MINUTES

 

July 20, 2004

 

            The Central Cumberland County Task Force on Regional Development met on Tuesday, July 20, 2004, 8:30 a.m., Business Central, 18 North Hanover Street, Carlisle, PA.  The meeting was called to order by Chairman Frank Rankin with the following members present: Frank Rankin, Vic Stabile, Barb Wilson, Deborah Ealer, John Ward, John Blair, and August Ginter.  Also attending were: Commissioner Bruce Barclay; Chief Clerk John Connolly; Kirk Stoner, Cumberland County Planning Commission; Jay Swisher, Todd Platts' Carlisle Office and guests as per attached sign-in sheet.

      Chairman Rankin thanked everyone for attending the meeting and noted they have

two sets of minutes to approve for this meeting.  Ms. Wilson made a motion to approve the minutes of May 18 and June 15, 2004. Ms. Ealer seconded the motion and it unanimously carried.

Mr. Connolly gave an update on the County Emergency Services Action Panel.  He said this group will focus on fire and fire support.  Mr. Connolly said at the first meeting they compiled a list of 20 issues to look at.  The Panel will review the issues that impact the safety

of Cumberland County residents and recommend action on those choices. Mr. Connolly discussed the problem of the difficulty in attracting volunteers for the fire companies.

 Mr. Swisher from Todd Platts' Carlisle office explained that the ALS personnel or paramedics are often needed to assist the local ambulance services during an emergency. Mr. Swisher noted

-2-

that the ALS services are billed separately from the local ambulance service.  He said that the problem is that Medicare and some HMO insurances do not cover these costs and become the responsibility of the person using the service. The out-of-pocket costs are usually hundreds of dollars. Mr. Swisher asked who he should contact regarding Medicare procedures.   Mr. Connolly said most of the ambulance membership programs already include the ALS service and do not hold their members responsible for what the insurance companies will not cover as the result of a medical emergency.  The Task Force asked Mr. Connolly to write a paragraph for their municipal newsletters explaining that most insurance companies do not recognize the Advanced Life Support (ALS) services that are routinely used during a medical emergency.

            Mr. Stoner from the County Planning Commission said that the Cumberland County Hazard Mitigation Plan has been sent to the municipalities for adoption.  He said the Plan is due back to the Planning Office by August 31.  Mr. Stoner noted that he talked with the

consultant and they are having some problems with their model.  They were talking about hiring an outside consultant to develop the model at a cost of $1,500.  Mr. Stoner said the Planning Department staff has been conducting inspections of all farms enrolled in the Agriculture Preservation Program. He said the inspections are required by the Department of Agriculture and they have approximately 105 farms to inspect.

            Mr. Ward said PennDOT is looking at Exit 44 and had some questions on the environmental end of the project.  He said they have resubmitted the final draft to the

environmentalists.  Mr. Ward said discussion was held on paying off the loan with PennDOT, which has to be paid by September 3, 2004. He said they also talked about the final design and

-3-

hopes to have it started by sometime in 2005. Discussion was held on the TIP list and how those projects are moving along. Chairman Rankin asked that the monthly minutes from the two COGS be sent to the Task Force members for information purposes.

            Chairman Rankin said the Cumberland County Emergency Services Action Panel asked the Task Force to work with this Panel.  Chairman Rankin asked how the Task Force would play a role in this process. He noted the top five issues the Panel is looking at are:

·        Recruitment and Retention of Volunteers

·        Information and Communications

·        Common Purchasing – Initiatives

·        Training

·        Worker’s Compensation and Mutual Aid

                                    Chairman Rankin said the Task Force needs to play a role in this Panel because they are                                    all subject to the concerns that are being elevated by the Panel. Commissioner Barclay said that                                     this Board could bring information from their municipality back to the Task Force and Chairman                         Rankin, also a   member of the Panel, could convey the information to the Emergency Services                           Panel.  Commissioner Barclay said that way everyone is informed on the same level with                                                information.  Mr. Stabile suggested doing a large scale emergency demonstration in different                               parts of the County and figure out what the response is going to be.  He said this needs to         

                        be played out, starting with the accident and then see if what is done on paper will work. There                          was further discussion with the Board on different ways they can help the Panel.

                                    Mr. Connolly discussed BRAC and noted the next meeting is in September.

                                    Commissioner Barclay discussed a traffic study being done on the old exit off the                                               turnpike to the Carlisle Fairgrounds.  He said Carlisle Productions has hired a consultant to do                           the traffic study.
