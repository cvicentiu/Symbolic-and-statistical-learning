U.S. Secretary of homeland Security Tom Ridge was the keynote speaker at the 140th anniversary of Abraham Lincoln's Gettysburg Address.  Even though inclement weather called for the first ever Dedication Day speech to be delivered in the Chapel at Gettysburg College, the event was still a success.

Former Governor of Pennsylvania Tom Ridge received a warm and  welcomed reception from the large audience at the Gettysburg College Chapel on November 19.  The newly elected Secretary to the Department of Homeland Security, gave a inspirational speech about the sacrifice of the men 140 years ago being no different than the sacrifice that our young men and women make today for the cause of freedom.  The members of the Lincoln Fellowship, Gettysburg National Military Park, and the Civil War Institute are commended for another well organized event.  Thanks also to all who attended all the events for Dedication Day including the Fortenbaugh Lecture and the Lincoln Fellowship Luncheon.
