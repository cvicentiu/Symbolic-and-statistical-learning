Legislators Counter SHARE with FAIR

FAIR claims that if enacted as part of reauthorization of TEA 21, raising the return rate to 95 percent will cause 17 states and the District of Columbia to lose out on more than $1 billion in federal transportation funding.

In May, another group of legislators formed the States’ Highway Alliance for Real Equity (SHARE), which wants to raise the minimum guarantee. But FAIR backers say the current redistribution of transportation dollars in TEA-21 more adequately addresses the funding levels needed by states with greater highway and transit demands. “This kind of loss would greatly impact New York’s ability to maintain and enhance our existing infrastructure and transit needs,” said Rep. Sherwood Boehlert, a founding member of FAIR along with Reps. Jerrold Nadler (D-NY) and Nick Rahall (D-WV). “It’s critical that we make sound highway investments to ensure safe, efficient travel and create jobs to boost our competitiveness and economic health.”

New York, FAIR contends, would lose out on $300 million in transportation funding if changed. West Virginia and Hawaii would each see $36 million vanish.

Other members of FAIR include the following Representatives: California, Ellen Tauscher (D); Connecticut, Nancy Johnson (R), John Larson (D), Chris Shays (R), Rob Simmons (R); Delaware, Michael Castle (R); District of Columbia, Eleanor Holmes Norton (D); Hawaii, Ed Case (D); Massachusetts, Barney Frank (D), Michael Capuano (D); New Hampshire, Charlie Bass (R); New York, Tim Bishop (D), Sue Kelly (R), Jack Quinn (R), Anthony Weiner (D), Eliot Engel (D), Vito Fossella (R), Steve Israel (D), Nita Lowey (D), Maurice Hinchey (D), Carolyn Maloney (D), Michael McNulty (D), Thomas Reynolds (R), José Serrano (D), John Sweeney (R), Edolphus Towns (D); Pennsylvania, Todd Platts (R), Tim Holden (D); Rhode Island, Jim Langevin (D); West Virginia, Shelly Moore Capito (R) and Alan Mollohan (D). SHARE, announced by House Majority Leader Tom DeLay (R-TX), Rep. Baron Hill (D-IN) and Sens. Carl Levin (D-MI) and George Voinovich (R-OH), includes sponsors from the following states: Arizona, Colorado, Florida, Georgia, Indiana, Kentucky, Louisiana, Michigan, Mississippi, New Jersey, North Carolina, Ohio, Oklahoma, South Carolina, Tennessee, Texas and Virginia.

“Too many states send dollar bills to Washington only to get coins in return, while others get back much more than they send,” said DeLay. “Our proposed reforms are about fairness and fairness dictates that Texas dollars should be spent creating Texas jobs.”
