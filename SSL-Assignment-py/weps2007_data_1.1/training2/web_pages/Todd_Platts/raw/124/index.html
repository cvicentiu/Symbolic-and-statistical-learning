American Lung Association Commends House Defeat of Tobacco Buyout

July 13, 2004

New York, NY – The American Lung Association applauds the House of Representatives for today approving an amendment offered by Reps. Jeff Flake (R-AZ) and Chris Van Hollen (D-MD) to block expenditure of taxpayer dollars to support the tobacco industry.  The amendment cosponsored by Reps. Todd Platts (R-PA), Henry Waxman (D-CA), Roscoe Bartlett (R-MD), Lloyd Doggett (D-TX) and Martin Meehan (D-MA), was approved on a voice vote. 

The amendment sends a clear signal that the $10 billion tobacco buyout should be removed from the Foreign Sales Corporation bill passed by the House of Representatives last month. The Flake-Van Hollen amendment to the House Department of Agriculture Appropriations bill prohibits the expenditure of taxpayer funds for the tobacco buyout.

The tobacco buyout would have been a good deal for the tobacco industry and a bad deal for taxpayers. The tobacco companies would get cheaper tobacco and taxpayers would get the $10 billion bill. The Library of Congress' Congressional Research Service estimates that tobacco companies will save $270 million on tobacco this year alone because the tobacco purchased from growers will be cheaper.  

Representatives Flake and Van Hollen are to be congratulated for stopping this fleecing of the American taxpayer.

Celebrating its 100th anniversary, the American Lung Association works to prevent lung disease and promote lung health. Lung diseases and breathing problems are the leading causes of infant deaths in the United States today, and asthma is the leading serious chronic childhood illness. Smoking remains the nation’s leading preventable cause of death. Lung disease death rates continue to increase while other leading causes of death have declined.

The American Lung Association has long funded vital research on the causes of and treatments for lung disease.    It is the foremost defender of the Clean Air Act and laws that protect citizens from secondhand smoke.  The Lung Association teaches children the dangers of tobacco use and helps teenage and adult smokers overcome addiction. It educates children and adults living with lung diseases on managing their condition. With the generous support of the public and volunteers, the American Lung Association is “Improving life, one breath at a time.” For more information about the American Lung Association or to support the work it does, call 1-800-LUNG-USA (1-800-586-4872) or log on to www.lungusa.org.
