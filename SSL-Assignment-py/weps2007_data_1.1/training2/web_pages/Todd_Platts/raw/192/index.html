Financial Planning Week Resolution Introduced in Congress By Rep. Platts


Atlanta, GA/Washington, D.C…. May 20, 2003… Congressman Todd Platts, R-Penn., has introduced a concurrent resolution in the U.S. House of Representatives recognizing Financial Planning Week in 2003. The resolution recognizes the designation of the week of October 6, 2003, as “Financial Planning Week” by the Financial Planning Association (FPA) and resolves that Congress “acknowledges and commends the millions of American families across the United States, as well as the financial planning profession, for their adherence and dedication to the financial planning process.”

“FPA greatly appreciates the strong support of financial planning by Congressman Platts in introducing this resolution, said FPA President David Yeske, CFP®, a San Francisco-based financial planner. “We call on Congress to formally recognize the increasing participation by American families in the financial planning process by supporting this resolution. Last year 28 state governors, the District of Columbia, and a number of cities supported Financial Planning Week. We are pleased and gratified that this effort is becoming national in scope.”

The resolution notes that the financial planning process “can play a vital role in helping American workers achieve financial independence,” and that “all Americans can improve their quality of life by securing competent, objective and comprehensive financial advice to assist them in attaining their financial goals.”

Mr. Yeske urged all FPA members and their clients to contact their representatives in Congress and support H. Con. Res. 176. To review the resolution, please go to http://thomas.loc.gov/ and type in “H. Con. Res. 176.”
