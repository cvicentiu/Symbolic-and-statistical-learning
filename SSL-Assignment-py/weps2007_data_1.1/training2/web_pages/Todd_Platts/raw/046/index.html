Subject:
 Help

To:
Rep. Todd Platts

July 31, 2004

July 31, 2004
Rep Todd Platts, R-Pa 19th District
United States House of Representatives
Washington, D.C.
Dear Rep Todd Platts,
I am writing you on behalf of my mother Marian R. Patrick of Camp Hill, Pennsylvania.
My mother, who will be 83 years old in October, is the victim of the unscrupulous mortgage practice that AARP wrote about in their recent magazine. My mother was enticed into refinancing her home at an obscene interest rate and for twice what her home is worth. She is now being threatened with foreclosure since she is behind in her payments. The company, City Financial, must have known that this refinance would have this result based on the income and payment amount. They refuse to lower their rate and because of the accruing penalties all the payments now being made go towards fees. The home will never be paid off at this rate, and the home cannot be sold because of the value difference (worth $70,000 owe in mortgage $136,000).
AARP warned that there are companies targeting the elderly and offering to refinance their mortgage to provide them extra cash to use for whatever they wanted, knowing all along that they couldn’t possibly keep up the payments. This is obviously what has happened to my mother, and I find it disgusting that a company could do this and remain within the confines of the law.
One of the agencies that is been trying to help my mother suggested that I write you since they believe you to be a supporter of the elderly in your district. I pray that you will be able to help my mother, and prevent this from ever happening again to any elderly members of your district.

Sincerely,
Ronald Wenrich, for Marian Patrick


Camp Hill , PA
