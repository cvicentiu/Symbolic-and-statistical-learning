2/5/2002 - (From left to right): Terry Craig, Barnett Township Supervisor; Mary L. Austin Barnett Township Treasurer; Forest County Commissioner, Skip Cussins; Greg Burkett, Cook Forest Assistant Superintendent; DEP Secretary David Hess; Douglas Carlson, Executive Director Forest County Conservation District & Planning Board; Mark Kullich, DCNR Regional Recreational and Park Advisor, and; Harvey Pinkerton, PennSoil Resource Conservation and Development Area.

2/4/2002 - Andrew Shanbarger was made an Eagle Scout during a special ceremony at St. Paul's Chapel United Methodist Church in Red Lion, York County Feb. 3. The volunteer project he choose as part of the Eagle Scout requirements was to help identify and then sample areas for mosquitoes as part of York County's West Nile Virus Monitoring Program working with the York County Senior Environment Corps. Joining Andrew in the first row are his parents Marjorie and Dean Shanbarger and DEP Secretary David Hess (right) who presented a message of congratulations from Gov. Schweiker. Also extending congratulations were (in the second row from left) Hoover Yount, Continental Congress Chapter Sons of the American Revolution, Congressman Todd Platts, Rep. Stan Saylor and Marine Sergeant Add Hall. Sen. Mike Waugh could not attend the event, but sent a special resolution adopted by the Pennsylvania Senate.

2/4/2002 - Franklin Grove became an Eagle Scout during a special ceremony at St. Paul's Chapel United Methodist Church in Red Lion, York County on Feb. 3. Franklin organized a "Don't Dump - Chesapeake Bay Drainage" storm drain painting project in the City of York to educate the pubic about the connection between dumping oil and waste down storm drains and the impact on the Bay working with other scouts and the York County Senior Environment Corp as his Eagle Scout volunteer project. Joining Franklin in the first row are his parents Franklin and Beth Grove and DEP Secretary David Hess (right) who presented a congratulatory message from Gov. Schweiker. Also congratulating Franklin were (in the second row from left) Hoover Yount, Continental Congress Chapter Sons of the American Revolution, Congressman Todd Platts, Marine Sergeant Add Hall and Rep. Ron Miller. Sen. Mike Waugh could not attend the event, but sent a special resolution adopted by the Pennsylvania Senate.

2/4/2002 - DEP Secretary David Hess addresses the Air & Waste Management Association's Act II Seminar in Wexford, Allegheny County.

2/4/2002 - DEP Secretary David Hess joins the Southwestern Pennsylvania Ozone Action Partnership's Annual Recognition Meeting. Secretary Hess congratulates the partners for the role they played in helping the Pittsburgh area achieve 'attainment' status, but reminds everyone that we still have work to do to continue to improve air quality in western Pennsylvania. (Seated to Secretary Hess's left is DEP Community Relations Coordinator Betsy Mallison).
