Senator Mike Waugh's Photo Gallery

 

Eagle Scout Ceremony- April 5, 2003

Along with Congressman Todd Platts, I joined Keith Nelson, Michael Simmons, and A.J. Specter on April 5 to celebrate their individual accomplishments in earning their Eagle Scout Awards by presenting each with a commemorative citation.  I thank David Nelson for providing this photo to me.

Guest in Chamber – March 3, 2003 

           

Each year I have the opportunity to mentor a student participating in Leadership York’s Future

Leaders of York (FLY) program.  On March 3, my student Andrew Brookhart, spent the day in my Capitol office and joined me in the Senate Chamber during session. 

 

Agriculture Loan Programs Press Conference – February 27, 2003

At the end of February I held a press conference at Shiloh Nurseries to announce the expansion of three agricultural loan programs, from which agricultural producers were once excluded.  Carl Jacobs and Michael Stebbins, owners of Shiloh Nurseries, participated in the event, and I thank them for the wonderful hospitality. 

 

Unity Banner – February 3, 2003  

I had the pleasure of helping North Eastern High School’s National Honor Society to promote York Unity in the Capitol by having their Unity Banner hung in the East Wing Rotunda in February.  Representatives from the Honor Society, Lindsey Smith, Laura Thomas, and Sarah Myers, were able to visit the Capitol to see the banner hanging proudly and expressing such a bold message.

 

Agriculture Secretary Confirmation Hearing  March 5, 2003 

 

On March 5, 2003, the Senate Agriculture and Rural Affairs Committee, of which I am the Chair, confirmed Dennis Wolff, a dairy farmer from Millville, PA, for the appointment as Secretary of Agriculture.

 

Intern Visit – March 11, 2003

 

From January through April, Penn State Sophomore Bob Kikola provided service to my District Office as an intern, and he had the chance to visit the Capitol and join me in the Senate Chamber on March 11.

 

Citation Presentation – March 26, 2003 

 

Tammy Blymire, who was inducted into the York College Athletic Hall of Fame in February for her outstanding accomplishments on the college’s basketball team, visited Harrisburg on March 26, 2003 to be honored by the Senate with the presentation of a Citation.

 

Swearing-In Ceremony – January 7, 2003 

 

On January 7, along with my fellow Senators, I was sworn in to once again proudly serve the 28th Senatorial District.  Senator Rob Wonderling, left, was sworn in for his first term, and Senator Noah Wenger, right, began his sixth term.

 

Farm Show Complex Tour- December 10, 2003

I hope those of you who attended the annual Farm Show in January not only enjoyed the Show but the new complex as well.  In December I got a sneak peak while taping the January edition of my monthly show, The Commonwealth Report, with Dennis Grumbine, the Executive Director of the Bureau of Farm Show, and Charlie Itle, the Bureau’s Assistant Director.

I recently had the special opportunity to visit Central School District’s Sinking Springs Elementary School on Dr. Seuss’s birthday. 

 Many of the staff and students chose their favorite characters and donned costumes to add to the day’s festivities.

 I was invited to read to Mrs. Kelly’s third-grade class, during which the students enjoyed Dr. Seuss’s “McElligot’s Pool.” 

I was pleased to welcome Reverend Kris Lentz as the Guest Chaplain in the Senate on April 9, 2002.
For all of you PA fairgoers, keep your eyes open for this year’s PA State Fair Queen, Jacqueline Pratt, a well-rounded and highly active student at Lampeter-Strasburg High School and in her community. 

Representative Bev Mackereth and I held a Senior Informational Meeting at the Holiday Inn Holidome, during which seniors learned more about many of the issues they face.

Veteran's Day Weekend provided many celebrations throughout the district.  Market Square in Shrewsbury held a special event honoring all heroes, and I was pleased to present a check on behalf of Market Square to Chris Santoro, president of the Shrewsbury Volunteer Fire Company, for the company's Community Fund.

I often have the opportunity to read to the fun-loving children in the Hanover Head Start program.

On October 15, 2001, I was given the opportunity to speak at a rally in the Capitol rotunda to raise awareness of the difficulties that Pennsylvania’s firefighters and EMS personnel continuously face.  I was joined by my co-chair of the legislature’s Firefighters and Emergency Services Caucus and fellow York County legislator, Representative Stan Saylor.
