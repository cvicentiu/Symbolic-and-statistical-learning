OIG/GAO Reports & Publications as of February 19, 2004

Tobacco Use and Public Health: Federal Efforts to Prevent and Reduce Tobacco Use Among Youth. GAO-04-41, November 21. This is 32 pages long. This report does not contain any recommendations. GAO was asked to provide information on federal efforts to prevent and reduce youth smoking. The report describes (1) federal programs, research, and activities that aim to prevent and reduce tobacco use among youth, (2) the efforts of federal departments and agencies to monitor their programs, and (3) the coordination among federal departments and agencies in efforts to prevent and reduce tobacco use among youth. Several programs at NIH participated and provided important information to this review.  (Highlights )

Information Security: Status of Federal Public Key Infrastructure Activities at Major Departments and Agencies. GAO-04-157, December 15. This is 58 pages long. At the request of Congressmen Adam Putnam and Tom Davis, GAO was asked to follow up on a previous work on (1) the status of public key infrastructure (pki) and (2) identifying challenges encountered by the 24 agencies in implementing PKI initiatives since the 2001 report. HHS is one of the agencies surveyed. (Highlights)

Prescription Drugs: Factors That May Have Contributed to OxyContin Abuse and Diversion and Efforts to Address the Problem. GAO-04-110, December 23. This is 63 pages long. At the request of Congressment Frank Wolfe and James Greenwood GAO was asked to examine the marketing and promotion of OxyContin and its abuse and diversion. Specifically they addressed: (1) How has Purdue marketed and promoted OxyContin? (2) What factors contributed to the abuse and diversion of OxyContin? (3) What actions have been taken to address Oxycontin abuse and diversion? GAO recommends to improve efforts to prevent or identify the abuse and diversion of schedule II controlled substances such as oxycodone, FDA's risk management plan guidance encourage the pharmaceutical manufacturers that submit new drug applications for these substances to include plans that contain a strategy for monitoring the use of these drugs and identifying potential abuse and diversion problems. (Highlights)

Centers for Disease Control and Prevention: Agency Leadership Taking Steps to Improve Management and Leadership, but Challenges Remain. GAO-04-219, January 30. This is 44 pages long. GAO examined the Office of the Director's (OD) (1) executive management structure, (2) approach to overseeing the agency's work, and (3) approach to setting the agency's priorities. GAO recommends that the CDC Director ensure OD's oversight of the centers' programmatic work at a level below the Director, improve OD's monitoring of the centers' operations and programmatic activities, and ensure that the agency's strategic and human capital planning are coordinated and done expeditiously. (Highlights)

Human Capital: Selected Agencies' Experiences and Lessons Learned in Designing Training and Development Programs. GAO-04-291, January 30. This is 64 pages long. At the request of Senator George Voinovich GAO was asked to identify samples of selected federal agencies' experiences and some key lessans learned in designing their training and development programs. GAO focused on ways that these agencies (1) assessed agency skills gaps and identified training needs, (2) developed strategies and solutions for these training and development needs, and (3) determined methods to evaluate the effectiveness of training and development programs. (Highlights)

Performance Budgeting: Observations on the Use of OMB's Program Assessment Rating Tool for the Fiscal Year 2004 Budget. GAO-04-174, January 30. This is 73 pages long. At the request of Senator Voinovich and Congressman Todd Platts GAO was asked to examine (1) how PART changed OMB's fiscal year 2004 budget decision-making process, (2) PART's relationship to to the Government Performance and Results Act of 1993 (GPRA), and PART's strengths and weaknesses as an evaluation tool. GAO recommends that OMB (1) address the capacity demands of PART, (2) strengthen PART guidance, (3) address evaluation information availability and scope issues, (4) focus program selection on crosscutting comparisons and critical operations, (5) broaden the dialogue with congressional stakeholders, and (6) articulate and implement a complementary relationship between PART and GPRA. (Highlights)

Humane Methods of Slaughter Act: USDA Has Addressed Some Problems but Still Faces Enforcement Challenges. GAO-04-247, January 30. This is 45 pages long. (Highlights)

Reliability of Federal Procurement Data. GAO-04-295R, December 30. This is 4 pages long. The purpose of this letter directed to the Director OMB was to convey GAO's serious and continuing concerns with the reliability of data contained in the Federal Procurement Data System (FPDS) and to recommend steps to help improve date reliability.
