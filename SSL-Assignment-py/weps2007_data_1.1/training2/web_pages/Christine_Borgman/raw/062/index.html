User Services for Digital Libraries

October 31st, 2005 john.gladstone

Christine Borgman / UCLA
Maurita Holland / School of Information - U. of Michigan
Gary Marchionini,/ Univ. of NC

First and foremost - Congratulations to Jeff Pomerantz for a doing dandy job of juggling Net Meeting’s not-ready-for-prime-time software.
Originally, three panelists were set to perch on the lectern of Providence Ballroom II, with a fourth speaker videoconferenced.
A late breaking wrinkle in travel plans left Gary Marchionini live with three virtual speakers piped in.
The video/audio feed only worked one way (incoming) and was as out of sync as a Japanese Kung Fu movie translation.
The video was typically informationally dysfunctional and distracting when compared to the content rich audio feed..

____________________

Maurita:
Internet Public Library is having a birthday party. Ten years old and still spunky. Students maintain the site with an assist from a part-time programmer, a couple of grad students, administrator overseers, and a cool $200k per year from foundation, corporate and Univ. of Michigan School of Information backing.
30-40% of users are from outside US
30-40% are kids
___________________

Christine:
Video/audio sync was better with Ichat, but the visual was just as meaningless. The video feed was mercifully pulled in favor of a powerpoint show in tandem with audio.
Tech foibles made this presentation on data sharing difficult to follow so I won’t try.
___________________

Gary:
The Open Video Project is a repository of 2500 videos available for download for classroom and the research community. Gary describes the site as using the “Costco Model” of self service.
Future efforts will focus on:
1/ Online help
Just in time, content sensitive, personalized, layered.

2/ Recommendors that work
Implicit - Large scale user interaction (page/citation rank - popularity based) / learned behavior

3/ Social Networking, open commentary (wiki-esque), “Answer Gardens”

4/ User Friendly Interfaces that are agile and metadata rich.,
