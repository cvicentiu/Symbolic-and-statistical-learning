WORKSESSION TOPIC: American Skiing Company Update. This worksession was held from 5:15 to approximately 6:45 p.m. Cooper Black stepped down. Lamont distributed a set of minutes prepared from the Noon Planning Commission worksession, in which American Skiing Company provided an overview. Greg Spearn, American Skiing Company (ASC) representative spoke on the following issues, but not limited to: Land Use; Master Planning for Tennis Meadows and Gondola Square; Community Development Code (zoning and density); process of an overall master plan; day skier lots; community amenities; people mover; phasing parking and retail component(s) of the plan. Basic feedback was desired on the proposed long-range village area in Tennis Meadows. Spearn noted the various development permits they would be seeking, including a development permit for a Marriott. He spoke of support for the Downtown Development Authority to assist in the enhancement of the resort area. Spearn indicated the completion of the Grand Summit would be achieved by the end of summer 1999. Bennett clarified that the construction contract for Phase I, not Phase II has been signed. Spearn stated the ski market needed to be assessed prior to Phase II. Spearn requested Council input on "how good of a neighbor have we been?" He stated ASC would like regular reports from City staff on where are we with resort planning. Bennett suggested Council members respond to each question poised individually. 1. Consideration should be given to the appropriateness of the land use proposed, in particular, the village-like intensity of Tennis Meadows? and Should employee housing be incorporated into the Master plan for Tennis Meadows? Gondola Square? Engelken: feels good development needs to refer to the SS Community Plan. He felt the Gondola Plaza plans fits in nicely, but did not support the Tennis Meadows proposal. He prefers existing in-fill area be built out, prior to accommodating sporadic commercial and retail land use. Generally agreed with PC comments. Stettner: Felt Gondola Square development/enhancement was appropriate. Voiced concern with the Tennis Meadows plan, feel that it is not appropriate with the Community Plan. Brenner: hopes to see employee housing incorporated into existing structures rather than having it removed. He prefers to see focus on the Gondola Base area. Connell: noted a weakness in the pedestrianization of the base area. She suggested focus be in that area and supports employee housing. Bennett: suggested ASC work with the Mtn Town Plan and DDA. He voiced concern with the timing of the Tennis Meadows area being in competition with the pedestrianization of the base area. Supports employee housing being integrated and would like to see all the development work in harmony with the airport and air service of the community.... noting the vitality of the community needs to be maintained. 2. Are timeshare hotels and condominium hotels appropriate in Tennis Meadows given its distance from the base area? Engelken: supports keeping density at base area, until there is a demonstrated need for dwelling units that far away from the base...if high demand, Grand Summit would be proceeding with Phase II. Stettner: felt timeshares are not appropriate in the area. She voiced concern with the capability of infrastructure to handle the proposed build out at this time. She suggested community capacity be evaluated. Brenner: preferred in-fill at the base area, prior to construction in Tennis Meadows. He was concerned with too much happening too fast. Connell: voiced concern with the economic vitality and viability of existing retail and lodging. Consideration needs to be taken with the existing community...what is the future...what is the Steamboat statement...concern with diluting base and development with time shares. Bennett: cannot support timeshares in Tennis Meadows. 3. What Code should apply to the developments? CONSENSUS: Council supported the new Code being used for the proposed development. 4. Should the height of the development in Tennis Meadows be limited to 3 stories? CONSENSUS: 3 stories max for the development in Tennis Meadows. Brenner would look at additional should employee housing be considered. 5. Is the City approval of an overall Master Plan supported? CONSENSUS: Council stated yes. 6. Should consideration of the Master Plan wait until the Mountain Town Plan is adopted? CONSENSUS: Council stated yes. 7. If it went concurrently should ASC contribute to the cost of the consultants review of the Master Plan for Tennis Meadows? CONSENSUS: Council stated yes. 8. Should individual development applications wait for the adoption of the Master Plan or may they proceed concurrently? CONSENSUS: Council stated yes. 9. Is a small village retail component in the first phase of development appropriate in Tennis Meadows? CONSENSUS: Council stated NO. 10. Would it be appropriate for ASC to transfer some retail development rights from Steamboat Village to Tennis Meadows through a master planning process? CONSENSUS: Council stated NO. Brenner further suggested he would like to see what retail was specified. 11. Should a retail study be required with the conceptual plan submission? CONSENSUS: Council stated yes. 12. Should ASC be permitted to redevelop existing parking structures? CONSENSUS: Council stated yes. Engelken stated all new development would need parking. 13. What should the obligation for replacing those parking spaces be? CONSENSUS: Council supported PC comment of "no net loss." Connell felt the Mountain Town Plan needed to be followed in this area. 14. Should ASC be able to use the Tennis Meadows day skier parking lot for gross density calculations? CONSENSUS: Council stated NO. 15. Is a temporary location for Strings at Tennis Meadows an incentive for the approval of development in this area? Connell stepped down on this Strings question. Bennett felt the Strings question was inappropriate. 16. Should the master plan consider a permanent location for Strings? Connell stepped down on this Strings question. CONSENSUS: Council stated yes. 17. Would the dedication of the civic center site enhance the master plan direction? CONSENSUS: Council stated yes. Connell depending upon the Mountain Town Plan deeming it appropriate. 18. Is it important to include the recreation center in the Tennis Meadows master plan? CONSENSUS: Council stated yes, but let's consider all other sites that may be appropriate as well. 19. Is the people mover a good idea? Engelken: okay from parking lot area, but not for housing owners. Stettner: concern with the people mover being a public disincentive. Brenner: not within the next few years. Connell: not be the only way to move people. Spearn clarified a shuttle would also be included. 20. How should the phasing of the development correspond with the people mover installation? Engelken, Brenner and Stettner: would like people mover first. Bennett: tie in with long term plan down the road. 21. Is the phasing program acceptable? It was clarified that Snowflower II and Gondola Plaza would be first and Tennis Meadows would be reviewed in approximately one year. Engelken, Brenner, Connell and Stettner: okay, but prefers Christie Base development prior to Tennis Meadows development. Bennett: wants to keep phasing in line with airport issues for viability reasons. 22. Should ASC be subject to higher standards of development or requirements? CONSENSUS: All members suggested that ASC set the standard and raise the bar to ensure for a good community project. 23. Citizen's Committee? CONSENSUS: Council supported the committee being outside the City's charge.
