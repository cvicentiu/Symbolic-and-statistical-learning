Entry for June 15, 2006 - Democratic or Democrat?

Look at this email I got. Do you think it is a little biased?

The poor Democrats. They are always being attacked and they are never attacking. As one coworker pointed out, when a candidate is being picked on it makes you take a closer look at them. If the opponent must resort to defaming then there must be a real threat here. This other candidate must have something good going. For those of us sick of negative campaigning, it just makes us want to vote the other way.

The Democratic party has some good things going for it but they have the disadvantage of having the support of so many small disassociated groups that they cant keep them all happy at the same time. Some of those groups may actually be attacking the "Bible". Some sections of the Party will then support these efforts.  It makes this all to easy for the Republican party to Attack.

This is the real reason that I believe Bush was re-elected. The Republican party is just more united (I said "more")

Dear ANDREW,

Elections are always bare-knuckled fights to the finish.

But there are certain ethical lines that should never be crossed. Unfortunately, Republicans have proven over the last few years that they are willing to commit the most egregious acts of character assassination and voter intimidation to get their candidates elected. I know, because in 2004 I was the target of an unprecedented two-year slander campaign that questioned my patriotism, attacked my integrity and in many cases, perpetuated outright lies.

I wish I could say my experience was an isolated case. But time and again, the Republican Party and special interest groups allied with it have stooped to new lows to win races. They sent African-American voters a flyer saying that voting without a driver's license was a felony. They impugned the patriotism of Max Cleland, a triple amputee war hero. They told voters in West Virginia and Arkansas that Democrats planned to ban the Bible. And the list goes on. They simply have no shame.

Unfortunately, these underhanded campaign tactics work unless they are met with a forceful and immediate response. Devoid of any positive agenda, Republicans are already resorting to the same tactics in states across the country in advance of the November elections.

Democrats are fighting back against these dishonest attacks. Click here to see how, by visiting our Media Response Project.

Pennsylvania is offering a preview of things to come. Trailing by double digits in the polls against Democratic challenger Bob Casey, Rick Santorum has already released a series of dishonest radio attack ads. But hard as he tries, Santorum can't run from the central truth of his Senate tenure. He has voted for President Bush's misguided agenda 98% of the time.

The DSCC will be working to make sure the truth gets out, and I hope you'll be right there with us.

Sincerely,

Tom Daschle
