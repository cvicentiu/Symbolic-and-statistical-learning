George Clinton Wins Back Masters

George Clinton (photo by Ros O'Gorman)
Related Content:
Buy CD: George Clinton Greatest Funkin Hits

by Paul Cashmere

6 June 2005

Funk star George Clinton has won a court battle to have his early albums returned to his possession.
Clinton recorded the four titles "Hardcore Jollies," "One Nation Under A Groove," "Uncle Jam Wants You" and "The Electric Spanking of War Babies" as Funkadelic in the 70s.

The court decision gives ownership of the masters back to Clinton for the first time in 15 years.

Judge Real who presided over the case concluded that Clinton was defrauded of his creative works by a member of his former management team and his lawyers.

The albums, recorded in the late 70s for Warner Music, have been available through Priority Records, a subsidiary of EMI
