Tony Abbott's Shameful Record


Nicola Roxon

Media Statement - 23rd January 2007

Shadow Health Minister Nicola Roxon today condemned Tony Abbott’s shameful record, and questioned John Howard’s decision to leave him in the Health portfolio.

“Tony Abbott has consistently failed to address some of the biggest challenges facing the Australian health system,” Ms Roxon said.

“Quality healthcare is increasingly unaffordable for millions of Australian families, we have a shortage of doctors, dentists and nurses, and Mr Abbott refuses to take responsibility for any of it.

“I’m surprised Mr Howard doesn’t think the health of Australian families is important enough to replace Mr Abbott with somebody more competent – or simply more interested,” said Ms Roxon.

“People should never have to think about whether they can afford to go to the doctor. But sadly, under the Howard Government, they have to do exactly that.

“Health prices are increasing faster each year than the general inflation rate.

“At the same time, there are simply not enough doctors and nurses in this country. Every Australian must be able to get help when they need it – but that’s just not the case right now.

“And to make matters worse, Tony Abbott has consistently failed to take responsibility for any of these issues. He is always playing the blame game – blaming doctors, blaming the States, blaming anybody but himself.”

Ms Roxon challenged the Minister to take responsibility for the problems facing Australia’s health system.

“I challenge Tony Abbott to bring health costs down, to start fixing the workforce crisis, and to end the blame game.”
