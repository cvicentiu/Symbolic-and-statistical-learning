Task Force to Hear Albertans' Ideas for Affordable Housing Solutions

The Alberta government has assembled a task force to find innovative and practical ways to make affordable housing more accessible to Albertans.  The focus of the task force is to consider solutions for homelessness and affordable housing, including subsidized rental and home ownership.

The Alberta Affordable Housing Task Force was established at the request of the Premier and reflects the priority the Government of Alberta places on affordable housing.  The task force is responsible for identifying short and long-term housing solutions that will have a positive impact on Alberta's competitiveness and enhance Albertans' quality of life.

The Alberta Affordable Housing Task Force brings together representatives from municipalities, businesses, communities, industry associations, political parties and the non-profit sector to provide a broad base of expertise and knowledge in housing matters.

The task force will travel to nine Alberta communities in February to meet with key stakeholder organizations and individual Albertans.  A final report outlining specific cost-effective solutions will be presented to the Minister of Municipal Affairs and Housing in March.

The task force will hold community meetings in Calgary, Edmonton, Elk Point, Fort McMurray, Grande Prairie, Hinton, Lethbridge, Medicine Hat and Red Deer from February 16 to 28.

Individuals and groups can get more information on the task force and its activities on the Municipal Affairs and Housing website at www.municipalaffairs.gov.ab.ca.  Albertans can also make written submissions to the task force by faxing (780)422-4923 or emailing affordable.housing@gov.ab.ca.  Submissions may also be mailed to Municipal Affairs and Housing, Affordable Housing Task Force, 18th floor, 10155-102 Street, Edmonton, Alberta T5J 4L4.  An online submission form will be also available on the website soon.  Submissions will be accepted until February 28, 2007.

Albertans Receive Natural Gas Rebates in February

More than one million Alberta homes and businesses will receive an automatic rebate of $1.50 per gigajoule (GJ) on their February natural gas bills.  The rebate will save the average Alberta residential consumer around $27 this month thanks to the Natural Gas Rebate Program.

Rebates are triggered when two of the three regulated natural gas providers charge regulated rates above $5.50/GJ.  Rebate amounts increase as the price of natural gas rises, with the maximum cost of natural gas to most Alberta consumers capped at $8.75/GJ.

All eligible homes and businesses, regardless of whether they pay the regulated rate or are part of a competitive contract, receive rebates under the program. The structure of the Natural Gas Rebate Program enables the Alberta government to be responsive when changes occur to market prices.

In addition to natural gas, rebates will be provided for heating with propane, kerosene, heating oil and in certain circumstances, electricity.  Users of these other fuels need to apply for rebates.  Application forms are available at MLA offices, Alberta registry agents, competitive retailers and at propane and heating oil dealers.

More information on Alberta's Natural Gas Rebate Program can be found at: www.energy.gov.ab.ca, by clicking on 'Natural Gas Rebates' in the 'Spotlight' section.

Alberta Geological Survey Releases Coal Bed Methane Water Study

The Alberta Geological Survey (AGS) group of the Alberta Energy and Utilities Board (EUB) has completed a study entitled Water Chemistry of Coalbed Methane Reservoirs which examined the natural chemistry of water from both domestic water wells and coal bed methane wells in central Alberta.

Highlights of the study include:

  • Age dating, as well as biological and chemical analyses of water samples in the central Alberta study area, has found that water from coal bed methane wells and domestic water wells have separate and distinct chemical compositions.

  • Indications that bacteria in water wells could be generating naturally occurring methane in varying amounts depending on the location and condition of the well. Although these naturally occurring bacteria do not thrive in aquifers, they flourish and multiply in water wells where the pumping action of the well provides them with an enhanced level of nutrients.

The study collected and examined water samples from 20 water and coal bed methane wells in a 38,300 square kilometre area of central Alberta that included four major water drainage basins, those of the Athabasca, North Saskatchewan, Battle and Red Deer rivers. Water samples were collected from existing domestic water wells and existing coal bed methane wells and age dated according to established scientific methods.

The findings of the study will add to the existing knowledge base about coal bed methane development in Alberta.

Sampling and testing of water was completed according to protocols compiled by the AGS and based on those developed by the Geological Survey of Canada, the University of Calgary, the University of Alberta, the University of Saskatchewan and the United States Geological Survey.

Water samples were tested at the University of Toronto, Purdue University and independent labs within Canada and the United States.

The two year study was partially funded by the Alberta Energy Research Institute.

The EUB ensures that the discovery, development and delivery of Alberta's resources and utility services take place in a manner that is fair, responsible and in the public interest.

The study can be found at: http://www.ags.gov.ab.ca/publications/newreports.shtml.

First Alberta Communities Receive Funding for Public Transit and Water, Sewage and Waste Management Systems

Municipalities across Alberta are set to benefit as Canada's New Government and the Government of Alberta announce $130 million in project funding through the Canada-Alberta Municipal Rural Infrastructure Fund (CAMRIF).

The communities of Medicine Hat and Brooks received $12 million last week for local projects.  Eligible projects under CAMRIF include water and sewage treatment, solid waste management, public transit and energy improvements to municipal buildings.  The fund also supports better roads and bridges, cultural, recreational and tourism projects and improved broadband Internet access.  Projects are selected on a competitive basis from applications received from Alberta communities.

The Canada-Alberta Municipal Rural Infrastructure Fund is a joint initiative between the Governments of Canada and Alberta that enables communities to address local infrastructure needs.  The federal and provincial governments will each contribute $88 million to the program.

CAMRIF is implemented by Western Economic Diversification Canada and Alberta Infrastructure and Transportation and is administered by a joint secretariat.

Alberta Gaming & Liquor Commission (AGLC) Board Intervenes on Connect Logistics February Rate Hike                                                                                          The AGLC Board has asked Connect Logistics to defer a portion of its previously announced February rate increase for liquor agents, suppliers and retailers.  Based on feedback from major liquor industry stakeholders, the AGLC Board requested that Connect Logistics only continue with the increased costs for storage and transportation of liquor products.

The increased costs for storing and transporting liquor products will have the following average effects on these typical products:

  • 10 cents per 750 ml bottle of wine or spirits;
  • 40 cents per dozen of bottled beer (imported); and
  • 25 cents per dozen of canned beer (imported).

These cost increases will be implemented February 25, 2007.

Connect Logistics has agreed to defer additional rate increases to agents, suppliers and retailers until after the completion of the AGLC's review of the liquor supply chain, which is expected to be complete by March 1, 2007 and until after they have justified these increases to the Board of the AGLC.

The Board has been concerned with the timing of this rate increase because of the AGLC's review of the liquor supply chain currently underway.  As well, the rate increase is coming on the heels of several months of challenges for the industry given the liquor distribution storage and delivery problems experienced in 2006.  The Board wants to ensure any rate increase to the industry is justified.

The above rate increases to agents, suppliers and liquor retailers may or may not be passed along to the consumer as the purchase price for liquor is set by individual liquor stores. The beer increases do not apply to domestic beer—Sleemans, Big Rock, Labatt and Molson beer products—which are handled through warehouses located elsewhere in the province.

The AGLC has asked Connect Logistics to do a series of information workshops across the province with retailers, licensees, agents and key stakeholders to explain the rate increases in detail to these groups.

The AGLC is in the process of doing a review of the liquor supply chain which covers all aspects from the handling, receipt, storing, ordering, assembly and distribution of liquor products.  The AGLC has now received stakeholder input and is working with a third party consultant to analyze the input and complete a summary report for the AGLC Board by March 1.  The Board will consider the report and make a decision on or before April 30, 2007.

Awards Honour Albertans for Volunteer Service to Seniors                        Nomination packages are now available to help Albertans recognize those who provide outstanding volunteer service to seniors in their community.  The nominations are for the 10th-annual Minister's Seniors Service Awards, which honour individuals and organizations for their exceptional service and commitment to Alberta's seniors.

Nomination packages are distributed throughout the province to organizations that work with seniors.  The deadline for submissions is March 15.  Award recipients will be honoured at a special ceremony in June during Seniors' Week 2007.

This past June, five individuals and three organizations received the 2006 Minister's Seniors Service Awards.  Individual recipients were Nellie Befus of Calgary, Reta Elder of Innisfail, Douglas Heine of Medicine Hat, Edna Jolly of Edmonton and Esther McIntosh of Calgary.  Organization recipients were Calgary Meals on Wheels, the Mary Immaculate Hospital of Mundare Auxilians and the Northgate Senior Citizens Association of Edmonton.

For nomination forms and more information on the 2007 Minister's Seniors Service Awards, contact Alberta Seniors and Community Supports by phone toll free at 1-800-642-3853 or 427-7876 in Edmonton and area, or visit the Alberta Seniors and Community Supports website at www.seniors.gov.ab.ca.
