Lancer CEO quits after board finds mischief
by Elliot Blair Smith | Mar 01 '04

Lancer CEO George Schroeder resigned Friday after the board found that he had made improper personal use of corporate assets -- such as signing checks to himself for unapproved loans -- and that management records were disorganized, missing and mislabeled.

Schroeder, 64, will go to work today, as he has for 37 years, but as a non-officer employee of the soft-drink fountain maker. He keeps his board seat next to his brother Alfred Schroeder, 67, founder and chairman of the company, one of Coca-Cola's largest equipment suppliers, according to company regulatory filings and a news release.
