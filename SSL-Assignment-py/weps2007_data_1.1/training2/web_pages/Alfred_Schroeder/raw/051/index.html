Lois Magner has brought together biographical information on people in the medical profession other than the "great doctors". Throughout history most biographical works have been written about famous doctors. In this work are compiled brief sketches of fifty-six nurses, public health workers, practitioners, educators, and lesser-known doctors who have contributed to the social, cultural, and institutional foundations of healthcare, especially women and minorities. Their contributions span the world to reflect the issues that have been central to healthcare from the late 1800s to current practice. Each entry focuses on the person's life and contribution to healthcare, a bibliography including archival material locations, works about the person, works by the person, and any current research being conducted.

The book is easy to read and informative. The entries are arranged alphabetically but the multiple appendices allow a search for entries by date of birth, place of birth, occupation, and women practitioners. A bibliography of other medical biographical works is appended. Biographical information about the authors of the entries can be found at the end of the book. The book's organization makes it easy to use and useful as a reference tool. There are few other works that provide this broad coverage of contributors to the healthcare profession. Undergraduate students will find it useful as a beginning source for information on people in the medical field. This is a recommended book.
