AM,1984:

Steve Sailah

The most popular licensed gambling in West Germany is the numbers lottery, lotto, with millions taking part every week, and millions of marks distributed amongst the lucky few who guessed the six right numbers from among 49 numbers. One man who thought he was a winner had the disappointment of his life, as Alfred Schroeder reports from Bonn.

Alfred Schroeder

He's just been named Bad Luck Man of the Year '83 by German Television, having had half a million Marks on his account for four days, blowing some of it, and then being told it was all a computer error; he must pay everything back.

Deiter Zeibkard was informed by the Lotto Numbers Lottery Authorities early last year, he had been favoured by Lady Fortune, and won half a million Marks, a sum of money for which the 43-year-old steelworker, father of six children, would have to work for a good 15 years. He received the cheque, deposited it on his bank account and withdrew 66,000 Marks for his personal use. And what a use he made of it. He spent all of it in a giant drunken spree in three days with friends, women, gambling and drinking in the amusement district of his home town, Dusseldorf.

When he was empty-handed and returned back home 66,000 Marks were blown and nothing to show for it, except a king-sized hangover which doubled when the family told him it was all a mistake, a computer had made a tragic error. He had to return the remaining 420,000 Marks to the Lotto company, but had no idea how he could pay back the remaining 66,000. He lost his job meanwhile.

The case is now before a Dusseldorf court, where Mr Zeibkard's lawyer argued it was not his client's fault that the computer went haywire. It was his right to withdraw money from his bank account and spend it. The Lotto company's lawyer charged he had been negligent, he had not compared his Lotto ticket with the winning numbers, and was thus not authorised to accept one single Mark.

The judges will now have to decide. But with the enormous bad luck he had, there is also a tiny bit of good news: Mr Ziebkard did win the amount of 153 Marks, and no computer error there.

This is Alfred Schroeder in Bonn for AM.
