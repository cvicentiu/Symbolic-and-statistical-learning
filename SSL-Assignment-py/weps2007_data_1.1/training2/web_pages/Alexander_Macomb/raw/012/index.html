In 1755 John Gordon Macomb left his home in Dunturkey, Ballynure with his wife, sons Alexander and William and his daughter Anne to sail to America.

He became a successful business man by supplying the army, was appointed a judge in Albany and provided troops for Britain in the War of Independence

His son Alexander who left here at seven became a great landowner in possession of much of New York State. He had a home on Broadway which he leased to George Washington.

His grandson Alexander was to be a hero in the War of 1812 and eventually Commander in Chief of the Army

Today there are American towns and counties which bear the Macomb name of this family from Ballynure
