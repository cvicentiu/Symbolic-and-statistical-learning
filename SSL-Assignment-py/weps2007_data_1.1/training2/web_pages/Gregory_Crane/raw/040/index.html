YP Corp.One Step Closer To The End

 

They Got Their Hand Caught in the Cookie Jar and Now They are Trying to Put Back the Cookie

 

Part 1 of 2 of the M&M Ownership

 

With all that's been written about YP Corp recently, the issue of "The M&Ms" might be unclear to some.  There's a good reason for that.  The company's CEO Angelo Tullo wants the investing public to be as confused as possible, and he works hard at it.  Why?   He absolutely cannot afford for the truth to be known. 

 

It is the opinion of Stocklemon that the recent news of advance repayment of the loans by the M&Ms is a desperate attempt by YPNT to cover up its tracks.  There is no consideration for the accelerated repayment and appears to Stocklemon as being a ploy.  Attention YP.Net..Life is not a Etch A Sketch..you can't shake it up and make the past go away.

Just to review, since it's the date of its inception as a public company by reverse merger five years ago, YP Corp's controlling shareholders have been two Antiguan companies "Mathew & Markson", and "Morris & Miller", collectively referred to in all of YP's SEC filings as "The M&M's".  YP Corp has entered into a series of obvious  "sweetheart deals" with the M&M's, most notably a royalty payment agreement for what YP's SEC filings calls "Intellectual Property", which is actually a "discounted, 20-year license of the URL 'Yellow-Page.Net'", for the eye-popping sum of $5 million dollars. 

At other times the M&M's have borrowed money from YP, loaned money to YP, received YP stock as collateral, and extended YP stock as collateral.  The URL royalty and other deals have been the predicates by which YP has transferred more than $14 million cash offshore to the M&M's over the last four years.  These transfers are the single largest explanation of how YP can have a mere $2 million cash on hand after many quarters of its supposed "profits from operations".

If it is proven that these offshore entities do belong to any officer or director of YP Corp then:

·        All of YP's financial statements for the last four years must be restated.

·        It's $5 million "intellectual property" asset is a fraud.

·        The $1.4 million depreciation of that asset to date is a fraud.

·        The "loans" to and from the M&M's are fraudulent.

·        The payment of over $2 million to the M&M's to buy off Tullo's adversaries in the ABF Funding lawsuit is a fraud.

·        Every single statement filed in YP's SEC's filings with regard to negotiation and renegotiation of deals with the M&M's is a fraud, and subject to Sarbanes-Oxley criminal sanctions.

·        YP's auditor will be compelled to resign for failing to detect the fraud.

·        YP will lose its OTC BB listing for failure to maintain timely financial filings, and it will be a long, long time before that listing can be restored.

·        YP will be the subject of numerous shareholder class-action lawsuits.

·        YP and its control persons will inevitably be the target of regulatory intervention.

·        YP could lose its ability to bill through 3rd party billing on telephone bills, devastating its cash flow

 "Who are the M&M's ?"

CEO Angelo Tullo has steadfastly insisted that the identity of the M&M's is unknown, cloaked by Antigua's (tax haven) privacy laws.  "I wish I knew" Tullo sighed longingly when asked about the M&M's during the recent analyst conference call. 

Stocklemon has presented a trust deed showing Gregory "never keep anything in your own name" Crane, a YP founder, director, and Executive Vice President, grants a trust deed on his home to an M&M's.  The company explains it was "just a financing".

THE $5 MILLION DOLLAR QUESTION:

WILL THE REAL URL OWNER PLEASE STAND UP ?

When YPNT did its reverse merger, it leased rights to the URL www.yellow-page.net from Mathew & Markson for the eye-popping sum of $5 million dollars.  This would lead you to believe that the M&M's owned this URL, and YPNT usage of it was a critical piece of YPNT's business model.  Indeed Mathew & Markson, registered the URL on January 4, 1999.

Interestingly, the FTC, states in its case against YP

Gregory "mail fraud is just business experience" Crane and his incarcerated partner Michael Bloomquist  began operating Yellow-Page.Net from "at least April 1998".  Even more curious, although in an abundance of meticulousness the FTC Complaint named both Crane's and Bloomquist's wives as associated entities, as well as their "Publication Management Inc." entity and Telco Billing, nowhere are the M&M's mentioned.  Why?  Because they weren't part of the business then.  Which leads one to ask: "so who was the registered owner of "Yellow-Page.Net" prior to January 1999? 

Unfortunately for YPNT, prior URL registrations are available and Stocklemon has again found the truth:  the URL www.yellow-page.net was registered by administrator Gregory Crane (or more precisely, the Greg Crane Trust), to the "Brovo Holding Trust" (same address as YP Corp and its other businesses), through Network Solutions on August 2, 1997.   So YP's website and business were operating at that URL well before the M&M registered that URL in January 1999. 

 

Otherwise, how could the FTC have complained about YP's operations since "at least April 1998"?  (Here you can see how YP's website looked, substantially the same business, same content, etc, the month before the M&M's registered it.

So prior to the reverse merger, and prior to any recorded existence of the M&M's, Yellow-Page.Net was alive and well as the main domain name of Crane and Bloomquist's business Telco billing.  The URL was transferred to the M&M entity on January 4th 1999.  But how?  Although it is deemed to have a value of $5 million dollars, there is no record of consideration being paid by the M&M for receipt of this valuable piece of intellectual property.  It was first registered by Crane.are we to believe that he gave it away to an entity unknown to himself two years later?
