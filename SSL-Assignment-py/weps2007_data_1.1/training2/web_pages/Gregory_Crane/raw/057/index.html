The Melbern G. Glasscock Center for Humanities Research at Texas A&M 
University is pleased to announce the third lecture in its continuing 
Humanities Informatics series.

Gregory Crane, Professor of Classics at Tufts University and 
Editor-in-Chief of The Perseus Project  will be lecturing on "Humanities 
and Information Technology" at 7:00 p.m. on Wednesday 23 April 2003 in Room 
204E of the Sterling C. Evans Library.

Gregory Crane is the author of The Blinded Eye: Thucydides and the New 
Written Word (1996) and The Ancient Simplicity: Thucydides and the Limits 
of Political Realism (1998).  He currently directs a major grant from the 
<http://www.dli2.nsf.gov>Digital Library Initiative to study general 
problems of digital libraries in the humanities. He is also at work 
refining the classical collections in the Perseus Project, "an evolving 
digital library, engineering interactions through time, space, and 
language" that aims to bring an array of source materials - from 
collections on ancient Egypt to those on the American Civil War - to as 
many people as possible.  He is deeply committed to creating new audiences 
outside the university for all sorts of cultural materials, and part of his 
ongoing research focuses on "computational humanities"and how this new 
field can help to democratize information without compromising intellectual 
quality.

We hope to see you there.
