SHARING NATURE’S INTEREST
Ecological Footprints as an Indicator of Sustainability
Nicky Chambers et al
Every organism, from human or whale to bacterium, has an impact which the Earth has to support. If our ecological bank account is too depleted, the Earth cannot sustain those organisms. This book explains a practical method of managing our impact on the environment by using ecological footprint analysis. It is the first book since Our Ecological Footprint, published in the mid-90s, that offers practical advice backed up by sound science to enable everyone to reduce their ecological impact. 185pp

SHORT CIRCUIT
Strengthening Local Economics for Security in an Unstable World
Richard Douthwaite
Describes the environmental and social havoc caused by the blind pursuit of economic growth. Short Circuit turns its attentions to practical steps needed to build stable, sustainable economies. Drawing on the experience of communities in the industrialised world which have already achieved greater self-reliance, Douthwaite shows how local currencies such as LETS and credit unions can be developed to create a social microclimate in which a wider range of local production for local uses becomes possible. Full of solutions. 386pp
