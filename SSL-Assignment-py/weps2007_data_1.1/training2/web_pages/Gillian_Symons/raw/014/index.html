THIS SERVICE IS ONLY AVAILABLE FOR SCHOOLS WITHIN WEALDEN DISTRICT

To borrow books - please make a note of the numbers of the books you would like to borrow.  Then contact the Recycling Team at Wealden District Council:

By phone: 01892 602626

By e-mail: recycling@wealden.gov.uk

By post / County Courier: Recycling, Wealden District Council, Pine Grove, Crowborough, TN6 1DH.

The books will be posted to you for a standard three-week loan period (longer by prior agreement) and will need to be posted back to the above address at the end of the loan period.  State schools can use the free County Courier service for this.

0012

Worms Eat Our Garbage. (Classroom activities for a better environment). Mary Applehof. 1993. ISBN 0942256050
Literally hundreds of classroom-based activities linked to earthworms and worm composting. Divided into three sections: The world of worms; worms at work and beyond the bin. Curriculum links to science, art, reading, maths, geography, language and history.

0013

Don’t Throw It Away. 1992. ISBN 1857502000
Friends of the Earth’s guide to waste reduction and recycling.

0014

The Young Green Consumer Guide. John Elkington & Julia Hailes. 1990. ISBN 0575047224
Packed full of advice for young people, covering everything from felt tips to rubbish tips and fizzy drinks to kitchen sinks.

0015

Rescue Mission Planet Earth – a children’s edition of Agenda 21. 1994. ISBN 1856971740
The 1992 Earth Summit produced Agenda 21 – a blueprint for saving Planet Earth. In this book thousands of young people from 100 countries have worked together to find out exactly what was agreed in this important document. The aim of the book is to inspire young people all over the world to join the rescue mission to save our planet.

0017

Crafty Ideas from Junk. Myrna Daitz. 1993. ISBN 1850153930
Contains 25 projects for children aged 5-10, all using scrap materials found around the home. Examples include bedroom name plates, book ends, butterfly plant holders, a mad robot and various games.

0019

Scraps of Wraps. Vicki Cobb. 1988. ISBN 0713629932
Go on a science safari and discover the fun of being a scientist. This lively book is full of ideas for simple experiments to try at home or at school with packaging. Aimed at primary school age.

0020

Reuse, Repair, Recycle. Jan McHarry. 1993. ISBN 1856750450
Divided into three parts: Ideas and Actions; The world of recycling and The recycler’s A-Z, with myriad suggestions for reusing and recycling materials as well as providing facts and figures.

0021

The Dustman. 1984. ISBN 0241112109
From the Cherrystones series for young children about people’s working lives. Follows a Brighton Council crew on their daily round. (Shows how refuse collection used to be in Wealden before the introduction of wheeled bins).

0045

Youth Action and the Environment. Alan Dearling. 1997. ISBN 1898924074
This book is designed to bring environmental issues alive for anyone who works with young people. Young people themselves can use much of the material. It is all-embracing covering such issues as: outdoor activities; woodcraft; conservation working holidays; Agenda 21; earth spirit activities; land access; protecting local trees; alternative lifestyles; environmental counter culture; food and agriculture; home-made personal care recipes; recycling; alternative technology; networking; LETS systems and children’s rights.

0046

Backyard Composting. John Roulac. 1996. ISBN 1900322048
An excellent concise book covering everything you need to know about choosing and building compost bins, making and using compost.

0047

How to Make Hand-hooked Rag Rugs. Ann Davies. 1996. ISBN 085532807X
Clearly explained and illustrated. Rag rugs are fun and inexpensive and can be made from a range of scrap textiles and plastics.

0048

Paper into Pots and Other Fun Objects. Gerry Copp. 1995. ISBN 0855327723
This easy to follow book shows how to make papier-mâché bowls, plates, clocks and jewellery and decorate them with colourful hand-made papers – all from waste paper.

0049

The Recycling Book. Elizabeth MacLeod. 1991. ISBN 0920775586
65 craft activities for children using everything from plastic bottles to coat hangers, e.g. mobiles, musical instruments, puppets, comic keepers and decorations.

0050

The Blue Peter Green Book. Lewis Bronze et al. 1990. ISBN 0563208864
Explains the main threats to our planet and has action boxes with ideas for making a better world.

0051

The Conservation Project Book. Hilary Thomas & Shirley Thompson. 1990.  ISBN 0340516615
This book is full of activities and projects to help children think about the world in which we live and the effect we have on our environment. Includes: making bird feeders from milk cartons, testing the acidity of rain water and designing a green game.

0052

Finding out…About Managing Waste. Colin Harris & Jane Bickerstaffe. 1990.  ISBN 1853243760
A resource book for national curriculum science, geography and technology with applications for cross-curricular themes. Includes chapters on: The history of waste; what happens to waste; designing to reduce waste; conserving resources and recycling in practice.

0053

Finding out…About Packaging. 1993. ISBN 1853247383
A cross-curricular GCSE resource book for technology, science, EIU and environmental education. Includes chapters on: What is packaging; shopping in the good old days; materials for packaging; case studies; packaging and the world around us.

0054

Inside Outside. Cherry Mares & Robert Stephenson. 1987. ISBN 0905277058
An action plan for improving the school environment. Includes chapters on: Planning; classrooms; corridors; school grounds; curriculum links and reducing pollution & waste.

0055

Environmental Projects. 1989.
A directory of information for schools and community groups. Includes sections on: Starting projects; grants; publications; materials and demonstration sites.

0056

Ali-Can Colouring and Activity Book. 1996.
Activities designed to introduce young children to the concept and benefits of recycling aluminium drinks cans. Aimed at 5-11 year olds.

0057

Clean the Sea! The effects of sewage and garbage on seabirds and other marine wildlife.  1995.
Includes chapters on: The marine food chain; efforts to clean the sea; Regulations; Who pays?; sewage treatment methods; garbage and how you can help.

0058

Save the Sea! The effects of oil and chemicals on seabirds and other marine wildlife. 1993.
Three sections on: Oil; chemical pollution at sea and checking oil and chemical pollution.

0059

Work at Waste at School. Waste Watch. 1997. ISBN 1898026475
An essential guide for schools providing information on how to reduce, reuse and recycle waste, save money on waste disposal costs, audit school waste arisings and introduce practical environmental education into the classroom. Also contains several case studies of successful school initiatives to reduce and recycle waste.

0060

All about Compost. Pauline Pears. 1999. ISBN 0855328681
Over 160 informative and clear step-by-step photographs illustrate all you need to know about making and using compost. Lots of ideas on reusing household waste within the garden.

0061

Creative Handmade Paper. David Watson. 1991. ISBN 0855327308
Packed with creative and environmentally friendly ideas, this colourful book shows how to transform waste papers and plants into an array of beautiful handmade papers and sculptured landscapes.

0062

A Day in the Life of a Refuse Collector. Franklin Watts Books. ISBN 0749623357
Suitable for years 1-2, Key Stage 1. Informative series for first readers, suitable for developing pre-reading skills and useful for group reading sessions.

0063

First Starts – Waste and Recycling. Franklin Watts Books. ISBN 0749607122
Suitable for years 2-4, Key Stages 1-2. Key language structures and contains basic requirements to develop key research skills.

0064

I Can Help – Recycle Rubbish. Franklin Watts Books. 1999. ISBN 0749631961
Suitable for years 1-3, Key Stages 1-2. Useful for examining sentence construction, perfect for group reading. Lively investigation of environmental issues surrounding waste and recycling with lots of practical, simple activities that show what the problems are and how we can make a difference to where we live.

0065

I Can Help – Save Energy. Franklin Watts Books. 1999. ISBN 074963197X
Suitable for years 1-3, Key Stages 1-2. Useful for examining sentence construction, perfect for group reading. Lively investigation of environmental issues surrounding energy use with lots of practical, simple activities that show what the problems are and how we can make a difference to where we live.

0066

I Can Help – Save Water. Franklin Watts Books. 1999. ISBN 0749631961
Suitable for years 1-3, Key Stages 1-2. Useful for examining sentence construction, perfect for group reading. Lively investigation of environmental issues surrounding water use with lots of practical, simple activities that show what the problems are and suggest how we can make a difference to where we live.

0067

Mapworlds – Resources. Franklin Watts Books. ISBN 0749619880
Suitable for years 3-6, Key Stage 2. Contains basic requirements to develop key research skills. Theme-based series that helps children to locate different aspects of life around the world.

0068

What if? A Book about Recycling. Franklin Watts Books. ISBN 0749629991
Suitable for years reception-3, Key Stages 1-2. Suitable for shared and guided reading sessions, helpful for examining sentence construction. Brightly illustrated picture book with lively narrative. Raises green issues for young children while following the life of a discarded green bottle.

0069

Recycling – A Practical Guide for the School Environment. Learning Through Landscapes.  Dr John Feltwell. 1991. ISBN 1872865062
Full of activities, all curriculum linked. For example: carrying out a recycling audit of the school; looking at natural recycling cycles and undertaking a school water audit.

0070

How to Make Your Own Recycled Paper. Malcolm Valentine. ISBN 0855326700
Instead of throwing away waste paper, why not try recycling it to produce writing paper or gift wrap? This is an excellent step-by-step guide.

0071

Crafty Containers. Lois Walpole. ISBN 085532810X
Shows how to create a stylish and brightly coloured selection of baskets, bowls and containers using household waste. Newspapers can be painted, twisted and woven into bins and baskets; plastic bottles make excellent pencil holders; tins, bottle tops and wire can be formed into bowls.

0072

Making it Happen – Agenda 21 and Schools. Gillian Symons, WWF. 1999.
Provides the background to Agenda 21 and focuses on ways in which schools can engage with the process at a local level. It offers a variety of case studies, activity ideas and approaches developed to explore environment and development issues across the curriculum.

0073

Pl@net.com Teacher’s Guide. Channel 4 Learning. 1999.
Includes background information, programme outlines and looks at the impact of environmental change. Supports the Channel 4 series aimed at ages 9-13, Key Stages 2 & 3. It offers advice and information to young people on how they can make a difference to the environment. In the wake of Agenda 21, the programmes familiarise viewers with the major environmental challenges of the day, and their underlying economic and social causes. Case studies from around the world, chosen for their relevance and interest to children, highlight the scale and impact of problems. Access the website via www.channel4.com/schools/planet
