An opportunity to help influence the direction of DfES support and funding!

Education for Sustainable Development (ESD) is becoming a familiar term in schools, but is far from universally understood, and is not being widely taught. In an initial survey by the DfES, teachers reported that they need 'help' to introduce ESD themes.

The DfES is now funding a needs analysis for ESD in subject teaching to find out the kind of 'help' that teachers need, the obstacles to progress and what would help to overcome them. This will be carried out through a series of teachers' focus groups in various subject areas.

You are invited to participate in one of the following groups:-

Citizenship co-ordinators (primary and secondary)
Wednesday 19 May, 4.30 -6.30, near Finsbury Park underground station

Cross-subject group (primary and secondary)
Tuesday 25 May, 4.30 -6.30, near Finsbury Park underground station

A £50 fee for attending plus travel expenses will be paid.
Prior knowledge of ESD is not necessarily a requirement.

Responses from the focus groups will help to shape the way the DfES and subject associations use money that has been earmarked to support the development of ESD in the next few years.

If you are interested in attending either meeting, please contact Gillian Symons, email gsymons@dircon.co.uk, tel: 020 7241 6914

   

Calling all Teachers!

Would you like to be a part of an exciting new Development Education project?

The Consortium for Street Children is producing a new teaching resource based around issues relating to street children.  The resource will be for use principally in KS3 Citizenship programmes, but will be cross-curricular and applicable to PSHE, Geography and English programmes. 

As part of the process of producing the resource, we have formed a Consultation Group of teachers who will meet several times over the course of the project. We are still looking for more participants and would love to hear from anyone who would like to be a part of this exciting project.  The first meeting will be on Saturday 8th May at the CAFOD headquarters in London.

Travel expenses will be covered.

This project cannot run successfully without input from teachers.  We need your enthusiasm, commitment and experience to help produce an indispensable and effective teaching resource, created by teachers for teachers. There will also be opportunities to involve your pupils in trialling the resource and in the Young People’s Consultation Group which we will be setting up.

For more information or to register your interest please contact Hermione Ruck Keene on 020 7274 0087 or enquiries@streetchildren.org.uk.

The Consortium for Street Children is a membership organisation whose 35 members include the majority of UK NGOs working in the field of street children.  Working collaboratively with its members, the Consortium for Street Children co-ordinates a network for distributing information and sharing expertise around the world.  Representing the voice of many, we speak as one for the rights of street children wherever they may be.  For more information please see our website www.streetchildren.org.uk
