I've been looking at some entries for a future fiction write-off on lit.org, and I'd like to help the organiser, Richard Dani, out. I have a boss idea for an opening that he should perhaps use.

-------------
It was bound to be another tough night for Officer Matt Pershing. He was serving an evening shift, looking out for the antics of "Rocket Rick" Dunbar, car mechanic by day, street gang member and king of illegal drag racing by night. A slick fortysomething tough guy, Dunbar was. Pershing has been determined to bust this perp for a while. If only Dunbar wasn't hot for Pershing's widowed older sister, Katie Harding, and a wannabe father figure to her 14-year-old son, Ashley. That very notion disgusted him.

Tonight was no different. In the distance, he saw Dunbar's familiar blue car with the purple flames he painted on the doors, turning sharply onto Main Street. He drove down to where that car turned. Pershing saw stop-and-go racelights, a large wild crowd on the sidewalks, and a red sportscar waiting to race him. Another illegal race. He stayed around to watch it start up.

In five minutes, it did, and that's when Pershing took off after Dunbar. He turned onto another street and drove all the way to where the race ended as quickly as he could. He planned to get Dunbar there. He found the other stoplight on Main, just ten streets down. Just in time, he decided. Here come the racecars now.

Dunbar was victorious in another race, and that's when he caught Pershing's disapproving look. Dunbar stuck his head out the window and said, "Hey, Pershing, what the hell gives?"

Pershing went over to Dunbar and replied, "You know what the hell gives, Dunbar. Step out of the car."

But as Pershing opened Dunbar's door and got him out, he noticed a familiar teenage boy sitting in the passenger's seat. He had auburn-coloured hair, and wore those wire framed glasses that looked so familiar to him. He went to open the passenger's door and discovered something shocking.

"Ashley?" Pershing whispered.
------------------

I think this would make a good 4,500-5,000 word story for write-off contestants to do in 60 hours.

And now, I'd like to hear ideas from all of you. Write out some good openings - a paragraph, or maybe a whole opening scene, something that will catch the interest of Richard Dani. Note to Chrispian, if you see this, inform Richard of this thread right away.

Thanks for your time, and happy helping.

Dave.
