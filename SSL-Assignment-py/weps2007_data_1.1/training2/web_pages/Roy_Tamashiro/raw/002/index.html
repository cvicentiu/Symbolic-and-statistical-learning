The Faculty Development Center?

The Faculty Development Center (FDC) is part of the Institute for Excellence in Teaching and Learning and was funded through a Title III grant from the US Department of Education. The FDC is the second phase of the grant and was designed to support faculty in developing effective strategies and tools to increase student learning and retention. Located on the 4th floor of the Emerson Library, the FDC houses the following resources for faculty: video editing systems, PC and Macintosh computers, scanners, color printers, and network connections for personal laptop access to the campus network. The computers are equipped with a variety of advanced software applications as well as CD burners for saving projects.

What’s Happening at the FDC?

More of Webster’s faculty is discovering the Faculty Development Center. It’s a beautiful space to work in and the equipment there is state of the art. As of October 2, Daniel Bell, our Instructional Designer Developer, is on location for consultations and support. The FDC sponsored its first activity, a Teaching Strategies workshop, on Oct. 28, from 3:00-4:15 P.M. This Interactive Panel Presentation featured Monica Moore, Theresa Prosser, Dan Viele, and Kat Singleton. The members of the panel presented some of their successful teaching strategies. Afterwards, there was time for feedback and discussion.

During the month of November the FDC held several events. 2 information sessions about the Title III Fellowships were full with faculty discussing ideas and guidelines for submitting project ideas.The FDC also held its first open house on 4th of November. Faculty were give a tour of the center and shown demonstrations of the video editing stations and computer workstations. Several people have scheduled individual sessions with Dan Bell to discuss project ideas. If you would like to set up an individual appointment either email Dan Bell or call ext. 8648 on campus.

December 2nd , 3:00-4:30pm - Beware: Teaching With Technology Can Change Your Life! Presentation by Roy Tamashiro, Emerson Library Conference Room.

  • Does teaching with technology affect student learning?  Research results are equivocal.
  • Does teaching with technology affect the instructors doing the teaching? In more ways than we expect!

This multimedia presentation integrates the research on classroom technology and instructional effectiveness with examples of teachers who discuss the impact of technology on their students, their schools and themselves. Live interviews with teachers via videoconferencing included. Open discussion / dialogue.

This workshop is highly interactive where participants actually do the techniques. The discussion that follows each activity is from the student as well as the faculty perspective. About 10 different techniques are demonstrated and others are talked about. The workshop is based on Dr. Morrison-Shetlar’s book Teaching creatively: Idea in action.
