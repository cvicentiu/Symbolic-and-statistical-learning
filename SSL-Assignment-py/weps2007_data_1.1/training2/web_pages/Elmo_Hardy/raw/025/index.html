Author: Barefoot, Daniel W.



 

  

"Give me your tired, your poor,
Your huddled masses yearning to breathe free,
The wretched refuse of your teeming shore.
Send these, the homeless, tempest-tossed to me.
I lift my lamp beside the golden door."
By Emma Lazarus

 
 
This above passage can be found on the base of the Statue of Liberty and represents what is so great about America. The idea of opening our lands to the wretched refuse of foreign lands is noble in itself. We sought the homeless and the poor and became the greatest country on earth probably because of it. Today, our politicians have adopted a policy of acting only in self interest. We fear other foreign peoples because of their culture and poverty, we need to ask ourselves, are these really the values we want to show the world. Acting in self interest and fear shows a lack of understanding and compassion that will ultimately lead to our decline as a great nation.

Of all the natural gifts of life, time is the most precious, for upon time the use of all other gifts depends. It is said that Queen Elizabeth, when she lay dying, offered her kingdom for a moment of time; but there was no wise man in all the land who could give her that. But you and I have thousands and millions of these moments, and we waste perhaps thousands of them. There are people who are thrifty with money, who throw away golden moments as if they were of no value. Yet moments are golden things; for time, which we all share alike whether we are kings or beggars or ordinary people makes up our lives, and every bit of time we waste is a bit of wasted life. A moment here, and then gone forever.
