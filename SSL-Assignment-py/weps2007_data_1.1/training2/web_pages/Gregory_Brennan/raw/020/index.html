Wilkes-Barre Township Talk
By:Bernadine Elick 08/26/2004
All you wanted to know about the township's Kennedy Park

Today's subject will be how and why Kennedy Park came about.
Several decades ago, the water company decided to abandon the no longer useful Warsaw reservoir in the Warsaw section of the township. A group of civic-minded neighbors in the area got together and suggested that instead of having the land stand unused and vacant, it could be turned into a nice community park.
The neighbors who were responsible for spearheading the drive for a park were Walter "Lefty" Dorzinsky, Fred Dotzel, Matt Frankowiak Sr., Tony Klutz and Frank Danilowicz.
Their idea for establishing a park in the area brought prompt response and approval from the Board of Commissioners, George Sisko, Chet Welebob, Marty Flaherty, Stanley "Bud" Stavish and Mike Butchko.
The township officials went ahead and filed an application for a grant, Project 70, from the state, which was earmarked especially for the expansion of park facilities. The grant was approved and used to purchase the land. There were no local tax dollars involved.
The commissioners then sought help from Congressman Dan Flood and, through his efforts, were able to obtain the local National Guard Corps of Engineers to drain the reservoir and level the banks.
The next project was the construction of a combination hard and softball field. Tom Elick used his heavy equipment to construct the first baseball field.
The pump house was renovated as a meeting center and was the first senior citizens center in the township. Seen meeting daily at the center were Joe "Chief" Geffert, Andy Bella, Tony Bella, Andy "Pete the Cop" Kuren, Frank Danilowicz, George "Zeke" Yedlock, Frank Sulitka, Andy Wabik and Frank Wabik, among others.
Long ago, there was only a cow path overlooking the reservoir. Reservoir Street was built later and linked Center Street with Metcalf Street.
A chain link fence was installed around the ball field and the entrance road to the park was paved.
The following members of the Warsaw Sportsmen Club volunteered their services and erected the flag staff in the playground area as their community project 35 years ago: Joseph "Rosie" Pekol, Paul Weiss, Don Callahan, Paul Levandowski, Joseph Konopczyk, Tony Klutz, Ed Klauss, Bob Brozowski and president Tom Elick
During the terms of former Mayors George Sisko, George Daru, Bob Delescavage and present Mayor Carl Kuren, many additions and improvements were made such as the establishment of the playground area, new playground equipment, basketball and volleyball courts.
The combination playground/ball park is a popular field used by local ball players and also by the children who live in the area.
The park was named after the late President John F. Kennedy.
Pete "McGee" Papsun, who contributed to this article, has lived his entire life in his family homestead, which is adjacent to the former reservoir and now Kennedy Park.

New police to be sworn in
The township council has called a special meeting prior to the work session on Monday, Aug. 30, at 7 p.m. in the municipal building for the swearing-in of three new full-time police officers. Mary Yuknavich will preside.

Wedding anniversary wishes
Congratulations and best wishes to John and Debbie Molitoris who celebrated their 25th wedding anniversary yesterday, Aug. 25. They were married in St. Joseph's Friary by the Rev. Gregory Brennan. They are the proud parents of a son, John III. Debbie is the daughter of Carl Alber of Wapwalloppen and the late Celia Alber. John is the son of John and Margaret "Midge" Molitoris of the township.
