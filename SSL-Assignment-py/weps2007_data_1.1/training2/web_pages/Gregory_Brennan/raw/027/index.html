What is my userid?
Your userid (also known as login, username or UWinID) is a string that uniquely identifies you on our network. It appears on the front of the "@" sign in your University of Windsor's e-mail address and it is being used as login name for all University computer systems. If you are a student and are now sure what your UWindsor.ca e-mail address is, you may determine it at http://www.uwindsor.ca/uwinid

What is my password?
Students: Your default password is your student number (a nine digit string, no spaces or other characters between individual digits). This password, referred to as "Domino password", can now be synchronized with your UWinID password (more...).
  • If your student number password does not work, try your UWinID password.
  • If your class ViCKI site has been assigned a "group login", you would use this login with password assigned and distributed by your professor. The HelpDesk does not have access to this information. Please contact your professor.
  • If you are or have been employed by the University and have had a student’s Lotus Notes account generated, you will use the password that was assigned to you then.

Faculty and staff: Your password has been assigned to you when your Lotus Notes account was created. This password is referred to as "Domino password" and it is separate from your Lotus Notes password. You will use your Domino password to access your mailbox using Webmail (iNotes) interface or IMAP, as well as any areas of the University Web site that are password protected. You will use your Lotus Notes password only in Lotus Notes. Your Domino password can now be synchronized with your UWinID password (more...).
