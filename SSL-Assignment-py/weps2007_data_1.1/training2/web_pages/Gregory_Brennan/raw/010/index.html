Board of Directors

WiTec Alberta's Board is represented by a strong mix of senior leaders in the technology space. A brief bio on each Board member follows:

Derek Ball,  CEO of Sonic Mobility Inc. comes to the Board with over 13 years of experience operating within the technology sector. Derek brings a broad knowledge of wireless business applications, mobile device technology and a network to some of the major industry players such as Microsoft, Palm and RIM.

Gregory Brennan, Tax Partner with more than 25 years of experience with KPMG Calgary is currently the Industry Tax Leader in the Information, Communications and Entertainment Group, which he co-founded in 1990.  He has also provided consultation to the Alberta and Federal Governments on issues germane to taxation in the technology industries.

Peter Garrett, An industry consultant with over 23 years experience in corporate management and technology commercialization. Over his career, Pete has held a variety of senior positions within Nortel Networks and was recently CEO of Global Thermoelectric. Pete also participates on numerous boards and advisory commitees.

Bill Hews (Chair), President of Fideliter Inc. has been involved in the wireless telecom industry for 25 years. He worked for Nortel Networks in a variety of operations roles with global scope before moving to Wi-LAN Inc. as President and COO. Mr. Hews is chairman of the board of directors for WiTec Alberta.

Tim Hodge, Bell Mobility’s General Manager of Enterprise Sales for Alberta brings an in depth understanding of the Canadian wireless industry gained through his senior roles in Marketing, Strategy, Merger and Acquisitions, Distribution Management, and Sales.

Don Kjosness, Chairman and co-founder of illumiCell corporation Don has over 30 years experience in the development and management of diversified, advanced technology organizations in information, communications and space systems technologies. Don is currently also Chair of NEWT's advisory board.

Martin P.J. Kratz, Partner with Bennett Jones LLP has 22 years legal experience in the information technology and communications industry combined with substantial experience in the protection, enforcement and financing of wireless and telecommunications companies.

Michael Leung, Vice President Business Development with TRLabs has more than 30 years experience in Canada’s ICT sector, including Bell Canada, and SaskTel. His 15 years with TRLabs has been dedicated to growth and expansion of the research and development community in western Canada.

John Masters, President and CEO, Calgary Technologies Inc. leads the economic development agency focused on building Calgary’s hi-tech community. Active on a wide variety of boards and committees John was a driving force behind Calgary’s selection as the co-recipient of the World’s Top Intelligent Community award in 2002.

Drew McNaughton, President of Axia SuperNet Ltd. offers 23 years experience in building, managing and operating large information technology programs and networks and has held senior roles at both Axia and Nortel Networks.

David Miles, Telus's VP Engineering and Operations (West) has been part of the Senior Management team of Telus Mobility for the last 9 years. Prior to joining Telus, David held a variety of Senior Engineering positions with Bell Mobility and Motorola.

Charles Reichert, Director of INFOPORT with Calgary Technologies Inc. has over 34 years experience within the technology sector in the Province. The last 14 years has centered on roles focused on strengthening the economic development activity within these sectors.

Barb Richardson, Director of SpringBank TechVentures brings 20 years of experience in telecom and technology with extensive knowledge and contacts in the local and international wireless industry. Barb is currently involved with venture financing in the industry and sits on a number of boards including Calgary Economic Development.
