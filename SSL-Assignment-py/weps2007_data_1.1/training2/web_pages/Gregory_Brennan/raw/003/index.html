What Happened to the Loafers and Blue Blazers? by Gregory J. Brennan
~~~ A New College Conservative

The New York Times in a recent magazine article Armies of the Right announced that a new generation of college conservative has emerged on the nations college campuses and declared themselves the grass roots defenders of opportunity, individual freedom and free speech. Yet, upon closer examination, it would seem these conservatives hardly warrant the attention they have garnered, or the designation grassroots movement. After all according to a conservative strategist quoted in the article these students need pointers on everything from how to dress cool to how to respond to liberal policy attacks. They dont even raise their own money, but instead receive checks to fund campus newspapers and organizing activities direct from Republican consultants in Washington.

If There Were a Real Alternative in '04 by Gregory J. Brennan
If there were a genuine Democratic alternative to George W. Bushs current conservative government in the 2004 election I wonder what it would look like. If one wanted to answer that question and went to South Carolina to observe the Democratic debate and get an idea they probably would have left disappointed and with no better an understanding of what ideas distinguish Democrats from Republicans than they had before they arrived. With the exception of Richard Gephardts health care plan, there was not a single idea expressed that substantively distinguished Democrats from Republicans. The debate could not have been an encouraging sign to Democratic voters who are hoping their party can somehow emerge from the 2004 election with at least a foot hold in one branch of federal government.
