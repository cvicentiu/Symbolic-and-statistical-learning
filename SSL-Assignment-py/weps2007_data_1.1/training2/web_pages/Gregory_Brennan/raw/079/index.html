Local teen awarded rank of Eagle Scout
By AMANDA GRABOWSKI, Lifestyles Editor March 31, 2004
GREAT VALLEY — The rank of Eagle Scout was awarded to Justin Ward of Ellicottville Troop 652 at a Court of Honor held Sunday at the Great Valley Firemen’s Clubhouse by Scoutmaster Wayne Schuver of Boy Scout Troop 663.
Justin is the 18-year-old son of Mark and Barb Ward of Great Valley, a senior at Salamanca High School and a member of the Great Valley Volunteer Fire Department.
He has been active in scouting since the age of seven when he started as a Cub Scout.
The rank of Eagle is the highest awarded in Boy Scouting and is earned by less than two percent of scouts nation-wide.
In order to achieve the rank of Eagle, a scout must earn 21 merit badges, 11 of which are required.
The Eagle candidate must serve actively in a troop position for six months after becoming a Life Scout. He must also plan, develop and carry out a service project.
For his service project, Justin created a computer data base, listing the grave sites of all area veterans in area cemeteries. He and his grandfather, Joe Ward, placed flags on all veteran’s graves for Memorial Day and he hopes it will stay an annual event.
The candidate must appear before a review board and satisfy them that he has fulfilled the requirements of Eagle Scout and has lived up to the to the Boy Scout oath and laws.
The Rev. Ronald Mierzwa, pastor of the Holy Name of Mary Church offered the Invocation at the event, which was attended by about 50 friends, relatives teachers and fellow scouts.
The closing prayer was offered by Fr. Gregory Brennan, OFM.
As part of the Eagle Charge,received won his own Eagle Award in 1962, the new Eagle scout was encouraged to “live up to the obligation of an Eagle Scout.”
