Background
The SR&ED Program has been in place since 1985. It affords Canadian companies an avenue to write off expenditures incurred on R&D carried out in Canada. In addition, the Program provides tax credits at the rate of 20 to 35% of eligible expenditures (depending on the size of the company). Small companies do actually get refund cheques, while larger companies accumulate credits. These credits can be carried forward for up to ten years, and can be used to offset income taxes payable. Ample guidelines exist for claiming SR&ED credits and CCRA will be willing to assist claimants in explaining the rules and regulations. We encourage you to join us to learn more about the SR&ED Program  which is considered to be the best among the OECD countries for promoting and leveraging industrial R&D.

Benefits to Participants
This workshop will provide valuable information to oil and gas executives on how to use the Federal Government's Scientific Research and Experimental Development (SR&ED) Program to leverage expenditures on R&D carried out in Canada. The session will also serve as the inaugural meeting of the proposed CCRA SR&ED Technical Working Group of petroleum services companies that carry out R&D in Canada.
