ENTERPRISE
From the July 4, 1997 print edition

Achieving success in 4 paragraphs

If you haven't met a prospective customer socially or at a business event, your first contact is usually by letter.

"The objective of the introductory letter is to get the prospect to take your call so you can ask for an appointment," said Gregory Brennan of Brennan Communications in Cotati, author of "Successfully Self-Employed."

Brennan recommends an introductory letter with four brief, single-spaced paragraphs on one page. It should be addressed to a specific person, not to a title, like "office manager."

  • In the first paragraph, describe how you got the prospect's name.

  • In the second paragraph, state what you can do for the prospect.

  • In the third paragraph, tell the prospect how you have helped similar customers in similar ways. "Don't be shy or modest," said Brennan. To get the business, you need to tell the person how successful your similar efforts were for other companies.

  • In the fourth paragraph, tell the prospect when to expect your call. "Don't ask them to call you at their earliest convenience," Brennan said. "You have to be proactive."
