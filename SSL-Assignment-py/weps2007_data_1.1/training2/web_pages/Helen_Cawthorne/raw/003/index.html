Windsor Triathlon – 13th June 2004

 

A record field of 2250 saw G.B. Olympic star Andrew Johns beat fellow Olympian Tim Don (3rd ) with crowd favourite & 2 x World Champion Spencer Smith into 5th. The first 4 all sped out together onto the 1st of the 3 lap run course but after weighing up the opposition on the first lap there was only ever going to be one winner. In the ladies race Athens bound Julie Dibens was one of 2 women that broke away on the bike and ran to a comfortable victory as the “true” runners in the field toiled to make up any distance in the heat.

Nottinghamshire X/C County Champs, Markeaton Park – 3rd January 2004

 

Redhill win 3 team medals & 3 individual titles from the County Champs!

 

Markeaton Park, Derby was cold & still come race day (change that to very cold) with the underfoot conditions ideal on the course. The men were off 1st on the usual 3 lap 10k North Midlands X/C course and it was soon evident that Notts AC & Derby AC would dominate their respective County races. With the runners soon split across the course it also became apparent that the minor team medals were up for grabs and that the Vets race would be a straight fight between Mansfield & Redhill with the other teams way off the pace. Calculating individual & team positions was complicated by the presence of the Derbyshire runners and certainly the Vets race was too close to call. Andy was 1st home for Redhill (11th overall) & 8th Notts finisher therefore achieving his goal of qualifying for the Inter Counties in March. He was followed in by Paul, Mark and the rest of the team. Team placings were too close to call, the men would have to wait for their results.

 

The ladies were off next running their own 4 (yes 4) lap 8k course. Debbie Robinson (Tipton) stormed to the front as expected and the field immediately split. Team positions were easier to calculate with the smaller field suggesting Mansfield were leading followed by Charnwood, Redhill, Notts & then Sutton. Helen was leading for Redhill followed by Ann, Sarah (who had latched into one of the only small groups on the course) & then Chloe. The field was very strung out and positions settled in the race. We calculated we were in with a shout of a team bronze, which was more certain than what we could possibly calculate from the men’s race. Helen was first home in 8th and as with Andy qualified for the Inter Counties, Ann was next in 9th followed by Sarah & then Chloe. We awaited the results. 

 

After an age the preparation for the awards commenced, a quick sneaky look at the hand written results confirmed a team ladies bronze (Mansfield 1st & Charnwood 2nd) and also a less certain senior mens bronze (behind Notts AC “A” & Notts AC “B”), the mens Vets race was really close but a team silver (only 1 point behind Mansfield) was the reward. County individual honours for the club came from Andy (Vet M40 Champion), Helen (Vet, F35 Champion) & to Ann (Vet F45 Champion).

 

Several Redhill club members went home proud & very satisfied with County X/Country team medals, well done to all!
