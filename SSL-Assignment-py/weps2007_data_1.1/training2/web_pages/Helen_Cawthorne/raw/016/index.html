U.S. Age Groupers Claim
39 Medals at Worlds

Taking advantage of a world championship almost in their own back yard, 29 members of the U.S. age group triathlon team stood upon the podium at the International Triathlon Union (ITU) World Triathlon Championships on Saturday in Edmonton, Alberta, Canada.

Twelve members of Team USA won gold medals at the Olympic-distance (1.5K swim, 40K bike, 10K run) race. 

At least two of the U.S. winners were repeat world champions, including Sister Madonna Buder (Spokane, Wash.), who competed in the women’s 70-74 age group, and won her eighth world title. Buder completed the 1.5K swim, 40K bike and 10K run in 3 hours, 3 minutes and 21 seconds.

Karen McKeatchie (Ann Arbor, Mich.) won her fourth world title as she placed first in the women’s 45-49 age group in 2:19:58.

The youngest U.S. winner was Ashley Carusone (High Spring, Fla.), who won the women’s 16-19 age group in 2:17:28.

Heather Gollnick (Hartford, Wis.) won the women’s 30-34 age group (2:09:50) and Donna Kay-Ness (Enfield, Conn.) took the women’s 35-39 category (2:10:23). Harriet Anderson (San Carlos, Calif.) finished first in women’s 65-69 (3:04:13).

In the men’s races, Des Nunan (Ocean City, N.J.) won the men’s 45-49 title (2:02:30). Duane Fritchie (Lees Summit, Mo.) won the men’s 50-54 crown (2:09:14). Dick Robinson (Bonita, Calif.) was the men’s 70-74 winner (2:44:07), Walter Palmer (Indianapolis, Ind.) took men’s 75-79 (3:04:59) and Charles Futrell (The Village, Fla.) won the men’s 80-84 category (4:38:44).

In the physically challenged race, Willie Stewart of California, who is missing an arm, crushed the field in a time of 2:18:11.

Complete results are available at www.triedmonton2001.com.
