  Marathon effort rewarded
Longford Marathon may not have been quite as dramatic as its Athens counterpart - run on the same day - but it still attracted the hundreds of supporters who came out to cheer on the brave participants of the half and full marathon lengths.
While Olympic runners had to contend with a mad Irish priest before finishing their run along the ancient route of the first ever Marathon, their Longford counterparts luckily had only to contend with one enemy: themselves.
A total field of over 500 men and women taking part in the Longford marathon and half marathon pushed themselves to the limit of their endurance, and beyond, to complete what was for them all, a great personal achievement.
Sponsored by Joe Duffy Motors, Longford the main event was won by Paul Buckley of the Ferbane Athletic Club, Co. Offaly in a time of 2.39.14, only a couple of minutes off the course record.
Next came Joe O’Sullivan of Bandon, Co Cork in 2.45.44 and third was Vincent Mulcahy, Nenagh, Co. Tipperary in 2.52.12.
The first woman arrived back to Ballymahon street just before the three hour mark, with Helen Cawthorne of Derbyshire, England taking the female race in 2.58.59. Second was Catherine Dunne of Dunboyne, Co. Meath in 3.17.12, and third was Mary Jennings of Waterford in 3.17.50, just 38 seconds behind again.
In the men’s half marathon Tony Dalton of the Belfast Harriers won in 1.13.33. While in the hotly contested women’s half marathon Josephine Killeen from Clare beat off fierce early competition from the Leader’s own Sheila Reilly, to win in a time of 1.28.30: Page 4.
