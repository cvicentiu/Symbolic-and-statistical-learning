Chairman Continues Discussion on Global Climate Change

Chairman Gordon spoke to a group of congressional staff and scientists on the importance of understanding the science behind sea level rise during a recent briefing sponsored by the Pew Center on Global Climate Change.

Sea level rise is one of the most widespread climate impacts expected as a result of global warming.

"We know with 100 percent certainty that global warming is occurring and with 90 percent certainity that humans are causing it. Case closed," Gordon said to staffers.

The day before, Gordon and Members of the House Committee on Science and Technology held the first Congressional hearing on Section One of the 2007 Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change (IPCC) assessment report, Climate Change 2007: The Physical Science Basis - Summary for Policymakers.

Members heard from House Speaker Nancy Pelosi and four authors of the report produced by Working Group One of the IPCC.  The report represents a comprehensive appraisal of the current state of scientific knowledge on climate change.

 

 Gordon on President's FY08 Budget: Lacks Priorities, Consistency to Ensure U.S. Competitiveness

On February 5, the Administration submitted its budget request for Fiscal Year 2008 to Congress.  The almost $3 trillion request includes $142.6 billion for research and development.

"While the President’s budget includes some important funding increases, it lacks the priorities and consistency to ensure our competitiveness now and in the long run," said House Committee on Science and Technology Chairman Bart Gordon (D-TN).

Chairman Works to Bolster Science and Math Education

"Innovation and competitiveness are just buzzwords until Congress takes action to make them a reality," said Chairman Gordon as he re-introduced several pieces of legislation aimed at improving math and science education, and putting the U.S. on the cutting edge of innovative energy research.

"10,000 Teachers, 10 Million Minds" Science and Math Scholarship Act (H.R. 362) [section-by-section]

Sowing the Seeds Through Science and Engineering Research Act (H.R. 363) [section-by-section]

Advanced Research Projects Agency - Energy (ARPA-E) Act (H.R. 364) [section-by-section]

Versions of both H.R. 362 and H.R. 363 passed the full Committee in June of last year.  "These bills are familiar and their provisions are widely acknowledged.  They serve to enact measures to strengthen the U.S. standing in the global economy, make sure our kids are prepared for the careers of the future, and usher in bold changes to this country’s approach to energy research," said Gordon.

The legislative package will serve as the vehicle for a broader discussion of these issues by the Committee.  Chairman Gordon intends to move all three bills through the Committee early this year and send them on to the full House for consideration.  This package will serve as a cornerstone of the Democrats’ Competitiveness and Innovation Agenda.
