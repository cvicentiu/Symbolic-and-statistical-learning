Three Texas freshmen on Final Four rosters…two Texans are finalists for national Player of Year…more area all-star teams announced by local newspapers...recruiting updates...a few more notes from the playoffs…and more

December 17 – Three former Texas high school stars will be returning home when the NCAA Final Four tips off Thursday at Reunion Arena in Dallas. Florida has all three former Texas stars on its roster led by 2002 LoneStarVolleyball All-Texas Team member and Outstanding Hitter Amber McCray, a freshman opposite from Longview Pine Tree. Also on the squad are Stefanie Douglas, a freshman middle from Belton, and Rhian Davis, a freshman libero from Austin Bowie. McCray's sister, Nicole, was a member of last year's Final Four team.

In naming Clark coach Kirby Jameson the San Antonio Area Coach of the Year, San Antonio Express News writer Matt Sherer tells an interesting story about the Clark coach.

According to Sherer, after losing to arch-rival Clark in the playoffs Churchill OH Morgan Spencer sat on the bench in tears. Jameson walked over to console the Chargers' star and said, "You have nothing to be ashamed of, Morgan, you and your team had a great year, and you should be proud of what you have done this year."

"I was totally amazed that coach Jameson took some time to talk to me after they had beaten us," Spencer told Sherer. "That was truly a classy thing to do."

Finally, we have one more favorite quote from the state tournament that probably expresses the feeling of many seniors hoping to continue their volleyball careers.

Following her team's loss to Wallis Brazos in the 2A championship, Poth 6'2" senior Amanda Garza was asked about her college plans. Garza, who had 30 kills in two matches at San Marcos, said, "I hope somebody takes me...anybody takes me. I love volleyball."
