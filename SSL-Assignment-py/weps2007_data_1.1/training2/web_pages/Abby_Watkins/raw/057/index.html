Volunteers
AWIS-SA intends this page as a message board for programs of interest to AWIS members to advertise their events and seek volunteers. As the Seattle Chapter of AWIS is not the sponsoring organization, please use the contact information below to learn more about individual opportunities. Please email submissions to awis@scn.org for consideration.

Programs involving K-12 students:

  • Interested in helping Puget Sound area middle and high school students sequence DNA? The High School Human Genome Program at the University of Washington provides equipment, reagents and volunteers to more than 20 schools annually. Time commitments vary from one to four hours and training is available. Contact Kristi Martinez (hshgp@u.washington.edu, 206-616-4538) or visit the program web site (http://hshgp.genome.washington.edu) for more information.

  • Mentors are needed for the Fourth Annual Student Biotech Expo (www.studentbiotech.org)! The Student Biotech Expo is hosted by the Washington Biotechnology Foundation (WBF), a non-profit organization dedicated to increasing the understanding of biotechnology through education. The 2004 Expo will be held March 18th at Meydenbauer Center, Bellevue. The Student Biotech Expo draws together high school students from thirteen Seattle-area biology, biotechnology and molecular biology classes and allows them to present their work to each other and to the public. The Expo promotes science literacy and allows students to showcase work that reflects different aspects of biotechnology and biomedicine. Two key elements of the Expo distinguish it from conventional science fairs. First, each student is matched with a community mentor who has experience in the student's area of interest. This program facilitates connections between the scientific and education communities and provide increased opportunities for student access to resources. Second, students projects can be presented in a variety of forms - from art, drama, or music to molecular modeling, web design or traditional scientific research. For all projects, students must research the science behind their chosen topics. Students have the opportunity to see the relevance of science to their own interests, and can demonstrate their learning in ways that best suit them.

    Mentors are invaluable assets to students. The role of the mentor is to serve as a reference to the student for their project. Time Commitment varies from 2 - 20 or more hours depending on type of contact. We work with mentors to accommodate their availability! Mentorships can range from email contact to internships. Mentors who sign up within the next few weeks will have the opportunity to attend one of the mentor/student get-together at a host research facility.

    Information about Mentorship, including Sign-Up Forms, can be found at http://www.studentbiotech.org/mentor_info.htm or contact Abby Watkins at awatkins@nwabr.org.

Programs involving undergraduate students:

  • AWIS-SA and WISE have teamed up to provide mentors to undergraduate women in the biological sciences. Currently we are in need of 25 mentors. For more information or to volunteer your time, contact us at awis@scn.org.

  • The Making Connections program at the University of Washington Women's Center is seeking mentors for high school girls from the Seattle area. As a female student or professional in a math, science or technology field, this is a wonderful opportunity to make a difference in the life of a high school student interested in pursuing higher education and exploring different career paths. For more information email or telephone Priya at (206) 616-2336.
