Juvenile Literature Collection Mission Statement


The Juvenile Literature Collection supports curricular, research, and scholarly activities of the students and faculty of the university. Adults are the intended users of this collection, which contains a broad spectrum of materials published for young people. The availability of any book should not be considered endorsement of that book.

Opie Collection


The Opie Collection: Chiefly British Children's Books, from the earliest to the present, collected by Iona and Peter Opie, housed in the Bodleian Library of Oxford University, England.

BYU's Opie Collection is on microfiche. Printed guides for each unit contain four indexes organizing the information by fiche number, author, title, and Opie number, and containing complete citations for each entry. You may access the guides in Juvenile Reference PN 1009 .A1 O64x Guide and the microfiche in Humanities Reference under the same call number.

The Treasures of Childhood: Books, toys and games from the Opie Collection can be found in Juvenile Reference PN 1009 .A1 O64 1989. It is a delightful full color photographic treatment of selected treasures from the Iona and Peter Opie collection that was assembled over a period of forty years.

1999 12th Annual BYU Symposium on Books for Young Readers

Friday and Saturday, 16-17 July 1999, Provo Marriott Hotel

Approximately 390 persons enrolled for the two-day event which featured: Kathryn Hewitt, Kathleen Krull, Sid Hite, Louise Plummer, Ted Rand, Cynthia Voigt and Paul O. Zelinsky.

  • Kathryn Hewitt and Kathleen Krull have collaborated on several books for children, including the award-winning Lives of the Musicians: Good Times, Bad Times (and What the Neighbors Thought); Lives of the Writers: Comedies, Tragedies (and What the Neighbors Thought); and a collection of hymns entitled Songs of Praise
  • Sid Hite is the author of many outstanding books for young readers, including Dither Farm, It's Nothing to a Mountain, Answer My Prayer, and Those Darn Dithers.
  • Louise Plummer is a Utah writer who writes books for both children and adults. The Romantic Obsessions and Humiliations of Annie Schlmeier, The Unlikely Romance of Kate Bjorkman and My Name is Sus5an Smith, the 5 is Silent are some of Plummer's titles.
  • Ted Rand has illustrated several dozen books, including Keepers byAlice Schertle and Mailing May by Michael O. Tunnell.
  • Cynthia Voigt's works include Dicey's Song (Newbery Medal), The Callender Papers (Edgar Allan Poe Award), Solitary Blue, The Runner and Come a Stranger.
  • Paul Zelinsky illustrated Swamp Angel and Hansel and Gretel, among other books. He authored and illustrated Rapunzel (Caldecott Medal).

Children's Book and Play Review

Children's Book and Play Review (CBPR) is published five times annually, once every two months from September through June, by the Department of Elementary Education, the Department of Theatre and Film, and the Harold B. Lee Library of Brigham Young University. Statements and opinions of CBPR are the responsibilities of the authors and reviewers and do not necessarily reflect views, constitute endorsement, or set policy of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints or Brigham Young University.

Subscription rates are $15.00 for one year and $28.00 for two years.
