Elske
by Cynthia Voigt

Thirteen-year-old Elske escapes rape and certain death at the hands of the leaders of her barbaric society and later becomes handmaiden to a rebellious noblewoman whose rightful throne together they reclaim.

Genre: Fantasy | Historical Fiction

reviewed by Virginia Johnson

Plot Summary: Elske, a child of the barbarian Volkaric people, is chosen to be the sacrificial maiden when the old king dies. With the help of her grandmother, Elske escapes her gruesome doom and travels into the civilized land of Trastad where her fate will entwine with that of a banished princess.

Review: Newbery Medal-winner (Dicey's Song)Cynthia Voigt has given us yet another strong and memorable heroine in a world drawn from the early days of European history. Elske changes as she meets people who value her bravery and intelligence. We watch as she sheds her earliest understanding of the hardness of a girl-child's existence in a warrior society. Later she uses that early knowledge to fight the tribes who would destroy her adopted civilization.

Favorite: Elske's reaction to a society so different from the one in which she was raised is beautiful in its combined innocent wonder and stoicism.
