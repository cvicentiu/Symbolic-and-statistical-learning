Review by Alice Cary

After reading just a few pages of Cynthia Voigt's Bad Girls, I was instantly transported back to fifth grade, when notes fly back and forth faster than a stockbroker's faxes. Mikey Elsinger and Margalo Epps discover each other on the first day of school, both newcomers in strict Mrs. Chemsky's class. They soon realize they share more than the same initials: they are bad.

The deliciousness of their devilment stems from the fact that while hardly criminals, they become partners in pranks, and, indeed, rather serious mischief. As each mother notes when she meets the other girl: "But she looks perfectly ordinary. She looks like a nice girl." Only a talented writer like Newbery winner Voigt could handle Margalo and Mikey so wonderfully.

Alice Cary is at work on biographies of Jean Craighead George and Katherine Paterson.
