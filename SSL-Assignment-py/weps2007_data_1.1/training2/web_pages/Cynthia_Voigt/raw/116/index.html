Mikey Elsinger and Margalo Epps are two naughty girls who are to determined to cause havoc in the fifth grade. But are they friends? Could these two naughty yet very different girls be friends? Perhaps they will be after a pet day that goes very wrong and an ongoing battle with Louis Caselli. But will their newly found friendship be wrecked by an argument over class president?

Find out in Born to be Bad. You can read for yourself about their unbelievable adventures and see what you think of these bad, bad girls. Cynthia Voigt has produced yet another entertaining book for us to read. What’s more, look out for Bad, Badder, Baddest and It’s Not Easy Being Bad, so you can find out about all of theses wicked girls hair raising adventures. 

Cynthia Voigt has certainly produced a masterpiece.

Charlotte, aged 11, Canberra, ACT

Born to be Bad, by Cynthia Voigt is a great book with the funniest happenings. It is about two Year 5 girls who are well….BAD!

When Mikey Elsinger and Margalo Epps meet in Year 5, they instantly realise that they don’t have much in common. Mikey has rich parents, is a sporty person and has a big mouth. Margalo hates sports and will do WHATEVER she can do get her own way. But one big thing they have in common is that they are bad. Margalo and Mikey team up together and figure out ways to put people like Louis Casseli in his place. The two go through good and bad times, but the whole book is hilarious. The book shows that no-one is the same and everyone should have a mind of their own.

I give this book a 7 out of 10 for its hilariousness. I recommend this book for people in Years 4, 5 and 6 because the age groups are the same.

Kate, Year 6, Canberra, ACT
