Past Reviews

Anne Frank: Beyond the Diary by Ruud Van Der Rol

     This book is about Anne Frank's life before she went into hiding, and more background information on the things written in her diary. There are many pictures of her, her family, and of the 'Secret Annex.' I thought the pictures in this book were great, and also all the things that Anne Frank didn't mention in her diary. I would recommend this book to people who want to learn more about Anne Frank and the Holocaust.

Anne Frank - the Diary of a Young Girl by Anne Frank

     Although I thought this book was very boring and uninteresting, many people would disagree with me. I must admit that it is the best account of the Holocaust and the horrors the Jews had to live through. Millions of other people just like Anne Frank were deported to concentration camps and killed. I would recommend this mainly to teenage girls and adults, because of the things Anne writes about.

The Bookstore Mouse by Peggy Christian

     Cervantes is a mouse who lives in a bookstore behind the books in the reference section. It is there that he hides from Milo, the bookstore cat. Until one day, when Cervantes falls on the floor far away from his home in the reference section and nearby where Milo is on the prowl.
     Reading has always helped him, and when he stumbles upon a very old book, he stops to look at it. "Far away, and long ago..." is how the book - and Cervantes' adventure - starts.
     This book is a funny, unique adventure through strange and unusual words. I like this book because in it Cervantes learns to conquer his fears, and face life with a sense of adventure. Fans of "Redwall" will definitely enjoy this book.

The Boxes by William Sleator

     This book was about a girl named Annie, whose Uncle Marco gives her two boxes to look after when he goes on one of his many, strange trips. She was told not to open them, but she does. A strange creature crawls out and goes into their basement. Annie must decide whether or not to open the other box. This book was strange and interesting. I would recommend this to people who like science fiction books. 

Ella Enchanted by Gail Carson

     Ella was given a fairy "gift" at birth. It was more like a curse to her. The foolish fairy, Lucinda, had given her obedience. She had to do whatever she was ordered to do - whether it was giving away her mother's necklace or cutting off her own head! Despite this curse, she manages to live a fairly happy life with her mother and Mandy, the cook. Until one day, her mother falls gravely ill and dies. Then Ella's father, a merchant, returns home and decides to send her away to a boarding school. At the school there are two girls who find out about her curse and set to treating her like a servant. It is then that Ella decides she must find a way to break the curse - once and for all. I liked this book because it was a wonderful retelling of the classic Cinderella story, and had the most surprising plot twist at the end. It makes you wonder if Ella will ever live "happily ever after" up to the last chapter of the boo. I would recommend this book to any classic fairy tale lover.

The Firegold by Dia Calhoun

     Jonathan has blue eyes. Because of this, he is afraid he will be insane when he grows up. His friends tease him and call him "loony-blue". His father is obsessed with finding the legendary firegold apples and doesn't speak to Jonathan much. The only one who ever seemed to care about him was his mother. One day while he was fishing, he saw a girl. A barbarian Dalriada girl with blue eyes. Jonathan wondered if she was afraid of becoming insane, like him. But when she saw him, he ran. As the year went on, things start happening faster than Jonathan could keep up with. Then tragedy strikes and Jonathan has to make his decision to go to the Red Mountains or stay in the Valley. I liked this book because it was suspenseful and had good story action. I would recommend it to any horse-lover and teens from thirteen to fifteen.

The Folk Keeper by Franny Billingsley

     Corinna was abandoned by her father on the steps of an orphanage by her father as a baby, and grew up slaving and serving for those who didn't care for her. It was then that she burned her skirts and became a boy - Corin Stonewall, Folk Keeper. She ruled the cellar, keeping the Folk from turning the milk sour and other minor inconveniences. Then she is taken from the orphanage and taken to a strange man who knows her secrets. He commands her to be Folk Keeper of a great manor by the coast. Strange things begin to happen to her, things she can't explain. She tends to the Folk of the manor and spends time with a young man, Finian. This is her Folk Record and journal - The Folk Keeper. This book keeps you wondering what is going to happen and turning the pages until the end. I liked this book and its mythical creatures and how it tells about a girl trying to figure out just what and who she is. I would recommend this book to girls thirteen to fifteen.

The Forestwife by Theresa Tomlinson

     Mary is a young girl who ran away from an arranged marriage. She an her nurse, Agnes, together went searching for the Forestwife, only to find out that she had died. From that point, Agnes took over as the Forestwife, and Mary was called Marion. This is a stunning re-telling of the classic Robin Hood Story, a must-read for anyone who enjoys medieval period literature. I would recommend it to anyone who loves an adventure of has a fascination with Sherwood Forest and Robin Hood.-

I recommend this book to everyone. If you like Sci Fi or Fantasy this book's for you. First in the seven book series, this book is the shortest and one of the best. if you like this book then read Harry Potter Year Two the Chamber of Secrets.
