The Newbery Award was established in 1922, to commemorate John Newbery, a highly successful eighteenth-century bookseller who was the first to carve out a profitable niche for children's books.

The Newbery honors the author of the most distinguished contribution to American literature for children published in a given year. It is intended to encourage original and creative work, to publicize contributions to literature for children, and to spur good writing in the field.
