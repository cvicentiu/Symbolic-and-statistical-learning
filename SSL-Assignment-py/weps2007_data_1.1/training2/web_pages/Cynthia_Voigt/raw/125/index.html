W.P.S. Summer Reading

    Welcome to our exciting summer reading program! This book list represents just a few of the wonderful books that will help you escape into the world of reading. We realize reading should be fun and we hope you'll have fun reading this summer. We encourage you to read books, but we also know reading does not always mean books. Other types of reading material include newspapers and magazines. This book list represents just a few of the wonderful offerings that your classmates have suggested.
    If you need more ideas to help you select wonderful summer reading, ask your teacher, ask your librarian, ask your parents, ask your friends. But most of all, READ!

James A. Caradonio, Ed.D.
Superintendent of Schools

SUMMER ACTIVITIES

    Students should keep a reading log or journal of their summer reading. While schools and teachers may give other assignments in September, a journal will allow you to recall the book when you return to school in the fall. Remember: Summer reading counts for 10 percent of your first-quarter grade.
    This journal could consist of three entries per book:one entry either before or shortly after you begin reading; one entry part way through the book; and one entry when you finish the book.
    These entries can take any form, but they should record your thoughts while you are reading the book-what you predict might happen, how the story connects with your life, or how you liked the book. You may also draw pictures to represent the events or characters in the book in other ways.
