The Aftermath Of Izzy, Willy-Nilly

A WebQuest for 9th Grade English

Bob Andresen

 

Introduction | Task | Process | Evaluation | Conclusion | Credits


Introduction

Crash!  In an instant, Izzy�s life has changed forever.  She has many decisions to make, and she has to adjust her life in many ways.  Her view of her self will also change.  Among her large decisions was to keep the secret that she was not driving the car, considering that she was too young to drive.  What pressures guided Izzy�s decision to keep secret that she was not the driver of the car, and was the decision an appropriate decision? 


The Task

As Izzy�s true friends, you and your group are going to help her in one of two ways:

After reviewing the information, you are going to reassure Izzy that keeping her secret is the correct thing to do.

Or

After reviewing the information, convince Izzy that she should go to the police with the information about the accident.

Your group will do an oral presentation using PowerPoint. Since this assignment will be completed while you are reading the book, your group will have two weeks to prepare for the presentation.   Also, upon completion of the presentation, each student will do a reflection essay, either defending the group�s position, or explaining the reasons that you disagree with the group�s conclusion.


The Process

 

I. After being broken into groups of 4, students will go to the following links and answer the questions. 

Topic: From the students�standpoint

Website 1: About Marco and his Drinking

1.     Answer the checklist for Marco, assuming that Marco is completely honest.  What questions were easy to answer, what questions were hard to answer?

2.     Would the author of the website recommend that Marco see a counselor about his drinking?

Website 2: More About Marco and his Drinking

3.     Answer the 20 questions the way Marco would.  Would Alateen be appropriate for him or for Izzy for that matter)?

4.     According to Alateen, does Marco have a problem with drinking?

5.     Would you recommend that Marco talk to an Alateen representative?  Why or why not?

6.     If he does have a problem, with what steps could Alateen help him?

Website 3: Izzy after the Accident?

7.     What are the signs of depression?

8.     Which signs does Izzy show after the accident?

9.     What type of treatment does Izzy receive?

10.  How does she react to the treatment?

Topic: Peer Pressure

Website 4: Bullies?

11.  What is bullying?

12.  Look at the �Signs that you are Bullying Others�.  Is Izzy every bullied?  If so, when?

13.   Does Izzy ever become the bully?

Website 5: Peer Pressure

14.  What is peer pressure?

15.  Why do students pressure other students?

16.  Why do students allow them selves to be pressured?

17.  In what ways does Izzy allow herself to give in to peer pressure (both before and after the accident)? Please give specific examples?

18.  At what time could Izzy have said no to the peer pressure and avoided the whole situation?

Topic: Other Consequences

Website 6: For Marco

19.  For Marco, what would be the punishment if he were found guilty of DWI  (in Minnesota) at the scene of the accident (answer this question for both the short term and long term)?

20.  What would be the implications for the other characters at the party? How about the homeowners?

Website 7: For Izzy

21.  According to the website, what things does Izzy need to know?

22.  How should people treat �new amputees�?  Specifically, how do the characters treat Izzy (give examples)?

23.  Look at the list of Do�s and Don�ts.  Which characters are good about the do�s?  Specifically, what do they do?  Which characters use the don�t list?  Specifically, what do they do that they shouldn�t?

II. After collecting the information, meet as a group to share the information. 

1.     Decide what stance you will take as a group.  In your presentation, make sure that you address each topic.

2.     Design PowerPoint slides.

3.     Write a script, utilizing the notes part of the PowerPoint window.

 

III. Presentations to be completed in Class.

IV. Reflection essay.  Answer the question about whether you agree with your group, or your conclusion is different from your group.  Explain your reasoning.

Conclusion

Many of the issues in Izzy, Willy-Nilly are not as clear as they would seem.  Izzy has many resources that she never uses, family, counselors, police, and other groups.  Hopefully, you will see that there are many available resources when you need help.
