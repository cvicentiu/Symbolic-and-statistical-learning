Upper Dublin HS: 8th grade --> 9th grade

Honors (091): Read one book from the list below, one of student choice, and The Hessian by Howard Fast.
College prep (092, 093): Read one novel from the list below and one of student choice.
 Choose only one book from each author.

Bury my heart at Wounded Knee by Dee Brown. A honest view of Native Americans being driven from their native land. 

Ender’s game by Orson Scott Card. One of a group of children is bred to be a military genius. 

Into thin air by Jon Krakauer. In 1996, Jon Krakauer went with an expedition to Mount Everest. Disaster struck. 

Uncle Tom’s Cabin by Harriet Beecher Stowe. This book includes the experiences of a slave in the pre-Civil War South.

The adventures of Huckleberry Finn by Mark Twain. A young boy and an escaped slave experience adventures in the South. 

Modified college prep (094): Read one book from the following list or from the above list.

Little women by Louisa May Alcott. The four March girls during the Civil War. 

Sounder by William H. Armstrong. An African-American boy and his dog grow up together during a time of segregation. 

Harriet Tubman by Ann Petry. Harriet Tubman leads slaves to freedom through the Underground Railroad. 

Light in the forest by Conrad Richter. A young boy is captured and raised to be a Native American. 

Little house on the prairie by Laura Ingalls Wilder. A family moves to the Kansas prairies in the 1800s.

Student choice

The following books are suggestions for your summer reading pleasure. These books may be read as the “student choice” book for eighth graders entering grade nine. You may, of course, read a book that is not on the list as your “student choice” book.

Fiction
Speak by Laurie Halse Anderson. A high school girl, Melinda, is an outcast for reporting a party to police until the truth is discovered and her bravery exposed (National Book Award Finalist). 

Warriors don’t cry by Melba Patillo Beals. An inside look at the integration of Little Rock’s Central High School in 1957 through the memories and experiences of Melba Beals, one of the nine African-American students who first entered the school. 

The Martian race by Gregory Benford. If NASA lost funding and the commercial sector took over a manned Mars landing, this is what would happen.

Dandelion wine by Ray Bradbury. In a small town in 1928, a 12-year-old boy savors the magic of childhood and the wonders of summer (Reading Olympics selection 2003-2004).

The second summer of the sisterhood by Ann Brashares. Sequel to The sisterhood of the traveling pants. Once again Carmen, Bridget, Tibby, and Lena share adventures and pants over the summer. Once again, it's a summer filled with life-altering experiences for each of them.

The girl with the pearl earring by Tracy Chevalier. The story of Greit, a young servant girl hired by the famous painter, Vermeer, takes place in Delft during the 1660s. Vermeer employs her as his assistant--and ultimately has her sit for him as a model.

8 plus 1 by Robert Cormier. Nine realistic short stories that are warm, touching and intensely personal. 

I am the cheese by Robert Cormier. An innocent young boy recounts a suspenseful tale of government corruption and espionage. 

Andromeda strain by Michael Crichton. For five days American scientists struggle to identify and control a deadly new form of life (Reading Olympics selection 2003-2004). 

The Crazy Horse electric game by Chris Crutcher. A high school athlete, frustrated at being handicapped after an accident, runs away from home and is helped back to mental and physical health by a benefactor and the people in his new school. 

Running loose by Chris Crutcher. Louie, a high school senior in a small Idaho town, learns about sportsmanship, love, and death as he matures. 

Catch-22 by Joseph Heller. A bombardier, based in Italy during World War II, repeatedly tries to avoid flying bombing missions while his colonel tries to get him killed by demanding that he fly more and more missions (Reading Olympics selection 2003-2004).

Dune by Frank Herbert. Book one in the Dune chronicles. Forced by the Emperor of the universe to live in exile on a barren planet, Duke Leto Atreides and his son lead the struggle against the Empire (Reading Olympics selection 2003-2004).

Looking for Alibrandi by Melina Marchetta. Josie, a senior, meets the father she never knew, deals with two vastly different love interests, and the pressures of being different in an elite private girls school. 

Letters from the inside by John Marsden. When Tracey and Mandy first became pen pals, they never guessed that they would soon discover the darkest secrets of each other’s troubled lives. 

The golden compass by Philip Pullman. Accompanied by her daemon, Lyra sets out to rescue her best friend and other kidnapped children. A captivating fantasy filled with excitement, suspense, and unusual characters. The first book in the “Dark Materials” trilogy. 

The coffin quilt by Ann Rinaldi. The story of the famous Hatfield and McCoy feud in the 1880s.

Stargirl by Jerry Spinelli. The life of a 10th grader is difficult and confusing for most teens, but Stargirl enters her first year at Mica High unusually self-assured and generous. This insightful novel is the story of her struggle between acceptance and individuality. 

Seventeen against the dealer by Cynthia Voigt. Dicey struggles to make a go of a boat-building business while facing family concerns, romantic problems, and the uncertainties of a drifter who offers to help her in her work. Sequel to Homecoming and Dicey’s song, but stands on its own. 

The color purple by Alice Walker. The story of two sisters: Nettie, a missionary in Africa, and Celie, a child-wife living in poverty in the American South.

Dragonflight by Robb White. An exciting adventure as Lessa of Pern wages a single-handed secret battle against her sworn enemies--the dragonmen! (Reading Olympics selection 2003-2004).

True believer by Virginia Euwer Wolff. Living in the inner city amidst guns and poverty, 15-year-old LaVaughn learns from old and new friends, and inspiring mentors, that life is what you make it (Reading Olympics selection 2003-2004).

My darling, my hamburger by Paul Zindel. Four high school seniors struggle with the responsibilities of growing up, particularly the problems of an intimate relationship. 

Nonfiction
 I know why the cages bird sings by Maya Angelou. The author tells of her painful childhood and adolescence. She grew up to be a professional dancer, actress, poet, jounalist, and television producer (Reading Olympics selection 2003-2004).

Growing up by Russell Baker. The memoirs of the Pulitzer prizewinning columnist of the New York Times (Reading Olympics selection 2003-2004).

Bird watching: on playing and coaching the game I love by Larry Bird. Larry Bird discusses his basketball career with the Boston Celtics, and tells what it was like to start a new coaching career with the Indiana Pacers. 

Bound feet & Western dress by Pang-Mei Natasha Chang. The story of Chang Yu-i, a woman who challenged Chinese tradition, and ran the Shanghai Women's Savings Bank during the 1930s (Reading Olympics selection 2003-2004).

What are you? Voices of a mixed-race young people edited by Pearl Fuyo Gaskins. A thoughtful collection of poems and essays on the realities of growing up as a mixed-race child. 

Red scarf girl: a memoir of the cultural revolution by Ji-Li Jiang. The author tells of her happy life in China until the age of 12, when the Cultural Revolution began.

No pretty pictures by Anita Lobel. Anita Lobel’s gripping memoir of surviving the Holocaust. From age 5 to age 10, Lobel was hiding from the Nazis, protecting her younger brother, and surviving completely dehumanizing conditions in a concentration camp. 

Dry tears: the story of a lost childhood by Nechama Tec. The memoir of the author during the years she and her family struggled for survival in Nazi-occupied Poland.

Old man and the sea by Ernest Hemingway. A Cuban fisherman tries to catch an elusive marlin. 

Their eyes were watching God by Zora Neale Hurston. An African-American woman learns about love and life in the 1930s. 

Flowers for Algernon by Daniel Keyes. A man undergoes a brain operation to increase his intelligence. 

The fixer by Bernard Malamud. A Russian Jew tries to defend himself after being accused of a brutal murder. 

My name is Asher Lev by Chaim Potok. A young man is torn between his religion and his talent for painting and drawing. 

Marjorie Morningstar by Herman Wouk. A woman who dreams of becoming an actress learns that life does not always turn out as planned.

A child called It by David Pelzer.

Izzy, willy-nilly by Cynthia Voigt. Izzy’s life changes after she loses a leg in a drunk-driving accident.

The pigman by Paul Zindel. Two teens learn about life from an older man in their neighborhood.
