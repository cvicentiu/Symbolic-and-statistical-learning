Izzy, Willy-Nilly
BY, Cynthia Voigt
Sophia S.
7th grader
Van Antwerp Middle School
One of my favorite books that I read this year is, Izzy, Willy-Nilly. The reason is, I loved the story, characters, and moral. It was sad and a little upsetting, but it was still wonderful. It was written very well and thought out wonderfully.
Izzy, Willy-Nilly, is about a young girl who's life totally changes after a car accident with a drunk driver. She has to have half her left leg removed, do to the accident. The author, Cynthia Voigt, does a great job showing the reaction of family and friends and their change in behavior. But Izzy, discards this and seeks comfort and friendship with a girl named Rosamunde. Rosamunde, being called ugly and a little over weight, can understand how Izzy feels, for everyone has always treated Rosamunde as though she does not belong because of her appearance. They all think she is weird. And Izzy does at first too, but she learns that she is the only friend she has that understands her pain. Together they learn how to make people not feel sorry for them, but instead look up to them, It is a wonderful story, showing how cruel people can and will be if your outside changes, even if your still the same inside.
