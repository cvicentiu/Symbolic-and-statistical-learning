Introduction:

"'You be good,' she said. 'You hear me? You little ones, mind what Dicey tells you. You hear?'" With that, a disoriented Liza Tillerman disappears into the crowd of shoppers at a Connecticut mall leaving her four children--Dicey, age 13; James, 10; Maybeth, 9; and Sammy, 6--abandoned, frightened, and confuses. Finally, Dicey makes a decision: they will travel on foot to their Aunt Cilla's home in Bridgeport, the only other family tie they know. Homecoming is the story of the Tillerman children's unforgettable odyssey. (source: Ballantine Teacher's Guide)

Objectives:
  • to complete the reading comprehension questions based upon chapters 1 and 2 of this story
  • to create a one minute dramatic monologue based upon one of the characters and present it to the class

Reading Comprehension Questions:

Please answer all questions using complete sentences, proper format, spelling, punctuation, capitalization, etc. Should a question be worth two points, a minimum of two sentences is required; if it is worth three points, then three sentences are required, etc.
1.

Identify four clues that indicate there is a problem situation in chapter one. (3)
2.

What do you believe happened to Momma? (2)
3.

Think of four reasons that would lead someone to abandon another person (you may brainstorm with someone else). (2)
4.

Identify two elements working in the children's favour; identify two elements working against them. (4)
5.

Re-read the paragraph on pp. 32-33 starting at, "He was no less stubborn now..." Although Dicey is reflecting upon Sammy, who else might this be referring to? Provide evidence to support your answer. (2)
6.

Create a character sketch for Dicey. You are to analyze her motivations, determination, personality, maturity, her role as the protagonist. The sketch needs to be at least one hundred words and divided up into clear sections (paragraphs). Use references from the story to back up your opinion; you may use quotations from the story. (7)
7.

Identify and explain what you believe is one of the main theme in this story. Possible themes include:

  • Life is not always just.
  • Family bonds are important to succeeding.
  • Ingenuity is essential to survival outdoors.
  • The world is filled, at once, with good and evil people.
  • Desperate need gives rise to inventiveness.
  • Self-confidence breeds triumph despite adversity (3)
8.

As an adult, Dicey will probably suffer from a "lost childhood". Explain what is meant by this in relation to her situation. (3)
9.

At the beginning of chapter two, James stated, "It's still true." (p.21). What was James hoping would be different? (1)
10.

What is a heroine? List five words that describe Dicey. Explain why she is a heroine. (3)

The Dramatic Monologue:

  • To appreciate different points of view/perspectives, prepare a one-minute dramatic monologue for one of the characters in a specific situation. Here are some ideas:
    • Dicey as she urges the children to pack their paper bags with clothes;
    • Dicey as she wonders why her mother had wandered off in a shopping center crowded with Saturday morning shoppers.
    • James as he states, "It's still true."
  • Evaluation: total value = 10 points
    • 4 pts for content (reference to the story)
    • 2 pts for memorization
    • 4 pts for performance
