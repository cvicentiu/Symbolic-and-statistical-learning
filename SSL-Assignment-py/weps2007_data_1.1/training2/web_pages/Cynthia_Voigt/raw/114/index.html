Lake Ridge Middle School
Summer Reading Program

Phone: (703) 492-1311


The purpose of the Summer Reading Program is to encourage students to enjoy quality literature and to continue developing their independent reading skills. Student participation in the program voluntary.  Students who choose to participate in the program are required to complete at least two readings over the summer.
  • We encourage parents and/or guardians to aid their children in selecting books that are appropriate for them.  If any parent or student feels uncomfortable with any author or title selection for any reason, a new choice should be made immediately.
  • Students should read at least 2 books.  Students will provide the author, title, publishing information, and an explanation of the general topic or plot of each book, along with a parent or guardian statement that the student has completed the reading.
If students participate in the Prince William Public Library System's "Summer Quest" or "Teen Quest" summer reading program, program documentation will be considered sufficient.

 Note Keeping Strategies:
    Students may wish to keep a learning log, create a story web or take notes on the books they read so they can refer to one of these items as they complete the required assignments.

Explanation of Extra Credit:
    Students will receive an extra A for each book completed, for a total of two.  Extra credit will count 10% of the student's grade in the first marking period.  Grades will be provided to students by the end of the second week of school.
