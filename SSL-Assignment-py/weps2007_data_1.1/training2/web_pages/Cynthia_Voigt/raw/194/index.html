Riley Royal Reviews

 

All American Girl
 Meg Cabot
Fiction
Reviewed by Natalie

I read All American Girl by Meg Cabot..and I thought it was one of the best books I have ever read! Samantha, an unpopular sophomore, skips art class one day and ends up saving the President of the USA from assasination!! Meg Cabot makes you feel like you know Sam personally. Then, Sam has trouble figuring out who she likes: Jack, her sisters boyfreind, or David, the President's cute but geeky son? Read the book to find out how she thinks she screws up everything!!!!

Bad Girls in Love*
Cynthia Voigt
Fiction
Reviewed by Samantha

This is a very good book. It is about a girl named Mikey who falls in lovewith the star of the 8th grade school play. Mikey has never felt this way before so she depends on her best friend, Margalo, to help her through this year. Mikey doesn't know what to do so she starts to smother Shawn with cookies and pushes through the crowd to get to him. Mikey soon learns that being in love doesn't mean changing your personality for a certain person,and she learns that the hard way. As you read this book you will fall inlove with the way that Cynthia Voigt gives each character their own voice.This is an awesome book that I would recommend for 6th through 8th graders. If you like this book as much as I did then you should check out more of Cynthia Voigt's books such as, It's Not Easy Being Bad, Homecoming, and Dicey's Song.

Devils Night: And Other True Tales of Detroit*
Ze’ev Chafets
Non-Fiction
Reviewed by Nick

My book, Devils Night: And Other True Tales of Detroit, is the best book I have ever read. It is told in third person narration, which makes the book seem like you are in the story. The author, Ze’ev Chafets, gives a very detailed story on how Detroit was in the past. He talks about the colors of burning houses and the way the smoke smelt. Like I said, it is a very good book! As soon as I read the first page of Devils Night, the author had me hooked, and that’s what I like in a book. What also makes the book fun is the author tells the story from when he’s 13 years old. I would recommend this book for people from the age of 13 to adults.

Crispin: The Cross Of Lead
Avi
Reviewed by Jeanene
Fiction

Crispin: The Cross Of Lead is a book about a boy in old time Europe, who was convicted of stealing, but was innocent. John Aycliffe who told everybody of the crime, declared Crispin as a wolf’s head, meaning that anywhere, anytime and anyone can kill him. Crispin's friend told him of this news. He told Crispin to run away and he does. Not far along the way he meets a juggler who goes by the name of Bear. Bear takes him in as his own and together they sing songs and juggle. When they got to the town they were supposed to entertain at, Crispin and John Aycliffe meet again. Crispin narrowly escapes that time, but when John Aycliffe takes his best friend Bear and keeps him in the dungeons, Crispin goes to rescue him.

Will they make it out alive? What will happen to John? Will Crispin ever get away from him and become free? Read this exciting book to find out.

The Diary of Anne Frank
Anne Frank, Otto Frank
Biography
Reviewed by Anna

The Diary of Anne Frank is a wonderful book in describing the life of families that lived during the holocaust. Anne Frank was a typical 13-year old. She loved hanging out with her friends and talking about boys. She also loved to write in her new diary she got for her birthday. Unfortunately, all of that changed when she had to go into hiding because Adolf Hitler was a man who captured as many Jewish people as possible. Anne Frank was of Jewish descent. When Anne was in hiding, she wrote in her diary. This book was written as Anne Frank' s diary. It had the date of the entry so you knew when an important event happened, or just what happened that day. I felt like I was right there in the annex watching everyone. I would highly recommend this book to young adults who are interested in the holocaust or in Anne Frank

The Earth, My Butt, and Other Big Round Things*
Carolyn Mackler
Fiction

Virginia meets a boy named Froggy and they hang out with each other every Monday after school. Froggy doesn’t talk to Virginia in school though. Gathering Blue
By: Lois Lowry
Fantasy
Reviewed by Aimee

Gathering Blue is an interesting book by Lois Lowry. This book is about how a girl with a crippled leg, lives in a place where she is useless. Well, that’s at least what her rival, Vandara thinks and so she takes her to the Council of Guardians. They let her live, but she is given a job that no one else in the community can accomplish. She then lives at a place where other people have special talents too. She soon finds secrets and powers surrounding her and she wants to find the truth about her world. With the help of a scrawny boy, his dog, and a wood carver, she will uncover what hasn’t been for many years… This book can be complicated at points and I wouldn’t recommend it for anyone under the age of 10. The book has tiny print and is 215 pages long. For me the level of reading was a little above average. I think the moral of the story is to succeed, you must work hard. Lois Lowry has written many great books for young teens including The Giver, Number the Stars, and Find a Stranger, Say Good-bye. You might want to read those books if you like Lois Lowry’s writing!

Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix
J.K. Rowling
Fantasy
Reviewed by Graham

In this book 15-year old Harry Potter lives in a perfectly normal household. Most anyone who looks at that house and the people it contained would probably think nothing special of it. But Harry was not perfectly normal at all, at least not by our standards. He was a Wizard, and attended a Wizard training school called Hogwarts. His parents had been Wizards as well and had been murdered by a powerful Dark Wizard called Voldemort. Harry lived with his aunt and uncle and their son. This made him severely mad because they were Muggles (non-magic folk) and because of his abnormalities they treated him like scum.

Harry goes to his godfather's house after an infuriating summer and finds out about a group of Witches and Wizards fighting against Voldemort. Harry goes back to school after going to a trial for a false crime and not being convicted. There is a new teacher at his school that is reporting to the Ministry of Magic which is reporting to the public that Harry is lying about Voldemort's return to power. After telling the truth about the return of Voldemort in his new teacher's class, Harry has to bear a week's worth of cruel detentions. Harry has been having dreams about dark passageways and locked doors. In situations he can actually see what is happening at a certain time in the dreams. These dreams turn out to be both helpful and deadly.

I really liked this book. It really pulls you in. I read it in five days and it's over 800 pages long! I would recomend this book to anyone. Even if you don't think it's your style of book, you should just try it and start the series!

Island Book 3: Escape*
Gordan Korman
Fiction
Reviewed by Briauna

Island Book 3 is a real page-turner. It is the third book in a 3 book series. With 132 pages of pure fantastic reading, this is the ideal book for young teens and kids.

The book is about six kids, a shipwreck, and a Desert Island. The six kids are J.J., a movie star's snobby son, Carla, an addicted athlete, Luke, who was found with a gun, Lyssa and Will sibling rilvaries, and Ian, the discovery channel freak. Their parents thought they needed some help so they put them on this boat ride called Charting a New Course, a boat ride designed to teach kids discipline. That all changes when the crewmate abandons them, the boat sinks, and the captain is dead!! Now all six kids are stranded on a desert island in the middle of nowhere. The worst thing about it is that no one even knows that this island exists.

Will the kids survive this terrible accident or will they starve to death? Will they ever get back to their families who think they are dead because they have been gone for so long? You have to read the book to find out what happens to these six kids, and what terrible challenges they have to overcome.

The Sisterhood of the Traveling Pants
Ann Brashares
Realistic Fiction
Reviewed by Allie

I truly enjoyed reading The Sisterhood of the Traveling Pants by Ann Brashares. She is an outstanding author, and I would recommend the second book in this two-part series, The Second Summer of the Sisterhood. This is a story about four close friends who have spent every summer of their lives together! But now, they all have plans to be somewhere else over the summer, apart from each other. Lena flies to Greece to visit her grandparents, Bridget goes to Baja California, Mexico to attend a soccer camp, Carmen flies to South Carolina for a visit with her father, and Tibby stays home all summer. While on their separate vacations, each girl learns something about themselves. The girls keep in touch by writing, and by sending a magical pair of pants back and forth. You see, the day before they departed, Tibby found an old pair of pants stashed away in Carmen's bedroom, and asked if she could have them. Why did she want them, I do not know. When Carmen said yes; they were just some junky old jeans she had bought at a Thrift Store. Tibby decided to try them on. Tibby and Carmen were very different when it came to sizes, so no one expected the pants to fit Tibby´s frail figure. When they (amazingly) did, all the other girls tried them on as well. When they saw that they fit everyone, the Pants were declared magical and that´s how it all began. Anyway, this is a very bright, fun story. It will make you laugh and it will make you cry. But mostly laugh. This book is paperback and 290 pages long, but the reading goes by very fast. If you enjoy books about love, friendship, and finding the true you, then you should read this book!! It is also available in hardcover.

Treasure Island
By Robert Louis Stevenson
Adventure
Reviewed by Cara

This is a classic everybody should read. If you are into adventures, you should check this out. There were moments where I was truly terrified when I read this book. But the fear that I might never know what would happen next overcame that terror and I was encouraged to continue, page by page. The story is about a young boy named Jim Hawkins who comes across the treasure map of the infamous Captain Flint, a pirate who terrified any man who set out to sail the seas. He goes off in search of the treasure with his trusted friend, Dr. Livesey and a whole crew of men. Jim comes to discover that the ship's jolly one-legged cook, John Silver was actually Captain Flint's first mate and half the crew are bloodthirsty pirates. What will become of Jim Hawkins? Read to find out!

The Two Towers
By J.R.R. Tolkien
Fantasy
Reviewed by Amanda

The Two Towers is the second book of the Lord of the Rings trilogy. I suggest that you read the first part first, The Fellowship of the Ring. The sequel to The Two Towers is the Return of the King. I gave The Two Towers a four-crown rating because while I enjoyed it, The Two Towers was not my favorite of the three books.

The Two Towers takes place in the world of Middle-Earth. This story picks up where the first one left off. The hobbits Frodo and Sam are off to Mordor alone. Gandalf the Grey, a wizard, fell in Moria, a dwarf city, attempting to defeat a demon. Aragorn, Legolas, and Gimli are off to Rohan, to help the people of Rohan in the war against Saruman, another wizard. But where are the hobbits Merry and Pippin and the man from Gondor, Borimer?

This is a harder book about good versus evil. I also recommend that if you intend on reading the book and seeing The Two Towers movie, that you read the book first.
