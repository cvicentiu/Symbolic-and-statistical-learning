Literature   and   Life 

LITERATURE IS LIFE AND
	     LIFE IS LITERATURE. 


Because this is such a 
meaningful statement, 
I felt a good assignment
for my English 10 classes 
would be to show how
an important event in the 
life of an author influences
their writing.  Since we had 
already read stories by 
O. Henry, W.W. Jacobs, 
Shirley Jackson, Cynthia 
Voight, Joan Aikens; poems
by Dorothy Parker and
Robert Frost; a play by
Susan Glaspell, we 
started with these authors.

The first step was to find 
at least three good
sources for background
 information.  Then
each student needed to 
find at least one other
story, poem, or play 
by the same author.  

The second step was 
a bit harder: identify how  	
something important that 
had happened in the author's 
life influenced their writing.

The third step was to write 
the paper using citations 
and a work cited page.

The students who had
all selected a particular 
author then worked
in a group to develop 
a web page that would
contain at least a 
biography of the author, 
a timeline of the author's 
life, and links to each
student report.

I have listed the links
 to each of the websites.  
Take a few minutes 
to read a few.
