The Nightingale-Bamford School: Summer Reading List
For Students Entering Class VII
2004

The books listed here are recommendations from members of the English department and the librarians for summer reading.

Students should read a minimum of five books over the summer. Any book from this list, any book by an author on the list, or any grade-appropriate book sold at the school's Book Fair may be selected as one of the five required readings.

Keep a record of what you read by writing down the title and author of each book. Your list of books read is to be handed in on the opening day of school in September.

Classics       back to top

 
Little Women   Louisa May Alcott
The four March sisters--Meg, Jo, Beth, and Amy--are 
at the center of this classic story of friendship, love, 
and growing up. 


The Good Earth   Pearl S. Buck 
Story of the life of Wang Lung, from his beginnings as 
a poor peasant to his eventual position as a prosperous 
landowner during the reign of the last Emperor in 1920s 
China.


Alice in Wonderland   Lewis Carroll
A little girl falls down a rabbit hole and discovers a world 
of nonsensical and amusing characters.

 
Rebecca   Daphne Du Maurier
Max de Winter and his new bride settle in his country 
estate. But the unsettling presence of Rebecca, the 
deceased first Mrs. de Winter, lingers in the mansion.

 
The Autobiography of Miss Jane Pittman   Ernest Gaines
This is a fictional biography of a slave who lived for one 
hundred years after the Civil War.

 
Lord of the Flies   William Golding
Stranded on an island after a plane crash, a  group of 
young boys revert to savagery as they struggle to 
survive.

 
The Summer of My German Soldier   Bette Greene
When German prisoners of war are brought to her 
Arkansas town during World War II, Patty, a Jewish 
girl, befriends one of them and must deal with the 
consequences of that friendship.

 
Lost Horizon   James Hilton
After their airplane crashes high on a Tibetan plateau, 
three men and one woman are saved by a party of 
natives and taken to Shangri-La. 

 
The Member of the Wedding   Carson McCullers
Twelve-year-old Frankie is uttlery bored until she 
decides to go along, uninvited, on her older brother's 
honeymoon.

 
A Tree Grows in Brooklyn   Betty Smith
Francie Nolan experiences the problems of growing up 
in a Brooklyn slum.

 
The Red Pony   John Steinbeck
Ownership of a beautiful red pony teaches ten-year-old 
Jody about life and death as he journeys to manhood.

 
Kidnapped   Robert Louis Stevenson
After the death of his father, David Balfour sets out 
on a long journey through the highlands of Scotland 
to meet his uncle and claim his inheritance. 

 
Treasure Island   Robert Louis Stevenson
While looking through the possessions of a deceased 
guest who owed them money, the mistress of the inn 
and her son find a treasure map that leads them to a 
pirate's fortune. 

 
Lord of the Rings   J.R.R. Tolkien
Frodo and the Fellowship of the Ring journey across 
Middle-earth, deep into the shadow of the Dark Lord, 
to destroy the One Ring.


The Prince and the Pauper   Mark Twain
A beggar and a prince look so alike that they change 
places but then cannot immediately switch back.


Around the World in Eighty Days   Jules Verne
In 1872 Phileas Fogg accepts a bet by traveling around 
the world in seventy-nine days, twenty-three hours, 
and fifty-seven minutes.

Fiction         back to top

 
Go and Come Back   Joan Abelove
Alicia, a young tribeswoman living in a village in the 
Amazonian jungle of Peru, tells about the two American 
women anthropologists who arrive to study her people's 
way of life.


Backwater   Joan Bauer
As Ivy explores her family history, she discovers other 
Breedlove women who broke the mold. Ivy undertakes 
a dramatic journey up an icy mountain and into the 
backwater to try to find her aunt.


Squashed   Joan Bauer
Sixteen-year-old Ellie Morgan's life would be almost 
perfect if she could just get her potentially prize-winning 
pumpkin to put on two hundred more pounds.


The Lilies of the Field   William Barrett
A young black man driving through the Southwest helps 
a group of German refugee nuns build a church.


Tangerine   Edward Bloor	
Despite his near blindness, twelve-year-old Paul slowly 
begins to remember the incident that damaged his 
eyesight.


The Sisterhood of the Traveling Pants   Ann Brashares
Carmen decides to discard an old pair of jeans, but Tibby, 
Lena, and Bridget think they are great and decide that 
whoever the pants fit best will get them. When the jeans 
fit everyone perfectly, a sisterhood and a memorable 
summer begin.


Midnight Hour Encores   Bruce Brooks
Sibilance T. Spooner, a promising cellist, was raised by 
an unconventional father. Sib and her father travel across 
country to finally meet her mother, who gave up Sib on 
the day she was born.


What Hearts   Bruce Brooks
After his mother divorces his father and remarries, Asa 
must help him deal with the instabilities of his new world.


Where the Lilies Bloom   Vera and Bill Cleaver
In the Great Smoky Mountains region, a fourteen-year-old
girl struggles to keep her family together after her father dies.


The Chocolate War   Robert Cormier	
A high school freshman discovers the devastating
consequences of refusing to join in the school's annual
fundraising drive and arousing the wrath of the school
bullies.


Tunes for Bears To Dance To   Robert Cormier
Eleven-year-old Henry escapes his family's problems 
by watching the woodcarving of Mr. Levine, an elderly 
Holocaust survivor, but Henry soon encounters evil 
when he is manipulated into betraying his friend. 


We All Fall Down   Robert Cormier
When Karen Jerome walks in on the vandals who 
are destroying her family's house, she is thrown 
down the stairs and slips into a coma.


Love Among the Walnuts   Jean Ferris
Born and raised in isolation in a wealthy, eccentric 
family, Sandy is shocked when his family become 
victims of a vicious plot by his greedy uncles to harm 
them and take their money.


One-Eyed Cat   Paula Fox
(Newbery Honor 1983)
Ned fired the forbidden rifle just once, at a flickering 
shadow in the autumn moonlight. But someone--a face,
fleetingly seen staring at him from an attic window--was
watching. 


A Place Apart   Paula Fox
Shortly after her father's death, Victoria and her mother
move to a small village outside Boston.  She meets a
wealthy teenage boy who teaches her a valuable but
painful lesson about life.


The Moonlight Man   Paula Fox
Fifteen-year-old Catherine and her father take their 
first joint vacation in Nova Scotia and finally get to 
know each other.


The Other Shepards   Adele Griffin
Teenage Holland and her sister Geneva, having always 
lived under the shadow of siblings who died before they 
were born, struggle to establish separate identities and 
escape from the oppressive weight of their parents' grief.


Jazmin's Notebook   Nikki Grimes
Jazmin, an African-American teenager who lives with her 
older sister in a small 1960s Harlem apartment, finds 
strength in writing poetry and keeping a record of the 
events in her sometimes difficult life.


Plain City   Virginia Hamilton
Twelve-year-old Buhlaire, a child of mixed race who feels 
out of place, struggles to unearth her family history as 
she discovers more about her long-missing father.


Olive's Ocean   Kevin Henkes 
(Newbery Honor 2004)
Martha Boyle and Olive Barstow could have been friends. 
But they weren't--and now all that is left are eerie 
connections between two girls who were in the same 
grade and who both kept the same secret.


Phoenix Rising   Karen Hesse
Thirteen-year-old Nyle learns about relationships and 
death when fifteen-year-old Ezra, who was exposed to 
radiation leaked from a nearby nuclear plant, comes to 
stay at her grandmother's  farmhouse.


The Watcher   James Howe
As she sits watching a seemingly perfect family and 
a handsome lifeguard on the beach, a lonely, troubled 
girl projects herself into the fantasy lives she has 
created for them.


Kim/Kimi   Hadley Irwin	
Despite a warm relationship with her mother, stepfather, 
and half brother, sixteen-year-old Kim needs to find 
answers about the Japanese-American father she never 
knew.


Heaven   Angela Johnson
Marley's seemingly perfect life in the small town of 
Heaven is disrupted when she discovers a secret 
about her family.


Toning the Sweep   Angela Johnson
On a visit to her grandmother Ola, fourteen-year-old 
Emmie hears many stories about her family history and 
comes to a better understanding of relatives both dead 
and living.


And Both Were Young   Madeleine L'Engle
Philippa is miserable at an all-girls' boarding school in 
Switzerland until she forms a friendship with the 
mysterious Paul.


Camilla   Madeleine L'Engle
Life had always been easy for fifteen-year-old Camilla 
Dickinson.  But now her parents are behaving like 
strangers to each other and vying for her allegiance. 

 
A Ring of Endless Light   Madeleine L'Engle
During the summer fifteen-year-old Vicky's grandfather 
is dying, she finds comfort with the pod of dolphins she 
is studying.


Find a Stranger, Say Goodbye   Lois Lowry
Seemingly a girl who has everything, Natalie, at 
seventeen, is in pursuit of her real mother.


A Corner of the Universe   Ann Martin
(Newbery Honor 2003)
The summer that Hattie turns twelve, she meets the 
childlike uncle she never knew and becomes friends 
with a girl who works at the carnival that comes to 
Hattie's small town. 


Shizuko's Daughter   Kyoko Mori
After her mother dies, Yuki has to learn how to live 
with a father who doesn't seem to love her and a 
stepmother who treats her badly. 


The Other Side of Truth   Beverley Naidoo
Smuggled out of Nigeria after their mother's murder, 
Sade and her younger brother are abandoned in 
London, fearful of their new surroundings.


Lisa, Bright and Dark   John Neufeld
As her parents and teachers refuse to recognize Lisa's 
increasingly severe depression, three of her friends 
devise a plan to help her.


Habibi   Naomi Shihab Nye
The day after Liyana got her first real kiss, her life 
changed forever when her father announced that the 
family was moving from St. Louis to Palestine.


Cuba 15   Nancy Osa
(Belpré Honor 2004)
Violet Paz, growing up in suburban Chicago, barely
knows Spanish, and her dad refuses to talk about his
Cuban roots, so it's a real surprise when Abuela insists
that Violet have a grand quinceanero, the traditional
Latina fifteenth-year coming-of-age ceremony.


Jacob Have I Loved   Katherine Paterson
Deprived by her twin sister all her life of schooling, friends,
mother, and even her name, Louise finally begins to find
her identity.


A Day No Pigs Would Die   Robert Peck
To a thirteen-year-old Vermont farm boy whose father 
slaughters pigs for a living, maturity comes early as he 
learns about “what's got to be done.”


Freak the Mighty   Rodman Philbrick
At the beginning of eighth grade, learning disabled Max
and his new friend Freak, whose physical disability has 
not affected his brilliant mind, find that when they combine 
forces, they are a powerful team.


Woman of her Tribe   Margaret Robinson
Fifteen-year-old Annette, whose dead father was a
Nootka Indian, travels with her English mother from
their country home on Vancouver Island to the city of
Victoria to find her own cultural heritage. 


Esperanza Rising   Pam Munoz Ryan
Esperanza and her mother are forced to leave their life
of privilege in Mexico to go work in the labor camps of
Southern California, where they must adapt to the harsh
life of Mexican farm workers.


Stargirl   Jerry Spinelli
In this story about the perils of popularity, the courage 
of non-conformity, and the thrill of first love, an eccentric 
student named Stargirl changes Mica High School.


Shabanu   Suzanne Staples
(Newbery Honor 1990)
When eleven-year old Shabanu, the daughter of a nomad 
in present-day Pakistan, is pledged in marriage, she must 
either accept the decision or risk the consequences of 
defying her father's wishes.


Haveli   Suzanne Staples
The sequel to Shabanu finds eighteen-year-old Shabanu,
married and the mother of a young daughter, planning 
for Haveli's uncertain future and resisting her love for
the handsome Omar.


Surviving the Applewhites   Stephanie Tolan  
(Newbery Honor 2003)
Jake, a budding juvenile delinquent, is sent for home
schooling to the arty and eccentric Applewhite family's
Creative Academy, where he discovers talents and
interests he never knew he had.


Dicey's Song   Cynthia Voigt
Thirteen-year-old Dicey brings her abandoned family
to the home of their eccentric grandmother to learn
how to trust, and when to let go.


Izzy, Willy-Nilly   Cynthia Voigt
A car accident causes fifteen-year-old Izzy to lose one leg
and face the need to start building a new life as an amputee.


A Solitary Blue   Cynthia Voigt
Jeff's mother, who deserted the family years before,
reenters his life and widens the gap between Jeff and
his father, a gap that only truth, love, and friendship can
heal.


I Hadn't Meant to Tell You This   Jacqueline Woodson
Two girls: one white, one black; one abused, one protected;
both missing their mothers. An unlikely friendship ignites
between the two and, in sharing their differences, both
of their lives are transformed.


Locomotion   Jacqueline Woodson            
(National Book Award Finalist 2003)
Inspired by his teacher, eleven-year-old Lonnie begins 
to write about his life in a series of poems in which he 
discusses his feelings about his friends, his foster mom, 
his little sister, and the death of his parents.  


Make Lemonade   Virginia Euwer Wolff
High-school student LaVaughn, determined to earn 
money for college, baby-sits for Jolly, an unwed teenage 
mother of two, and matures in the process. 


True Believer   Virginia Euwer Wolff
(National Book Award 2001)
Living in the inner city amidst guns and poverty,
fifteen-year-old LaVaughn learns from old and new
friends, and inspiring mentors, that life is what you
make it--an occasion to rise to.

Mystery/Suspense back to top

 
Bone Dance   Martha Brooks
When her father wills her a cabin on land in rural Manitoba,
Alexandra meets a young man who shares her Indian heritage 
and her experience of being haunted by spirits.


Murder on the Orient Express   Agatha Christie
This is the best-loved and best-known of all of Agatha 
Christie's Hercule Poirot mysteries--the story of one 
thrillingly exotic journey, one brutally stabbed victim, 
and seventeen extremely suspicious suspects.


The Adventures of Sherlock Holmes   Arthur Conan Doyle
This collection of some of Holmes's most intriguing cases 
includes unabridged tales of blackmail, lost fortunes, and, 
of course, murder.


The House of Dies Drear   Virginia Hamilton
An African-American family moves into an enormous house 
once used as a hiding place for runaway slaves. Mysterious 
sounds and events and the discovery of secret passageways 
make the family believe they are in grave danger.


The Haunting of Hill House   Shirley Jackson	
Four seekers look for haunting evidence at the abandoned 
old mansion called Hill House. Their stay begins as a spooky 
encounter, but the house is gathering its powers and will 
choose one of them to make its own.


Dragons in the Waters   Madeline L'Engle
A thirteen-year-old boy's trip to Venezuela with his cousin 
culminates in murder and the discovery of an unexpected 
bond with an Indian tribe, dating from the days of Simon
Bolivar.


The Ruby in the Smoke (series)   Philip Pullman
In nineteenth-century London, sixteen-year-old Sally, a 
recent orphan, becomes involved in a deadly search for 
a mysterious ruby.


The Callender Papers   Cynthia Voigt
In nineteenth-century Massachusetts, orphan Jean, 
employed to sort out the family papers of a reclusive 
artist, becomes curious about the mysterious disappearance 
of his wife and young child.

Adventure    back to top

 
Beyond the Western Sea (Books 1 and 2)   Avi
Driven from their impoverished Irish village, fifteen-year-
old Maura and her younger brother meet their landlord's 
runaway son in Liverpool while all three wait for a ship 
to America.


Crispin: The Cross of Lead   Avi
(Newbery Medal 2003)
Falsely accused of theft and murder, an orphaned peasant 
boy in fourteenth-century England flees his village and 
meets a larger-than-life juggler who holds a dangerous secret.


A Girl Named Disaster   Nancy Farmer
(Newbery Honor 1997) 
When her family arranges her marriage to a cruel man with 
three wives, Nhamo escapes by canoe to seek a better future. 


Adam of the Road   Elizabeth Gray	
Eleven-year-old Adam travels the open roads of thirteenth-
century England searching for his missing father and his 
stolen red spaniel.


Stowaway   Karen Hesse
A fictionalized journal relates the experiences of a young 
stowaway from 1768 to 1771 aboard the Endeavor which 
sailed around the world under Captain James Cook.


The Young Unicorns   Madeline L'Engle
A seventeen-year-old boy, a former gang member, and a 
blind musician become involved in a frightening scheme 
relying on the ability of a refined laser to give complete power 
over people's minds.


The Call of the Wild   Jack London
Buck, a mixed-breed family dog, is stolen and sold to dog 
traffickers during the gold rush of the 1890s. Shipped to the 
Klondike as a sled dog, Buck takes an almost mythical journey 
encountering danger, adventure, and fantasy. 


Ramsay Scallop   Frances Temple
At the turn of the fourteenth century in England, fourteen-
year-old Elenor finds her betrothal to an ambitious lord's 
son launching her on a memorable pilgrimage to far-off Spain.


The Beduin's Gazelle   Frances Temple
In the sequel to The Ramsay Scallop, Halima and Atiyah, 
betrothed at birth, are separated by intrigue.  Halima, 
mourning the loss of Atiyah, is left to die in the desert and 
must marry a member of a rival tribe.


Jackaroo   Cynthia Voigt
When hard times among the People revive the old stories of 
the hero Jackaroo, an innkeeper's daughter follows her own 
quest to unlock the secret reality behind the legend.

Fantasy/Science fiction back to top

 
Hitchhiker's Guide to the Galaxy   Douglas Adams
Seconds before Earth is demolished to make room for a 
galactic freeway, an earthman is saved by his friend. 
Together they journey through the galaxy.


Watership Down   Richard Adams
This book chronicles the adventures of a group of rabbits 
searching for a safe place to establish a new warren 
where they can live in peace.


Tales from Watership Down   Richard Adams
Tells stories of what occurred in the lives of the rabbits 
after they defeated General Woundwort in the classic 
Watership Down.


Foundation   Isaac Asimov
As the Galactic Empire declines, psychohistorian Hari Seldon 
and his band of psybiologists form the Foundation, designed 
to be the nucleus of an ideal universal ruling corporation.


The Martian Chronicles   Ray Bradbury
Humans attempt to colonize Mars in this collection of stories.


The Dark Is Rising (series)   Susan Cooper
On his eleventh birthday, Will Stanton is plunged into a 
quest for the six magical Signs that will one day aid the Old 
Ones in the final battle between the Dark and the Light. 


The Ear, the Eye and the Arm   Nancy Farmer
(Newbery Honor 1995)
When General Matsika's three children are kidnapped, their 
mother hires the best detective team available in 2194 
Zimbabwe--the appropriately named mutant partners--
the Ear, the Eye, and the Arm.


The House of the Scorpion   Nancy Farmer
(Newbery Honor 2003)
In a future where humans despise clones, Matt enjoys 
special status as the young clone of El Patron, the 142-
year-old leader of a corrupt drug empire nestled between 
Mexico and the United States.


Wizard of Earthsea (series)   Ursula Le Guin	
In a compelling, richly developed fantasy world, Ged, the 
wizard hero, fights the evil he turns loose. 


A Swiftly Tilting Planet   Madeleine L'Engle
The youngest of the Murry children must travel through 
time and space in a battle against an evil dictator who 
would destroy the entire universe.


The Giver   Lois Lowry
(Newbery Medal 1994)
Given his lifetime assignment at the Ceremony of Twelve,
Jonas becomes the receiver of memories shared by only 
one other in his community and discovers the terrible 
truth about the society in which he lives.


Dragonsong (trilogy)   Anne McCaffrey
Forbidden by her father to indulge in music in any way, a 
girl on the planet Pern runs away, taking shelter with the 
planet's fire lizards who, along with her music, open a new 
life for her.


The Blue Sword   Robin McKinley	
(Newbery Honor 1983)
Harry Crewe is sent to live on the edge of the kingdom 
when her father dies. Kidnapped by King Corlath, the 
charismatic leader of the Hillfolk, Harry must discover her 
place in the world of the Damarians.


The Hero and the Crown   Robin McKinley
(Newbery Medal 1985)
Aerin, with the guidance of the wizard Luthe and the 
help of the blue sword, wins the birthright due her as 
the daughter of the Damarian king and a witchwoman 
of the mysterious, demon-haunted North.


Zel   Donna Jo Napoli
Based on the fairy tale Rapunzel, the story is told in 
alternating chapters from the point of view of Zel, her 
mother, and the prince, and delves into the psychological 
motivations of the characters.


Alanna (series)   Tamora Pierce
Somehow young Alanna knows she isn't meant to become 
a proper lady cloistered in a convent. Instead, she wants 
to be a great warrior maiden--a female knight.


Immortals (series)   Tamora Pierce
Daine, a thirteen-year-old orphaned girl with an extraordinary 
talent for communicating with animals, is swept up in the first 
skirmishes of a war.


The Golden Compass (series)   Phillip Pullman
Accompanied by her daemon, Lyra Belacqua sets out to 
prevent her best friend and other kidnapped children from 
becoming the subject of gruesome experiments in the Far 
North.


Harry Potter (series)   J.K. Rowling	
Orphaned as a baby, Harry Potter has spent eleven awful 
years living with his mean aunt, uncle, and cousin. Everything 
changes for Harry when an owl delivers a mysterious letter 
inviting him to attend a school for wizards.


Singularity   William Sleator
Sixteen-year-old twins Harry and Barry stumble across a 
gateway to another universe, where a distortion in time and 
space causes a dramatic change in their competitive relationship.

Historical Fiction back to top

 
The Slave Dancer   Paula Fox
Kidnapped by the crew of an Africa-bound ship, a thirteen-
year-old boy discovers to his horror that he is on a slaver 
and his job is to play music for the exercise periods of the 
human cargo.


Out of the Dust   Karen Hesse
In a series of poems, fifteen-year-old Billie Jo relates the 
hardships of living on her family's wheat farm in Oklahoma 
during the Dust Bowl years of the Depression.


A Proud Taste for Scarlet and Miniver   E.L. Konigsberg
While waiting in heaven for divine judgment to be passed 
on her second husband, Eleanor of Aquitaine and three of 
the people who knew her well recall the events of her life.


Letters From a Slave Girl:
                        The Story of Harriet Jacobs   Mary Lyons
A fictionalized version of the life of Harriet Jacobs, told in 
the form of letters that she might have written as a slave 
in North Carolina and as she prepared for escape to the 
North in 1842.


Stones in Water   Donna Jo Napoli
When Roberto and his friends sneak into a movie theater 
in World War II Venice, they suddenly find themselves 
trapped by German soldiers and transported by train out 
of Italy to a labor camp.


Devil in Vienna   Doris Orgel
A Jewish girl and the daughter of a Nazi have been best 
friends since they started school, but in 1938 the thirteen-
year-olds find their close relationship difficult to maintain.


A Single Shard   Linda Sue Park
(Newbery Medal  2001)
Tree-ear, a thirteen-year-old orphan in medieval Korea, 
lives under a bridge in a potters' village and longs to 
learn how to create delicate celadon ceramics himself.


When My Name Was Keoko   Linda Sue Park
Living under Japanese occupation during World War II, 
Korean children Sun-hee and Tae-yul become Keoko 
and Nobuo. Tae-yul risks his life to help his family, 
while Sun-hee guards vital secrets.


The Master Puppeteer   Katherine Paterson
A thirteen-year-old boy describes the poverty and 
discontent of eighteenth-century Osaka and his life 
among puppeteers.


A Year Down Yonder   Richard Peck
(Newbery Medal 2001) 
Fifteen-year-old Mary Alice is sent to live with her feisty, 
larger-than-life grandmother in rural Illinois during the 
Depression and comes to a better understanding of this 
fearsome woman.


The Bull from the Sea   Mary Renault
This novel reconstructs the legend of Theseus, the valiant 
youth who slew the Minotaur, became king, and brought 
prosperity to Attica. 


The King Must Die   Mary Renault
This is an adaptation of the Greek legend of Theseus 
who masters the art of bull leaping and slays the Minotaur 
in the process of fulfilling his destiny.


A Break with Charity:
            A Story about the Salem Witch Trials   Ann Rinaldi
Susanna desperately wants to join the circle of girls who 
meet every week at the parsonage. What she doesn't 
realize is that the girls are about to set off a torrent of 
false accusations about witchcraft.


Cast Two Shadows:
            The American Revolution in the South   Ann Rinaldi
In South Carolina in 1780, fourteen-year-old Caroline sees 
the Revolutionary War take a terrible toll on her family and 
friends and comes to understand the true nature of war.


The Fifth of March:
            A Story about the Boston Massacre   Ann Rinaldi
Fourteen-year-old Rachel Marsh, an indentured servant in 
the Boston household of John and Abigail Adams, is caught 
up in the colonists' unrest that eventually escalates into the 
massacre of March 5, 1770.


Let the Circle Be Unbroken   Mildred D. Taylor
Four African-American children growing up in rural
Mississippi during the Depression experience racial 
antagonisms and hard times, but learn from their 
parents the self-respect they need to survive.
