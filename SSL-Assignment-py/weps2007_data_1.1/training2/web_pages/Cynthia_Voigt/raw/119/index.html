Bad Girls in Love
by Cynthia Voigt

Best friends Mikey and Margalo, like most other eighth graders, are trying to figure out boys and understand that thing called love. Not easy when they've never been kissed, never been on a date and aren't invited to parties. They realize that ignorance puts them at a disadvantage, so they decide to gather information from secondary sources such as reports about parties, rumors and Mikey's divorced mother. They subsequently discuss and analyze all accounts.

Eighth grade seems to be a time of crushes and infatuations but Mikey and Margalo differ in their attitudes and how they handle this experience. Unlike Mikey, Margalo doesn't let the whole school know that she has relationships in the junior high school social milieu.
