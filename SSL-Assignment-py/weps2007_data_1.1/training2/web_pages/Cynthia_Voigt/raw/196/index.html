Summer Reading - Grade 7 - 2004

Students are required to read and respond to two books this summer. One book response will be submitted to your language arts teacher and the other to your history teacher for your first grade in these classes. Below you will find a list of book options in each subject area and instructions for your responses. Typing your responses is optional but appreciated.

Summer Reading - Grade 8 - 2004

  • Entering Grade 8 - Language Arts Summer Reading - Back to Top

    • Choose one book from the list below and write a review for this book. A sample review and an optional scoring rubric follow this list. Just a reminder: when you choose a book, use the library skills you have learned to preview your selection in order to determine if a book will hold your interest and is at your reading level.

      Dicey's Song by Cynthia Voigt
      Dicey has a flair for music, but she must overcome both disability and prejudice
       
      The Contender by Robert Lipsyte
      A high school dropout desperately wishes to change his life and prove to himself that he can be somebody. But does he have the courage to face new risks and responsibilities?

      The Bumblebee Flies Away by Robert Cormier
      A 16 year old boy faces the realization that his medical treatment is only experimental.

      Still Me by Christopher Reeve
      In this autobiography, Reeve describes his courageous struggle to live a productive life after a horseback riding accident left him a parapalegic.

      The Clay Marble by Mingfong Ho
      This historical novel is set in a Cambodian refugee camp in the early 1980's. Young Dara copes with the horrors of war.

      The Pigman or The Pigman and Me by Paul Zindel
      It was the year Paul Zindel met the pigman, a lonely old man with a twinkle in his eye and a mysterious secret.

      The House of Dies Drear by Virginia Hamilton
      Thomas moves into a bog, old house in Ohio that is supposed to be haunted.

      A Candidate for Murder by Joan Lowery Nixon
      Cary, the daughter of a candidate for governor of Texas, unexpectedly starts to receive strange phone calls and feels she is being followed.

      Child of the Owl by Laurence Yep
      A girl learns about her heritage when she is sent to live in Chinatown.

      The Edge of the Sea by Rachel Carson
      This nonfiction book introduces a world of teeming life where the sea meets the land.

      My Brother, My Sister, and I by Yoko Watkins
      In this sequel to So Far From the Bamboo Grove, Watkins describes her families struggle for survival in Kyoto.

      The Golden Compass, The Subtle Knife, or the Amber Spyglass by Phillip Pullman
      A fantasy series that explores the themes of good and evil and coming of age.

      The Adventures of Tom Sawyer by Mark Twain
      The classic adventures of young Tom and his cohorts.

      Zlata's Diary: A Child's Life in Sarajevo by Zlata Filipovic
      Zlata Filipovic describes her life in Sarajevo as the clouds of war build, advance and consume her city.

      Flowers for Algernon by Daniel Keyes
      The journal of Charlie Gordon, a mentally retarded adult who becomes a genius after undergoing a brain operation.

      Frankenstein by Mary Shelley
      The classic horror story of a science experiment that went awry.
