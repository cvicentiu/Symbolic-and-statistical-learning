1.  Tree by Leaf by Cynthia Voigt  ISBN: 0-689-31403-5 (hc) New York: Aladdin Paperbacks, 2000. 250 pages. $4.99 

2. Historical Fiction, Grades 6-9  

3. The main characters are Clothilde Speers, her mother, her father, her older brother Nate, and her younger sister Diedre.  Clothilde is only 8 years old when her father goes off to fight in WWI.  As a result of her fatehr's enlistment, the family is forced to move from the grandfather's palatial estate to a small cottage on the coast of Maine.  This land in Maine had been given to Clothilde by her grandfather's ill-liked sister when Clothilde was only a toddler.  Tree by Leaf depicts Clothilde's growth and development from age 3 until she becomes a famous psychotherapist and mother as an adult.

4.  Plot

After moving to Maine, Clothilde is saddled with many domestic chores.  Sometimes she feels that no one seems to care about her future and education.  She thinks that she is considered more of a servant than Lou, their hired servant girl.  Times are hard for the family.  Nate goes away to school, later choosing to live with the imperious grandfather rather than his immediate family. 

As an imaginative, curious child Clothilde often ponders that if she were God, she'd run the world differently!  She loves the sea, the peninsula, the trees and revels in their beauty and mystery.  On one of her forays across the peninsula, Clothilde hears a strange voice and she makes three requests of it.   Later, still confused by the voice, she questions the wisdom of her earlier wishes and experiences feelings of guilt when some of her wishes are coming true.

Meanwhile, Clothilde's father returns to Maine, terribly disfigured by his war injuries, becoming an apparition seeking refuge in the boathouse away from the eyes of his loved ones.  After a few years of estrangement, Clothilde discovers her father is a talented artist.  Eventually, the walls between them come tumbling down and they come to love, understand and accept each other.  The father returns to live in the house.  He achieves recognition as a world-famous watercolorist.  He is reunited with his family and friends.

The novel depicts Clothilde's growth from age 3 until she becomes a psychotherapist, mother, and the owner of the mitten-shaped peninsula that she loves.

5. Topics for Writing and Discussion.

  • Have you ever wondered like Clothilde, "If I was God, I'd...?"  Give some examples.
  • Does Clothilde experience guilt over such meanderings?
  • What things does Clothilde wish for?
  • Is it true that people need to be careful about what they wish/pray for?
  • Do you have regularly assigned chores to do around the house and farm/yard?  What chores did Clothilde have?
  • Was Clothilde overworked for a child of that time?
  • Do you think Clothilde's family took advantage of her?
  • What positive and negative traits does Clothilde have?
  • What does Clothilde's father's shattered face suggest about the nature of war?
  • Do you understand Clothilde's ambivalence toward her injured father?   Explain her mixed feelings.
  • Why does Grandfather's sister leave Speer point to Clothilde?
  • Why was Grandfather unhappy about the marriage of Clothilde's father and mother?
  • Should mothers/fathers/family members have any influence on the marital choices made by their children?  To what extent?
  • Why does Nate finally choose to live with Grandfather?  Would you do the same as Nate?
  • Why does Lou want to be away from her father?
  • What message is conveyed by the telegram?
  • Why does Clothilde say that Grandfather has stolen Nate away from them?  Do you agree/disagree?
  • Is mother correct in her assertion that "Boys don't like saying unhappy things?"
  • Why does Clothilde question her mother about Joan d'Arc's hearing voices?
  • Why does Clothilde's mother declare that "flirting can be dangerous?"  Do you agree/disagree?
  • What are some of Clothilde's fond memories of her father?  What is one of your favorite memories of your mother/father/another family member?
  • In Chapter 11, after the bodies were recovered, why does Clothilde feel remorseful?
  • Why does Clothilde feel that life is easier for boys?  Do you agree/disagree?
  • Who was Socrates and why was he put to death? 
  • What was noteworthy about the paintings done by Clothilde's father?
  • How can art be a way of seeing?
  • What does Clothilde's father tell her about The Point?  about Aunt Clothilde's character?
  • When the Voice speaks to Clothilde for the last time, what does Clothilde wish for?
  • Why doesn't Clothilde grieve when the Voice leaves?
  • How was Clothilde able to pay her college bills?
  • What vocation does Clothilde follow?
  • Who were the 2 famous Speers?
  • What lifelong dream did Clothilde's mother finally achieve?
  • At the end of the novel, what happened to Nate, Diedre, Polly, and Lou?
  • Was Clothilde's love for the land obsessive?  What special place do you revere?  Why?
  • In what ways was Clothilde's life in the early 1920's easier/more difficult than yours today?
  • Explain the persona of the Voice.

8. Evaluation

As a historical novel, Tree by Leaf gives a glimpse of what family life was like in Maine in the 1920s.  Clothilde, the heroine, is an interesting little character with her reverence for nature and her love/hate relationships with her family and the girls at school.  Her human foibles only make her more ture to life.

Because of the unsolved mystery of the Voice, students will either like or strongly dislike the book.  Because of the ambiguity of the persona of the Voice, some may find the novel appealing in the same manner that readers responded to The Lady or the Tiger, a short story written by Frank Stockton.  In that classic story the reader determined for himself the outcome of the story.  The reader of Tree by Leaf defines the identity of the Voice for himself/herself.
