Charlotte Bergeron
Research Analyst/Staff Attorney
Charlotte Bergeron is a research analyst and attorney with the Public Affairs Research Council (PAR). Her research focuses on open meetings, public records, ethics, lobbying and campaign finance laws. Her report entitled, "Louisiana's Sunshine Laws: The Promise and Peril of New Technology," examining the impact of new technology on the state's sunshine laws, received honors in policy achievement from the national Governmental Research Association in 2003.

Ms. Bergeron graduated summa cum laude from Loyola University in New Orleans and received her law degree from Louisiana State University Law Center. Prior to joining PAR in 2001, Ms. Bergeron worked in private law practice specializing in commercial transactions.
E-mail: charlotte@la-par.org



James C. Brandt
President
Jim Brandt was appointed President of the Public Affairs Research Council of Louisiana (PAR) in May 1999. He has over thirty years of experience in government administration, public policy research, and nonprofit management. He is the author of over 75 studies or reports in the areas of governmental finance, state and local tax issues, public administration, and governmental structure and organization. In addition, he has received numerous national, state and local awards for his work.

Prior to joining PAR, Mr. Brandt served for twelve years as President and CEO of the Bureau of Governmental Research in New Orleans. His previous professional experience also includes his affiliation with the Institute for Governmental Studies at Loyola University, where he was the Assistant Director and Senior Associate.

Mr. Brandt is an honors graduate of the University of Colorado where he earned a Bachelor of Arts degree in History and Political Science and was elected Phi Beta Kappa. He received his Masters degree in Community Organization and Planning from Tulane University.
E-mail: jimbrandt@la-par.org



Stephenie E. Franks
Research Analyst
Stephenie Franks joined PAR as a Research Analyst in July 2003. Her research focus is on education policy, and she recently completed a federally-funded study of the impact of the No Child Left Behind Act of 2001 (NCLB) on Louisiana's education accountability program entitled, "NCLB: A Steep Climb Ahead."

Prior to joining PAR, Ms. Franks served as a research graduate assistant at Louisiana State University (LSU), working on several notable research projects, including a study of gender issues for the LSU Commission on the Status of Women; a statewide survey of Louisiana for The Reilly Center for Media and Public Affairs; and a National Science Foundation-funded study of comparative court systems. In addition, she co-authored and presented her research at several academic conferences, including the Southwestern Political Science Association, the Southern Political Science Association and the American Political Science Association.

Ms. Franks graduated magna cum laude from LSU in 2001 with a Bachelor of Arts degree in sociology and in 2003 received a Master of Arts degree in political science.
E-mail: sfranks@la-par.org



Susan Mintz Kantrow
Director of Development
Susan Mintz Kantrow has been Director of Development for the Public Affairs Research Council since 1999. Prior positions include: Director of Development and Public Affairs, Odyssey House, Louisiana (New Orleans); Development Director, Lake Pontchartrain Basin Foundation (New Orleans); and Coordinator of Volunteer Services and Director of the Parkway Partners Program, New Orleans Parkway and Park Commission.

Ms. Kantrow served as Vice-President of the Greater New Orleans Chapter of the Association of Fundraising Professionals (AFP) and was named Outstanding Fundraising Executive by the Greater New Orleans Chapter. Richard Keller
Senior Research Associate
With nearly 40 years' experience in public policy research, Ty Keller holds the position of Senior Research Associate at PAR. Since joining PAR in 1971, he has authored or directed research on dozens of publications dealing with a wide variety of Louisiana state and local government issues. His studies on economic development, comparative taxes and community colleges have received awards for distinguished research and special achievement from the national Governmental Research Association.

Mr. Keller has done extensive research on tax and fiscal policy; property tax issues; vo-tech education; and education management. He has also conducted numerous management studies of state agencies and undertaken contract studies for local governments throughout the state.

Mr. Keller graduated cum laude from Brigham Young University in 1962 and did postgraduate work at BYU and the New York University Graduate School of Public Administration. Prior to joining PAR, he was an assistant professor at California State University at Fullerton and worked for the National Municipal League.
E-mail: tykeller@la-par.org



Art Theil
Consultant
Art Theil is serving as a local government consultant for PAR. Recently, he has assisted local groups or governments to develop or revise the home rule charter for Ascension Parish, Livingston Parish and the City of Donaldsonville.

Mr. Theil is a native of Wisconsin who found a home in Louisiana. After service in World War II he received his undergraduate degree from Lawrence College in Appleton, Wisconsin followed by a graduate degree from the University of Denver.

Following graduation, he worked as an assistant to a city manager in Colorado, followed by state and local governmental research in Wyoming and Wisconsin before moving to Louisiana in 1959 to become Research Director for the Public Affairs Research Council (PAR). He served in that position until 1975 when he was named President.

Mr. Theil retired from PAR at the end of 1984 and then worked four years with the Dean of the LSU College of Business Administration raising endowment funds to enable the College to add nationally recognized scholars to its faculty.



Sylvia H. Wilson
Office Manager/ Publications Editor/ Bookkeeper
Sylvia Wilson joined PAR in 1983 after graduating from Denham Springs High School. She has served as Office Manager since 1992. In addition, Mrs. Wilson has the duty of being PAR's Bookkeeper and Publications Editor. As Office Manager, she oversees various programs and projects, organizes PAR's annual conference, and supervises PAR's standing committees.

As Bookkeeper, Mrs. Wilson maintains the organization's financial records, including those for all project grants. As Publications Editor, she is responsible for the final layout and organization of each of PAR's publications. Her duties include proofing and editing all materials, gathering bids from printers she will work with for the projects, and handling the mass mail-outs to PAR members. Additionally, Mrs. Wilson supervises PAR's database of over 3,500 members.
