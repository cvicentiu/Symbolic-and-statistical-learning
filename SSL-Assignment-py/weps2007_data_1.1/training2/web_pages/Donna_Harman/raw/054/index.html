Forest Products Industry Applauds Senate Vote on Domestic Energy Access
July 26, 2006
WASHINGTON, DC  The United States Senate today voted to begin the debate on S.3711, a bill that would allow access to energy supplies in the eastern Gulf of Mexico.

We are pleased with the outcome of todays Senate vote, which will enable the chamber to debate important legislation on energy access, said Donna Harman, AF&PA Senior Vice President of Policy and Government Affairs. Todays vote signals that Congress is listening to American workers and manufacturers who need relief from sky-high energy prices.

The forest products industry, like many manufacturing industries, relies on natural gas to power facilities. Since the huge run-up in natural gas prices began six years ago, the forest products industry has shuttered 267 mills and lost 189,000 jobs. Today, we join consumers across the country in applauding Senators for their commitment to this crucial issue.

By allowing increased production in the Gulf of Mexico, S.3711 will enable us to reduce our dependence on foreign sources of energy and lower the cost of natural gas, which will help consumers and save American jobs. We commend todays action and urge Congress to pass this sensible legislation next week.
