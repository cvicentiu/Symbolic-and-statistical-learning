LooseyLucy's Commentary On Life
I discovered during geometry class my sophomore year of high school that I was indeed an anarchist. Why? It would be the ideal government. In an anarchy, there are no rules. Everyone can just be themselves. What makes it an ideal government is when everyone comes to the realization that there are other individuals with different beliefs, races, religions, etc. that everyone at least tolerates & at most embraces. A utopia would be a world-wide respect of the human condition & all of its rules of etiquette, traditions, etc. so that there would be no need for laws or government. A world-wide mutual understanding that everyone is an individual.
Don't get me wrong! I love the United States of America! It's the best country in the world. I think our government has quite a few kinks to work out, but on the whole, it seems to be doing moderately well. People mess things up though. Anarchy is nearly impossible in reality because people aren't perfect. People find the need to tell people what to say, tell people what to wear, tell people what music to listen to. And the sad thing is...they follow like sheep. Where is the individuality?
