6/20/04
Terrific show last night with Fatigo, NWM&WG, Cathy Rivers, and the Dagons. It was fun to be an audience member and just relax and enjoy the show. I missed the Victrola, but all the bands were smokin’! I’d never heard Cathy Rivers before last night (Tucson musicians never disappoint me). I thought Fatigo was going to crash through the stage! Last week’s benefit went really well. All the musicians were great, I’m glad L. spoke and Strangers on Tour were a pleasant surprise, they were the only ones I hadn’t heard before. Brandy’s voice reminded me of Billy Holiday meets Portishead meets PJ Harvey. I was really glad to see/hear lots of folks and the soundmen were heroes as well as Scott for donating Paper Heart’s portion of the cover to the cause as well. We were really grateful that so many folks came out to support the event and we got lots of positive comments on the collection of musical talent. -jf

3/14/04
A lady was on a giant swing, twirling and twisting and whatnot. M. was pretending to strip. Fatigo and That Mad Ahab played warm and happy music. It was good to finally hear DOF and SlackRope Walkers were a happy surprise. Jim was disco-medusa. A puppy got a home. Urbana is a wonderful, giant playground.

2/17/04
The Aux still felt like home, even though it's no longer the paper heart it still has a warm vibe. Our first "mini-set" was too long, didn't really have a chance to time it out. Nowhere Man and a Whiskey Girl were inspiring and they brought a really supportive audience with them so we felt pretty comfortable. They were selling action figures along with their new cd which is a charmer! Nick was a trooper and Zach played a couple of songs with us as we put in our usual last-minute request for his time. This time we played it safe and avoided the whiplash. I tried to sell Malkovich. (He even went to a garage sale with me last week and we waited and waited but despite all his charms, he is still homeless). Dale and Adam were looking glamorous. What would we do without Kenny?

2/12/04
Jim just put up the Patriots Square pictures that Dale's dad took. I find it funny to see the mystery photographer guy snapping pictures of Dale, you can see him in the right-hand corner of the Dale pic's. We were somewhat surprised by a herd of highschoolers right before we played. I think they were requesting Strokes' songs but sadly we didn't oblige them. We enjoy playing this downtown venue and are REALLY looking forward to playing there again in April during the Farmers Market! Saturday, Valentines Day, we play with Nowhere Man and a Whiskey Girl, a band from Bisbee that will surely charm and delight people. We welcome and congratulate Aron on his new space, the Aux, formerly Paper Heart, and we look forward to playing a "new" venue. Thugs classifieds are as follows: Adam is graduating in computer science and wants a job, help us obi wan kenobi you're our only hope; I'm looking for a home for an angel of a cat named Malkevich (formerly Headwound Harry) and Jim is looking for his sunglasses as he has again misplaced them.

10/11/03
We really enjoyed playing the show last night, thanks to the supportive audience. Also, Hayley’s support of local music is a blessing to the shizz. Our next show is going to be sans Nick so Dale, Jim and I will be scrambling to learn bass lines on our songs. Even though I know it sometimes makes my fellow band members grit their teeth, I absolutely love playing the bass and having band members switch instruments. Hopefully soon they let me play “I’m Bobby Brady” on the drums. Also, we should have some photos from last night’s show up soon, so we can view Adam’s and Dale’s elegant evening attire. Thanks Andrew, for the Halloween 2002 photos! Please consider shopping at Bashas with your member card and give them Arizona Animal Welfare League’s ID# 28678 so that a portion of your shopping expenditures goes to help AAWL’s animals. Check out www.aawl.org for more information on Arizona’s oldest and largest no-kill animal shelter. ~ JF

10/04/03
Fatigo had a great First Friday show last night, kept missing the other bands but at the end of the night people started pouring into Thought Crime and the energy level was beautiful. I saw people standing outside of the gallery watching and listening from the median in the middle of the street. They eventually got drawn into the gallery by the music. The Change Machine was a fun exhibit and I’m doing my best to follow the advice I was given. Thugs do not have a Halloween show but are scrambling for Halloween costumes anyway, it’s a very important holiday to us. I don’t want to be lame and be the same thing I was last year, Medusa-Cigarette girl. It’s like when I was a little kid and I’d get so excited about Halloween and then always end up with a last minute costume like a ghost or witch or Medusa-cigarette girl. ARGH, I want it to be a special, spectacular evening! Anyway, new songs are in the works, we had an angel save us at the pawn shop, and ANDREW WHERE ARE THOSE HALLOWEEN PICTURES???? -JF
