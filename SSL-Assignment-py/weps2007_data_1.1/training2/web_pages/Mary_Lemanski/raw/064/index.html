We have Just Plain Folks members in all 50 US States and over 100 Countries around the world. As our community continues to grow, it is important for us to create real life, face to face communities in every geographical area where we have interested active members. The Internet is great for communication and sharing of resources and information, but to really thrive it is equally important for our members to interact in real life in real time. We also want to create a network of resources for our members around the world, so that at any time, and in any location our members may find themselves, there will be Just Plain Folks friendly members, venues, music industry professionals, media contacts and other vital tools and resources to help you succeed. This network won't be created overnight, and requires the help of each of you to fill in the blanks and help identify the great people and resources in each of your own communities.

Current Just Plain Folks Chapters

Above is the current lineup of Chapters for Just Plain Folks. Each chapter is different and we are still in the process of launching many of those listed. The basic guidelines for a chapter are:

1. It takes at least 10 interested local members to form a live chapter. Until that time, we have a local contact who acts as a liason for us for that community and the music industry.

2. Once a chapter is formed, ususally they will meet 1 time per month for a friendly networking, communication, education type meeting. Chapters may meet at a members home, a local restaurant or music club, a public meeting place such as a library, school, church, college, bookstore, music store etc. Meetings usually involve discussions of the local scene and chapter activities, guest speakers from the music community, occassional workshops on various subjects and often they end with a friendly member song circle where folks can perform or play a recording of one of their songs for feedback.

3. Once a chapter has 20 or more active members who perform, we will normally host monthly member showcases in a local venue. The way these shows work vary widely depending on the number of interested performers, ability level of those involved and the personality of the venue. Showcases can feature artists, but there must be some open access to members of all levels to participate in a fair and unbiased manner.

4. As chapters continue to grow, they will often get involved in community service work such as performing at nursing homes, doing benefit shows, visiting schools and so on.

5. Chapters and their local members will also serve as the hosts for visiting Roadtrip events each year. This may involve setting up showcases, contacting local media, finding speakers and locations for workshops and so on.

6. With very few exceptions, all chapter membership and events are free. Occassionally events may have some type of fee, but this is the exception rather than the rule and we work very hard to provide as many free opportunities as possible all year long.

If you want to be a Coordinator or Contact: 

If you would like to be a Chapter Coordinator or contact, please fill out the interest form by clicking on the link below:

Chapter Coordinator or Contact Interest Form

Note: You do NOT have to be an industry expert or an active performer/writer to qualify. In some cases, coordinators may be restricted from performing at some events and taking advantage of some benefits because of conflict of interest. Therefore it is NOT a negative if you are not active in the business, just getting started or not a writer/performer at all. As long as you are professional, have great follow through and good people skills and are passionate about helping the local music community, you will do fine.

If chosen, you will become the Just Plain Folks contact point for industry professionals in your area and will help represent us at any events, industry functions or media opportunities. You will eventually manage a team of local volunteers who will do most of the nuts and bolts work. Your roll (after the chapter has become activated) will be to follow up, follow through, and help the community take advantage of all the resources and opportunities as well as run the chapter meetings. You'll also be responsible for all email communication issues involving interested chapter members and will head up all local recruiting efforts.
Joining a Local Chapter: 

For currently active chapters, all you need to do to join is fill out the form below and your information will be forwarded to the appropriate coordinator or contact. They will add you to the local mailing list and let you know when there are local meetings, showcases and other events. If a Chapter is not yet active, you'll be added to the list of interested local members and once it grows large enough to start having meetings and events, you'll be notified. If there are no Contacts or Coordinators listed, we need a volunteer!
