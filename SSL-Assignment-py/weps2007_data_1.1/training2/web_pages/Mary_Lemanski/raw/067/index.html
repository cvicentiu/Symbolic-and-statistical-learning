   About The Dames

This all-girl power punk trio isn’t what you would expect - Formed in early 2002, The Dames -Drea Doll (guitar/vox), Thrashley (drums/vox), and Ms. Butcher (bass/vox) - demonstrate an unusual combination of heavy belligerence with melodic flair, cynical wit with blatant sincerity. These ladies will rock your world in every room of the house, and maybe even the parking lot. While their energetic stage performance exhibits a “party hard and play harder” mentality, this tight, punchy three-piece’s unapologetic style will ravage your ears with their mix of punk and rowdy rock n' roll. The result - an aggressive charm that is sure to haunt you.

With the release of “Sin and Tonic” in 2003, the Dames gained momentum, being nominated at the 2003 AIM Awards, 2003 Ska Punk Awards, selected as AZPunk.com’s featured band for the month of October, and were chosen to perform in the 2004 New Times Music Showcase.

“Take a Number”, the Dames’ second release, is due out in the fall of 2004. “Their knockout looks, only thinly veiled behind flailing hair and tattoos, have made them the sweethearts of the thriving Arizona punk scene." (Phoenix New Times) More than just a pretty face – or three – this irreverent threesome will rock you with finesse and a style all their own.

This is the way punk should sound: Honest and experienced. The Fight are an unpop punk band from the UK that could very easily lead the next British evasion, if one ever takes shape. Regardless, there is no reason in the world why you shouldn’t like them or give their music a listen. It is catchy with out being poppy and honest without being preachy. Loved by thousands and soon to be copied by millions, you’ll defiantly want to get on the “I knew them when” stage of a band that has no choice but to go to the top. They are old enough to know better, but still too young (the oldest member(s) being 20) to care. Their EP, “Home Is Where the Hate Is” is steady gaining fans and praise. It is off the Fat Wreck Cords label, a label that most bands would kill to be on. If you love music, you owe it to yourself to give this band a listen. Kerrang is all ready calling them the next big thing. Before the age of twenty, they have managed to secure two record deals (Fat and Repossession) and a deal with Atticus clothing. Their devotion to the music they love comes across at their live shows, an experience you should take in next time they come to your area. I’ve seen plenty of bands with ten times the hype, money and promotional teams behind that can’t put on a live show half as well as this foursome. Their love for music, each other and their die hard fans-as well as the people catching them for the first time, comes across at their live shows. Take a moment to hit up these links. These guys are easy on the eyes, awesome for the ears, and great to experience from the front row.
