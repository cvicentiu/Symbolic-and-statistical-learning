DISTRICT OF NEW YORK - PORT OF NEW YORK
I, C Ludwig, Master of the Cimbria, do solemnly, sincerely and truly swear that the following List or Manifest, subscribed by me, and now delivered by me to the Collector of the Customs of the Collection District of New York, is a full and perfect list of all the passengers taken on board of the said Cimbria at Hamburg via Havre from which port said Cimbria has now arrived; and that on said list is truly designated the age, the sex and the occupation of each of said passengers, the part of the vessel occupied by each during the passage, the country to which each belongs, and also the country of which it is intended by each to become an inhabitant; and that said Listor Manifest truly sets forth the number of said passengers who have died on said voyage, and the names and ages of those who died.

Correspondence 02/25/2004 passengers #66-72  Lund/Sund/Siegmeyer
I found my Great Great Great Grandparents on the Ship Cimbria, Hamburg 
via Havre to New York 16 November 1881.  I believe passengers 66 and 67 
are my GR Gr Gr Grandparents although it appears their names started 
with an “L” rather than an “S”.  I still think it is them and that an
error was made by the captain or his clerk on the original record.  I 
have learned in researching family records that the family name “Sund” 
was spelled “Lund” in several records.  I honestly can not tell you 
why this is.  One of the other reasons I think it is them is that 
their son in law was Carl Siegmeyer and his wife was their daughter 
Henrietta.  Passengers 68 and 69 on the list are “arl Sigmeyer 
and Henr?. Sigmeyer.  They had a daughter named Bertha and passenger 70 
is B. Sigmeyer.  They also had sons by the name of Fritz and John.  I 
figure this is just too many coincidences for this to be other people.
Greg Sund
Note: both the transcriber and the descendant above believe name is correctly 
transcribed as Lund from the original manifest but should have been Sund.
