Review of Glasstec 2002, Düsseldorf

glasstec 2002 Ends on a Positive Note

(Click here for picture page)

glasstec 2002, the International Glass Trade Fair, closed its doors in Düsseldorf on 1st November with a very positive bottom line. Over 54,000 sector specialists made the trip to the Trade Fair Centre on the Rhine during the five-day event. This exceeds the total for glasstec 2000 that drew an audience of just on 52,000. Trade visitors came from 76 countries all over the globe. More than half of all attendees were from outside Germany, spelling a further increase in glasstec's big foreign visitor contingent.

The calibre of both exhibitors and visitors was impressive. The 1,150 companies showcasing included all the market leaders, and 70% of visitors were top-echelon executives with decision-making powers.

Commenting on visitors' countries of origin, Siegfried Glaser, Chairman of the glasstec Advisory Council and also Chairman of Fachabteilung Glasmaschinen und -anlagen (Association of German Glass Machinery and Plant Manufacturers) within VDMA (German Machinery and Plant Manufacturers Association), remarked: 'Firming ties early on in up-and-coming markets such as CIS countries and Asia pays off.' He added: 'One of the industry's main future goals will be to create a contact exchange between German machinery manufacturers and customers worldwide.'


Pilkington's impressive stand at Glasstec



Martin Nagel, President of glasstec 2002 and Master of the Federal Association of German Glazing Guilds (BIV), stressed: 'We have steered clear of the prevailing mood of doom and gloom. We know the problems our medium-size facilities are facing, but we also know how flexibly the glazing industry responds. It has the innovative strength not only to access new market segments, but to blaze a trail. glasstec 2002 was a beacon along the way!'

glasstec visitors' main interest areas included glass processing and finishing (53%), glass production (40%), glass products and applications (40%) and tools, wear and spare parts (28%).
The 'glass technology live' special presentation with symposium proved a magnet for architects' practices and engineering consultancies as well as universities and technology colleges. As a result, these accounted for a notable 10 % plus of the visitor total. Almost 1,000 students from all over Europe came to Düsseldorf to view and discuss international architectural projects and designs that have already progressed to the construction stage. As summed up by Professor Stefan Behling, Director of Stuttgart University's Building Technology Institute and partner in London architecture practice Foster & Partners: 'The special presentation with symposium drew an incredible response, primarily because this year we highlighted the laminated glass tube - an innovation unique worldwide - and were able to recruit high-calibre speakers.'


Your intrepid publisher reaches hands across the sea: Tony Higgin with Nicole Harris of the NGA (right)


All in all glasstec 2002 was a complete success. As Horst Klosterkemper, Managing Director of Messe Düsseldorf, made clear at the close of the show: 'Messe Düsseldorf views glasstec as one of the most successful products in its exhibition portfolio. Quality and quantity are spot on! As regards exhibitor numbers, space rented and visitor calibre, it's the global No. 1.'

The next glasstec will take the stage in Düsseldorf in autumn 2004.
