Florida Nurses
by Victoria Langley

Wednesday December 12th, 2001

The Florida Nurses Association says nurse practitioners should be able to provide some narcotic prescriptions that right now only doctors are allowed to prescribe. The nurses gathered in the state capitol today to rally support for a bill that would let the nurse practitioners prescribe controlled substances like painkillers. The nurse practitioners say especially in nursing homes where doctors visits are infrequent, there needs to be someone on hand to alleviate patients' discomfort, and some doctors agree.
One bill supporter Dr. Celeste Paquette commented, "I just recently had an aunt die a very painful death with a large leg ulcer that needed amputation but she was in heart failure and really in her demise anyway so she needed a lot of pain medication. And the physician came once a month to the nursing home, and some of her other partners could have come more frequently and adjusted that medication but it was always a battle and that's not right."
Nurse practitioners say they can't even prescribe some cough syrups under the current law. 41 other states allow nurse practitioners to write the prescriptions. A proposal to give nurse practitioners in Florida the ability to prescribe controlled substances has failed to make it out of committee at the state capitol for the past nine years.
