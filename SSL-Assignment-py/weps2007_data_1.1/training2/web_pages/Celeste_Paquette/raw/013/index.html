FSU TAKES PRECAUTIONARY ACTION AGAINST 'SARS'

TALLAHASSEE, Fla.-Florida State University has taken several precautionary measures in the wake of the spread of Severe Acute Respiratory Syndrome (SARS) to the United States. There are no suspected cases of SARS reported at FSU. "FSU officials are aware of this disease, and we care about the health and safety of our university community members," said Fran Harley, director of Environmental Health and Safety. "That's why we have taken precautionary action."

FSU:

  • Is training key staff in offices, classrooms, service facilities and campus residences to ensure they are aware of the immediate procedures to take in the event of a reported suspected case.

  • Has initiated systems for distributing updated health alert notices to the University community.

  • Has alerted medical staff to be aware of patients with symptoms consistent with SARS and report such cases immediately.

In addition, Thagard Student Health Center is in close communication with the Leon County Public Health Department and the Centers for Disease Control infectious disease experts who are ready to assist in the investigation of any suspected cases here at FSU.

SARS is a respiratory illness that has recently been reported in Asia, North America and Europe. The illness usually begins with a fever (greater than 100.4 degrees F or 38.0 degrees C). The fever is sometimes associated with chills, headache and/or body aches. Some people also develop a dry, nonproductive cough that might be accompanied by or progress to the point of insufficient oxygen getting to the blood.

For more information about this disease, you may visit the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention Web site; or the World Health Organization Web site. For local updates, you can also check http://www.doh.state.fl.us/PHNursing/SARS/SARSindex.html.
