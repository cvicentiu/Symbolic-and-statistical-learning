Ernie Jose', Director of Human Resources
E-mail: ejose @ kss.org
Ernie Jose' joined Keystone in 1988 in a direct service capacity but soon became part of its Human Resources team as the Employment Specialist. From 1992 to 1996, he served as the HR Specialist for Keystone Residence of Lancaster. From 1996 to 2000, he functioned as the HR Specialist for Keystone Family Support Associates. In 2000, he became the HR Director of Keystone Children & Family Services when Keystone's various children's services were consolidated into one comprehensive umbrella agency.
Ernie is a graduate of the University of South Carolina with a BA in Psychology. He served four years with the U.S. Army in Germany, where he developed his skills as a Personnel Officer in a combat unit.
As an HR Professional, Ernie holds a special interest in staff and leadership development. He has made the training of employees one of the key focus of his job in KC&FS.

Anita Coleman, Director of Administration Services
E-mail: acoleman @ kss.org
Anita Coleman joined Keystone Service Systems, Inc. in October of 98. She began her career with Keystone as the Receptionist/Administrative Assistant for the 310 North Second Street location in Harrisburg, PA. Anita was promoted to Office Manager for the Psychological & Behavioral Services, formally known as Konrad Associates. Soon after Keystone Children and Family Services was organized Anita's position changed to Administrative Assistant to the CEO. She soon became the Assistant Director of Administration and now is the Director of Administration. Anita also heads the KC&FS Cultural Competence Committee and serves on the Medical, Safety and Risk Management Policy and Procedure Committee.
Anita has an Associates degree in Early Childhood Education and a minor in Child Psychology from Keystone College in La Plume, PA. She also attended Marywood College for Elementary Education in Scranton, PA.

Tia L. Haag, Regional Finance Director
E-mail: thaag @ kss.org
Tia L. Haag serves as the Regional Director of Finance for Keystone Service Systems. Her responsibilities include financial oversight for 3 of Keystone Service Systems' agencies: Keystone Children and Family Services, Keystone Residence of Lancaster and Keystone Service Systems North Central PA.
Tia has 7 years of public accounting experience and has spent 8 years in the private sector. She also serves on the Board of Directors as Treasurer for the Humane Society of Harrisburg Area, Inc.
Tia received her Bachelors of Science in Business Administration - Accounting from Shippensburg University, her Masters in Business Administration from Kutztown University and is a certified public accountant.

Dr. Mark Hite, Clinical Director
E-mail: mhite @ kss.org
Dr. Hite is currently serving as Clinical Director.  He came to Keystone Children & Family Services as a member of the psychology team of Psychological and Behavioral Services in October 2003.  His duties include providing leadership for the agency in the organization, coordination, development, and implementation of services and interventions.  In addition, Dr. Hite provides recommendations on programmatic and procedural changes needed for ongoing service improvement.  Dr. Hite received his doctorate from Brigham Young University, following which, he embarked on an 18 year career as a Psychologist working for Indian Health Services.  His work took him to many Indian Reservations, including the White Mountain Apache Tribe and most recently the Paiute Indian Tribe of Utah.  In addition, Dr. Hite has been a Treatment Coordinator at an adolescent psychiatric hospital in Phoenix, Arizona, and a Clinical Director for an adolescent dual diagnosis residential program in Sacaton, Arizona (Gila River Reservation), and a adjunct Professor of Psychology at Southern Utah University.  He has also worked extensively with children with Mental Retardatiom and their families.  Dr. Hite's clinical training includes extensive work in systems theory of marriage and family  therapy and he has two years of post-doctoral training in Biofeedback.

Nancy Fierer, Director of Susquehanna Service Dogs Program
E-mail: nfierer @ kss.org
Nancy Fierer is the founder and director of Susquehanna Service Dogs. She is a member of the Board of Directors of Assistance Dogs International. She and Susquehanna Service Dogs mentor several other service dog programs in the United States, Germany and Chile. In addition to training and placing assistance dogs, Nancy is a dedicated and enthusiastic clicker trainer, environmentalist, mother, grandmother and wife. In 1999 she and her husband were awarded the Edna Silberman Humanitarian Award.

Rebecca Fassl, Director of Outpatient Services
E-mail: rfassl @ kss.org
Rebecca Fassl has been working with Keystone Children & Family Services since 2002. Since then she has served in the direct service field as a Therapeutic Staff Support, Behavioral SPecialist and an Outpatient Therapist.  Rebecca has also served as our Associate Clinical Director of Behavioral Health and Rehabilitation Services (BHRS).  She is current the Director of Outpatient Services.  Rebecca also has several years of experience and training working with children and Families with Autism.  Rebecca received her Bachelors in Psychology with an Art minor and her Masters in Psychology from Shippensburg University.  She is currently resides in Cumberland COunty with her husband and two children.

Joseph R. Manduchi Ed.D, CEO
E-mail: jmanduch @ kss.org
Dr. Manduchi is originally from the Lancaster area. He attended Millersville University for his undergraduate work and obtained his doctorate from Temple University. He has a total of 34 years of experience in the helping professions and obtained his professional license as a psychologist in 1976. He has enjoyed the lifestyle of central Pennsylvania and makes a home in Harrisburg with his wife Mary. Dr. Manduchi has five grown children.
He has worked at the Hershey Medical Center as a research assistant, the Loysville Youth Development Center as Director of Intake and Assessment, Self Help Counseling Center as Executive Director and lastly as Clinical Director of the Center for Behavioral Health in Lewistown PA. His work experience is broad and ranges form Early Childhood Education through to working with seriously mentally ill adults.
He arrived at Keystone Children and Family Services in 2001, and has continued the work of consolidating the six community agencies that merged into Keystone Children and Family Services.

Frank Koerber, Chief Operating Officer
