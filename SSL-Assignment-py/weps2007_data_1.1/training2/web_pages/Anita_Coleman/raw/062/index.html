VIRGINIA UNION CAMPUS NEW HOME FOR UNCF STATE OFFICE

Former St. Paul’s College Administrator to Head New Office


FAIRFAX, VA – The United Negro College Fund (UNCF) has opened a new office on the campus of Virginia Union University (VUU), a UNCF member institution. The new office will be responsible for all UNCF operations in the Commonwealth of Virginia, including fundraising activities that will benefit UNCF members VUU and St. Paul’s College in Lawrenceville, and the other 37 historically black colleges and universities (HBCUs) that make up the UNCF network. VUU has donated office space to UNCF for the continuation of its mission of providing scholarships and ongoing support for member institutions.

“This level of support from Virginia Union demonstrates that they truly understand and embrace UNCF’s 62-year legacy of support for historically black colleges and universities,” says Maurice Jenkins senior vice president for UNCF’s southern field operations. “We will continue to keep our administrative costs low, so that any funds raised will support the UNCF schools in Virginia and across the country and those students who desire to further their education.”

Named to head the new UNCF office is Anita Coleman Wynn, who most recently served as special assistant to the president at St. Paul’s College where she was a liaison for fundraising efforts. As UNCF’s Area Development Director for the State of Virginia, Wynn will work with volunteers and business leaders throughout the state on a wide range of fundraising activities. Wynn brings more than 20 years of experience at the supervisory level in the financial industry including project management, program development and marketing initiatives. A UNCF graduate, Anita earned her bachelor of arts in political science from Bennett College for Women, and has a bank management degree from the University of Virginia. Wynn fills the position previously held by Emma Ross Hoffler who passed away October 30, 2005.

The Richmond Office is part of a newly created UNCF region which includes North Carolina and Virginia. The regional UNCF office is headquartered in Charlotte, NC, under the direction of Marilyn Baldwin Richards, UNCF regional development director for Virginia and North Carolina.

“We are pleased to welcome Anita to the UNCF organization. She brings a wealth of experience that will help us to cultivate new relationships and strengthen campaign efforts in Virginia,” says Richards.

Activities in the UNCF North Carolina/Virginia Region support seven member institutions including Bennett College for Women in Greensboro, NC, Johnson C. Smith University in Charlotte, NC, Livingstone College in Salisbury, NC, St. Augustine’s College in Raleigh, NC, St. Paul’s College in Lawrenceville, VA, Shaw University in Raleigh, NC, and Virginia Union University in Richmond, VA.

About UNCF

As the nation’s oldest and most successful minority higher education assistance organization, the United Negro College Fund’s mission is to provide financial support to its 39 member institutions and increase minority degree attainment by reducing financial barriers to college. UNCF institutions and other historically black colleges and universities are highly effective, awarding 25 percent of African American baccalaureate degrees. UNCF administers nearly 450 programs, including scholarship, internship and fellowship programs, mentoring, summer enrichment, and curriculum and faculty development programs. Today, UNCF supports more than 65,000 students at over 900 colleges and universities across the country. Learn more about UNCF at www.uncf.org
