Gottlieb Lab Page
Our lab is interested in the early lineages (ectoderm, endoderm, and mesoderm) of mouse embryo. We use mouse embryonic stem cells as an in vitro system to study these early lineages. Techniques that we use to study these lineages include gene targeting (knock ins and knock outs), protein chemistry, biochemical and immuno- histochemical techniques. More details. Current Publications. 

Es cells in vitro can differentiate into Neurons, as well as many other cell types. 

Human ES cells have recently been discovered by Dr. James Thomson and Dr. John D.Gearhart.  Additionally, ES cells have been identified in other vertebrate animals.

ES cells are of intrest to many people and there are many other ES cell Researchers.
