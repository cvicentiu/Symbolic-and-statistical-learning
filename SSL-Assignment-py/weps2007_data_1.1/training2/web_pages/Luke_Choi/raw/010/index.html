    COE Training Room Sessions Descriptions
    Code begins with… A | H | M | S | T

    AS-4: Computer Aided Durability Analysis with CAT/FALANCS
    Abstract: LMS FALANCS implements fatigue life prediction for metals based on either the stress-life or the strain-life approach in a finite element environment. Its particular strength is a high speed implementation, which using special mathematical algorithms allows to find critical locations automatically even in large structures of 250,000 elements and more. With CAT/FALANCS, this application is now available for CATIA users.
    Speaker: Michael Hack, LMS International

    AS-10: Application of CAT/ADAMS to Automotive Body Hardware Simulation
    Abstract: Attendees will learn how to prepare a CATIA Assembly for Dynamic Motion simulation, including modeling sophisticated phenomena such as contact, perform the dynamic motion simulation, and use the results to drive refinements in the design. The examples will represent typical problems encountered in the design of automotive body hardware.
    Speaker: AJ Hoyt, Dassault Systemes

    AS-13: How to Predict Dynamic Loads in a Mechanical System Using CAT/DADS
    Abstract: Design modifications typically impact the way dynamic loads occur in mechanical systems. In this session, users will learn how to take a CATIA Kinematics model and perform a parametric study using CAT/DADS to predict dynamic loads due to various design changes.
    Speaker: Chris Paulson, CADSI

    AS-16: Vibro-Acoustic Performance Modeling from the CATIA Model
    Abstract: The training session focuses on the prediction of vibro-acoustic performance of system components. This process includes the creation of a dedicated acoustic mesh and boundary conditions, and recovers the noise radiation boundary conditions (or the acoustic loading) from the structural analysis model. In a post-processing phase, sound pressure levels at pre-defined visualization surfaces can be evaluated, along with the total radiated acoustic power - this post-processing phase thus establishes the validation of the component's functional performance as compared to imposed product specifications. The training session guides the CAT/SYSNOISE user through the entire process of model definition and results post-processing by means of a practical example.
    Speaker: Luc Cremers, LMS International

    H/S-9: IBM Windows NT Administration
    Abstract: In this session we will look at the processes to install and manage CATIA V5R3 on a WindowsNT Workstation. Topics to be discussed will include installing CATIA V5 code and documentation, managing CATIA software and service packs, and configuring licenses, environments and settings. Bring your raincoat because the fire hose will be wide open.
    Speaker: Douglas Harrings, IBM

    ME-1: Sheet Metal Formability Analysis with CAT/FORM3D
    Abstract: A series of parts will be run to demonstrate the ease of use and speed of operation for all of the features of CAT/FORM3D. The results of splitting, wrinkling and thinning along with Tailor Welded Blank analysis, blank size surface output and aluminum analysis will all be demonstrated.
    Speaker: Luke Choi, Forming Technologies

    SDS-2: Getting Started with GSM - Using the SKIN Function in V4
    Abstract: This hands-on training session will cover the fundamentals of the SKIN function. The SKIN function is part of the new Generative Shape Modeling (GSM) product. GSM is a powerful new tool that automates the surface design process. It has been introduced in V4 and will become an integral part of V5's surfacing strategy. Learn this functionality now!! Using Genesis' unique approach to CATIA education, students will learn how easy they can use this new technology productively. Although students must pre-register for this class, all in attendance will receive a copy of the training aids and access to the training models.
    Speaker: Tom Cisler, Genesis Modeling Technologies

    SDS-4: Surface Design Fundamentals in V5
    Abstract: This hands-on training session will cover the fundamentals involved in designing surfaced parts with Version 5. The latest release of CATIA is a new and powerful tool that is revolutionizing the CAD industry. Using Genesis' unique approach to Version 5 education, students will learn how easy they can get started in this new technology. Get jump started in V5 surfacing with this session!! Although students must pre-register for this class, all in attendance will receive a copy of the training aids and access to the training models.
    Speaker: Tom Cisler, Genesis Modeling Technologies

    TR-1: Part Design Fundamentals in V5
    Abstract: This hands-on training session will cover the fundamentals involved in designing parts with Version 5. The latest release of CATIA is a new and powerful tool that is revolutionizing the CAD industry. Using Genesis' unique approach to Version 5 education, students will learn how easy they can get started in the new technology. Get jump started in V5 with this session! Although students must pre-register for this class, all in attendance will receive a copy of the training aids and access to the training models.
    Speaker: Tom Cisler, Genesis Modeling Technologies

    TR-2: Leveraging the V5 CATIA Instructor
    Abstract: Even with the new CBT programs for learning CATIA, the on hand instructor still remains one of the leading resources for learning CATIA. But even the best instructors are limited on the number of students that they can influence. Self study training and reference guides can leverage CATIA knowledge, but the front end instruction (demo) and the on-the-job training has been missing. In this session we will demonstrate our new V5 On Demand computer based lecture series. Utilizing this three step approach (demo,work through, reference)frees up instructors for on-the-job training.
    Speaker: Stephan Lewis, Think CAD Bleu

    TR-3: An Introduction to FD&T
    Abstract: This hands-on session provides an overview of the Functional Dimensioning and Tolerancing(FD&T) function in Version 4 CATIA. Learn how to create dimensions, tolerances and datums on a solid part, that can be viewed when in the 3D space window.
    Speaker: Jim Steffens, Genesis Modeling Technologies

    TR-5: Analyzing a Version V5 Assembly
    Abstract: In this session attendees will learn how to analyze and reconnect V5 assembly constraints, how to measure components of an assembly and how to check for interferences between two components.
    Speaker: Jim Steffens, Genesis Modeling Technologies

    COE Academy Tracks

    Academy 1: Installing CATIA V4&V5 in a Networked Environment
    Participants will install the UNIX and NT machines used for COE's hands on training sessions. Some of the topics covered will be: Checking system hardware and software prerequisites. NFS and NIS concepts. Installing CATIA V4 and V5 from scratch. Editing Declarations. Building separate CATIA environments.
    This presentation to being done by the Hardware/Software PIC.
    Speakers: Craig Barney, IST; Frank Garcia, IST; Gary Hornyak, IBM; Kent Land, Tower Automotive

    Academy 2: NT/UNIX Interoperability and Practical Points
    This AES academy session will cover NT/UNIX interoperability and practical points. It will include file sharing, password synching, and will be CATIA specific.
    Speaker: Mike Mounier, AES

    Academy 3: Basic Introduction to Product Data Management
    Through this academy session, attendees will be presented with the key elements of data management and explore PDM-related concepts and processes. This speaker will briefly introduce Dassault Systemes' and ENOVIA's suite of PDM products (CATIA/CDM, ENOVIA/VPM1, ENOVIA PM and ENOVIA V5) and the potential of integrating PDM solutions to other product-related information systems used throughout the product life-cycle. A basic overview of the ENOVIA VPM1 product will follow with a hands on session using VPM1 configuration module.
    Speaker: Ianic Brisson, ACT

    Academy 4: Using Visual Basic to Customize V5
    Visual Basic will be the programming language used in V5 customization. Attend this VBasic class, which will be presented in the context of CATIA V5 customization, to learn how you can customize V5 to your way of doing business.
    Speaker: Belkacem Chahboune, ACT

    Academy 5: How to Setup and Customize CATIA Team PDM
    This session will address customization, including: handling objects, life cycle management, linking objects, searching and creating a new search, creating views, mapping properties, and overview to the system administrator functionality.
    Speaker: Salvatore Gianella, AES

    Academy 6: CATIA V5 Installation Academy
    If you have ever installed CATIA V4, you're in for a treat. In this session we will explore the process to install CATIA V5 on Windows NT. We will discuss topics like the prerequisite environments for V5, installing CATIA both locally and from the network, locating where the code actually goes, creating environments, managing licenses and managing settings. Remember, V5 isn't your daddy's CATIA!
    Speaker: Doug Harrings, IBM

    Academy 7: SMARTEAM from Smart Solutions: Windows and Web-Based PDM and e-collaboration
    This session is designed to give attendees a product overview and demonstration of SMARTEAM as well as offer hands-on training covering both end-user functionality and implementation. Topics will include: searching for standard parts; creating and saving new documents, files, parts, and assemblies; vaulting a design package; approving a drawing; mapping title blocks to SMARTEAM; reviewing a sample ECO process using SmartFlow; and discussing implementation roadblocks, common mistakes, and shortcuts. The session will also include a general overview of PDM and product case studies.
    Speakers: Guy Shani, SMARTEAM John Martin, SMARTEAM
