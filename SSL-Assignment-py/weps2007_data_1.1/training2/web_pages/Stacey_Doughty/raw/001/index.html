Coventry City Ladies Football Club

June 19

SKY BLUE LADIES APPOINT NEW BOSS BY KEV MONKS
 
Coventry City Ladies have appointed Paul Barnes as their new Head Coach. He is a City supporter who saw his first game at home to Arsenal (0-0) in 1982 and has Football League experience with Exeter City and non-league experience with Tamworth, Atherstone and Sutton Coldfield Town.
 
Barnes works as the Football Development Officer for North Warwickshire.as well for the Coventry City Centre of Excellence and Active Sport and takes over from Dave Cartwright and will be overseeing an open training session for players aged 15 or over at Henley College on Sunday July 6 at 9.30 am.

June 16

Coventry City Ladies Football Club and Coventry City Girls FC merge

Coventry City can now boast one of the largest Ladies football clubs in the country after their merger was approved at their AGM last weekend.

Coventry City Girls FC was set up by Coventry City Ladies in 1995 to provide talent for the Ladies team as players progressed, and over subsequent years further age groups were added. Next season over two hundred players and fourteen squads will represent the club across the midlands and in national competitions.

Coventry City Ladies was founded in 1991 and in the forthcoming season will play in the Midlands Combination League. A reserve team is also being established from rising talent from last season’s U16 League Cup winning squad.

Martin Igoe, one of the clubs founders and Vice-Chairman said of the merger "it was a simple decision to make, we have a thriving junior section providing a stream of talent for the senior squad. Many of the ladies wanted to become more involved with the juniors in terms of coaching and for their own development. We also studied the top ladies football clubs in the UK and sought advice from professionals in America who advised us we would be a much more powerful club if we joined forces and shared our talents".

End of Season April/May

Coventry City Girls have made the City of Coventry proud over the last fortnight with a fantastic treble cup haul over local rivals The Under 12’s comfortably beat Birmingham City ladies U12 2-0 with outstanding goals from India Koller and Stacey Doughty. It was always going to be a tight affair after the Sky Blues edged out Blues 1-0 the previous Saturday in the league.  Any Birmingham pressure was comfortably repelled by the Coventry defence, superbly marshaled by Kathy Pollock.  

Manager Brian Ellis said after the game: “The hard-working Coventry U12’s never took their foot off the gas and finished worthy winners. Now we must concentrate on securing the runners’ up spot in the league. Manager Brian Ellis said after the game: “The hard-working Coventry U12’s never took their foot off the gas and finished worthy winners. Now we must concentrate on securing the runners’ up spot in the league.

 The Under 14’s City took Wolves Ladies U14 right to the wire, equalizing in the last minute to take the game into extra time to win 3-2 with a golden goal! Coventry girls were completely dominated by Wolves in the first half and were lucky to be only one goal adrift at half time. Straight from the kick off Wolves increased their lead to two nil with a very well crafted goal. There looked no way back for Coventry, and Wolves looked like increasing their lead further. Coventry was then awarded a penalty for pushing. Emma Lipman coolly gave the goalkeeper no chance with an immaculate spot kick Coventry then started to believe in themselves and it was Coventry now dominating Wolves. With two minutes left on the clock England International Kelly Anne Willis took a corner and curled the ball straight into the net for the equalizer. Five minutes into extra time Katherine Vernon chased the ball down the right flank and managed a cross into All Forbes in the six yard box. Ally made no mistake and won the cup for Coventry. Manager Andy Vernon was delighted with the win: “The mood was summed up by the entire squad on the coach journey home, by singing ‘two nil down, three two up, what a way to win the cup!’

The Under 16’s Sky Blues faced top of the table Lichfield Diamonds, and got off to the best possible to start when Leanne Mckenna headed home a Jenny Igoe corner after three minutes. Lichfield created a couple of chances one hit the post after the Coventry defence was caught square after twenty minutes. Lichfield were able to put the ball in the net after half an hour but the goal was disallowed when the Coventry goalie Zoë Sykes was fouled. Carly Nevin and Leanne Mckenna went close for Coventry before the break but the half time score was 1 - 0.

After ten minutes of the second half Lichfield equalized when Coventry gave the ball away on the half way line, the ball was played over the Coventry defence and the pacey Lichfield striker lobbed ‘keeper Zoë Sykes from twenty yards. Coventry soon found themselves trailing by two goals to one when Lichfield broke down the right wing drove the ball past the keeper to give Lichfield the lead. With 90 seconds remaining Amanda Whalley equalized sending the game into extra time.

After the first five minutes of golden goal a long ball forward from the Coventry defence was flicked on by Mia Graham, Lauren Lockwood went clear to flick the ball over the Lichfield keeper and send the Coventry line ecstatic. Manager Tom Williamson was one of those: “We prepared well for this game and worked hard in training. Our girls deserved to win this, we are all proud of them for ensuring the City Girls secured a fine treble”<o:p></o:p>

Currently top of the league, the team is now gunning for the league and cup double, for the first time in the U16’s history.

We have had a great season so far, but it’s not over yet. You can see that we are chasing top positions in our respective tables and the U10’s are competing in a national competition over Easter. We hope to be able to report more great news before the end of the season, but even better we will bring the silverware to show you before the end of the season. Sing up and make the girls proud when you see them, they deserve your support. The Stoke game will feature some of our youngest players, running on ‘World Cup style’ to bring the lads some end of season luck, and the Gillingham game will feature some of our girls in an exhibition match before the game and at half time will parade the silverware haul of the season, so once more we’re hoping for your good voices around Highfield Road to reward their fantastic season so far.

'Sing Your Hearts Out For The Girls! '

Coventry City Girls FC are looking for the support of the City faithful as they bring home a haul of silverware to Highfield Road. 

The U10, U12, U14 and U16 will all parade their representative Open Cup Trophies at half time at the Gillingham game after they have toured Coventry in an open top bus arriving at the ground at 1.15pm.

They will leave after the ground giving the fans something to cheer.

It will be an exhilarating experience for the girls who have enjoyed a terrific season scooping top honours in tough competitions, and the possibility of more to come.

“Sing your hearts out for the girls” is the call from the Coventry City Girls Chairman David Singleton, (Pictured above).

“For those who have had the joy of being on the pitch before parading a trophy for their respective cup winning teams, it creates a life long memory. The girls of 2001 still remember the West End bowing to the U14 winning squad and at the Dundee game this season the U10’s did it all again. It would be a triumphant finish to our season to hear the home crowd sing out to the girls, they represent the City well all over the Midlands and in many cases across the country. Let’s All Sing Together!”

Coventry City Girls FC would like to thank Jo Lea and Mike McGinnity for their support this season. We aim to ‘realise the footballing dreams for the girls in Coventry’, and they have made many things possible despite all that is going on in the club. Our commitment to CCFC and the fans in Coventry is to continue to play hard and make Ladies football famous for the right reasons in the heart of the Midlands.
