Edward Fox
  • The link between Edward Fox and our ancestor, Justinian Fox, is circumstantial, but seems correct.
  • Edward Fox was a clothmaker, as was his brother Richard.

    Richard was a long time member of the Society of Friends, as was Francis Fox, the father of James Fox, who brought the 13 year old Justinian with him to the new Pennsylvania colony in 1686.
  • Edward married Orringe Prest on June 7, 1664, and they had a daughter, Susanna, 3/1664.
  • He married again, Mary Ball, May 25, 1667. The parish baptism records for St. Charles Church, in Plymouth show that Justinian Fox was baptized December 19, 1673. Another brother, John (4/1668), and sister, Naomi (4/1674), were baptized at St. Andrews Church, Plymouth, England.
  • Other official records concerning Edward Fox include:
    • An Exchequer Bill in connection with the collection of the customs at Plymouth in 1658.
    • From the Probate Registry of Exeter, England, in 1689, there appears a Letter of Administration, on the estate of Joseph Fox, of Plymouth, "Cloathyer," granted to one Peter Foote with an appended "renunciation" by "Edward Fox of Plymouth, Worted-comber uncle and next of kine." Joseph Fox was the son of Richard Fox, the previously mentioned brother of Edward.
    • A lease, dated September 2, 1700, for 63 years, taken by Edward Fox, of Plymouth, a Clothier," for a messauge, brewhouse, and appurtenances, situated in the town of Plymouth, near "the Great Tree."
  • The relationship between Edward Fox and Francis Fox, and subsequently between their respective sons, Justinian Fox and James Fox, is not known. The ties seem close though as the names of Richard and Francis Fox occur as witnesses to marriages on the same occasions and Richard Fox (Edward's brother) witnessed the marriage of Francis Fox's son.
The previous data was extracted from: The North American, Philadelphia, Sunday, 7 April, 1912 "Old Philadelphia Families: cv. FOX" By Frank William Leach.
