National Service in Ohio

Meeting community needs in Ohio. More than 59,000 people of all ages and backgrounds are helping to solve problems and strengthen communities through 133 national service projects across Ohio. Serving with national and community nonprofit organizations, faith-based groups, schools, and local agencies, these citizens tutor and mentor children, coordinate after-school programs, build homes, conduct neighborhood patrols, organize local homeland security efforts, respond to disasters, and recruit and manage volunteers. This year, the Corporation for National and Community Service will provide more than $21,800,000 to support Ohio communities through three through three major programs:

Senior Corps: More than 19,000 seniors in Ohio contribute their time and talents in one of three programs: Foster Grandparents, who serve one-on-one with more than 5,000 young people who have special needs; Senior Companions, who help more than 900 other seniors live independently in their homes; and RSVP volunteers, who work with more than 1,900 local groups to meet a wide range of community needs.

AmeriCorps: This year more than 1,200 individuals are serving in Ohio communities as AmeriCorps members. In return, AmeriCorps members receive an education award of up to $4,725 to help pay for college or pay back student loans. To date, more than 6,400 Ohio residents have qualified for education awards totaling more than $27,000,000. Two-thirds of AmeriCorps grants are made by the Ohio Community Service Council. National nonprofits make other assignments. Other members serve in AmeriCorps*VISTA, and AmeriCorps*NCCC (National Civilian Community Corps).

Learn and Serve America: Learn and Serve America provides schools, colleges, and community organizations with grants for service-learning. These grants help make it possible for more than 39,000 Ohio students to meet community needs while improving their civic and and academic skills and learning the habits of good citizenship.
