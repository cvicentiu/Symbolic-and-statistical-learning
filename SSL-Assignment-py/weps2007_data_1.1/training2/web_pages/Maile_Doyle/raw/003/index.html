Community Connection was created to help Findlay and Hancock County residents find out more about what is going on in the community. Organizations like the United Way, Arts Partnership and Hancock Park District keep viewers up to date about the activities everyone can be a part of. Debuting in the spring of 2002 as a monthly, this season we will bring viewers a bi-monthly show to keep events more fresh in everyone’s mind.


Hosts:

Kimberly Ritter is a sophomore at The University of Findlay, where she is involved with the UF dance team and UF-TV. She is majoring in communication with an emphasis in broadcast telecommunications. Ritter graduated from McComb High School in 2002. She attends McComb United Methodist Church, where she serves as a youth group leader, leads the children's singing, teaches the college Sunday School class and serves as the wedding assistant. She is employed at Stacy Insurance Agency. In her free time (which isn't much), Ritter tutors and enjoys reading, watching movies, shopping and spending time with friends and family.


Guests:

Maile Doyle is the Director of the Volunteer Center, United Way of Hancock County since April 1996. Before joining the United Way, she was the Managing Partner for KIDS II, a children's resale business in Findlay.

One of seven children, Maile is an “Army brat" raised all over the world. She has lived in Panama, Vietnam, South Korea and Germany, as well as many places in the United States. Her husband, Bill Doyle’s job with Marathon Oil Company brought her back to Findlay in September of 1990.

She began her lifelong commitment to volunteerism at the age of 14 when her parents encouraged her to help at a Vietnamese orphanage with babies desperately in need of human contact. The experience hooked her on the truly wonderful feeling derived from helping another human being.

She is married, the mother of two and the grandmother of six.

Throughout her life she has volunteered for her church, schools, youth organizations, health organizations, social organizations and the United Way.
