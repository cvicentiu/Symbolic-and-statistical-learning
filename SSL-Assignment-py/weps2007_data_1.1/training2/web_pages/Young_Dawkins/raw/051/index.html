How to Achieve Eternal Happiness in Development

Young Dawkins
President
University of New Hampshire Foundation

Description
As a seasoned advancement officer, Young shares his perspective on how to get happy and stay happy in a demanding profession. Young offers humorous and thoughtful insights, based on 20 years of experience at a variety of institutions, on how to fully enjoy our profession and ourselves.

Sponsored by Columbia Management Group.

Biography
Young Dawkins was named UNH Foundation president in September 1997. In 1999, under his leadership, the Foundation launched The Next Horizon: the Campaign for the University of New Hampshire, the most ambitious fundraising effort in the University's history. In just over two years, the campaign has raised $74 million of its $100 million goal. Before joining the UNH Foundation, Young served as Vice President for Development, Alumni Affairs, and Communications at Oberlin College. He began his development career at Dartmouth College in 1983, where he worked for ten years as a major gift officer and director. He is a 1997 recipient of the CASE Stueben Apple for Excellence in Teaching, and a past member of the CASE District I Board of Trustees. Young, who has authored several articles on major gift fundraising, received his Bachelor and Masters Degrees from Dartmouth College. He is also the 2001 recipient of the Seacoast Writers Association award for poetry.
