UNH Magazine in a nutshell

Many alumni remember the New Hampshire Alumnus, the university's alumni magazine for 60 years. But the Alumnus was actually not UNH's first magazine. That honor goes to the New Hampshire State Alumnus, which debuted in 1924. The second Alumnus ran until 1991, while its companion publication -- aptly named the Alumni Companion -- was published from 1988 until Fall 1998, when it was replaced by the new UNH Magazine.

UNH Magazine is published by the UNH Alumni Association and the Office of the UNH President, with funding from both offices. It is produced by the Alumni Association's publications office.

Published in fall, winter and spring, it is distributed to all alumni -- who currently number over 100,000 -- parents of current students, faculty, staff, donors, members of the Alumni Association's dues program, New Hampshire elected officials and business leaders.

In 2004, the magazine won the gold medal for best magazine from the Council for Advancement and Support of Education (CASE) District I, which includes New England and eastern Canada.

Also in 2004, the magazine won a national bronze medal for best periodical staff writing from CASE, for articles written in 2003 by Suki Casanave '86G and Ginny Stuart '75, '80G.

In 2000, the magazine was awarded American Society of Association Executives Gold Circle Award certificates for feature writing for "Inventing the e-Coast" (Spring 2000) and "See Me, Hear Me: How Can a Campus Isn't Very Diverse Come to Embrace Diversity?" (Spring 2000).

In 1999, the magazine won a gold medal for most improved magazine from the national Council for Advancement and Support of Education (CASE). The magazine also won a gold medal in 2000 for a series of advertisements for the Alumni Association's dues program from CASE District I.

The mission of the UNH Magazine is to "provide a window into the University of New Hampshire, and to tell thought-provoking stories of its people and its mission-related issues of teaching and learning, research, and public service."

Recycled paper in UNH Magazine

UNH Magazine is printed on recycled paper, effective with the Spring 2004 issue.

This is in keeping with the rest of UNH, which uses recycled paper whenever possible. (See UNH Printing's page on recycled paper use.)
