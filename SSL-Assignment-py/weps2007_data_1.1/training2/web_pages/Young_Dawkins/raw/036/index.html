There are many ways to make a gift to the University.

Cash Gifts

The simplest way to support the University is with a cash gift. Send your personal check to the UNH Annual Fund, UNH Foundation, John S. Elliott Alumni Center, Durham, NH 03824. Cash gifts are tax deductible according to federal law. You can also make a gift with a credit card. Many companies match cash gifts. Please check with your employer.

For more information: Contact Morgan Dudley, director, The Annual fund via phone (603) 862-1662 or e-mail: mwdudley@cisunix.unh.edu or click here to make a gift online.

Appreciated Securities

A gift of appreciated securities provides an income tax deduction equal to the fair market value of the donated securities. Capital gains taxes may also be avoided on the transfer.

For more information: Contact Diana Koski, vice president and director of planned giving via phone (603) 862-2570 or e-mail: ddkoski@cisunix.unh.edu or click here for information about how to transfer securities.


Real and Personal Property

Land or other real property may be given to the Foundation as an outright gift or used as an asset within a charitable trust. One can also opt to donate a home and retain the right to occupy the property throughout one’s lifetime. Personal property, such as paintings or rare books, can also be contributed. If you are interested in discussing the advantages of such a gift, please contact Young Dawkins III, president, at (603) 862-3694.

Planned Gifts

Planned gifts, such as charitable remainder trusts and gifts to the UNH Foundation Pooled Income Fund, benefit the University and can provide donors with income for life. Donors can also enjoy significant benefits, including tax deductions, avoidance of capital gains taxes, and increased income. For more information on planned gifts, please contact Diana Koski, vice president and director of planned giving, at (603) 862-2570 or ddkoski@cisunix.unh.edu.

Bequests
A gift to the University in a will allows donors to make a substantial contribution to the University without diminishing available assets. A bequest can also significantly reduce estate taxes.

Pooled Income Fund
Gifts of $5,000 or more to the Foundation's Pooled Income Fund are invested together with gifts of other donors, and the annual income is shared among participants throughout their lifetimes. Participants incur no capital gain on appreciated securities transferred to the fund, and receive a charitable deduction for a portion of the gift.

Charitable Trusts
Irrevocable charitable trusts can provide an income for life that varies with the type and assets of the trust. Part of the trust qualifies for an income tax deduction. At the death of the last beneficiary, the remainder of the trust is distributed to the University for the purpose selected by the donor.

Life Insurance
A life insurance policy is deductible as a charitable contribution. Premiums paid after the gift is made are also tax deductible.

Matching Gifts

Many companies/employers will match gifts for education. If yours is one, please complete the matching gift form supplied by your employer and send it to the UNH Foundation.

Matching gift instructions:

1. Obtain a matching gift form from your employer. (Please check with your human resources department.)

2. Fill it out completely.

3. Mail it to the UNH Foundation, Elliot Alumni Center, 9 Edgewood Road, Durham, NH 03824-1987.

See your company's matching gift form for full information on matching gift eligibility and program limitations.
