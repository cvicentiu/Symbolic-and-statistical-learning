SAINTS V SKIOLARS MATCH REPORT
Wednesday 1 Feb 2006 22:35
by Rugby League Ireland
Source: ConneXions

Full Time Score: St.Helens 42 London Skolars 16

A large crowd of both Irish and English Rugby League fans watched an exciting game of rugby league in Donnbrook this afternoon. However it proved to be a disappointing game for Vinnie O, Dowd and his Irish fans as the Blackrock and former Lansdowne player went off injured in the 47th minute.

The London Skolars failed to breakdown the mighty St Helens in a game that started at a frenetic pace with St Helens doing most of the attacking. Their constant pressure on the Skolars half paid off after four minutes when Ian Webster scored a try which was converted by Ian Hardman.

It wasn't until the 15th minute that Skolars got into the Saints half with Mike Castle scoring a try right in the corner. Paul Clough converted smoothly from a very difficult angle.

Great movement and solid passing made the Saints look the better team. Although the Skolars showed tremendous strength in their tackles, it was usually a poor final ball that stopped most of their attacks. The Saints lost Craig Little to a bad Knee injury in the 20th minute which resulted in him being replaced by the number 17, Dave Hall who had one of his best games for the club.

After 24 minutes Ste Bannister scored another Try for the Saints under the posts again but was given a thumping shoulder charge by Matt Pitman after he had touched down. Ian Hardman again made the easy conversion for the Saints to lead 12-6.

With 10 minutes to go in the 1st half, a great run by Ian Webster ultimately set up Dave Hull for another Try by St Helens which Hardman failed to convert.

Another great break by Sean Weed just before the half time whistle and a conversion made it 22- 6 to the Saints.

5th minute into the second half and Dave Hull scored his second try making the score 26-6 and Gareth Langley made it 30-6 10 minutes later running the length of the field for the try of the match.

Skolars Nathan Meischke almost scored a thrilling try in the 52nd minute when he too charged the length of the pitch, only to be stopped on the line by the heroic efforts of Saints Dave Hull who appeared to be having the match of his life.

Skolars Austin Aggrey finally put his team on the scoreboard for the first time in the second half in the 20th minute when he touched down for a badly needed try, unfortunately though for the Skolars, Ben Joyce failed to convert.

15 minutes to go and Paul Leyland scored another try in what was becoming a Saints routing of the Skolars. Hardman converted again.

Another spectacular try by Andy Smith for the Saints continued Skolars miserable afternoon with Hardman converting again in the 29th minute.

The Skolars fought back with a gallant run from South African Rubert Jonker from his own line but was caught 20 metres from the line and the pass went nowhere.

They scored a consolation 3rd try by Matt Pitman in the last minute which Ben Joyce failed to convert to make the final score St Helens 42, London Skolars 14.

Skolars battled bravely to the end but they were outclassed. It was a comprehensive victory from the premier league side which showed why they are regarded as one of the finest clubs in the Rugby League world.
